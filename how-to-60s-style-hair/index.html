How To 60S Style Hair Relaxed English Language Guide

If you're looking to add a touch of retro-chic to your hairstyle, 60s style hair is the way to go. This classic look is elegant, sophisticated, and perfect for any occasion. In this guide, we'll go through everything you need to know about how to 60s style hair, from the basics to advanced techniques. Whether you're a beginner or an experienced hairstylist, this guide will help you achieve the perfect 60s look.

Table of Contents:

  • How to 60s Style Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for 60s Style Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for 60s Style Hair

How to 60s Style Hair

Before we get started, it's important to understand what 60s style hair is. This look is all about volume, texture, and movement. To achieve this look, you'll need to use a combination of teasing, backcombing, and curling.

The first step is to choose the right hairstyle. The most popular 60s hairstyles include:

  • The beehive
  • The bouffant
  • The flipped-out bob
  • The pixie cut
  • The long, loose waves

Once you've chosen your hairstyle, it's time to get to work.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to help you achieve the perfect 60s style hair:

  1. Start by washing your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. This will give your hair the texture and volume it needs for the 60s look.
  2. Blow-dry your hair until it's about 80% dry. Use a round brush to lift the roots and create volume.
  3. Section your hair into small sections.
  4. Starting at the crown of your head, backcomb each section of hair. This will create volume and texture.
  5. Once you've backcombed your entire head, smooth the top layer of your hair over the backcombed sections.
  6. Use a curling iron or hot rollers to curl your hair. For the 60s look, use large rollers or a large-barrel curling iron.
  7. Once your hair is curled, gently brush out the curls to create soft, loose waves.
  8. Use a strong-hold hairspray to set your style in place.

Tips for 60s Style Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect 60s style hair:

  • Use a volumizing mousse or spray before blow-drying your hair for extra volume.
  • Don't be afraid to backcomb your hair. This is the key to achieving the 60s look.
  • Use a large-barrel curling iron for loose, natural-looking waves.
  • Finish off your style with a headband, scarf, or barrette for a touch of retro-chic.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about 60s style hair:

What hair length is best for 60s style hair?

60s style hair can be achieved on any hair length, from short pixie cuts to long, flowing locks. The key is to add volume and texture to your hair.

Can I achieve the 60s style hair without teasing or backcombing?

Teasing and backcombing are essential for achieving the 60s look. However, if you're not comfortable with these techniques, you can try using a volumizing powder or spray instead.

How long will the 60s style hair last?

The 60s style hair can last for several days with the right maintenance. Use a dry shampoo to keep your hair looking fresh and voluminous between washes.

Hair Care for 60s Style Hair

Here are some tips for taking care of your hair after styling it with the 60s look:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using hot tools like curling irons or straighteners every day to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Avoid using too much hairspray or other styling products, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.

With these tips and techniques, you'll be able to achieve the perfect 60s style hair in no time. Whether you're going for a retro-chic look or just want to add some volume and texture to your hair, this style is sure to turn heads.
