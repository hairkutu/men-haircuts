How To Cut Men s Longer Hair With Clippers A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Apr 04, 2023 · 8 min read

Cutting men's longer hair with clippers can be a daunting task, especially if you've never done it before. But with the right tools, techniques, and a little bit of practice, it's definitely doable. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process of cutting men's longer hair with clippers, from start to finish. We'll cover everything from how to prepare your tools and set up your workspace, to how to create different styles and keep your hair looking healthy.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Preparing Your Tools and Workspace
  • Sectioning Your Hair
  • Clipping Your Hair
  • Creating Different Styles
  • Tips for Cutting Men's Longer Hair with Clippers
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care Tips

Tools You'll Need

Before you get started, you'll need to gather a few tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Clippers with various guard sizes
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Cape or a towel to cover your shoulders
  • Clipper oil
  • Brush

Make sure your clippers are well-maintained and oiled before you start. Dull blades can cause pulling and uneven cutting, which can be uncomfortable and frustrating for both you and your client.

Preparing Your Tools and Workspace

Find a clean, well-lit area to work in. Set up your tools within easy reach, and cover your shoulders with a cape or a towel to prevent hair clippings from getting on your clothes.

Make sure your clippers are clean and oiled before you start. This will help them run smoothly and prevent pulling.

Sectioning Your Hair

Start by sectioning your hair. Use your comb to create a part down the center of your head, dividing your hair into two sections. Then, create a horizontal part from ear to ear, dividing your hair into four sections.

Clip the top two sections of your hair out of the way, leaving the bottom two sections loose.

Clipping Your Hair

Start with the longest guard size and work your way down to shorter guard sizes. Use your clippers to trim the sides and back of your hair, moving in an upward motion.

Next, use your scissors to trim the top of your hair. Hold a small section of hair between your fingers and cut at an angle, moving from the back of your head to the front. Repeat this process until you've trimmed all the hair on the top of your head.

Creating Different Styles

Once you've mastered the basic technique of cutting men's longer hair with clippers, you can start experimenting with different styles. Here are a few popular styles you can try:

  • The Undercut: This style involves shaving the sides and back of your head, leaving the hair on top longer.
  • The Fade: This style involves gradually blending the hair on top with the hair on the sides and back.
  • The Buzz Cut: This style involves using a very short guard size to create an all-over, even cut.

Tips for Cutting Men's Longer Hair with Clippers

Here are a few tips to help you get the best results:

  • Start with a longer guard size and work your way down to shorter guard sizes, to avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • Use your scissors to blend the hair on top with the hair on the sides and back, for a more natural look.
  • Be patient and take your time, especially if you're new to cutting hair with clippers.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are a few common questions about cutting men's longer hair with clippers:

Do I need to wet my hair before cutting it with clippers?

No, you don't need to wet your hair before cutting it with clippers. In fact, cutting dry hair can help you see the length and texture of your hair more clearly.

What guard size should I use?

The guard size you use depends on the length of your hair and the style you're trying to achieve. Start with a longer guard size and work your way down to shorter guard sizes, until you find the right length.

Hair Care Tips

Now that you know how to cut men's longer hair with clippers, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking healthy. Here are a few tips:

  • Shampoo and condition your hair regularly, using products that are designed for your hair type.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking neat and tidy.

Cutting men's longer hair with clippers can be a fun and rewarding experience, especially if you're able to create different styles and keep your hair looking healthy. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve great results and feel confident in your hair-cutting skills.
