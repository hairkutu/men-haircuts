What Does Broided Mean In The Bible

If you've ever read the Bible, you may have come across the word "broided" and wondered what it means. In this article, we'll explore the meaning of "broided" in the Bible and its significance.

What Does Broided Mean in the Bible?

Broided is an old English word that means braided or woven. This word appears several times in the King James Version of the Bible, mainly in the Old Testament.

In Exodus 28:8, the Lord commands Moses to make the ephod of the priest with "broidered work of blue, and of purple, and of scarlet, and fine twined linen." This means that the ephod was to be made of a fabric that was woven with threads of different colors.

In Proverbs 7:16, a harlot is described as having "broided hair." This means that her hair was braided or woven.

Broided is also used in the Bible to describe other things, such as the curtains of the tabernacle (Exodus 26:36) and the girdles of the priests (Exodus 28:39).

Significance of Broided in the Bible

In the Bible, broided items are often associated with beauty, wealth, and status. The ephod of the priest, for example, was a garment of great importance and was used in the performance of religious duties. The broided work of blue, purple, scarlet, and fine twined linen was meant to signify the priest's authority and status.

The harlot with broided hair in Proverbs 7:16 is also a symbol of beauty and seduction. Her braided hair was meant to attract men and lure them into sin.

How to Broid Hair

If you're interested in braiding or weaving your hair, there are many tutorials and videos available online. Here are some basic steps to get you started:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Brush your hair to remove any tangles.
  3. Divide your hair into sections. The number of sections will depend on the style you want to create.
  4. Take one section of hair and divide it into three equal parts.
  5. Cross the right section over the middle section, then cross the left section over the middle section.
  6. Keep repeating steps 4 and 5 until you reach the end of your hair.
  7. Secure the end of the braid with a hair elastic or clip.

There are many different types of braids and weaves you can try, such as French braids, fishtail braids, and cornrows. Experiment with different styles to find the one that works best for you.

Tips for Broiding Hair

Here are some tips to help you broid your hair successfully:

  • Use a detangling spray or conditioner to make your hair easier to work with.
  • Start with small sections of hair to keep your braids neat and tidy.
  • Keep your braids tight to prevent them from unraveling.
  • Use hair elastics or clips to secure the end of your braids.
  • Experiment with different styles and techniques to find the one that works best for you.

FAQ

What is the difference between braiding and weaving?

Braiding involves crossing three or more strands of hair over each other in a pattern. Weaving involves interlacing two or more threads or strands of hair over and under each other in a pattern.

Can anyone braid their hair?

Yes, anyone can learn to braid their hair. It takes practice and patience, but with time, anyone can master the art of braiding.

Conclusion

If you're interested in braiding or weaving your hair, there are many tutorials and videos available online to help you get started. Experiment with different styles and techniques to find the one that works best for you.
