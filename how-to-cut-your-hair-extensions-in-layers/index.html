How To Cut Your Hair Extensions In Layers A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Jul 05, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you looking to add some layers to your hair extensions? Cutting your extensions in layers can give your hair more volume, texture, and dimension. In this guide, we’ll show you how to cut your hair extensions in layers without damaging them. Read on for tips, tricks, and step-by-step instructions.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Cut Your Hair Extensions in Layers
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Cutting Your Hair Extensions
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care After Cutting Your Extensions

How to Cut Your Hair Extensions in Layers

Cutting your hair extensions in layers can be a bit intimidating, but it’s easier than you might think. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • A comb
  • A mirror
  • Clips to section your hair

Before you start cutting, it’s important to decide on the length and shape of your layers. You can either cut straight across for a blunt look, or angle your scissors for a more textured, feathery finish. It’s also a good idea to practice on a small section of your extensions before diving in.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Extensions

Start by washing and drying your extensions. This will make them easier to work with and ensure that they’re clean and free of any product buildup.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use clips to section your hair into manageable sections. Start at the bottom and work your way up.

Step 3: Trim the Ends

Using your scissors, trim the very ends of your extensions. This will help to get rid of any split ends and make your hair look healthier.

Step 4: Cut the First Layer

Decide on the length of your first layer and cut it straight across. It’s best to start with a longer layer and work your way up.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Take a small section of hair from the first layer and comb it straight up. Cut the second layer at an angle, using the first layer as a guide. Repeat this process for each layer, blending as you go.

Step 6: Check the Layers

Once you’ve finished cutting your layers, check them in the mirror to make sure they’re even. Make any necessary adjustments.

Tips for Cutting Your Hair Extensions

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your extensions
  • Practice on a small section of hair before cutting the entire extension
  • Cut your hair extensions when they’re dry for a more accurate cut
  • Don’t cut too much off at once, as you can always go back and trim more later
  • Be patient and take your time, especially if you’re new to cutting your own hair

FAQs

Can you cut your extensions when they’re wet?

We recommend cutting your extensions when they’re dry, as wet hair can be more difficult to work with and may result in an uneven cut.

How often should you cut your hair extensions?

It’s best to trim your extensions every 6-8 weeks to keep them looking healthy and prevent split ends.

Can you cut curly hair extensions in layers?

Yes, you can cut curly hair extensions in layers. Just be sure to cut them when they’re dry and be careful not to cut too much off at once, as curly hair tends to shrink when it dries.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Extensions

After cutting your hair extensions, it’s important to take good care of them to keep them looking healthy and prevent damage. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your extensions gently with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Use a heat protectant spray when you do use heat styling tools
  • Brush your extensions gently with a wide-toothed comb or a boar bristle brush
  • Avoid sleeping with wet hair extensions

Cutting your hair extensions in layers can be a fun and easy way to change up your look. With these tips and tricks, you can achieve a professional-looking cut without leaving your home. Happy cutting!
