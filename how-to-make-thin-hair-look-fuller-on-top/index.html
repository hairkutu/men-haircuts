How To Make Thin Hair Look Fuller On Top

Written by Daniel Mar 23, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Do you have thin hair that lacks volume and fullness on top? Don't worry; you're not alone. Many people struggle with this issue, but the good news is that there are several ways to make your hair look fuller and thicker. In this blog post, we'll share some tips and tricks on how to make thin hair look fuller on top.

How to Make Thin Hair Look Fuller on Top

Here are a few tips and tricks to make your thin hair look fuller on top:

1. Use Volumizing Products

Using volumizing products such as mousse, root lifters, and volumizing sprays can help add volume to your hair. Apply these products to the roots of your hair, and then blow-dry your hair upside down to create more lift and volume.

2. Try a Different Haircut

A shorter haircut with layers can help create the illusion of fuller hair. Ask your hairstylist to add layers to your hair, focusing on the top and crown area. This will add texture and volume to your hair.

3. Tease Your Hair

Teasing your hair at the roots can help create more volume and fullness. Use a teasing comb to gently backcomb your hair at the roots, then smooth the top layer of hair over the teased section.

4. Use Hair Extensions

If you want an instant solution to thin hair, consider using hair extensions. Hair extensions can add volume and length to your hair, giving you a fuller and thicker look. Choose clip-in extensions that match your hair color and texture.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to make thin hair look fuller on top:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a volumizing shampoo and conditioner. These products will help add body and volume to your hair.

Step 2: Apply Volumizing Products

Apply a volumizing mousse or spray to the roots of your hair. Use your fingers to work the product into your hair, focusing on the top and crown area.

Step 3: Blow-Dry Your Hair Upside Down

Flip your head upside down and blow-dry your hair using a round brush. This will help create more lift and volume in your hair.

Step 4: Tease Your Hair

Use a teasing comb to gently backcomb your hair at the roots, focusing on the top and crown area. Be gentle to avoid damaging your hair.

Step 5: Smooth Your Hair

Smooth the top layer of your hair over the teased section, using a brush or comb. This will help create a seamless look and prevent tangling.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks to make your thin hair look fuller on top:

1. Use Dry Shampoo

Dry shampoo can help add volume to your hair by absorbing oil and adding texture. Spray dry shampoo at the roots of your hair, then tousle with your fingers to create volume.

2. Avoid Heavy Products

Avoid using heavy products such as oils and serums, as these can weigh down your hair and make it look flat. Stick to lightweight products that won't weigh your hair down.

3. Sleep with Your Hair in a Bun

Wrap your hair in a loose bun before going to bed. This will help create natural waves and volume in your hair.

FAQs

Q: Can I use hair extensions if I have thin hair?

A: Yes, you can use hair extensions even if you have thin hair. Just make sure to choose clip-in extensions that match your hair color and texture.

Q: Will teasing my hair damage it?

A: Teasing your hair can cause damage over time, so be gentle and avoid over-teasing your hair. Use a teasing comb with wide teeth to minimize damage.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and lifestyle. If you have thin hair, you may want to avoid washing your hair every day, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it look flat. Aim to wash your hair every other day or every three days.

Hair Care Tips for Thin Hair

Here are some additional hair care tips for thin hair:

1. Use a Wide-Toothed Comb

Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair, as this will minimize breakage and damage.

2. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools such as flat irons and curling irons, as these can damage your hair and make it look thinner. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.

3. Get Regular Trims

Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends. Aim to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks.

By following these tips and tricks, you can make your thin hair look fuller and thicker on top. Experiment with different hairstyles and products to find what works best for you.
