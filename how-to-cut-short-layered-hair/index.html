How To Cut Short Layered Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank May 15, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Cutting short layered hair can be a challenging task, especially if you don't have any experience. But with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a perfect layered haircut at home. In this guide, we'll show you how to cut short layered hair step by step.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • How to Cut Short Layered Hair: Step by Step
  • Tips for Cutting Short Layered Hair
  • FAQs: Cutting Short Layered Hair
  • Hair Care After Cutting Short Layered Hair

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Sectioning clips
  • Spray bottle filled with water
  • Flat iron (optional)

Make sure your scissors are sharp, as dull scissors can cause split ends and damage your hair. A comb and sectioning clips will help you create clean sections and keep the hair out of your face. A spray bottle filled with water will help you dampen the hair and make it easier to cut. A flat iron is optional, but can be helpful if you want to straighten your hair before cutting.

How to Cut Short Layered Hair: Step by Step

Follow these steps to cut short layered hair:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and towel drying it. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start cutting.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Use a comb to create a part down the center of your head. Then, create two more parts on each side of your head, about an inch above your ears. Clip the top section of your hair out of the way.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Layer

Starting with the bottom layer, comb the hair straight down and clip the ends. Use your scissors to cut the hair straight across, making sure to cut all the way through. This will create a blunt edge.

Step 4: Cut the Middle Layer

Release the middle section of your hair and comb it straight down. Use the bottom layer as a guide and cut the middle layer slightly shorter, about an inch above the bottom layer. This will create a layered effect.

Step 5: Cut the Top Layer

Release the top section of your hair and comb it straight down. Use the middle layer as a guide and cut the top layer slightly shorter, about an inch above the middle layer. This will create a more layered effect and add volume to your hair.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

Use your scissors to blend the layers together by cutting into the hair at a slight angle. This will help create a seamless transition between the layers.

Step 7: Check for Evenness

Use a mirror to check if your layers are even on both sides. If needed, make small adjustments to even them out.

Step 8: Style Your Hair

Style your hair as desired, using a flat iron or curling iron if needed.

Tips for Cutting Short Layered Hair

Here are some tips to help you achieve a perfect short layered haircut:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once, as this can cause unevenness.
  • Use a comb to create clean sections and keep the hair out of your face.
  • Make sure your scissors are sharp to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Check for evenness frequently to avoid mistakes.

FAQs: Cutting Short Layered Hair

How often should I cut my short layered hair?

You should cut your short layered hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain the layers and prevent split ends.

Can I cut my own short layered hair?

Yes, you can cut your own short layered hair if you have the right tools and techniques. However, if you're not confident in your skills, it's best to visit a professional hair stylist.

What if I make a mistake while cutting my short layered hair?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always make small adjustments to even out the layers. If the mistake is too drastic, you may need to visit a professional hair stylist to fix it.

Hair Care After Cutting Short Layered Hair

After cutting your short layered hair, it's important to take care of it properly to maintain the layers and prevent damage. Here are some tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can cause damage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or a detangling brush to avoid pulling on your hair.
  • Trim your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain the layers and prevent split ends.

Cutting short layered hair can be a fun and rewarding experience if done correctly. By following these steps and tips, you can achieve a perfect layered haircut at home. Remember to take care of your hair after cutting it to maintain the layers and prevent damage. Happy cutting!
