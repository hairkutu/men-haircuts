If you have short hair, you know how annoying it can be to wake up with your hair all over your face. Not only is it uncomfortable, but it can also ruin your hairdo. How to Keep Short Hair Out of Face While Sleeping: Step by Step

Step 1: Tie Your Hair Up

One of the easiest ways to keep your short hair out of your face while you sleep is to tie it up in a loose bun or ponytail. This will prevent your hair from falling onto your face while you toss and turn in your sleep. Make sure to use a soft hair tie or scrunchie to avoid any damage to your hair.

Step 2: Use a Headband

If you donât want to tie your hair up, you can also use a headband to keep your hair in place. Choose a headband thatâs comfortable and not too tight, and make sure to position it properly so that it covers your hairline and keeps your hair away from your face.

Step 3: Try a Silk Pillowcase

Another great way to keep your hair out of your face while you sleep is to use a silk pillowcase. Silk is a smooth, soft fabric that wonât cause friction or pull on your hair, which can lead to breakage and split ends. Plus, a silk pillowcase is gentle on your skin and can help prevent wrinkles and acne.

Tips for Keeping Short Hair Out of Face While Sleeping

Tip 1: Trim Your Hair Regularly

One of the easiest ways to keep your short hair out of your face while you sleep is to keep it trimmed and tidy. Regular trims will help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends, which can make your hair more prone to falling onto your face.

Tip 2: Use Hair Products

Using hair products like hairspray or hair gel can help keep your hair in place while you sleep. Apply a small amount of product to your hair before you go to bed, and use a comb or brush to style your hair away from your face.

Tip 3: Sleep on Your Back

If youâre a restless sleeper, try sleeping on your back instead of your side or stomach. This will help keep your hair in place and prevent it from falling onto your face while you sleep.

Frequently Asked Questions

What kind of hair tie is best for keeping short hair out of your face?

A soft, fabric hair tie or scrunchie is best for keeping short hair out of your face. Avoid using elastic bands or tight hair ties, which can cause breakage and damage to your hair.

Can I use a regular pillowcase instead of a silk one?

Yes, you can use a regular cotton pillowcase, but keep in mind that cotton can cause friction and pull on your hair, which can lead to tangles and breakage. A silk pillowcase is a better option for keeping your hair smooth and tangle-free.

What should I do if my hair still falls onto my face while I sleep?

If your hair still falls onto your face while you sleep, try using a clip or bobby pin to secure your hair in place. You can also try a different hairstyle or hair product to keep your hair in place while you sleep.

Hair Care for Short Hair

Keeping your short hair in good condition is essential for preventing breakage and split ends. Here are a few tips for caring for your short hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and keep your hair looking tidy

By following these tips and tricks, you can keep your short hair out of your face while you sleep and wake up looking fresh and fabulous.
