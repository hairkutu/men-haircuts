How To Cut Girl Hair Straight A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel May 29, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of uneven or crooked haircuts? Cutting girl hair straight can be a challenge, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done at home. This guide will walk you through the steps to achieve a straight, professional-looking haircut for your little girl.

Table of Contents

  1. Tools You'll Need
  2. Prepping the Hair
  3. Sectioning the Hair
  4. Cutting the Hair
  5. Blending the Hair
  6. Finishing Touches
  7. Tips for Success
  8. FAQs
  9. Hair Care After the Cut

Tools You'll Need

Before getting started, you'll need a few essential tools:

  • Sharp haircutting scissors
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Comb
  • Sectioning clips or hair ties
  • Spray bottle with water
  • Sharpie marker (optional)

Make sure your scissors are sharp and clean to prevent split ends or damage to the hair. Clippers can be helpful for cutting the hair shorter, but are not necessary for a straight cut.

Prepping the Hair

Start by washing and drying the hair as you normally would. Brush out any tangles or knots, and part the hair down the middle to create two even sections. If the hair is particularly thick or long, you can use a sharpie marker to draw a straight line at the desired length to use as a guide.

Sectioning the Hair

Using your comb, create a horizontal parting at the nape of the neck, and clip the top section out of the way. Take a small section of hair at the bottom of the neck, and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers, and use your scissors to cut straight across at the desired length. Repeat this process, working your way up the back of the head, until you have cut all of the hair in the bottom section.

Once you have finished the bottom section, release the top section and repeat the process, working from the back to the front of the head. Make sure to use the same cutting technique and angle on both sides of the head to ensure a straight cut.

Cutting the Hair

When cutting the hair, it's important to use the right technique. Hold the hair between your fingers, and cut at a slight angle to create a soft, blended look. Avoid cutting straight across, as this can create a blunt, choppy look.

If you're using clippers, start with the longest guard and work your way down to the desired length. Use a comb to guide the clippers, and make sure to keep the blade of the clippers parallel to the scalp for an even cut.

Blending the Hair

Once you have cut all of the hair to the desired length, it's time to blend the layers together to create a seamless look. Take small sections of hair and comb them straight up, holding them between your fingers. Use your scissors to gently trim any longer pieces until the layers blend together smoothly.

Finishing Touches

After you have finished cutting and blending the hair, it's time to add any finishing touches. Use your scissors to trim any stray hairs or uneven pieces, and make sure the hair is even on both sides. If desired, you can add layers or texture to the hair using thinning shears or a razor.

Tips for Success

  • Start with small sections of hair, and cut slowly to avoid mistakes
  • Use a spray bottle to keep the hair damp while cutting, as this will make it easier to control
  • Take breaks if you need to, as cutting hair can be tiring
  • Use a mirror to check your progress from all angles
  • Be patient and take your time to achieve the best results

FAQs

Can I cut my daughter's hair straight myself?

Yes, with the right tools and techniques, cutting girl hair straight can be done at home. Just make sure to take your time and follow the steps carefully.

What if I make a mistake while cutting?

Mistakes can happen, but don't panic. If you make a mistake, try to blend the hair together to hide the mistake. If it's a major mistake, you may need to take your child to a professional stylist to fix it.

How often should I cut my daughter's hair?

This depends on the length and style of the hair, as well as your child's individual needs. As a general rule, it's recommended to cut hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Hair Care After the Cut

After cutting your daughter's hair, it's important to take care of it properly to maintain the style and prevent damage. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Regular trims every 6-8 weeks will also help keep the hair healthy and prevent split ends.

By following these steps and tips, you can achieve a straight, professional-looking haircut for your little girl right at home. With a little patience and practice, you'll be a pro in no time!
