How Do I Thin Out Curly Hair

Written by William Apr 30, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have curly hair, you know how difficult it can be to manage. Sometimes, it can even get overwhelming, especially if you have thick curly hair. One solution to make it easier to manage is to thin out your hair. In this blog post, we will discuss how to thin out curly hair and provide tips and tricks to make it easier to maintain.

Table of Contents

  • How to Thin Out Curly Hair
  • Step by Step Process

How to Thin Out Curly Hair

Before we dive into the step-by-step process, we need to understand what thinning out curly hair means. Thinning out hair involves cutting some of the hair's bulk, making it more manageable and less heavy. Here are some ways to thin out curly hair.

Cutting with Scissors

The most common way to thin out curly hair is by cutting it with scissors. However, this should only be done by a professional hairstylist. They will use thinning scissors to cut the hair in a way that will remove some of the bulk, but maintain the length and shape of the hair.

Thinning Shears

Thinning shears are another tool that can be used to thin out curly hair. This tool has a serrated edge that helps to remove some of the bulk while maintaining the hair's length. However, you need to be careful when using these shears as they can easily damage the hair if not used correctly.

Step by Step Process

Here is a step-by-step process for thinning out curly hair:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Before you start, make sure your hair is clean and free of any styling products. This will make it easier to work with.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into small, manageable sections using clips or hair ties.

Step 3: Choose Your Tool

Decide which tool you want to use to thin out your hair. If you're unsure, consult a professional hairstylist.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Begin cutting your hair using the tool you chose. Be sure to cut at an angle and not straight across. This will help to blend the hair and prevent any harsh lines.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

After you've cut a few sections, check your progress in the mirror. Make sure you're happy with the amount of hair that has been thinned out.

Step 6: Repeat Steps 4 and 5

Continue cutting and checking your progress until you've thinned out all of your hair.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips and tricks to make thinning out curly hair easier:

  • Consult a professional hairstylist if you're unsure about how to thin out your hair.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress as you go.
  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up.
  • Be patient and take your time.
  • Don't overdo it. Thinning out too much hair can make it look stringy and thin.

FAQ

Can I thin out my curly hair at home?

It's best to have a professional hairstylist thin out your hair, but if you're confident in your skills, you can try it at home.

What tools do I need to thin out curly hair?

You can use scissors or thinning shears to thin out curly hair.

Will thinning out my curly hair damage it?

If done correctly, thinning out your curly hair should not damage it. However, if done incorrectly, it can cause split ends and breakage.

Hair Care Tips

After you've thinned out your curly hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather conditions by wearing a hat or scarf.

Thinning out curly hair can be a great way to make it more manageable and easier to style. Just remember to take your time, use the right tools, and consult a professional if you're unsure. With a little bit of effort, you can have healthy, beautiful, and manageable curly hair.
