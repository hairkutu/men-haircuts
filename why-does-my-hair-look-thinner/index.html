Why Does My Hair Look Thinner

Written by Bardi Jul 24, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you noticing that your hair is looking thinner than usual? Many factors can contribute to hair thinning, including genetics, age, and certain medical conditions. However, if you have relaxed hair, there are specific reasons why your hair may look thinner. In this article, we will explore some of the reasons why your hair may appear thinner and provide tips on how to address the issue.

What is Relaxed Hair?

Relaxed hair is hair that has been chemically treated to break down the natural curl pattern. This process involves applying a relaxer to the hair, which can be harsh on the strands. The result is typically straighter hair that is easier to manage. However, relaxed hair can also be more prone to breakage and thinning.

Why Does Relaxed Hair Look Thinner?

There are several reasons why relaxed hair may appear thinner than natural hair. Some of these include:

Chemical Damage

Relaxers can be harsh on the hair, causing damage and breakage. Over time, this can lead to thinner-looking hair. It's important to use a mild relaxer and to follow the instructions carefully to minimize damage.

Overprocessing

If you relax your hair too frequently, you may be overprocessing it. Overprocessing can cause the hair to become weaker and more prone to breakage, which can make it look thinner. Try to wait at least 8-12 weeks between relaxer applications to give your hair time to recover.

Poor Hair Care Practices

If you're not taking care of your hair properly, it can become damaged and thin. This can include using harsh products, not moisturizing enough, and using heat styling tools too frequently. Be sure to use products that are formulated for relaxed hair, moisturize regularly, and limit heat styling to prevent damage.

How to Address Thin-Looking Relaxed Hair

If you're concerned about your hair looking thinner, there are several steps you can take to address the issue:

Use Thickening Products

There are many products on the market that are designed to thicken hair. Look for shampoos, conditioners, and styling products that contain ingredients like biotin, keratin, and collagen.

Consider Hair Extensions

If you're looking for a quick fix, hair extensions can be a great option. Extensions can add volume and thickness to your hair, giving it a fuller appearance. Just be sure to choose a reputable stylist and to care for your extensions properly.

Take Supplements

There are many supplements on the market that are designed to promote hair growth and thickness. Look for supplements that contain biotin, vitamin D, and iron.

Visit a Professional

If you're still concerned about your hair looking thin, consider visiting a professional stylist. They can assess the health of your hair and recommend treatments to promote thickness and volume.

FAQs

Can I relax my hair if it's already thin?

If your hair is already thin, it's best to avoid chemical treatments like relaxers. These treatments can further damage the hair and make it even thinner. Instead, focus on using products and treatments that promote thickness and volume.

How can I prevent my relaxed hair from looking thin?

To prevent your relaxed hair from looking thin, be sure to take care of it properly. Use mild products that are formulated for relaxed hair, moisturize regularly, and limit heat styling. Also, avoid overprocessing your hair by waiting at least 8-12 weeks between relaxer applications.

Final Thoughts

If you're noticing that your relaxed hair looks thinner than usual, there are several reasons why this might be happening. By taking steps to address the issue, such as using thickening products and taking supplements, you can promote thicker, fuller-looking hair. Remember to take care of your hair properly, and avoid overprocessing to prevent further damage and thinning.
