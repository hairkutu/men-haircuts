How To Get Hair Past Shoulder Length A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Apr 03, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you tired of short hair and want to grow your locks past shoulder length? It can be a frustrating journey, but with the right tips and tricks, you can achieve your desired length. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to get hair past shoulder length in relaxed English language. We will also answer some frequently asked questions and provide some hair care tips to keep your locks healthy during the process. Let’s get started!

Table of Contents

  • How to Get Hair Past Shoulder Length
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Growing Long Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

How to Get Hair Past Shoulder Length

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let’s first understand what factors affect hair growth. Genetics, diet, lifestyle, and hair care all play a role in hair growth. Though genetics and hair growth rate cannot be changed, you can control the other factors. Eating a balanced diet, exercising regularly, reducing stress, and maintaining a healthy hair care routine can help promote hair growth. Now, let’s move on to the step-by-step guide.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Trim your hair regularly
It may sound counterintuitive, but trimming your hair regularly can actually help it grow. Trimming your hair every 6-8 weeks removes split ends, which can cause breakage and slow down hair growth.

Step 2: Massage your scalp
Massaging your scalp with oil or a scalp massager can stimulate blood flow to the hair follicles, promoting hair growth. Use natural oils like coconut, olive, or castor oil and massage your scalp for 5-10 minutes before washing your hair.

Step 3: Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
Sulfates are harsh chemicals that can strip your hair of its natural oils, causing dryness and breakage. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

Step 4: Avoid heat styling
Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling wands can damage your hair and cause breakage. Avoid using them as much as possible or use a heat protectant spray before styling.

Step 5: Eat a balanced diet
Eating a diet rich in vitamins and minerals like biotin, iron, and zinc can promote hair growth. Include foods like eggs, spinach, nuts, and fish in your diet.

Step 6: Be patient
Hair growth takes time and patience. Don’t expect overnight results and don’t get discouraged if you don’t see progress right away. Keep up with a healthy hair care routine and give your hair time to grow.

Tips for Growing Long Hair

Tip 1: Protect your hair while sleeping
Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent hair breakage and tangling while you sleep. You can also tie your hair in a loose bun or braid to protect it.

Tip 2: Don’t over-wash your hair
Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils and cause dryness. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days.

Tip 3: Use a hair mask once a week
Deep conditioning your hair with a hair mask once a week can help keep it hydrated and healthy. You can make a DIY hair mask using natural ingredients like avocado, honey, and yogurt.

FAQs

Q: Can I still use heat styling tools?
A: Yes, you can still use heat styling tools, but make sure to use a heat protectant spray and avoid using them too often.

Q: Can I use hair extensions?
A: Yes, you can use hair extensions to add length to your hair, but make sure to take care of your natural hair as well.

Q: Does hair growth slow down with age?
A: Yes, hair growth rate can slow down with age, but maintaining a healthy hair care routine can still promote hair growth.

Hair Care Tips

Tip 1: Use a wide-tooth comb
Using a wide-tooth comb can help prevent breakage and damage to your hair.

Tip 2: Don’t brush wet hair
Wet hair is more prone to breakage, so avoid brushing it when it’s wet. Use a wide-tooth comb instead.

Tip 3: Protect your hair from the sun
UV rays from the sun can damage your hair and cause dryness. Wear a hat or use a hair sunscreen to protect your locks.

Congratulations, you now have a complete guide on how to get hair past shoulder length. Remember, patience and consistency are key in achieving your desired length. Keep up with a healthy hair care routine, eat a balanced diet, and be gentle with your hair. Happy growing!
