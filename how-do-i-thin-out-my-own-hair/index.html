How Do I Thin Out My Own Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Sep 03, 2023 · 7 min read

Thinning out your own hair can be a daunting task, especially if you’re not a professional hairdresser. But with the right tools and techniques, it’s possible to achieve a salon-worthy look at home. In this article, we’ll walk you through the process of thinning out your own hair, step-by-step. We’ll also share some tips and tricks for maintaining your newly thinned hair, as well as answer some frequently asked questions about the process.

Table of Contents

  2. Prepping Your Hair
  3. Sectioning Your Hair
  4. Thinning Your Hair
  5. Blending Your Hair

Tools You’ll Need

Before you begin, you’ll need to gather some tools:

  • Thinning shears
  • Regular scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Water spray bottle

Make sure your scissors and thinning shears are sharp and in good condition. Dull blades can damage your hair and make the process more difficult.

Prepping Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. It’s important to work with clean, dry hair so that you can see the natural texture and thickness. Once your hair is dry, comb through it to remove any tangles or knots.

Sectioning Your Hair

Next, section your hair into manageable pieces. Use hair clips to separate your hair into four sections: two in the front and two in the back. You can use a comb to create a straight part down the middle of your head, and then divide each side into two sections.

Thinning Your Hair

Now it’s time to start thinning out your hair. Begin with one section at a time. Use your regular scissors to cut off any split ends or damaged hair. Then, take your thinning shears and make small snips in the middle of the section, working your way down to the ends. Be sure to use a light touch and only thin out a small amount of hair at a time.

Repeat this process for each section of your hair, being careful to maintain an even look. It’s better to take your time and go slowly than to rush and create an uneven or choppy look.

Blending Your Hair

Once you’ve thinned out each section of your hair, it’s time to blend the layers together. Use your regular scissors to create soft, feathered edges, blending the thinned sections into the un-thinned sections. This will create a natural, seamless look.

Finishing Touches

Finally, brush through your hair to remove any loose hairs, and use your water spray bottle to mist your hair lightly. This will help to set your new style and give it a polished look.

Tips for Maintaining Thinned Hair

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment regularly to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair and make it look frizzy.
  • Consider using a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to give your hair a fuller look.
  • Visit a professional hairdresser every few months for a trim and to maintain your thinned-out style.

FAQs

Is it possible to thin out curly hair?

Yes, it is possible to thin out curly hair. However, it’s important to be careful when using thinning shears, as they can create a frizzy or uneven look. It’s best to work with a professional hairdresser who has experience working with curly hair.

Can I use regular scissors to thin out my hair?

While regular scissors can be used to thin out your hair, it’s best to use thinning shears. Thinning shears are designed specifically for this purpose and will create a more natural, even look.

How often should I thin out my hair?

It’s best to wait at least six to eight weeks between thinning out your hair. This will give your hair time to grow and recover from any damage.

Hair Care Tips

Remember to take good care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and beautiful. This includes washing your hair regularly, using a heat protectant spray when styling with heat tools, and avoiding harsh chemical treatments. With the right care and attention, you can maintain your thinned-out style and enjoy a confident, polished look.
