How To Make Boy Hair Fluffy Tips And Tricks

Written by Frank Jul 29, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you tired of your son's flat and lifeless hair? Do you want to add some volume and bounce to their locks? Look no further! In this article, we will show you how to make boy hair fluffy with some easy tips and tricks that you can apply at home. From using the right products to styling techniques, we've got you covered. Let's get started!

How to Make Boy Hair Fluffy: Step by Step

Step 1: Start with clean hair. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to wash your son's hair, and make sure to rinse thoroughly to avoid any product buildup that can weigh down the hair.

Step 2: Towel-dry the hair gently. Avoid rubbing the hair vigorously with a towel, as this can cause frizz and damage. Instead, use a soft towel to pat the hair dry and remove excess water.

Step 3: Apply a volumizing product. There are many products available on the market that can help add volume and texture to the hair, such as mousse, volumizing spray, or root-lifting powder. Apply the product to the roots and work it through the hair with your fingers.

Step 4: Blow-dry the hair upside down. This is a simple but effective technique to create volume and lift at the roots. Use a hair dryer with a diffuser attachment to dry the hair, and flip the hair upside down while drying. Use your fingers to scrunch the hair and create more texture.

Step 5: Style the hair. Once the hair is dry, you can style it in a way that adds more volume and fluffiness. You can use a round brush to lift the hair at the roots, or tease the hair gently with a comb to create more height.

Tips for Making Boy Hair Fluffy

Tip 1: Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle the hair gently, as brushing can cause frizz and breakage.

Tip 2: Avoid using heavy products such as wax or gel, as they can weigh down the hair and make it look greasy.

Tip 3: Use a dry shampoo in between washes to refresh the hair and add more volume.

FAQ: How to Make Boy Hair Fluffy

Q: Can I use a hair straightener to create volume?

A: No, using a hair straightener can actually make the hair flatter and smoother. Instead, use a hair dryer with a diffuser attachment to dry the hair upside down.

Q: How often should I wash my son's hair?

A: It depends on your son's hair type and lifestyle. If he has oily hair or plays sports regularly, he may need to wash his hair more often. Otherwise, washing his hair once or twice a week should be sufficient.

Hair Care for Fluffy Boy Hair

Now that you know how to make boy hair fluffy, it's important to take care of the hair to maintain its health and texture. Here are some tips:

- Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that are suitable for your son's hair type.

- Avoid using hot tools such as hair straighteners or curling irons too often, as they can damage the hair.

- Trim the ends regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.

- Protect the hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner before swimming.

By following these tips and tricks, you can make boy hair fluffy and voluminous without spending a lot of time or money. Experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for your son's hair, and enjoy the results!
