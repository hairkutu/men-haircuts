How To Keep Short Natural Hair Curly Tips And Tricks

Written by Oliver May 31, 2023 Â· 10 min read

Short natural hair can be a challenge to maintain, especially if you want to keep your curls looking their best. Whether you're new to natural hair or a seasoned pro, there are a few key things you can do to keep your hair looking curly, healthy, and beautiful. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks for how to keep short natural hair curly.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Keep Short Natural Hair Curly
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips and Tricks
  4. FAQ
  5. Hair Care for Short Natural Hair

How to Keep Short Natural Hair Curly

The key to keeping short natural hair curly is to create and maintain defined curls. To do this, you'll need to use the right products and techniques for your hair type. Here are the basic steps to follow:

1. Start with clean, conditioned hair

Before you can create defined curls, you need to start with a clean slate. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and a moisturizing conditioner to cleanse and condition your hair. This will help to remove any buildup and make your hair more receptive to styling products.

2. Apply a leave-in conditioner

After washing and conditioning your hair, apply a leave-in conditioner to add moisture and prevent frizz. This will also help to detangle your hair and make it easier to style.

3. Use a curl defining product

To create defined curls, you'll need to use a curl defining product that works for your hair type. This could be a curl cream, gel, or mousse. Apply the product to your hair in sections, making sure to coat each strand evenly.

4. Style your hair

Once you've applied your curl defining product, it's time to style your hair. There are a few different techniques you can use to create curls, including finger coiling, twist outs, and bantu knots. Experiment with different techniques to find what works best for your hair.

5. Dry your hair

After styling your hair, you'll need to let it dry completely before touching it. You can air dry or use a diffuser on a low heat setting to speed up the process. Once your hair is dry, you can fluff it out and style it as desired.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a more detailed step-by-step guide for how to keep short natural hair curly:

Step 1: Cleanse and condition

Start by washing your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioning it with a moisturizing conditioner. Rinse your hair thoroughly with cool water to seal in moisture.

Step 2: Apply a leave-in conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair, making sure to distribute it evenly from root to tip. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair and remove any knots or tangles.

Step 3: Apply a curl defining product

Apply a curl defining product to your hair, working in sections to ensure that each strand is coated evenly. Use your fingers to distribute the product and define your curls.

Step 4: Style your hair

Use your preferred styling technique to create curls in your hair. You can use finger coiling, twist outs, or bantu knots to create defined curls. Experiment with different techniques to find what works best for your hair.

Step 5: Dry your hair

Let your hair air dry or use a diffuser on a low heat setting to speed up the drying process. Once your hair is dry, fluff it out and style it as desired.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks for how to keep short natural hair curly:

  • Protect your hair at night by sleeping with a satin or silk scarf or pillowcase
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair and disrupt your curl pattern
  • Deep condition your hair regularly to keep it moisturized and healthy
  • Don't over-manipulate your hair, as this can cause breakage and damage
  • Experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for your hair

FAQ

1. How often should I wash my short natural hair?

This depends on your hair type and personal preference. Some people with dry hair may only need to wash once a week, while others with oilier hair may need to wash more frequently. Aim to wash your hair when it feels dirty or greasy, and use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.

2. How can I prevent frizz in my short natural hair?

To prevent frizz, make sure to use a leave-in conditioner and a curl defining product to add moisture and definition to your curls. Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can disrupt your curl pattern and cause frizz. You can also use a lightweight oil or serum to smooth down any flyaways.

3. Can I use the same products on my short natural hair as I do on my longer hair?

Not necessarily. Short hair may require different products and techniques than longer hair, so it's important to experiment and find what works best for you. Look for products specifically designed for short hair or for your hair type.

Hair Care for Short Natural Hair

Keeping your short natural hair healthy and beautiful requires a few key hair care practices. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair:

  • Moisturize your hair regularly with a leave-in conditioner or oil
  • Protect your hair from heat and sun damage by wearing a hat or scarf
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage
  • Eat a healthy diet that includes plenty of vitamins and nutrients for hair growth
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or styling products that can damage your hair

By following these tips and tricks, you can keep your short natural hair looking curly, healthy, and beautiful. Experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for your hair, and don't be afraid to try new things!
