How To Cut Your Hair To Make It Less Thick

Written by Luffy Jul 06, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Do you have thick hair that's difficult to manage? Are you tired of spending hours styling your hair every day? If so, then you might want to consider cutting your hair to make it less thick. In this article, we'll show you how to cut your hair to make it less thick, step-by-step. We'll also provide you with some tips and hair care advice to help you maintain your new hairstyle.

How to Cut Your Hair to Make It Less Thick

There are several different ways to cut your hair to make it less thick. Here are some of the most popular techniques:

  • Layering
  • Thinning
  • Razor Cutting

Each of these techniques can help reduce the volume of your hair, but they work in different ways. Layering involves cutting different lengths of hair to create texture and movement. Thinning involves using special scissors to remove some of the hair's thickness. Razor cutting involves using a razor to slice through the hair, which can create a softer, more feathered look.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you're planning to cut your hair at home, then here's a step-by-step guide to help you get started:

  1. Wash your hair and let it dry naturally.
  2. Brush your hair to remove any tangles.
  3. Decide on the type of cut you want to achieve (layering, thinning, or razor cutting).
  4. Part your hair into sections using clips or elastics.
  5. Start cutting your hair one section at a time, using sharp scissors or a razor.
  6. Trim the ends of each section to create a uniform length.
  7. Use thinning shears or a razor to remove some of the hair's thickness (if desired).
  8. Blend the layers together to create a seamless look.
  9. Style your hair as desired.
  10. Repeat the process every 6-8 weeks to maintain your new hairstyle.

Remember, if you're not confident about cutting your hair at home, then it's best to visit a professional stylist. They can help you achieve the look you want while ensuring that your hair remains healthy and strong.

Tips for Cutting Thick Hair

If you have thick hair, then there are a few things you should keep in mind when cutting it:

  • Use sharp scissors or a razor to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Take your time and cut small sections at a time.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once, as this can result in an uneven look.
  • Be patient and don't rush the process.
  • Use hair products designed for thick hair to help manage your new hairstyle.

FAQs

Q: Will cutting my hair make it less thick?

A: Yes, cutting your hair can help reduce its thickness. However, the amount of thickness reduction will depend on the type of cut you choose and how much hair you remove.

Q: Is it better to thin or layer thick hair?

A: Both techniques can work well for thick hair. Layering can help create texture and movement, while thinning can help reduce bulk and create a lighter look.

Q: How often should I cut my thick hair?

A: It's best to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain your new hairstyle and keep your hair healthy.

Hair Care Tips

After cutting your hair, it's important to take good care of it to keep it healthy and strong. Here are some hair care tips to help you maintain your new hairstyle:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner designed for your hair type.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Brush your hair gently to avoid tangles and breakage.
  • Apply a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to help nourish your hair.
  • Eat a healthy diet and drink plenty of water to promote healthy hair growth.

By following these tips and techniques, you can cut your hair to make it less thick and achieve a look that's easy to manage and style. Whether you choose to layer, thin, or razor cut your hair, remember to take your time and be patient. With a little practice, you'll be able to create a beautiful new hairstyle that you'll love!
