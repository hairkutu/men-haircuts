How To Cut Soft Layers In Medium Length Hair Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Introduction

If you have medium length hair and are looking for a change, cutting soft layers is a great option. Not only will it add dimension and movement to your hair, but it can also give you a more youthful and trendy look. However, going to a salon for this hairstyle can be expensive. Luckily, cutting soft layers in medium length hair yourself is possible with the right tools and technique. In this step-by-step guide, we will show you how to achieve this look at home.

How to Cut Soft Layers in Medium Length Hair Yourself

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let's go over the tools you will need:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips to section your hair
  • A spray bottle filled with water

Now that you have your tools ready, let's get started.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Start with clean and dry hair. Using a comb, part your hair in the middle or on the side, depending on your preference.

Step 2: Using the comb, section your hair horizontally at the back of your head. Clip the top section away, leaving the bottom section loose.

Step 3: Take a small section of hair from the bottom and comb it straight down. Hold the scissors vertically and make small snips upwards, starting at the ends of your hair.

Step 4: Continue to take small sections of hair and cut upwards, making sure to blend in with the previous sections.

Step 5: Once you have finished the bottom section, unclip the top section and repeat steps 3 and 4.

Step 6: Move to the sides of your hair, taking small sections and cutting upwards to blend in with the back.

Step 7: Finally, move to the front of your hair, taking small sections and cutting upwards to blend in with the sides and back.

Step 8: Once you have finished cutting, shake your hair out and check for any uneven sections. If you find any, trim them to blend in with the rest of your hair.

Step 9: Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Cutting Soft Layers in Medium Length Hair Yourself

- Start with small snips and work your way up to avoid cutting too much off.
- Cut in a well-lit area to avoid any mistakes.
- Don't be afraid to ask a friend to help you cut the back of your hair if needed.
- Always cut upwards to avoid creating harsh lines.
- Remember that it's easier to cut more hair off than to add hair back on.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can soft layers work on any hair type?
A: Yes, soft layers can work on any hair type including curly, straight, and wavy hair.

Q: Do I need to wet my hair before cutting?
A: Wetting your hair can make it easier to cut, but it's not necessary. If you prefer to cut your hair dry, make sure to comb it straight before cutting.

Q: How often should I cut my soft layers?
A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how often you want to maintain your layers. On average, cutting soft layers every 6-8 weeks can help maintain the look.

Hair Care Tips for Soft Layers in Medium Length Hair

- Use a heat protectant before using any hot tools to avoid damaging your hair.
- Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.
- Avoid using too much product in your hair as it can weigh down your layers.
- Trim your layers regularly to maintain the shape and prevent split ends.
- Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair to avoid pulling on your layers.

Conclusion

Now that you know how to cut soft layers in medium length hair yourself, you can save money on salon visits and achieve this trendy hairstyle at home. Remember to take your time and start with small snips, and don't be afraid to ask for help if needed. With practice, you'll be able to perfect this look and enjoy the benefits of soft layers in your medium length hair.
