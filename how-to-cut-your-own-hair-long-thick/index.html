How To Cut Your Own Hair Long Thick

Written by Bardi Aug 23, 2023 · 6 min read

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, especially if you have long, thick hair. However, with the right tools and techniques, cutting your own hair at home can save you time and money. In this article, we will provide step-by-step instructions and tips on how to cut your own hair long thick.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Your Own Hair Long Thick
  • Tools Needed
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care After Cutting

Tools Needed

Before starting, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair ties or clips
  • Mirror
  • Spray bottle filled with water

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to cut your own long, thick hair:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Wash and dry your hair thoroughly. It's best to cut your hair when it's clean and dry. This will help you see any split ends or uneven sections.

Step 2: Comb Your Hair

Comb your hair straight down and remove any tangles. Make sure your hair is completely smooth before proceeding.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into four sections: top, bottom, left, and right. Use hair ties or clips to secure each section.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Start with the bottom section of your hair. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to trim the ends of the hair. Remember to cut small sections at a time.

Step 5: Continue Cutting

Move on to the left and right sections of your hair. Trim the ends of each small section of hair. Make sure to keep each section even with the others.

Step 6: Cut the Top Section

Finally, tackle the top section of your hair. Pull small sections of hair straight up and trim the ends. Make sure to blend the top section with the rest of your hair.

Step 7: Check Your Work

Check your work in the mirror. Look for any uneven sections or missed spots. Make any necessary adjustments.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

  • Don't cut too much hair at once. It's better to cut small sections at a time.
  • Use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can damage your hair.
  • Use a spray bottle to wet your hair if it starts to dry out.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I cut my own hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can cut your own curly hair. However, make sure to cut it when it's dry so you can see the curls and how they fall.

How often should I cut my long, thick hair?

It's recommended to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and maintain healthy hair.

Hair Care After Cutting

After cutting your own long, thick hair, make sure to take care of it properly. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep it healthy and hydrated. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, and use a heat protectant when you do. And, of course, don't forget to schedule regular trims to maintain your new haircut.

Now that you know how to cut your own hair long thick, give it a try and save yourself a trip to the salon!
