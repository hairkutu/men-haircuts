How To Cut The Back Of Your Own Hair With Clippers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby May 07, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you tired of spending money on haircuts every month? Or maybe you just don't have the time to make it to the salon? Whatever the reason, cutting your own hair can be a great way to save money and time. However, cutting the back of your own hair can be tricky. In this article, we'll show you how to cut the back of your own hair with clippers in a few simple steps.

Description

If you've never cut your own hair before, it can be intimidating. But with a little practice and the right tools, it's not as difficult as it seems. In this article, we'll walk you through the process of cutting the back of your own hair with clippers. We'll cover everything from choosing the right clippers to finishing touches. By the end of this article, you'll be able to confidently cut the back of your own hair.

How to Cut the Back of Your Own Hair with Clippers

Before we get started, it's important to gather all the necessary tools. You'll need a pair of clippers, a mirror, a comb, and a spray bottle of water. Once you have everything you need, follow these simple steps:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Before you start cutting, make sure your hair is clean and free of any product. This will make it easier to cut and give you a better idea of how your hair naturally falls.

Step 2: Choose the Right Clippers

When choosing clippers, look for ones with adjustable guards. This will allow you to choose the length you want to cut your hair. It's also important to choose clippers with a cord so you don't have to worry about them running out of battery mid-cut.

Step 3: Decide on the Length

Using your comb, determine the length you want to cut your hair. Start with a longer guard and work your way down if you want a shorter cut.

Step 4: Start at the Bottom

Using the clippers, start at the bottom of your hairline and work your way up. Be sure to use long, even strokes and move the clippers in an upward motion.

Step 5: Blend the Sides

Once you've cut the bottom, blend the sides by using a higher guard and moving the clippers in an upward motion. This will create a natural-looking fade.

Step 6: Check Your Progress

Use your mirror to check your progress. Make sure the sides are blended evenly and the back is the length you want it to be.

Step 7: Finish the Top

Using scissors, trim the top of your hair to the desired length. Be sure to use small, even snips to avoid cutting too much off at once.

Step 8: Clean Up

Once you're finished cutting, use your comb to comb out any loose hairs. Then, use a damp cloth to wipe your neck and shoulders clean.

Tips for Cutting the Back of Your Own Hair

Here are a few tips to make cutting the back of your own hair easier:

  • Use a spray bottle of water to dampen your hair. This will make it easier to cut and give you more control.
  • Take your time. Cutting your own hair can be nerve-wracking, but rushing through it can lead to mistakes.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress from all angles.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help. If you're having trouble with a certain area, ask a friend or family member to help you out.

FAQ

Can I cut my hair with regular scissors?

You can, but it's not recommended. Regular scissors are not designed for cutting hair and can lead to split ends and uneven cuts.

How often should I cut my hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how short you want to keep it. On average, most people need a haircut every 4-6 weeks.

What should I do if I make a mistake?

If you accidentally cut your hair too short, try not to panic. Depending on the severity of the mistake, you may be able to fix it by trimming the surrounding hair. If it's a major mistake, you may need to visit a salon to have it fixed.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy and looking great. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat tools like blow dryers and straighteners too often.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote healthy hair growth.

Cutting the back of your own hair with clippers may seem daunting at first, but with a little practice and the right tools, it's easy to do. Follow these simple steps and you'll have a great-looking haircut in no time!
