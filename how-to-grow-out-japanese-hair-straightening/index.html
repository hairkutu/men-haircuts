How To Grow Out Japanese Hair Straightening A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Aprilia Sep 12, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of having straight hair and want to grow out your Japanese hair straightening? We know that the process can be daunting, but it's not impossible. In this guide, we'll provide you with tips and tricks on how to grow out Japanese hair straightening with ease.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Grow Out Japanese Hair Straightening
  • Step by Step Guide

Description

Japanese hair straightening, also known as thermal reconditioning, is a hair treatment that permanently straightens hair. It's a popular treatment because it produces silky, smooth, and straight hair that can last for six months or longer.

However, as your hair grows out, you'll notice that the new growth is curly or wavy, while the rest of your hair is straight. This can make your hair look odd and uneven. If you're looking to grow out your Japanese hair straightening, this guide is for you.

How to Grow Out Japanese Hair Straightening

Growing out Japanese hair straightening is a process that takes time, patience, and care. The first step is to stop getting the treatment done. If you continue to get the treatment done, you'll never be able to grow out your hair naturally.

The next step is to cut your hair regularly. This will help get rid of the straightened hair and promote healthy hair growth. Cutting your hair also helps to blend the curly or wavy new growth with the straightened hair.

You can also try using hair products that promote hair growth. These products can help to nourish your hair and promote healthy hair growth.

Step by Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to grow out Japanese hair straightening:

  1. Stop getting the treatment done.
  2. Cut your hair regularly.
  3. Use hair products that promote hair growth.
  4. Be patient and wait for your hair to grow out.
  5. Trim your hair regularly to get rid of the straightened hair.
  6. Be gentle with your hair and avoid using heat styling tools.
  7. Use a deep conditioning treatment regularly to keep your hair healthy.
  8. Try using a hair mask to nourish your hair.
  9. Consider getting a short haircut to start fresh.
  10. Be patient and embrace your natural hair.

Tips to Help You Grow Out Japanese Hair Straightening

Here are some tips to help you grow out your Japanese hair straightening:

  • Be patient and don't expect immediate results. Growing out Japanese hair straightening can take months or even years.
  • Use hair products that promote healthy hair growth.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair.
  • Consider getting a short haircut to start fresh.
  • Be gentle with your hair and avoid harsh chemicals.
  • Eat a healthy diet and drink plenty of water to promote healthy hair growth.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I dye my hair while growing out Japanese hair straightening?

Yes, you can dye your hair while growing out Japanese hair straightening. However, it's important to wait at least two weeks after getting the treatment done before dyeing your hair.

How often should I cut my hair while growing out Japanese hair straightening?

You should cut your hair every six to eight weeks while growing out Japanese hair straightening. This will help to get rid of the straightened hair and promote healthy hair growth.

How long does it take to grow out Japanese hair straightening?

It can take anywhere from six months to two years to grow out Japanese hair straightening, depending on how fast your hair grows.

Hair Care Tips for Growing Out Japanese Hair Straightening

Here are some hair care tips to help you grow out Japanese hair straightening:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment regularly to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Be gentle with your hair and avoid harsh chemicals.
  • Eat a healthy diet and drink plenty of water to promote healthy hair growth.

Growing out Japanese hair straightening can be a challenge, but it's not impossible. With patience, care, and the right hair care products, you can grow out your Japanese hair straightening and embrace your natural hair. Remember to be gentle with your hair, avoid using harsh chemicals, and be patient. Good luck!
