How To Do A Pixie Cut At Home With Clippers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Apr 09, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking for a bold and edgy hairstyle that's low-maintenance and easy to style, a pixie cut might be just what you need. And the good news is that you don't have to visit a salon to get one. With a pair of clippers and a bit of confidence, you can give yourself a pixie cut at home. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process step-by-step, provide some tips and tricks, and answer some frequently asked questions about pixie cuts.

How to Do a Pixie Cut at Home with Clippers

Before we get started, gather your supplies. You'll need:

  • A pair of clippers with guards
  • A comb
  • Scissors
  • A mirror
  • A towel or cape to catch the hair

Step 1: Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Wash and dry your hair before you begin. Pixie cuts work best on dry hair because it's easier to see the length and texture.

Step 2: Choose Your Guard Size

Choose the guard size you want to use based on how short you want your hair to be. Start with a longer guard and work your way down if you want it shorter.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Use a comb to section your hair into four parts: front, sides, and back. Use hair clips to hold each section in place.

Step 4: Start with the Sides

Start with the sides of your head. Attach the guard to your clippers and begin cutting from the bottom up. Use short, upward strokes to create an even length.

Step 5: Move to the Back

Move to the back of your head and repeat the same process. Use the mirror to make sure the length is even on both sides.

Step 6: Trim the Top

Remove the guard from your clippers and use scissors to trim the top of your hair. Start at the front and work your way back. Cut small sections at a time and use the comb to lift the hair and create an even length.

Step 7: Refine the Edges

Use the clippers or scissors to refine the edges around your ears and neckline.

Step 8: Style Your Pixie Cut

Style your pixie cut with your fingers or a comb. Use a small amount of styling product to add texture and hold.

Tips for a Successful Pixie Cut

  • Don't rush. Take your time and cut small sections at a time.
  • Use the mirror to check the length and symmetry as you go.
  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down if you want it shorter.
  • Be confident and have fun!

FAQ About Pixie Cuts

1. Will a pixie cut work for my face shape?

Pixie cuts can work for any face shape, but it's important to choose a style that flatters your features. Talk to your stylist or do some research to find the best cut for your face shape.

2. How often do I need to trim my pixie cut?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how short you keep it. Most people need to trim their pixie cut every 4-6 weeks to maintain the style.

3. Can I color my pixie cut?

Yes, you can color your pixie cut just like any other hairstyle. Talk to your stylist about the best color options for your skin tone and hair type.

Hair Care for Pixie Cuts

Pixie cuts are low-maintenance, but they still require some care to look their best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often to prevent damage.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and length of your pixie cut.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and heat with a hat or scarf.

Now that you know how to do a pixie cut at home with clippers, it's time to give it a try. With a bit of practice, you'll be able to master this edgy and stylish cut.
