How To Cut Full Fringe Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jun 06, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, but with a little practice and patience, you can achieve great results. In this article, we will guide you through the process of cutting a full fringe, also known as bangs, yourself. We will cover everything from preparation to hair care, so you can confidently cut your own fringe at home. Here's what you need to know:

Table of Contents

  • Preparation
  • How to Cut Full Fringe Yourself
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Your Own Fringe
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care after Cutting Your Fringe

Preparation

Before you start cutting your fringe, you need to make sure you have the right tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clips or hair ties
  • Water and spray bottle
  • Mirror

It's also important to choose a style that suits your face shape and hair type. If you're unsure, we recommend consulting with a hair stylist before attempting to cut your own fringe.

How to Cut Full Fringe Yourself

Cutting your own fringe can be tricky, but with the right technique, you can achieve a professional-looking result. Here's how:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Wash your hair and let it dry naturally or blow-dry it straight.
  2. Part your hair in the middle, and comb it straight down.
  3. Decide on the length of your fringe and mark it with a clip or hair tie.
  4. Take a small section of hair from the middle of your forehead and comb it straight down.
  5. Using your scissors, cut the hair at an angle, starting from the center and moving towards the side of your face. Do this for both sides, making sure the length is even.
  6. Check your fringe in the mirror and make any adjustments if necessary.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Fringe

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results when cutting your own fringe:

  • Start by cutting a little bit of hair at a time, as it's easier to cut more than to fix a mistake.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging your hair and creating split ends.
  • Make sure your hair is wet or damp when cutting, as it's easier to control and less likely to move around.
  • Always cut your fringe in a well-lit area, preferably with natural light.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I cut my own fringe if I have curly hair?

Cutting a fringe on curly hair can be tricky, as the curls can affect the length and shape. We recommend consulting with a stylist before attempting to cut your own fringe if you have curly hair.

How often should I trim my fringe?

It's recommended to trim your fringe every 4 to 6 weeks to maintain the length and shape.

What should I do if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic. You can always trim a little more or visit a stylist to fix the problem.

Hair Care after Cutting Your Fringe

After cutting your fringe, it's important to take good care of your hair to maintain the style and health. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair and make it brittle.
  • Use a hair mask or oil treatment once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.
  • Trim your fringe every 4 to 6 weeks to maintain the length and shape.

Now that you know how to cut a full fringe yourself, you can create a stylish and personalized look from the comfort of your own home. Remember to take your time, be patient, and follow the steps carefully for the best results. Happy cutting!
