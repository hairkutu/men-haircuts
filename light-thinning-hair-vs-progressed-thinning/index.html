Light Thinning Hair Vs Progressed Thinning Understanding The Difference

Written by Robby Apr 13, 2023 · 6 min read

Thinning hair is a common problem faced by both men and women. It can be a result of various factors like genetics, age, poor diet, stress, and hormonal changes. However, not all thinning hair is the same. There are two types of thinning hair: light thinning hair and progressed thinning hair. In this article, we will discuss the differences between these two types of hair loss and how to care for them.

  • Causes of Thinning Hair
  • Signs of Thinning Hair
  • Hair Care Tips for Light Thinning Hair
  • Hair Care Tips for Progressed Thinning Hair

What is Light Thinning Hair?

Light thinning hair is the early stage of hair loss. It is characterized by a slight decrease in hair density and volume, which may not be noticeable to others. In this stage, the hair follicles are still active and can produce new hair. Light thinning hair can be caused by stress, poor nutrition, hormonal changes, and genetics.

What is Progressed Thinning Hair?

Progressed thinning hair, on the other hand, is the advanced stage of hair loss. It is characterized by a significant decrease in hair density and volume, which is noticeable to others. In this stage, the hair follicles shrink and produce thinner and weaker hair, which eventually stops growing. Progressed thinning hair can be caused by genetic factors, age, and hormonal changes.

Causes of Thinning Hair

Thinning hair can be caused by a variety of factors, including:

  • Genetics
  • Aging
  • Hormonal changes
  • Poor nutrition
  • Stress
  • Medical conditions
  • Medications

Signs of Thinning Hair

The signs of thinning hair include:

  • Increased hair shedding
  • Decreased hair density and volume
  • Visible scalp
  • Thinning hairline
  • Bald spots

Hair Care Tips for Light Thinning Hair

If you have light thinning hair, there are several things you can do to care for your hair, including:

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using heat styling tools
  • Avoid tight hairstyles
  • Eat a balanced diet
  • Take vitamins and supplements
  • Reduce stress

Hair Care Tips for Progressed Thinning Hair

If you have progressed thinning hair, you may need to take more drastic measures to care for your hair, including:

  • Use a hair growth shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid chemical treatments and hair dyes
  • Avoid tight hairstyles
  • Eat a balanced diet
  • Take vitamins and supplements
  • Consider hair transplant surgery

Frequently Asked Questions

Can stress cause thinning hair?

Yes, stress can cause thinning hair. When you are under stress, your body produces more cortisol, which can affect the hair growth cycle and lead to hair loss.

Can medications cause thinning hair?

Yes, some medications can cause thinning hair as a side effect. If you are experiencing hair loss while taking medication, talk to your doctor about possible alternatives.

Can hair loss be reversed?

It depends on the cause of hair loss. Some types of hair loss can be reversed with proper treatment, while others may be permanent.

Final Thoughts

Thinning hair can be a frustrating and emotional experience, but it is important to understand the difference between light thinning hair and progressed thinning hair and how to care for them. By following the hair care tips and seeking professional help when necessary, you can maintain healthy hair and feel confident in your appearance.
