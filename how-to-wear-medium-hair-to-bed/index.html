How To Wear Medium Hair To Bed Tips And Tricks

Written by Aprilia Jun 07, 2023 · 6 min read

Wearing medium hair to bed can be tricky, especially if you want to keep your hair looking great the next day. Whether you have natural or relaxed hair, there are several things you can do to protect your hair while you sleep. In this article, we’ll share some tips and tricks for how to wear medium hair to bed.

Table of Contents

  • How to Wear Medium Hair to Bed
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care

How to Wear Medium Hair to Bed

Before we get into the specific tips and tricks, let’s review some general guidelines for how to wear medium hair to bed:

  • Avoid sleeping with wet hair. This can cause breakage, tangles, and frizz.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase. This will help reduce friction and prevent hair breakage.
  • Try to avoid sleeping with your hair loose. This can cause tangles and knots.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to wear your medium hair to bed:

  1. Detangle your hair: Before you go to bed, make sure your hair is tangle-free. Use a wide-tooth comb or detangling brush to gently remove any knots or tangles.
  2. Protect your ends: Apply a small amount of oil or leave-in conditioner to the ends of your hair to help prevent split ends and breakage.
  3. Choose a protective style: Depending on your hair length and texture, you may want to choose a protective style for bedtime. This could be a loose braid, a bun, or a pineapple (a high ponytail on top of your head).
  4. Wrap your hair: If you have relaxed hair, you may want to wrap your hair using a silk or satin scarf. This will help protect your hair and prevent frizz.
  5. Use a silk or satin bonnet: If you prefer not to wrap your hair, you can also use a silk or satin bonnet to protect your hair while you sleep.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some additional tips and tricks for how to wear medium hair to bed:

  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or oil before bed to help keep your hair moisturized.
  • If you have natural hair, you may want to try the LOC method (leave-in conditioner, oil, and cream) before bed to help lock in moisture.
  • If you have a silk scarf or bonnet, you can use it to protect your hairline and edges.
  • Try sleeping on a satin pillowcase or using a satin hair wrap to help reduce friction and prevent hair breakage.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about how to wear medium hair to bed:

Q: Can I sleep with my hair loose?

A: It’s best to avoid sleeping with your hair loose, as this can cause tangles and knots. Instead, try a protective style like a loose braid, bun, or pineapple.

Q: Should I use a silk or satin pillowcase?

A: Yes, using a silk or satin pillowcase can help reduce friction and prevent hair breakage.

Q: How often should I wrap my hair?

A: It’s best to wrap your hair every night to help protect it and prevent breakage.

Hair Care

Remember to take good care of your hair, both during the day and at night. This includes washing and conditioning your hair regularly, using a wide-tooth comb or detangling brush to remove tangles, and avoiding heat styling tools as much as possible. With the right care and attention, you can keep your medium hair looking healthy and beautiful.
