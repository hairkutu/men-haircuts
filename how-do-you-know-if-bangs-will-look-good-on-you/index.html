How Do You Know If Bangs Will Look Good On You

Written by Aprilia Apr 21, 2023 · 11 min read

Are you thinking about getting bangs but not sure if they will suit your face shape or hair type? Bangs can be a great way to change up your look and add some personality to your hairstyle. However, it can be a tricky decision to make. In this article, we will discuss how to know if bangs will look good on you, including tips, FAQs, and hair care.

Table of Contents

  • Face Shape
  • Hair Type
  • Forehead Size
  • Eye Shape
  • Face Features
  • Hair Growth
  • Hair Care
  • FAQ

Face Shape

The first thing to consider when deciding whether bangs will look good on you is your face shape. Different face shapes suit different types of bangs. For example, if you have a round face, you might want to consider long, sideswept bangs to elongate your face. If you have a square face, you might want to consider soft, wispy bangs to soften your features.

It is also important to consider the width of your forehead, as this can affect the type of bangs that will suit you. If you have a wide forehead, you might want to consider thick, blunt bangs to balance out your face. If you have a narrow forehead, you might want to consider wispy, layered bangs to add some volume to your hairline.

Hair Type

The second thing to consider when deciding whether bangs will look good on you is your hair type. Different hair types suit different types of bangs. For example, if you have thick, straight hair, you might want to consider blunt, choppy bangs to add some edge to your look. If you have fine, thin hair, you might want to consider wispy, layered bangs to add some volume to your hair.

It is also important to consider the texture of your hair, as this can affect the way your bangs sit. If you have curly hair, you might want to consider longer, sideswept bangs to avoid any awkward kinks. If you have straight hair, you might want to consider shorter, blunt bangs to add some structure to your face.

Forehead Size

As mentioned earlier, the size of your forehead can affect the type of bangs that will suit you. If you have a small forehead, you might want to consider wispy, layered bangs to add some volume to your hairline. If you have a large forehead, you might want to consider thick, blunt bangs to balance out your face.

It is also important to consider the shape of your forehead. If you have a high forehead, you might want to consider longer, sideswept bangs to cover it up. If you have a low hairline, you might want to consider shorter, blunt bangs to frame your face.

Eye Shape

The shape of your eyes can also affect the type of bangs that will suit you. If you have large, round eyes, you might want to consider shorter, blunt bangs to draw attention to them. If you have small, almond-shaped eyes, you might want to consider longer, sideswept bangs to elongate your face.

It is also important to consider the distance between your eyes. If you have close-set eyes, you might want to consider longer, sideswept bangs to visually widen your face. If you have wide-set eyes, you might want to consider shorter, blunt bangs to visually narrow your face.

Face Features

Lastly, it is important to consider your face features when deciding whether bangs will look good on you. If you have strong, angular features, you might want to consider soft, wispy bangs to soften your face. If you have soft, rounded features, you might want to consider blunt, choppy bangs to add some edge to your look.

Hair Growth

Another thing to consider when deciding whether bangs will look good on you is how fast your hair grows. Bangs require regular trimming to maintain their shape and length. If you have fast-growing hair, you might find that you need to trim your bangs every few weeks. If you have slow-growing hair, you might find that your bangs take longer to grow out, which can be frustrating if you change your mind about them.

Hair Care

It is also important to consider the amount of time and effort you are willing to put into styling your bangs. Bangs require regular styling to look their best. If you have a low-maintenance hair routine, you might want to consider longer, sideswept bangs that require less styling. If you have a high-maintenance hair routine, you might want to consider shorter, blunt bangs that require more styling.

FAQ

Can I try out bangs before committing?

Yes! You can try out faux bangs by pinning up a section of your hair to create the illusion of bangs. You can also try out clip-in bangs that you can attach and remove whenever you want.

What should I do if I don't like my bangs?

If you don't like your bangs, don't panic! You can always grow them out or get them trimmed to a different style. It is important to communicate with your hairstylist to ensure that you get the look you want.

How do I maintain my bangs?

Regular trimming is key to maintaining your bangs. You should also wash and condition your bangs separately from the rest of your hair to avoid any product buildup. You can also use dry shampoo to keep your bangs looking fresh between washes.

What should I do if my bangs get oily?

If your bangs get oily, you can use dry shampoo to absorb excess oil. You can also use a small amount of baby powder to absorb oil and add some volume to your bangs.

Can I still wear my hair in a ponytail with bangs?

Yes! You can still wear your hair in a ponytail with bangs. You can either leave your bangs down for a more casual look, or pin them back with bobby pins for a more polished look.

Can I get bangs if I have curly hair?

Yes! You can still get bangs if you have curly hair. It is important to communicate with your hairstylist to ensure that your bangs are cut to suit your curl pattern.

In conclusion, there are many factors to consider when deciding whether bangs will look good on you. By considering your face shape, hair type, forehead size, eye shape, face features, hair growth, and hair care, you can make an informed decision that will suit your personal style and preferences. Remember to communicate with your hairstylist to ensure that you get the look you want, and don't be afraid to experiment with different styles!
