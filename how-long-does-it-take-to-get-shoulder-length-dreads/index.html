How Long Does It Take To Get Shoulder Length Dreads

Written by Christine Jul 11, 2023 · 8 min read

Dreadlocks have been around for centuries and have become a popular hairstyle in recent years. One of the most common questions people ask when considering getting dreadlocks is how long it will take for their hair to grow into shoulder-length dreads. In this post, we’ll explore the timeline for growing shoulder-length dreads and provide tips for maintaining them once you’ve achieved your desired length.

Table of Contents

  • How to Grow Shoulder-Length Dreads
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Growing Shoulder-Length Dreads
  • Tips for Maintaining Shoulder-Length Dreads
  • FAQs About Growing Shoulder-Length Dreads
  • Hair Care for Shoulder-Length Dreads

How to Grow Shoulder-Length Dreads

The amount of time it takes to grow shoulder-length dreads depends on several factors, including hair texture, length, and thickness. On average, it can take anywhere from six months to two years to grow shoulder-length dreads. However, some people may be able to achieve this length in as little as three months, while others may take longer than two years.

The key to growing shoulder-length dreads is patience and commitment. Dreads require regular maintenance, which includes washing, conditioning, and separating the hair to prevent matting. You’ll also need to avoid using products that contain chemicals, as they can weaken the hair and prevent it from locking up properly.

Step-by-Step Guide to Growing Shoulder-Length Dreads

1. Start with clean, dry hair. Wash your hair thoroughly with a residue-free shampoo and let it dry completely.

2. Section your hair into small, even sections. Use a rat-tail comb to divide your hair into sections that are approximately one inch wide.

3. Backcomb each section. Starting at the roots, use a dread comb to backcomb each section of hair. Hold the hair taut with one hand and use the comb to tease the hair towards your scalp.

4. Twist each section. Once you’ve backcombed a section, twist it tightly to create a dreadlock. Continue twisting the section until it feels tight and secure.

5. Repeat the process. Repeat steps 3 and 4 for each section of hair until your entire head is covered in dreadlocks.

Tips for Maintaining Shoulder-Length Dreads

1. Wash your hair regularly. Dreads require regular washing to prevent buildup and keep them clean. Use a residue-free shampoo and make sure to rinse your hair thoroughly.

2. Avoid using products that contain chemicals. Chemicals can weaken the hair and prevent it from locking up properly. Stick to natural products and avoid using anything that contains harsh chemicals.

3. Separate your dreads regularly. Use your fingers to separate your dreads regularly to prevent them from matting together.

4. Keep your dreads dry. Wet dreads can take a long time to dry and can lead to mold and mildew growth. Make sure to dry your dreads thoroughly after washing or swimming.

FAQs About Growing Shoulder-Length Dreads

Q: How often should I wash my dreads?

A: It’s important to wash your dreads regularly to prevent buildup and keep them clean. Aim to wash your dreads every two weeks.

Q: Can I dye my dreads?

A: Yes, you can dye your dreads. However, it’s important to use a dye that is safe for dreadlocks and won’t cause damage to your hair.

Q: Can I swim with my dreads?

A: Yes, you can swim with your dreads. However, it’s important to rinse your hair thoroughly with clean water after swimming to remove any chlorine or salt.

Hair Care for Shoulder-Length Dreads

When it comes to caring for shoulder-length dreads, it’s important to use natural products that won’t damage your hair. Here are some tips:

1. Use a residue-free shampoo to wash your dreads.

2. Avoid using products that contain harsh chemicals, as they can weaken the hair and prevent it from locking up properly.

3. Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your dreads moisturized.

4. Avoid using a hair dryer, as the heat can cause damage to your hair.

5. Wrap your hair at night to prevent your dreads from getting tangled while you sleep.

In conclusion, growing shoulder-length dreads can take anywhere from six months to two years, depending on your hair texture, length, and thickness. The key to achieving this length is patience and commitment. Once you’ve achieved your desired length, it’s important to maintain your dreads with regular washing, conditioning, and separating. By following these tips, you can keep your shoulder-length dreads healthy and looking great.
