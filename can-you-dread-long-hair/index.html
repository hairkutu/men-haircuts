Can You Dread Long Hair A Guide To Dreading Long Hair

Written by Robby Apr 25, 2023 · 7 min read

If you have long hair, you might be wondering if you can dread it. The answer is yes, you can dread long hair! In this guide, we'll go over everything you need to know about dreading long hair, including how to do it, step by step instructions, tips, frequently asked questions, and hair care.

Table of Contents

  • How to Dread Long Hair
  • Step by Step Instructions
  • Tips for Dreading Long Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care for Dreadlocks

How to Dread Long Hair

Dreading long hair is similar to dreading short hair, but it can take longer and requires more maintenance. Here are the basic steps:

  1. Wash your hair with a residue-free shampoo.
  2. Section your hair into small, even sections.
  3. Backcomb each section from the ends to the roots.
  4. Twist the section into a dreadlock shape.
  5. Secure the dreadlock with a rubber band or hair tie.
  6. Repeat steps 2-5 until your entire head is dreaded.

It's important to note that dreading long hair can take several hours or even days, depending on how much hair you have and how tight you want your dreadlocks to be. It's best to have a friend or professional help you with the process.

Step by Step Instructions

Here is a more detailed step by step guide to dreading long hair:

Step 1: Wash your hair

Use a residue-free shampoo to wash your hair. This will remove any oils or dirt that could prevent your hair from knotting up properly. Rinse your hair thoroughly and towel dry.

Step 2: Section your hair

Use a comb to section your hair into small, even sections. The sections should be about the size of a pencil or smaller. Secure each section with a hair tie or clip.

Step 3: Backcomb each section

Starting at the ends of each section, use a fine-toothed comb to backcomb your hair. This means combing your hair towards your scalp, which will create knots and tangles. Do this all the way up each section until you reach the roots.

Step 4: Twist each section

Once you've backcombed each section, twist it into a dreadlock shape. You can twist it clockwise or counterclockwise, depending on your preference. Keep twisting until the entire section is twisted into a tight spiral.

Step 5: Secure each dreadlock

Use a rubber band or hair tie to secure each dreadlock at the base. Make sure the rubber band is tight enough to hold the dreadlock in place, but not so tight that it cuts off circulation to your scalp.

Step 6: Repeat

Repeat steps 2-5 until your entire head is dreaded. This can take several hours or even days, depending on how much hair you have and how tight you want your dreadlocks to be.

Tips for Dreading Long Hair

Here are some tips to make the process of dreading long hair easier:

  • Get help from a friend or professional.
  • Use a crochet hook to tighten up loose hairs and smooth out the dreadlocks.
  • Wear a hat or headscarf to protect your dreadlocks while they are still forming.
  • Be patient! Dreading long hair takes time and effort.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can you dread long hair without cutting it?

Yes, you can dread long hair without cutting it. However, it's important to note that dreading long hair can take longer and requires more maintenance than dreading short hair.

How long does it take to dread long hair?

The time it takes to dread long hair depends on how much hair you have and how tight you want your dreadlocks to be. It can take anywhere from several hours to several days.

Can you wash your hair with dreadlocks?

Yes, you can wash your hair with dreadlocks. However, it's important to use a residue-free shampoo and to be gentle when washing your hair to avoid unraveling the dreadlocks.

Hair Care for Dreadlocks

Once your hair is dreaded, it's important to take care of it properly to keep it healthy and looking good. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or conditioners, as they can cause buildup and loosen the dreadlocks.
  • Use a crochet hook to tighten up loose hairs and smooth out the dreadlocks.
  • Wear a hat or headscarf to protect your dreadlocks from sun damage.

With proper care, your dreadlocks can last for years and look great!
