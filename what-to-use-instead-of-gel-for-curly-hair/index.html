What To Use Instead Of Gel For Curly Hair The Ultimate Guide

Written by Aprilia Jul 16, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you have curly hair, you know that finding the right products to tame your mane can be a challenge. While gel is a popular choice for many, it can leave your curls feeling crunchy and stiff. Plus, not everyone wants to use a product that contains alcohol or other potentially harmful ingredients. Luckily, there are plenty of alternatives to gel that can help enhance your curls without sacrificing moisture or health. In this article, we'll explore some of the best options for what to use instead of gel for curly hair.

Description

If you're tired of using gel but still want to achieve defined, bouncy curls, you're in luck. There are several alternatives to gel that can provide hold, moisture, and frizz control without the crunch. The key is to find a product that works for your hair type and texture, and to experiment with different application techniques to get the best results.

How to

When it comes to using products other than gel for curly hair, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, start with clean, well-moisturized hair. This will help your curls hold their shape and reduce frizz. Depending on your hair type, you may want to use a leave-in conditioner or curl cream before applying your chosen product.

Next, apply your product of choice to wet or damp hair, section by section. You can use your fingers or a wide-toothed comb to distribute the product evenly. Be sure to focus on the ends of your hair, where curls tend to be the driest and most prone to frizz.

Finally, let your hair air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your curls gently. Avoid touching your hair too much while it's drying, as this can disrupt the curl pattern and cause frizz.

Step by Step

Step 1: Start with clean, moisturized hair

Before applying any styling product, make sure your hair is clean and well-moisturized. You can use a gentle shampoo and conditioner or co-wash to cleanse your hair without stripping it of natural oils. Then, apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to help define your curls and reduce frizz.

Step 2: Choose your product

There are several options for what to use instead of gel for curly hair, depending on your hair type and texture. Some popular choices include:

  • Curl cream
  • Mousse
  • Gel-serum hybrid
  • Styling milk

Experiment with different products to find what works best for your hair.

Step 3: Apply product to wet or damp hair

Section your hair and apply your chosen product evenly from root to tip. Use your fingers or a wide-toothed comb to distribute the product and define your curls. Be sure to focus on the ends of your hair, where curls tend to be the driest and most prone to frizz.

Step 4: Dry your curls gently

Let your hair air dry or use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your curls gently. Avoid touching your hair too much while it's drying, as this can disrupt the curl pattern and cause frizz.

Tips

When using products other than gel for curly hair, it's important to keep a few things in mind:

  • Less is more: Start with a small amount of product and build up as needed.
  • Be gentle: Avoid touching your hair too much while it's drying to prevent frizz.
  • Experiment: Try different products and application techniques to find what works best for your hair.

FAQ

What should I use instead of gel for curly hair?

There are several options for what to use instead of gel for curly hair, including curl cream, mousse, gel-serum hybrids, and styling milk. Experiment with different products to find what works best for your hair type and texture.

Will using products other than gel make my curls crunchy?

Not necessarily. The key is to find a product that provides hold without leaving your curls feeling stiff or crunchy. Look for products that contain moisturizing ingredients like shea butter, coconut oil, or argan oil.

Can I use gel and other products together?

Absolutely. You can layer products to achieve your desired level of hold and definition. Just be sure to apply each product evenly and avoid using too much.

Hair Care

No matter what products you use on your curly hair, it's important to take good care of your locks. This means washing and conditioning your hair regularly, using a deep conditioner or hair mask once a week, and avoiding heat styling tools whenever possible. You can also protect your curls by sleeping on a silk or satin pillowcase and using a microfiber towel or t-shirt to dry your hair.

In conclusion, there are plenty of alternatives to gel for curly hair that can help you achieve beautiful, defined curls without sacrificing moisture or health. Experiment with different products and application techniques to find what works best for your hair type and texture. And don't forget to take good care of your curls with regular washing, conditioning, and deep treatments.
