How To Train Hair Slick Back A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jun 12, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of your hair falling flat and looking unkempt? Slicking back your hair is a classic hairstyle that can give you a polished and put-together look. However, training your hair to stay slicked back can be a challenge. In this guide, we will go over how to train hair slick back in relaxed English language. We will cover the basics, step-by-step instructions, tips, frequently asked questions, and hair care tips.

Table of Contents

  • The Basics of Slicking Back Hair
  • Step-by-Step Instructions
  • Tips for Training Hair Slick Back
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care Tips for Slicked Back Hair

The Basics of Slicking Back Hair

Slicking back your hair involves using a product to smooth your hair back and keep it in place. There are different products that you can use, such as pomades, gels, and waxes. These products differ in their hold and shine levels. Pomades provide a medium hold and high shine, while gels provide a strong hold and wet look. Waxes provide a strong hold and low shine.

Before you start slicking back your hair, make sure that your hair is clean and dry. Wet hair will dilute the product and make it less effective. You can use a blow dryer to dry your hair quickly.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Now that you know the basics, let's go over the step-by-step instructions on how to train hair slick back.

Step 1: Apply Product

Take a small amount of product and rub it between your palms to warm it up. Apply the product evenly to your hair, starting from the back and working your way to the front. Use a comb to distribute the product evenly and smooth out any bumps.

Step 2: Comb Hair Back

Use a comb to comb your hair back, starting from the hairline and working your way to the crown. Make sure that your hair is slicked back evenly and there are no bumps or flyaways.

Step 3: Train Hair

Use your hands to push your hair back and train it to stay slicked back. You can also use a hairbrush to brush your hair back and train it. Repeat this step until your hair stays slicked back on its own.

Step 4: Finish

Use a small amount of product to touch up any areas that need it. Use a comb or brush to smooth out any bumps or flyaways.

Tips for Training Hair Slick Back

Training your hair to stay slicked back takes time and patience. Here are some tips to help you along the way.

  • Start with a small amount of product and add more as needed. Too much product can weigh your hair down and make it look greasy.
  • Use a comb or brush to distribute the product evenly and smooth out any bumps.
  • Don't overwork your hair. Too much brushing or touching can cause your hair to become greasy and lose its shape.
  • Use a hair tie or bandana to keep your hair slicked back while you sleep. This will help train your hair to stay in place.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about training hair slick back.

How long does it take to train hair slick back?

It can take anywhere from a few weeks to a few months to train hair slick back. The amount of time it takes depends on the length and thickness of your hair, as well as your hair's natural texture.

Can I slick back curly hair?

Yes, you can slick back curly hair. However, you may need to use a stronger hold product to keep your curls in place.

How often should I wash my hair when slicking it back?

You should wash your hair every 2-3 days when slicking it back. Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils and make it dry and brittle.

Hair Care Tips for Slicked Back Hair

Here are some hair care tips for slicked back hair.

  • Use a clarifying shampoo once a week to remove any product buildup.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, such as blow dryers and straighteners.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and maintain its shape.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to nourish and strengthen your hair.

Training hair slick back takes time and patience, but the results are worth it. With these step-by-step instructions, tips, and hair care advice, you'll be able to achieve a polished and put-together look in no time.
