Why Does My Baby s Hair Stand Straight Up

Written by William Aug 09, 2023 · 6 min read

Have you ever noticed that your baby's hair seems to stand straight up, no matter how much you try to brush it down? This can be a common occurrence in babies, and it's nothing to worry about. In this article, we'll explore why your baby's hair stands up, and what you can do to help keep it under control.

What Causes Baby Hair to Stand Up?

There are a few reasons why your baby's hair may stand up:

  • Genetics: Baby hair tends to be very fine and thin, which can make it more prone to standing up. If you or your partner have naturally curly or wavy hair, your baby may have inherited those genes, which can cause their hair to stand up.
  • Dryness: If your baby's scalp is dry, their hair may stand up. This is because the hair follicles are not weighed down by the natural oils produced by the scalp.
  • Static electricity: Dry air, synthetic fabrics, and certain types of brushes can cause static electricity to build up, which can cause hair to stand up.
  • Hair growth: As your baby's hair grows, it can become too heavy for the scalp to hold down, causing it to stand up.

How to Keep Baby Hair Under Control

If you're concerned about your baby's hair standing up, there are a few things you can do to help keep it under control:

  • Use a gentle shampoo: Make sure you're using a gentle shampoo that won't dry out your baby's scalp. Look for shampoos that are specifically designed for babies.
  • Moisturize: After washing your baby's hair, use a gentle conditioner or oil to help moisturize their scalp and hair. This can help weigh down the hair and prevent it from standing up.
  • Avoid synthetic fabrics: Synthetic fabrics can cause static electricity to build up, which can cause hair to stand up. Stick to natural fabrics, like cotton, whenever possible.
  • Use a soft brush: A soft brush can help distribute natural oils throughout your baby's hair, which can help keep it under control. Avoid using brushes with plastic bristles, which can cause static electricity to build up.

FAQ About Baby Hair Standing Up

Is it normal for baby hair to stand up?

Yes, it's completely normal for baby hair to stand up. Baby hair is very fine and thin, which can make it more prone to standing up.

Will my baby's hair always stand up?

No, as your baby's hair grows and becomes heavier, it will become easier to control. In the meantime, try using a gentle shampoo and moisturizer to help weigh down their hair.

Should I be concerned if my baby's hair stands up?

No, it's nothing to worry about. Baby hair is very delicate, and it can take time for it to grow and become more manageable.

Conclusion

If your baby's hair stands up, don't worry. It's completely normal, and there are a few things you can do to help keep it under control. Use a gentle shampoo and moisturizer, avoid synthetic fabrics, and use a soft brush to distribute natural oils throughout their hair. With a little bit of patience, your baby's hair will eventually grow and become more manageable.
