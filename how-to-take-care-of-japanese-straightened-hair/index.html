How to Take Care of Japanese Straightened Hair: A Comprehensive Guide

If you have recently opted for Japanese straightening, congratulations! You now have sleek, manageable, and straight locks that will last for months. However, since Japanese straightened hair is chemically treated, it requires special care to keep it healthy and shiny. In this article, we will discuss how to take care of Japanese straightened hair, including the dos and don'ts, step-by-step instructions, and tips to maintain your gorgeous locks.

  2. How to Take Care of Japanese Straightened Hair

  4. Tips to Maintain Your Japanese Straightened Hair

  6. Hair Care Products for Japanese Straightened Hair

Description

Japanese straightening, also known as thermal reconditioning, is a popular hair treatment that can give you permanently straight hair. The process involves using chemicals and heat to break and reform the bonds in your hair, essentially resetting your hair's natural texture.

Japanese straightening is an effective way to achieve straight hair that is easy to manage, especially for those with naturally curly or wavy hair. However, since the process involves harsh chemicals and heat, it can damage your hair if not properly taken care of.

How to Take Care of Japanese Straightened Hair

Here are some dos and don'ts to keep in mind when taking care of your Japanese straightened hair:

Do

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Use a heat protectant before using any heat styling tools.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment at least once a week to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or a brush with soft bristles to detangle your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or a swim cap.

Don't

  • Use any hair products that contain alcohol, as they can dry out your hair and make it brittle.
  • Over-wash your hair, as it can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it dry.
  • Use any heat styling tools on high heat, as it can damage your hair and make it frizzy.
  • Use a brush with hard bristles, as it can damage your hair and cause breakage.
  • Use any hair accessories that are too tight, as it can cause tension on your hair and damage it.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Here are the steps you should take to take care of your Japanese straightened hair:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to wash your hair. Massage your scalp gently, and rinse thoroughly with lukewarm water.

Step 2: Apply a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Apply a deep conditioning treatment to your hair, making sure to focus on the ends. Leave it on for 5-10 minutes, and rinse thoroughly with lukewarm water.

Step 3: Towel Dry Your Hair

Gently towel dry your hair by patting it with a towel. Do not rub your hair vigorously, as it can damage your hair and cause frizz.

Step 4: Apply a Heat Protectant

Apply a heat protectant to your hair before using any heat styling tools. This will help protect your hair from heat damage and keep it healthy.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Use a low heat setting on your heat styling tools, and style your hair as desired. Be gentle when using any hair accessories, and avoid using anything that is too tight.

Tips to Maintain Your Japanese Straightened Hair

Here are some tips to help you maintain your Japanese straightened hair:

  • Avoid using any hair products that contain alcohol, as they can dry out your hair and make it brittle.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to avoid friction and prevent hair breakage.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as it can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it dry.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or a brush with soft bristles to detangle your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends and keep your hair healthy.

FAQs

Q: How often should I wash my Japanese straightened hair?

A: You should avoid over-washing your hair, as it can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it dry. Try to wash your hair every 2-3 days, and use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.

Q: Can I still color my Japanese straightened hair?

A: Yes, you can still color your Japanese straightened hair. However, you should wait at least 2 weeks after getting the treatment to color your hair.

Q: Can I swim with Japanese straightened hair?

A: Yes, you can swim with Japanese straightened hair. However, you should protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or a swim cap.

Hair Care Products for Japanese Straightened Hair

Here are some hair care products that are specifically designed for Japanese straightened hair:

  • Sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Deep conditioning treatment
  • Heat protectant
  • Silk or satin pillowcase
  • Wide-tooth comb or a brush with soft bristles

By following these tips and using the right hair care products, you can keep your Japanese straightened hair healthy and beautiful. Remember to be gentle with your hair, and avoid any harsh chemicals or heat styling tools that can damage your locks. With proper care, you can enjoy your sleek, straight hair for months to come!
