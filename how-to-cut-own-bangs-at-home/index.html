How To Cut Your Own Bangs At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Aug 12, 2023 · 6 min read

Are you tired of waiting for your next salon appointment to get your bangs trimmed? Or maybe you just want to save some money by doing it yourself? Whatever your reason, cutting your own bangs at home can be a daunting task. But don't worry, we've got you covered. Read on for our step-by-step guide on how to cut your own bangs at home.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Cut Your Own Bangs at Home
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Cutting Your Own Bangs
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Bangs

How to Cut Your Own Bangs at Home

Before we dive into the step-by-step process, let's go over some things you'll need to prepare:

  • Sharp scissors
  • A fine-tooth comb
  • Hair clips
  • A mirror
  • Patience and a steady hand

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you've got everything you need, let's get started:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Wet hair can be misleading and may result in uneven bangs.
  2. Section off the hair you want to cut. Use the fine-tooth comb to create a triangle section of hair in the front of your head. The base of the triangle should be at the top of your head and the point should be in the center of your forehead.
  3. Clip back the rest of your hair. Use the hair clips to secure the rest of your hair out of the way.
  4. Start cutting. Hold the triangle section of hair between your fingers and cut straight across. Start with small snips and work your way up to your desired length.
  5. Check for symmetry. Use the comb to make sure both sides are even.
  6. Blend the bangs. Take a small section from each side of your bangs and pull them straight up. Cut into the ends to blend the bangs with the rest of your hair.
  7. Style your new bangs. Use a blow dryer and a round brush to style your bangs.

Remember, it's always better to cut less than to cut too much. You can always go back and trim more if needed.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Bangs

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind:

  • Don't rush. Take your time and cut small sections at a time.
  • Use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can result in split ends and an uneven cut.
  • Consider your face shape. Different bang styles work better with different face shapes. Do some research beforehand to find the best style for your face shape.
  • Watch tutorials. There are plenty of tutorials online that can guide you through the process.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your own bangs:

1. How often should I trim my bangs?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how long you want your bangs to be. As a general rule, you should trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks.

2. Can I cut my bangs when they're wet?

No, it's best to cut your bangs when they're dry. Wet hair can be misleading and may result in uneven bangs.

3. What if I make a mistake?

Don't panic! If you make a mistake, you can always go back and trim more. It's better to cut less than to cut too much.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Own Bangs

Once you've cut your own bangs, it's important to take proper care of them. Here are some tips:

  • Wash and style your bangs regularly.
  • Use a heat protectant when using hot tools.
  • Avoid touching your bangs too much, as this can cause them to become greasy.
  • Consider using dry shampoo to extend the life of your bangs between washes.

Cutting your own bangs at home can be daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, it's totally doable. Just remember to take your time, use sharp scissors, and have patience. Good luck!
