What Deficiencies Cause Brittle Nails

Written by Aprilia Jun 03, 2023 · 6 min read

Brittle nails can be a frustrating and painful problem to deal with. If you’re experiencing this issue, you may be wondering what’s causing it. One possible cause of brittle nails is a deficiency in certain vitamins and minerals. In this article, we’ll explore what deficiencies can cause brittle nails and what you can do to prevent and treat this condition.

Table of Contents

  • Deficiencies That Cause Brittle Nails
  • How to Prevent Brittle Nails
  • Tips for Treating Brittle Nails
  • FAQ About Brittle Nails
  • How Brittle Nails Relate to Hair Care

Deficiencies That Cause Brittle Nails

There are several vitamins and minerals that are essential for healthy nails. If you don’t get enough of these nutrients, your nails may become weak and brittle.

Vitamin B7 (Biotin)

Biotin is a water-soluble vitamin that is essential for healthy skin, hair, and nails. If you don’t get enough biotin in your diet, you may experience brittle nails, hair loss, and skin rashes.

Iron

Iron is an important mineral that is necessary for the production of hemoglobin, a protein in your red blood cells that carries oxygen throughout your body. If you don’t get enough iron in your diet, your nails may become brittle and weak.

Zinc

Zinc is a mineral that is essential for many bodily functions, including wound healing and cell growth. If you don’t get enough zinc in your diet, your nails may become weak and brittle.

How to Prevent Brittle Nails

The best way to prevent brittle nails is to make sure you’re getting enough vitamins and minerals in your diet. Here are some tips:

  • Eat a balanced diet that includes plenty of fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean protein.
  • Take a multivitamin that contains biotin, iron, and zinc.
  • Avoid harsh chemicals, such as nail polish remover and cleaning products, which can dry out your nails.
  • Keep your nails clean and dry.
  • Wear gloves when doing household chores or working with chemicals.

Tips for Treating Brittle Nails

If you’re already experiencing brittle nails, there are several things you can do to help strengthen and repair them:

  • Apply a moisturizer to your nails and cuticles daily.
  • Use a nail hardener or strengthening treatment.
  • Avoid biting your nails or picking at them.
  • Trim your nails regularly to prevent them from breaking.

FAQ About Brittle Nails

Q: Can brittle nails be a sign of an underlying health condition?

A: Yes, brittle nails can sometimes be a symptom of an underlying health condition, such as anemia or thyroid problems. If you’re experiencing other symptoms, such as fatigue or hair loss, you should see a doctor.

Q: Are there any home remedies for brittle nails?

A: Some people find that soaking their nails in olive oil or coconut oil can help moisturize and strengthen them. However, there is limited scientific evidence to support these remedies.

How Brittle Nails Relate to Hair Care

Brittle nails and hair can often be caused by the same nutrient deficiencies. If you’re experiencing both of these issues, it’s important to make sure you’re getting enough biotin, iron, and zinc in your diet. Additionally, using gentle hair care products and avoiding heat styling can help prevent hair breakage and promote healthy hair and nails.
