How To Thin Out Long Thick Hair With Thinning Scissors A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Apr 06, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Thinning out long thick hair can be a daunting task, especially if you don't have the right tools. Using thinning scissors is a great way to get the job done without sacrificing length or volume. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps of how to thin out long thick hair with thinning scissors.

Description:

Thinning scissors are a type of shears that have one serrated edge and one straight edge. They are designed to remove weight and bulk from the hair without altering the overall length. Thinning scissors work by cutting some of the strands of hair while leaving others intact, creating a more textured and layered look.

How to Thin Out Long Thick Hair with Thinning Scissors:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. It's important to work with clean, dry hair so that you can see the natural texture and movement of your hair. Avoid using any styling products or heat tools before thinning your hair.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, section your hair into small sections using hair clips. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that you don't miss any areas. Start with the bottom layer of your hair and work your way up to the top.

Step 3: Hold the Thinning Scissors Correctly

Hold the thinning scissors with the serrated edge facing down and the straight edge facing up. Use your thumb and middle finger to hold the scissors, leaving your index finger free to guide the hair.

Step 4: Cut Small Sections of Hair

Take a small section of hair and hold it between your index and middle fingers. Slide the thinning scissors down the length of the hair, making small cuts along the way. Repeat this process until you have thinned out the desired amount of hair.

Step 5: Blend the Hair

After thinning out a section of hair, use your regular scissors to blend the hair together. This will create a seamless transition between the thinned out sections and the rest of your hair.

Step 6: Repeat the Process

Continue thinning out small sections of hair and blending them together until you have thinned out all of your hair. Be sure to take breaks and step back to assess your progress.

Tips:

  • Start with a small amount of hair and work your way up. You can always thin out more hair, but you can't add it back once it's been cut.
  • Use a comb to help guide the hair while cutting.
  • Be patient and take your time. Thinning out hair can be a time-consuming process, but the end result is worth it.

FAQ:

1. Can I use regular scissors to thin out my hair?

While you can use regular scissors to thin out your hair, they are not as effective as thinning scissors. Regular scissors can create a choppy or uneven look, while thinning scissors create a more natural and textured look.

2. Will thinning out my hair make it look thinner?

No, thinning out your hair will not make it look thinner. Thinning out your hair removes weight and bulk, creating a more textured and layered look. Your hair will still have the same amount of volume, but it will be distributed differently.

3. Can I thin out my hair if it's already thin?

Thinning out your hair is not recommended if you already have thin or fine hair. Thinning out your hair can make it look even thinner and may cause damage to the hair follicles.

Hair Care:

After thinning out your hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair nourished and hydrated. Avoid using heat tools and harsh chemicals on your hair, as this can cause damage and breakage. Trim your hair regularly to maintain the shape and style.

Thinning out long thick hair with thinning scissors can seem daunting, but with the right technique and tools, you can achieve a natural and textured look. Follow these steps and tips to achieve the perfect look.
