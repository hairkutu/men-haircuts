What Is A Low Fade Undercut

Written by Daniel Jul 15, 2023 · 8 min read

If you’re looking for a new haircut that’s stylish, trendy and easy to maintain, then a low fade undercut may be the perfect choice for you. This haircut features a short, buzzed cut on the sides and back of the head, with longer hair on top that’s styled in a variety of ways. It’s a versatile cut that can be worn by men of all ages and hair types, and it’s especially popular among those who want a modern, edgy look.

Description

A low fade undercut is a haircut that involves shaving the hair on the sides and back of the head down to the skin, gradually fading up to longer hair on top. It’s called a “low” fade because the transition from short to long hair is gradual and starts just above the ear. The undercut refers to the fact that the longer hair on top is disconnected from the shorter hair on the sides and back, creating a bold, contrasted look.

How to Get a Low Fade Undercut

To get a low fade undercut, you’ll need to visit a skilled barber or hairstylist who is familiar with the cut. Here’s a step-by-step guide to what you can expect:

Step 1: Consultation

Your barber or hairstylist will start by discussing your hair type, face shape and personal style goals. They’ll also assess the condition of your hair and scalp to determine if it’s healthy enough for the cut.

Step 2: Clipper Work

The barber or hairstylist will use clippers to shave the hair on the sides and back of your head down to the desired length. They’ll start with a longer guard and gradually work their way down to a shorter guard to create the fade.

Step 3: Scissor Work

Once the sides and back are clipped, the hairstylist will use scissors to cut and shape the longer hair on top of your head. They’ll work with you to create a style that’s tailored to your face shape and personal preferences.

Step 4: Styling

Finally, the hairstylist will apply styling products to the hair on top of your head and show you how to style it at home. They may also offer advice on hair care and maintenance to keep your cut looking fresh.

Tips for Maintaining a Low Fade Undercut

Here are some tips to help you keep your low fade undercut looking sharp:

  • Visit your barber or hairstylist regularly for touch-ups and trims.
  • Wash your hair with a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo to prevent dryness and breakage.
  • Use a lightweight, non-greasy styling product to keep your hair in place without weighing it down.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like blow dryers and straighteners too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and other environmental stressors by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner with SPF.

FAQ

Is a low fade undercut suitable for all hair types?

Yes, a low fade undercut can be worn by men with straight, wavy, curly or coily hair. However, the length and texture of your hair may affect the final look of the cut, so it’s important to discuss your hair type with your barber or hairstylist before getting the cut.

What face shape is best suited for a low fade undercut?

A low fade undercut can be adapted to suit a variety of face shapes, including oval, square, round and heart-shaped. Your barber or hairstylist can help you choose a style that flatters your face shape and enhances your features.

Can I style my low fade undercut in different ways?

Yes, one of the best things about a low fade undercut is its versatility. You can style the hair on top in a variety of ways, including slicked back, messy, textured, or with a side part. Experiment with different styling products and techniques to find a look that works for you.

Hair Care

A low fade undercut requires minimal maintenance, but it’s important to take good care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and stylish. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid applying too much heat to your hair, as it can dry it out and cause damage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when wet, then let it air dry or use a diffuser attachment with your blow dryer.
  • If you have curly or coily hair, consider using a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to define your curls and reduce frizz.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth and overall health.

By following these tips, you can enjoy a low fade undercut that looks great and feels comfortable for years to come.
