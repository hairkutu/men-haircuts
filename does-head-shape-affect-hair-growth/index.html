Does Head Shape Affect Hair Growth

Written by Aprilia May 18, 2023 · 7 min read

When it comes to hair growth, there are many factors that come into play. Genetics, diet, and lifestyle all have a role to play in how fast and healthy your hair grows. But what about head shape? Does it have an impact on hair growth? In this article, we'll explore the relationship between head shape and hair growth.

Table of Content

  • Description
  • How to Determine Your Head Shape
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Measuring Your Head Shape
  • Tips for Hair Growth
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care for All Head Shapes

Description

Head shape refers to the shape of your skull. Some people have a round head shape, while others have an oval or square shape. There are also people with a heart-shaped or diamond-shaped head. The shape of your head is determined by the size and shape of your skull.

Many people believe that head shape affects hair growth. The theory is that people with a larger head shape have more hair follicles, which leads to thicker and fuller hair. However, there is no scientific evidence to support this theory.

How to Determine Your Head Shape

Determining your head shape is easy. All you need is a measuring tape and a mirror. Stand in front of the mirror and measure the following:

  • Measure the distance from the top of your forehead to the back of your head.
  • Measure the distance from ear to ear.
  • Measure the distance from the top of your head to the base of your skull.

Step-by-Step Guide to Measuring Your Head Shape

  1. Using the measuring tape, measure the distance from the top of your forehead to the back of your head.
  2. Measure the distance from ear to ear.
  3. Measure the distance from the top of your head to the base of your skull.
  4. Write down these measurements.
  5. Compare your measurements to the following head shapes:
    • Oval: The length of your head is about one and a half times the width.
    • Round: The length and width of your head are almost equal.
    • Square: The length and width of your head are almost equal, but the angles are more defined.
    • Heart: Your forehead is wider than your jawline.
    • Diamond: Your cheekbones are wider than your forehead and jawline.

Tips for Hair Growth

While head shape may not have a direct impact on hair growth, there are other factors that can affect the health and growth of your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals
  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated
  • Reduce stress levels through meditation, yoga, or exercise
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends

FAQ

Does head shape affect hair density?

There is no scientific evidence to support the theory that head shape affects hair density.

Can hair grow in different directions on different head shapes?

Yes, it is possible for hair to grow in different directions on different head shapes. This is due to the shape of the hair follicles and the direction in which they grow.

Is it true that people with larger heads have more hair follicles?

There is no scientific evidence to support the theory that people with larger heads have more hair follicles.

Hair Care for All Head Shapes

Regardless of your head shape, taking care of your hair is important for healthy growth. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair moisturized
  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair as it can dry out your scalp
  • Use a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet as it can cause breakage
  • Use a heat protectant spray when using heat styling tools
  • Avoid using too many hair products as they can weigh down your hair

In conclusion, there is no scientific evidence to support the theory that head shape affects hair growth. While head shape may affect the direction in which your hair grows, it does not impact the number of hair follicles or the thickness of your hair. To promote healthy hair growth, focus on maintaining a healthy diet, reducing stress levels, and taking care of your hair through regular washing, conditioning, and avoiding heat styling tools.
