Does Short Hair Make You Look Skinnier Or Fatter

Written by Robby Mar 29, 2023 · 9 min read

If you're considering getting a new haircut, you may be wondering whether choosing a short style will make you look skinnier or fatter. This is a common concern for many people, and it's understandable – after all, our appearance plays a big role in how we feel about ourselves. In this article, we'll explore the question of whether short hair makes you look skinnier or fatter, and provide some tips on how to choose the right hairstyle for your body type.

Description

Short hair has been a popular trend for many years, and for good reason. It's low-maintenance, versatile, and can be styled in many different ways. However, when it comes to the question of whether it makes you look skinnier or fatter, the answer is not straightforward. The truth is, it depends on a variety of factors, including your face shape, body type, and the specific style of haircut you choose.

How to

If you're considering a short haircut and want to make sure it's flattering for your body type, there are a few things to keep in mind:

Consider Your Face Shape

The first thing to consider when choosing a short haircut is your face shape. Certain styles will be more flattering for round faces, while others will be better suited to oval or square faces. A good hairstylist can help you determine which style will work best for your face shape.

Choose the Right Length

The length of your hair can also affect how it looks on your body. If you're petite, a very short pixie cut may make you look even smaller. On the other hand, if you're tall and slim, a longer bob may be more flattering than a super-short cut.

Consider Your Body Type

Your body type is another important factor to consider when choosing a short haircut. If you're curvy, a short haircut with lots of volume can balance out your proportions and make you look slimmer. If you're very thin, a shorter, more structured cut may add some definition to your face and make you look more proportional.

Step by Step

When you're ready to get your new short haircut, follow these steps to ensure you get the best possible result:

Step 1: Do Your Research

Before you go to the salon, spend some time researching different short haircuts to get an idea of what you like and what might work for your face shape and body type.

Step 2: Find a Good Hairstylist

Choose a hairstylist who is experienced with short haircuts and can help you choose a style that will flatter your face shape and body type.

Step 3: Communicate Clearly

When you're at the salon, be clear about what you want and don't want. Bring pictures if you can, and be open to your hairstylist's suggestions.

Step 4: Style It Right

Once you have your new short haircut, it's important to style it properly to get the most flattering look. Your hairstylist can show you how to style your hair at home, or you can experiment with different products and techniques until you find what works best for you.

Tips

Here are a few additional tips to keep in mind when choosing a short haircut:

- Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles until you find what works best for you. - Consider your hair texture and how it will affect the final look of your haircut. - Be realistic about the amount of time you're willing to spend styling your hair each day. - Remember that confidence is key – if you feel good about your haircut, you'll look great no matter what.

FAQ

Q: Will a short haircut make me look skinnier or fatter?

A: It depends on a variety of factors, including your face shape, body type, and the specific style of haircut you choose. A good hairstylist can help you choose a style that will flatter your unique features.

Q: How do I know if a short haircut will look good on me?

A: The best way to know if a short haircut will look good on you is to consult with a hairstylist. They can evaluate your face shape, body type, and hair texture to recommend a style that will be flattering.

Q: Is short hair more low-maintenance than long hair?

A: Generally speaking, short hair is more low-maintenance than long hair. However, it still requires regular trims and styling to look its best.

Hair Care

Whether you have short hair or long hair, it's important to take care of it properly to keep it looking healthy and shiny. Here are a few tips for caring for short hair:

- Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that are formulated for your hair type. - Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair. - Trim your hair regularly to keep it looking neat and healthy. - Consider using a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to keep your hair moisturized and nourished.

Conclusion

So, does short hair make you look skinnier or fatter? The answer is that it depends on a variety of factors, including your face shape, body type, and the specific style of haircut you choose. The most important thing is to choose a haircut that makes you feel confident and comfortable – when you feel good about yourself, you'll look great no matter what.
