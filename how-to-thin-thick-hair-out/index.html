How To Thin Thick Hair Out A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jul 31, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Thick hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it may look voluminous and healthy, it can also be heavy and difficult to manage. Thinning out your hair can help reduce the weight, making it easier to style and manage. In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to thin thick hair out.

How to Thin Thick Hair Out

Thinning out hair involves removing some of the hair strands to reduce the overall volume. This can be done in a few different ways, including using thinning shears, razor cutting, or point cutting. Here is a step-by-step guide on how to thin thick hair out:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair as you normally would. It's important to have clean, dry hair before you begin thinning it out.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Next, section your hair into manageable pieces using hair clips. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that you are thinning out your hair evenly.

Step 3: Decide on a Thinning Technique

There are a few different techniques you can use to thin out your hair. Thinning shears, razor cutting, and point cutting are all effective methods. Choose the technique that you feel most comfortable with.

Step 4: Begin Thinning Your Hair

Start at the back of your head and work your way forward. Take a small section of hair and use your thinning shears, razor, or scissors to remove some of the hair strands. Work in small sections, thinning out your hair gradually.

Step 5: Check Your Progress

As you work, check your progress in the mirror. Make sure that you are thinning out your hair evenly and that you are happy with the overall look.

Step 6: Finish Thinning Your Hair

Once you have thinned out all of the sections of your hair, use a comb to blend the layers together. This will ensure that your hair looks natural and not choppy.

Step 7: Style Your Hair

Once you have thinned out your hair, you can style it as you normally would. You may find that your hair is easier to manage and style now that it is thinner.

Tips for Thinning Out Thick Hair

Thinning out thick hair can be a bit tricky, so here are some tips to help you achieve the best results:

  • Start by thinning out small sections of hair. You can always thin out more if needed, but it's difficult to add hair back once it's been removed.
  • Use sharp scissors or thinning shears to ensure a clean cut.
  • Don't thin your hair too much. You still want to have some volume and thickness to your hair.
  • Consult with a hairstylist if you're unsure about how to thin out your hair.

FAQs

Can I thin out my hair at home?

Yes, you can thin out your hair at home. However, it's important to use the proper tools and technique to ensure that you achieve the best results.

What is the best tool to use for thinning hair?

Thinning shears are the best tool to use for thinning out hair. They are designed to remove some of the hair strands while leaving the rest intact.

How often should I thin out my hair?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how thick it is. As a general rule, you should aim to thin out your hair every 6-8 weeks.

Hair Care for Thinned Out Hair

Once you have thinned out your hair, it's important to take good care of it to ensure that it stays healthy and looks its best. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that are designed for your hair type.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often, as this can damage the hair strands.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking neat and healthy.
  • Avoid over-styling your hair, as this can cause breakage and damage.

Thinning out thick hair can be a great way to reduce the weight and make it easier to manage. With the right tools and technique, you can achieve a natural-looking, voluminous hairstyle. Follow our step-by-step guide and tips to achieve the best results.
