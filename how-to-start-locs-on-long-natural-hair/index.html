How To Start Locs On Long Natural Hair A Step By Step Guide

Are you thinking of starting locs on your long natural hair but don't know where to begin? Don't worry, we've got you covered! In this article, we will provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to start locs on long natural hair, along with tips and FAQs to help you along the way. Let's get started!

How to Start Locs on Long Natural Hair

Starting locs on long natural hair requires patience, dedication, and a lot of care. There are several methods to start locs, including the traditional method, freeform method, and interlocking method. It is essential to choose a method that works best for your hair type and lifestyle.

Before starting the process, make sure that your hair is clean and well-moisturized. Avoid using heavy oils or products that may weigh down your hair. It is also recommended to seek the help of a professional stylist who specializes in natural hair care.

Step-by-Step Guide

1. Section your hair

Using a rat-tail comb or your fingers, section your hair into small, even parts. The size of the sections will depend on the method you choose to start your locs. The traditional method requires smaller sections, while the freeform method allows for larger sections.

2. Start the locs

There are several ways to start your locs, including the comb coil method, two-strand twist method, or braids method. Each method requires twisting or braiding the hair until it forms a coil or knot. It is essential to keep the coils or knots tight to prevent them from unraveling.

3. Maintain the locs

Once the locs are formed, it is essential to maintain them by keeping them clean and moisturized. Use a residue-free shampoo to wash your hair and avoid using heavy oils or products that may cause buildup. It is also recommended to retwist or interlock the locs every 4-6 weeks to maintain their shape.

Tips for Starting Locs on Long Natural Hair

  • Be patient. Starting locs on long natural hair takes time and dedication.
  • Keep your hair clean and well-moisturized.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that may cause buildup.
  • Choose a method that works best for your hair type and lifestyle.
  • Seek the help of a professional stylist who specializes in natural hair care.

FAQs about Starting Locs on Long Natural Hair

1. How long does it take to start locs on long natural hair?

It can take anywhere from 6 months to a year to start locs on long natural hair, depending on the method used and how fast your hair grows.

2. Can I start locs on relaxed hair?

Yes, it is possible to start locs on relaxed hair, but it may take longer for the hair to form knots due to the straighter texture.

3. Can I wash my hair after starting locs?

Yes, it is essential to keep your hair clean and well-moisturized after starting locs. Use a residue-free shampoo to wash your hair and avoid using heavy oils or products that may cause buildup.

Hair Care Tips for Maintaining Locs on Long Natural Hair

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that may cause buildup.
  • Retwist or interlock the locs every 4-6 weeks to maintain their shape.
  • Wrap your hair at night with a silk or satin scarf to prevent breakage.
  • Avoid using heat on your hair, as it can cause damage and dryness.

Starting locs on long natural hair may seem daunting, but with patience and dedication, you can achieve the look you desire. Remember to choose a method that works best for your hair type and lifestyle, and seek the help of a professional stylist if needed. With proper care and maintenance, your locs will thrive and grow beautifully.
