How To Cut Toddler Boy Hair At Home With Scissors

Written by Eveline Jun 06, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're a parent of a toddler boy, you know how quickly their hair can grow out of control. While taking them to a professional barber or stylist is an option, it can also be costly and time-consuming. That's why cutting their hair at home with scissors can be a great alternative. In this article, we will guide you on how to cut toddler boy hair at home with scissors, step by step.

How to Cut Toddler Boy Hair at Home with Scissors

Step 1: Preparation

Before starting the haircut, it's essential to prepare the area and your toddler. Choose a well-lit area and place a towel around your child's shoulders to catch the hair. It's also a good idea to have some distractions like a favorite toy or a video playing to keep them calm and still.

Step 2: Choose the Right Scissors

It's important to use the right type of scissors when cutting toddler boy hair. Choose a pair of sharp, straight scissors that are specifically designed for cutting hair. Avoid using regular household scissors or dull ones as they can cause uneven cuts or damage hair.

Step 3: Start with the Top

Begin by cutting the top of your toddler's hair. Take a small section of hair between your fingers and gently trim with the scissors. Continue to cut small sections of hair until you reach the desired length. Remember to cut slowly and carefully, so you can avoid any mistakes.

Step 4: Trim the Sides and Back

Once you've cut the top, move on to the sides and back. Use the same technique of taking small sections of hair between your fingers and trimming to the desired length. Be mindful of any cowlicks or natural patterns of hair growth and adjust accordingly.

Step 5: Fine-Tuning

After you've finished cutting your toddler's hair, it's time to fine-tune it. Use the scissors to trim any stray hairs or make small adjustments to the length. You can also use a comb to even out the hair and ensure it's all the same length.

Tips for Cutting Toddler Boy Hair at Home with Scissors

  • Start with a longer length and gradually cut shorter to avoid any mistakes
  • Use a spray bottle to dampen the hair before cutting to make it easier to work with
  • Take breaks if your toddler becomes fidgety or uncooperative
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your child's head as you cut
  • Be patient and take your time to ensure a neat and even cut

FAQs

Can I cut my toddler boy's hair with regular scissors?

No, it's not recommended to use regular scissors to cut toddler boy hair. They can cause uneven cuts or damage hair, which can be painful for your child.

Do I need to wet my toddler's hair before cutting?

Absolutely! Wetting the hair before cutting makes it easier to work with and ensures a more even cut.

What if I make a mistake while cutting?

Don't worry! If you make a mistake while cutting, you can always take a break and come back to it later. You can also use a comb to even out any uneven spots or make small adjustments to the length.

Hair Care after Cutting Toddler Boy Hair at Home with Scissors

After you've finished cutting your toddler's hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Use a child-friendly shampoo and conditioner to wash their hair and avoid any harsh chemicals or products. You can also use a leave-in conditioner or detangler to make it easier to comb through. Regular haircuts every 6-8 weeks will also help maintain a tidy and healthy head of hair.

With these tips and steps, you can confidently cut your toddler boy's hair at home with scissors. Remember to take your time, be patient, and have fun!
