Best Eye Makeup For Over 60

Written by Eveline Jun 23, 2023 Â· 9 min read

As we age, our skin and features change. This includes our eyes, which can become more hooded, saggy or wrinkled. However, this doesn't mean that we can't still enjoy wearing makeup and enhancing our natural beauty. In fact, makeup can be a great way to feel confident and youthful, no matter what your age is. Here are some tips on the best eye makeup for over 60.

Description

As we get older, our skin loses its elasticity and becomes thinner, which can result in wrinkles, fine lines and sagging. Our eyes are particularly vulnerable to these changes, as they are one of the most expressive and delicate parts of our face. Therefore, it's important to choose eye makeup products that are gentle, nourishing and flattering.

How to

The best eye makeup for over 60 should be simple, natural and elegant. Here are some steps to follow:

Step 1: Prep your skin

Before applying any makeup, make sure to cleanse, moisturize and prime your skin. This will create a smooth and hydrated base for your makeup and help it last longer. Use a gentle cleanser, a hydrating serum and a primer that suits your skin type.

Step 2: Choose your eyeshadow

For a natural and youthful look, choose soft and neutral eyeshadow shades that complement your skin tone and eye color. Avoid using bold or glittery colors that can draw attention to wrinkles or creases. Matte shades are usually more flattering than shimmery ones, but you can add a subtle highlight on your brow bone to create dimension.

Step 3: Apply your eyeliner

Eyeliner can help define your eyes and make them appear more awake and alert. However, avoid using harsh or thick lines that can make your eyes look smaller or droopy. Instead, use a soft and smudgy pencil or a liquid liner with a fine tip to create a thin and natural-looking line. You can also use a brown or gray shade instead of black to soften your look.

Step 4: Add mascara

Mascara is a great way to make your lashes look longer and fuller, which can enhance your eyes and make them appear more youthful. However, avoid using heavy or clumpy formulas that can weigh down your lashes or create a messy look. Instead, use a lightweight and volumizing mascara that separates and lifts your lashes. You can also use a lash curler to create a natural curve.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when applying eye makeup for over 60:

  • Use a gentle and fragrance-free makeup remover to avoid irritating your eyes or skin.
  • Avoid using too much powder or glitter on your eyelids, as they can settle into creases or wrinkles.
  • Use a concealer or color corrector to cover dark circles or discoloration around your eyes.
  • Choose an eyebrow pencil or powder that matches your natural hair color and shape your brows to frame your eyes.
  • If you wear glasses, choose frames that flatter your face shape and don't hide your eyes. You can also adjust your makeup accordingly.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about eye makeup for over 60:

  • Can I still wear eyeshadow if I have wrinkles or hooded eyes?

    • Yes, you can still wear eyeshadow, but choose soft and matte shades that don't emphasize your wrinkles or hooded areas. Avoid using heavy or dark colors that can make your eyes look smaller or tired.

  • What type of eyeliner is best for mature eyes?

    • A soft and smudgy pencil or a liquid liner with a fine tip is best for mature eyes. Avoid using harsh or thick lines that can make your eyes look smaller or droopy. You can also use a brown or gray shade instead of black to soften your look.

  • Do I need to use a primer before applying eyeshadow?

    • Yes, using a primer before applying eyeshadow can help create a smooth and long-lasting base for your makeup. It can also prevent creasing, fading or smudging.

  • How can I make my eyelashes look longer and fuller?

    • You can use a lightweight and volumizing mascara that separates and lifts your lashes. You can also use a lash curler to create a natural curve. Avoid using heavy or clumpy formulas that can weigh down your lashes or create a messy look.

  • Should I match my eyeshadow to my outfit or accessories?

    • No, it's not necessary to match your eyeshadow to your outfit or accessories. Instead, choose eyeshadow shades that complement your skin tone and eye color, and create a natural and cohesive look.

Hair Care

Along with your makeup, your hair can also play a role in enhancing your overall appearance and confidence. Here are some hair care tips for over 60:

  • Choose a hairstyle that suits your face shape and hair type, and complements your natural features.
  • Use nourishing and hydrating hair products that prevent dryness, frizz or breakage.
  • Consider getting a professional haircut or color that flatters your skin tone and enhances your complexion.
  • Use heat protectant sprays or serums when styling your hair with hot tools, and avoid using them too often.
  • Experiment with different hair accessories, such as headbands, clips or scarves, to add a pop of color or texture to your look.

With these tips and tricks, you can enjoy wearing eye makeup and feeling confident and radiant at any age. Remember to choose products that suit your skin type and needs, and don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and looks.
