How To Layer Short Hair In The Back Yourself

If you have short hair, you might feel like there's not much you can do with it. But, by layering your hair, you can add texture and dimension to your style. In this article, we'll show you how to layer short hair in the back yourself. By following our step-by-step guide, you'll be able to achieve a chic and modern look that will turn heads.

How to Layer Short Hair in the Back Yourself

Layering short hair in the back can be a bit tricky, but with the right tools and technique, it's definitely doable. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp haircutting scissors
  • Comb
  • Sectioning clips

Once you have these tools, you're ready to start layering your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Wash and dry your hair

Start by washing your hair and drying it completely. Make sure your hair is completely dry, as wet hair can be more difficult to cut and style.

Step 2: Section your hair

Use your comb to section your hair into three parts: the top, middle, and bottom. Use the sectioning clips to keep each section in place.

Step 3: Start cutting

Start with the bottom section of your hair. Take small sections of hair and hold them between your fingers. Use the scissors to cut the hair at an angle, starting at the ends and working your way up. Repeat this process for the middle and top sections of your hair.

Step 4: Blend the layers

Once you've cut all the layers, use your comb to blend them together. This will create a seamless look and prevent any harsh lines or choppy layers.

Step 5: Style your hair

Now that your hair is layered, you can style it any way you like. Add some texture with a texturizing spray or use a straightener to create sleek and smooth locks.

Tips for Layering Short Hair

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when layering short hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair to ensure that you don't cut too much off at once.
  • Always cut at an angle to create a layered effect.
  • Don't forget to blend the layers together to create a seamless look.
  • If you're not sure how short to cut your hair, start with a longer length and work your way shorter.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I layer my hair if it's already short?

Yes! You can layer short hair to add texture and dimension to your style.

Do I need to use special scissors to cut my hair?

Yes, it's important to use sharp haircutting scissors to ensure a clean and precise cut.

Should I layer my hair all over or just in the back?

This depends on the look you're going for. If you want a more dramatic layered look, you can layer your hair all over. If you just want to add some texture to the back of your hair, you can focus your layering in that area.

Hair Care for Layered Short Hair

Now that you've layered your short hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips for maintaining your layered style:

  • Use a hydrating shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Get regular trims to keep your layers looking fresh and prevent split ends.
  • Consider using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to add extra moisture to your hair.

With these tips and our step-by-step guide, you'll be able to layer your short hair in the back yourself and achieve a chic and modern look. Whether you're going for a sleek and smooth style or a textured and tousled look, layered hair is always on-trend.
