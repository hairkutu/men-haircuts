How To Cut Your Own Thick Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Jun 18, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune at the hair salon every few weeks just to get a trim? Cutting your own hair can save you time and money, but it can be intimidating, especially if you have thick hair. However, with the right tools and techniques, cutting your own thick hair at home can be easy and stress-free. In this article, we'll provide a step-by-step guide on how to cut your own thick hair at home, along with tips, FAQs, and hair care advice.

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting your own hair, make sure you have the right tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Clip or hair tie
  • Comb or brush
  • Handheld mirror or second mirror

Make sure your scissors are sharp and specifically designed for cutting hair. Dull scissors can cause split ends and uneven cuts.

Also, make sure you have a clear workspace with good lighting.

Preparing Your Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, wash and dry it as you normally would. Don't apply any products or heat styling tools, as this can affect the way your hair falls and make it harder to cut.

If you have curly or wavy hair, consider cutting it when it's dry. This will help you see how your hair falls naturally and avoid any surprises once it dries.

Sectioning Your Hair

Once your hair is dry, section it off into four quadrants using clips or a hair tie. This will make it easier to cut each section evenly.

Start by sectioning off the top half of your hair, then divide the bottom half into two sections.

Use a comb or brush to smooth out each section before cutting.

Cutting Your Hair

Start by cutting the ends of each section. Hold the ends of your hair between two fingers, then cut straight across.

If you want to create layers, take a small section of hair from the top layer and twist it. Cut the ends at an angle, then release the twist. Repeat this process for each layer.

If you're not sure how much to cut, start with a small amount and work your way up. You can always cut more later, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut.

Blending Your Layers

Once you've cut your layers, use a comb or brush to blend them together. This will help create a seamless look and prevent any harsh lines.

If you notice any uneven spots or long pieces, go back and trim them.

Trimming Your Bangs

If you have bangs, trim them last. Start by sectioning off your bangs and combing them straight down. Use your fingers to hold the ends of your bangs, then cut straight across.

If you want side-swept bangs, cut them at an angle.

Finishing Touches

Once you've finished cutting your hair, use a handheld mirror or second mirror to check the back. Look for any uneven spots or pieces that are too long.

If you're happy with the way your hair looks, give it a final trim and style it as you normally would.

If you're not happy with the results, don't panic. You can always book an appointment with a professional stylist to fix any mistakes.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

  • Start with small sections and work your way up
  • Use sharp scissors
  • Don't cut too much at once
  • Take breaks if you need to
  • Don't rush the process
  • Have a backup plan in case you make a mistake

FAQs About Cutting Your Own Hair

Is it easy to cut your own hair?

It can be easy if you have the right tools and techniques. However, it can also be challenging, especially if you have thick hair or want to create a specific style.

How often should I cut my own hair?

It depends on your hair type and how fast it grows. On average, you should trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and maintain healthy hair.

Can I cut my own hair if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your own hair if you have curly hair. However, it's important to cut it when it's dry and use caution when cutting each curl individually.

Hair Care Tips for Thick Hair

Thick hair requires extra care to keep it healthy and manageable. Here are some tips:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week
  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals and dyes
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends

With these tips and techniques, you can confidently cut your own thick hair at home. Remember to take your time, use caution, and have fun with it!
