How To See If A Haircut Would Look Good On Me Tips And Tricks

Written by Aprilia Jun 26, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you thinking of getting a new haircut but not sure if it would look good on you? Don't worry, you're not alone. Choosing the right haircut can be a daunting task, especially if you're not sure what will suit your face shape, hair type, and lifestyle. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks on how to see if a haircut would look good on you. Read on to find out more!

Step 1: Determine Your Face Shape

The first step in choosing the right haircut is to determine your face shape. Your face shape plays a crucial role in determining which hairstyles will flatter your features. There are several face shapes, including oval, round, square, heart, and diamond. To determine your face shape, pull your hair back and look in the mirror. Take note of your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline. Once you've identified your face shape, you can look for hairstyles that will enhance your features.

Step 2: Consider Your Lifestyle

When choosing a new haircut, it's essential to consider your lifestyle. Are you low-maintenance or high-maintenance when it comes to hair? Do you have time to style your hair every day, or do you prefer a wash-and-go style? If you're a busy mom or have a demanding job, you'll want a low-maintenance haircut that you can style quickly. On the other hand, if you have more time to devote to your hair, you can opt for a more complicated style.

Step 3: Look for Inspiration

If you're not sure what kind of haircut you want, look for inspiration online or in magazines. You can find plenty of haircut ideas by searching for "haircut ideas for [your face shape]" or "trendy haircuts for [your hair type]." You can also save pictures of haircuts you like and show them to your hairstylist.

Step 4: Use Virtual Try-On Tools

If you're hesitant to commit to a new haircut, you can use virtual try-on tools to see how different haircuts would look on you. Many hairstyling apps and websites offer virtual try-on tools that allow you to upload a photo of yourself and try on different hairstyles. This can give you a good idea of how a particular haircut would look on you.

Step 5: Consult with Your Hairstylist

Once you've narrowed down your options and have a good idea of what you want, consult with your hairstylist. Your hairstylist can give you valuable advice on which haircuts will flatter your features and work with your hair type. They can also make recommendations based on your lifestyle and maintenance preferences. Be open to your hairstylist's suggestions and trust their expertise.

Tips for Choosing the Right Haircut

  • Choose a haircut that flatters your face shape.
  • Consider your lifestyle and maintenance preferences.
  • Look for inspiration online or in magazines.
  • Use virtual try-on tools to see how different haircuts would look on you.
  • Consult with your hairstylist for advice and recommendations.

FAQs

Q: Which face shape is the most common?

A: The most common face shape is oval, which is characterized by balanced proportions and a gently rounded hairline.

Q: What should I do if I don't like my new haircut?

A: If you're not happy with your new haircut, talk to your hairstylist. They may be able to make adjustments or suggest a different style that will suit you better.

Hair Care Tips

Once you've chosen a new haircut, it's essential to take care of your hair to keep it looking its best. Here are some hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as hair straighteners and curling irons, too often.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and heat by wearing a hat or using a UV-protectant spray.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to nourish your hair.

By following these tips and tricks, you can choose the right haircut for you and keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful. Remember, your hair is an extension of your personality, so choose a style that reflects your unique style and personality.
