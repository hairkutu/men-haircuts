How To Cut Thick Hair Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank Aug 04, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, especially if you have thick hair that requires extra attention. However, with the right tools and techniques, it is possible to achieve salon-quality results from the comfort of your own home. In this article, we will cover everything you need to know about how to cut thick hair yourself, including tips, tricks, and frequently asked questions.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Thick Hair Yourself
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Thick Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care for Thick Hair

How to Cut Thick Hair Yourself

The first step to cutting your own hair is to invest in the right tools. For thick hair, you will need a set of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section your hair. It is also helpful to have a handheld mirror or a way to see the back of your head.

Before you start cutting, it is important to have a clear idea of what you want your hair to look like. You can find inspiration from magazines, social media, or by looking at pictures of yourself with a hairstyle you liked.

Once you have your tools and inspiration, it's time to start cutting! Follow these steps for a successful at-home haircut:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Wash and dry your hair
  2. Section your hair into four parts: top, sides, and back
  3. Starting at the back, take a small section of hair and comb it straight
  4. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and snip the ends with your scissors
  5. Continue working in small sections, moving from the back to the sides and top
  6. When you reach the top, be careful not to cut too much off
  7. Check your work by using a mirror or asking a friend to look at the back of your head
  8. If necessary, make small adjustments until you are happy with the length and shape of your hair
  9. Style your hair as desired

Tips for Cutting Thick Hair

Cutting thick hair can be a challenge, but these tips can help:

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid splitting or damaging your hair
  • Cut your hair when it is dry, as wet hair can appear longer and may result in uneven cutting
  • Work in small sections to ensure an even cut
  • Don't be afraid to make small adjustments as you go
  • Consider investing in hair thinning scissors if you have extremely thick hair

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I cut my own hair if I have never done it before?

Yes, you can! It may take some practice, but with the right tools and techniques, cutting your own hair can be a rewarding experience.

What if I make a mistake?

Don't worry! Hair grows back, and you can always make small adjustments or visit a salon to fix any mistakes.

How often should I cut my thick hair?

This depends on the length and style of your hair, as well as how quickly it grows. As a general rule, it is recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy hair.

Hair Care for Thick Hair

Cutting your own hair is just one part of maintaining healthy, beautiful hair. Here are some tips for caring for your thick hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent stripping your hair of natural oils
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, and always use a heat protectant spray
  • Try using a hair mask once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, and avoid brushing it when wet to prevent breakage

With these tips and techniques, you can confidently cut your own thick hair at home. Remember to take your time, work in small sections, and don't be afraid to make small adjustments as you go. Happy cutting!
