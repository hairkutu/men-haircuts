How To Cut A Man s Curly Hair At Home

Written by Daniel May 29, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Curly hair can be a challenge to cut, especially if you're not a professional hairstylist. However, with the right tools and techniques, you can give your curly-haired partner or family member a fresh haircut right at home. In this article, we'll go over how to cut a man's curly hair at home, step by step. We'll also provide some tips, answer frequently asked questions, and go over some hair care tips to keep his new haircut looking great.

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting, make sure you have the right tools on hand:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Electric clippers with guard attachments
  • Comb
  • Hair clips or ties
  • Mirror

It's also a good idea to have a clean towel or cape to drape around the person's shoulders to catch any hair clippings.

Step 1: Prepare the Hair and Tools

Start by washing and conditioning the hair, and then towel-dry it until it's damp but not dripping. Comb through the hair to remove any tangles, and then section it off with hair clips or ties. Use the electric clippers with the appropriate guard attachment to trim the hair on the sides and back to the desired length. You can use a shorter guard for a closer cut, or a longer guard for a more natural look. Be sure to follow the natural curve of the head and use a steady hand to avoid any uneven patches.

Step 2: Trim the Sides

Start by removing the guard from the clippers and using the bare blades to trim around the ears and neckline for a clean look. Use the scissors to trim any longer hairs that the clippers may have missed, and then move on to the top of the head.

Step 3: Cut the Top

Using the scissors, cut the hair on top to the desired length. Keep in mind that curly hair will shrink up as it dries, so it's best to err on the side of caution and cut less than you might think. Start by cutting small sections of hair at a time, and then comb through each section to make sure it's even. You can also twist small sections of hair around your finger and cut off any stray ends that stick out.

Step 4: Blend the Top and Sides

To blend the top and sides, use the scissors to cut at a slight angle, starting at the top of the head and working your way down to the sides. This will create a natural-looking transition between the shorter sides and longer top. Use the comb to lift sections of hair up and away from the head, and then trim any longer hairs that stick out.

Step 5: Cut the Back

Finally, use the scissors to trim the hair at the back of the head. You can either cut it straight across or at a slight angle, depending on the desired style. Be sure to keep checking in the mirror to make sure everything looks even.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging the hair.
  • Work in small sections to ensure evenness.
  • Don't cut too much at once, as curly hair shrinks up as it dries.
  • Use the point-cutting technique to create texture and remove bulk.
  • Be patient and don't rush the process.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I cut curly hair?

It depends on the length and style of the hair, but on average, every 6-8 weeks is a good rule of thumb.

What's the best way to cut curly hair?

The best way is to work in small sections, use sharp scissors, and cut the hair when it's damp but not soaking wet.

What if I make a mistake?

Don't panic! Mistakes happen, and hair grows back. If you make a mistake, try to fix it with the scissors or clippers, or seek the help of a professional stylist if needed.

Hair Care Tips

After the haircut, it's important to take care of the hair to keep it looking healthy and fresh:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping the hair of natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage the hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to help define and control the curls.
  • Get regular trims to keep the hair looking neat and healthy.

Cutting curly hair at home can be a fun and rewarding experience, but it does require some patience and practice. By following these steps and tips, you can give your curly-haired partner or family member a great new haircut right in the comfort of your own home.
