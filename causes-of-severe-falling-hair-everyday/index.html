Causes Of Severe Falling Hair Everyday Understanding The Problem

Written by Christine Aug 18, 2023 · 7 min read

If you’re experiencing severe hair loss every day, it can be a stressful and frustrating experience. It’s important to understand the causes of this problem and how to address it. In this article, we’ll cover the common causes of severe falling hair everyday, how to prevent hair loss, and hair care tips to promote healthy hair growth.

Table of Contents

  • Causes of Severe Falling Hair Everyday
  • Preventing Hair Loss
  • Hair Care Tips for Healthy Hair Growth
  • FAQ

Causes of Severe Falling Hair Everyday

There are several factors that may contribute to severe falling hair everyday. Some of the common causes of hair loss include:

Hormonal Imbalance

Hormonal imbalances, such as those that occur during pregnancy, menopause, or thyroid problems, can lead to hair loss. The hormone dihydrotestosterone (DHT) is produced in higher quantities in men and women with hormonal imbalances, which can cause hair follicles to shrink and eventually stop producing hair.

Stress

Stress can cause hair loss by disrupting the natural hair growth cycle. When you’re stressed, your body produces more hormone cortisol, which can lead to hair thinning and falling out. Stress can also cause scalp inflammation, which can damage hair follicles and cause hair loss.

Nutritional Deficiencies

Nutritional deficiencies, such as iron, vitamin D, and protein, can lead to hair loss. Your hair needs nutrients to grow and stay healthy. If you’re not getting enough nutrients, your hair may become weak and fall out.

Medications

Some medications can cause hair loss as a side effect. These include chemotherapy drugs, blood thinners, and antidepressants. If you’re experiencing hair loss and you’re taking medication, talk to your doctor about possible alternatives.

Hairstyles and Hair Treatments

Tight hairstyles, such as braids and ponytails, can cause hair loss by pulling on the hair follicles. Chemical treatments, such as perms and hair dyes, can also damage hair and cause it to fall out.

Preventing Hair Loss

While some causes of hair loss may be beyond your control, there are steps you can take to prevent or reduce hair loss:

Eat a Balanced Diet

Eating a diet rich in nutrients, such as iron, vitamin D, and protein, can help keep your hair healthy and prevent hair loss.

Reduce Stress

Reducing stress through exercise, meditation, or other relaxation techniques can help prevent hair loss.

Avoid Tight Hairstyles and Chemical Treatments

Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on the hair follicles and chemical treatments that can damage hair and cause it to fall out.

Use Gentle Hair Care Products

Use gentle hair care products that don’t contain harsh chemicals that can damage hair.

Hair Care Tips for Healthy Hair Growth

Here are some tips for promoting healthy hair growth:

Wash Your Hair Regularly

Wash your hair regularly to remove dirt and oil that can clog hair follicles and lead to hair loss.

Massage Your Scalp

Massaging your scalp can help improve blood flow to the hair follicles, which can promote healthy hair growth.

Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools, such as blow dryers and flat irons, which can damage hair and cause it to fall out.

FAQ

Q: How much hair loss is normal?

A: It’s normal to lose 50 to 100 hairs per day. However, if you’re experiencing more hair loss than this, it may be a sign of a problem.

Q: Can hair loss be reversed?

A: Hair loss may be reversible depending on the cause. If your hair loss is due to a nutritional deficiency or hormonal imbalance, it may be reversible with proper treatment.

Q: Are there any natural remedies for hair loss?

A: Some natural remedies for hair loss include massaging the scalp with essential oils, such as lavender and rosemary, and taking supplements, such as biotin and zinc.


By understanding the causes of severe falling hair everyday and taking steps to prevent and treat hair loss, you can promote healthy hair growth and feel confident and beautiful.
