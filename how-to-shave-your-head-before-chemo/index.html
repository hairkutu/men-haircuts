How To Shave Your Head Before Chemo A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel May 30, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Dealing with cancer can be overwhelming, and the side effects of chemotherapy can be brutal. One of the most difficult side effects of chemo is hair loss. For many people, shaving their head before chemo can be a way to take control of the situation and prepare themselves for what's to come. In this post, we'll provide a step-by-step guide on how to shave your head before chemo, as well as some tips and FAQs to help you through the process.

How to Shave Your Head Before Chemo: Step-by-Step

Before you start, gather all the necessary tools and supplies:

  • Razor or clippers with a guard
  • Shaving cream or oil
  • Towel or cape to protect your clothes
  • Handheld mirror
  • Electric trimmer (optional)

Now, let's get started:

Step 1: Trim your hair

If your hair is long, start by trimming it with scissors or an electric trimmer. This will make the shaving process easier and less messy.

Step 2: Apply shaving cream or oil

Apply a generous amount of shaving cream or oil to your scalp. This will help lubricate the skin and prevent razor burn.

Step 3: Start shaving

Using a razor or clippers with a guard, start shaving your head. Start at the top and work your way down, going against the grain of your hair. Be sure to rinse the razor or clippers frequently to remove hair and shaving cream.

Step 4: Check for missed spots

Once you've finished shaving, use a handheld mirror to check for any missed spots. If you find any, go over them again with the razor or clippers.

Step 5: Rinse and dry

Rinse your scalp with cool water to remove any remaining shaving cream. Pat your scalp dry with a towel.

Tips for Shaving Your Head Before Chemo

Here are some tips to make the process easier:

  • Use a handheld mirror to see the back of your head.
  • Trim your hair before shaving to make the process easier.
  • Consider using an electric trimmer to remove the bulk of your hair before shaving with a razor.
  • Apply a cold compress to your scalp after shaving to reduce inflammation and soothe your skin.

FAQs About Shaving Your Head Before Chemo

Will shaving my head before chemo make my hair grow back faster?

No, shaving your head before chemo will not make your hair grow back faster. Hair growth is determined by genetics and other factors, not by shaving.

How long does it take for hair to grow back after chemo?

It varies from person to person, but hair usually starts to grow back a few weeks after the last round of chemo. It may take several months for your hair to grow back to its pre-chemo length.

What can I do to take care of my scalp after shaving?

After shaving, it's important to keep your scalp moisturized. Use a gentle moisturizer or oil to prevent dryness and irritation. Wear a hat or scarf to protect your scalp from the sun.

Hair Care After Chemo

After chemo, your hair may grow back differently than it was before. It may be thinner or have a different texture. Here are some tips for taking care of your hair after chemo:

  • Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair to avoid breakage.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner designed for thinning hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as hair dryers and flat irons.
  • Wear a hat or scarf to protect your scalp from the sun and cold weather.

Shaving your head before chemo can be a difficult but empowering experience. By following these steps and tips, you can prepare yourself for the side effects of chemo and take control of your situation. Remember to take care of your scalp and hair after chemo, and don't hesitate to seek support from friends, family, or a support group.
