Short Hairstyles For Square Faces And Thin Hair A Guide To Flattering Cuts

Written by Eveline Aug 27, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have a square face shape and thin hair, finding the right hairstyle can be a challenge. You want to find a cut that flatters your face shape without drawing attention to your fine hair. In this guide, we'll walk you through some of the best short hairstyles for square faces and thin hair, along with tips for maintaining healthy locks.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to Choose the Right Hairstyle
  3. Step by Step: How to Style Short Hair
  4. Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair
  5. FAQs
  6. Hair Care Products for Thin Hair

Description

A square face shape is characterized by a strong, angular jawline and a broad forehead. If you have thin hair, you may struggle to add volume and texture to your locks. Short hairstyles can help create the illusion of fuller, thicker hair, while also accentuating your facial features.

How to Choose the Right Hairstyle

When it comes to choosing the right hairstyle for a square face and thin hair, there are a few key things to keep in mind:

  • Look for cuts that add volume and texture to your hair, such as layered bobs or pixie cuts.
  • Avoid straight, blunt cuts that can make your hair look flat and lifeless.
  • Consider adding bangs to soften your features and draw attention to your eyes.
  • Experiment with different lengths and styles to find what works best for you.

Bobs

One of the best short hairstyles for square faces and thin hair is the bob. A layered bob can add volume and movement to your hair, while also framing your face in a flattering way. Consider asking your stylist for a chin-length or collarbone-length bob with layers that start at the chin or cheekbones.

Pixie Cuts

Pixie cuts are another great option for square faces and thin hair. A short, layered pixie can add texture and volume to your hair, while also drawing attention to your facial features. Consider adding side-swept bangs to soften your features and create a more feminine look.

Step by Step: How to Style Short Hair

Styling short hair can be quick and easy, especially if you have the right tools and products. Here's a step-by-step guide to styling short hair:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Apply a volumizing mousse or spray to add texture and volume to your hair.
  3. Use a round brush to blow dry your hair, focusing on the roots to add lift and volume.
  4. Use a flat iron or curling wand to add texture and create loose waves or curls.
  5. Finish with a lightweight hairspray to hold your style in place.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Hair

Thin hair can be prone to breakage and damage, so it's important to take good care of your locks. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair:

  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling wands too often, as they can cause damage to your hair.
  • Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of natural oils.
  • Invest in a high-quality hairbrush with soft bristles to minimize breakage.
  • Consider using a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to nourish your locks.

FAQs

What hairstyles should I avoid if I have a square face and thin hair?

Avoid straight, blunt cuts that can make your hair look flat and lifeless. Instead, opt for layered cuts that add volume and texture to your hair.

Should I add bangs to my short hairstyle?

Adding bangs can be a great way to soften your features and draw attention to your eyes. Consider trying side-swept bangs or wispy bangs to create a more feminine look.

Hair Care Products for Thin Hair

If you have thin hair, using the right hair care products can help add volume and texture to your locks. Here are some products to consider:

  • Volumizing mousse or spray
  • Dry shampoo
  • Texturizing spray
  • Lightweight hairspray

With the right hairstyle and hair care routine, you can rock a short, flattering cut that accentuates your best features. Keep these tips in mind and experiment with different styles to find what works best for you.
