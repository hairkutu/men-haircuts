Which Guards To Use Cutting Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Description

Guard sizes refer to the length of hair that will be left after cutting. They clip onto your hair clippers and come in different sizes, ranging from #0.5 to #8. The smaller the guard number, the shorter the hair will be. For example, a #0.5 guard will leave hair 1/16 of an inch long, while a #8 guard will leave hair 1 inch long. Choosing the right guard size will depend on the type of haircut you want to achieve.

How to Choose Guards for Cutting Hair

When choosing guards for cutting hair, consider the length of hair you want to leave and the type of haircut you want to achieve. For shorter buzz cuts, use a #1 or #2 guard. For longer haircuts, use a #3 or #4 guard. For fades, start with a longer guard size and work your way down to achieve the desired length.

It's important to note that guard sizes can vary between clipper brands, so always consult your clipper's manual to ensure you're using the correct guard size.

Guard Sizes for Different Haircuts

  • Buzz Cut: #1-#2 guard
  • Short Haircut: #3-#4 guard
  • Medium Haircut: #5-#6 guard
  • Long Haircut: #7-#8 guard
  • Fade: Start with a longer guard size and work your way down

Step by Step Guide to Cutting Hair with Guards

Now that you know which guard sizes to use for different haircuts, here's a step by step guide to cutting hair with guards:

  1. Choose the correct guard size for the desired haircut.
  2. Attach the guard to your clippers.
  3. Start at the bottom of the head and work your way up, using the clippers to cut the hair.
  4. Go over the same area multiple times to ensure an even cut.
  5. Blend the hair between different guard sizes for fades.
  6. Use scissors to trim any longer hairs that the clippers missed.
  7. Brush any loose hairs off the client's neck and shoulders.
  8. Clean your clippers and guards after each use.

Tips for Cutting Hair with Guards

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting hair with guards:

  • Always start with a longer guard size and work your way down for fades.
  • Use a comb to lift the hair and create a clean, even cut.
  • Make sure the client's hair is dry and free of tangles before cutting.
  • Use a hair vacuum or cape to catch loose hairs and prevent them from sticking to the client's skin.
  • Don't forget to trim the hair around the ears and neckline for a polished look.

FAQs

1. Can I use guards on wet hair?

No, it's not recommended to use guards on wet hair as it can cause uneven cutting and damage to the clippers.

2. How often should I clean my clippers and guards?

You should clean your clippers and guards after each use to prevent buildup of hair and debris.

3. Can I use different clipper brands with the same guards?

Guard sizes can vary between clipper brands, so it's best to consult the manual or try the guards on a small section of hair before cutting the entire head.

Hair Care After Cutting Hair with Guards

After cutting hair with guards, it's important to care for the hair properly. Here are some tips:

  • Wash the hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or oil to prevent dryness and split ends.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools for a few days to prevent further damage.
  • Trim the hair regularly to maintain the desired length and shape.

Now that you know which guards to use when cutting hair, you can confidently achieve the desired haircut for your clients or yourself. Remember to always consult your clipper's manual and follow proper hair care techniques to keep the hair healthy and looking its best.
