Does A Full Fringe Make Your Face Look Fatter

Written by Luffy Jul 26, 2023 · 7 min read

Table of Contents:

  1. Description
  2. How To
  3. Step By Step
  4. Tips
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care

Description

Many people are curious about whether a full fringe makes their face look fatter. The answer to this question is not straightforward as it depends on several factors such as the shape of your face, the thickness of your hair, and the length of the fringe. In this blog post, we will discuss the impact of a full fringe on your facial appearance and provide tips on how to choose the right fringe style for your face shape.

How To

The first step to determine whether a full fringe makes your face look fatter is to identify your face shape. The most common face shapes are oval, round, square, heart, and diamond. Each face shape has specific characteristics that determine the most suitable fringe style.

Oval Face Shape

If you have an oval face shape, you are lucky as almost any fringe style will suit you. A full fringe can make your face look more symmetrical and balanced. You can opt for a blunt or wispy fringe depending on your hair texture and personal preference.

Round Face Shape

If you have a round face shape, a full fringe can make your face look wider. It is best to avoid a straight and heavy fringe that falls below the eyebrows. Instead, opt for a wispy or side-swept fringe that creates an angle and lengthens your face.

Square Face Shape

If you have a square face shape, a full fringe can soften your jawline and create a more feminine look. It is best to choose a wispy or layered fringe that falls below the eyebrows and blends with the rest of your hair.

Heart Face Shape

If you have a heart face shape, a full fringe can balance your forehead and cheekbones. It is best to choose a wispy or side-swept fringe that falls below the eyebrows and creates an angle.

Diamond Face Shape

If you have a diamond face shape, a full fringe can make your face look wider. It is best to choose a wispy or side-swept fringe that falls below the eyebrows and creates an angle.

Step By Step

Here are the steps to choose the right full fringe for your face shape:

  1. Identify your face shape
  2. Research the most suitable fringe styles for your face shape
  3. Consult with your hairstylist to determine the best fringe length and thickness for your hair texture
  4. Consider your lifestyle and maintenance level before choosing a fringe style
  5. Experiment with different fringe styles using a wig or clip-in extensions before committing to a cut

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when choosing a full fringe:

  • Choose a fringe style that complements your facial features
  • Consider your hair texture and thickness when choosing a fringe length and thickness
  • Opt for a lighter and wispy fringe if you have fine hair
  • Avoid a heavy and straight fringe if you have curly or wavy hair
  • Trim your fringe regularly to maintain its shape and length

FAQ

Q: Does a full fringe make your face look fatter?

A: It depends on your face shape, hair texture, and fringe length. A full fringe can make your face look wider if you have a round or diamond face shape. It is best to choose a wispy or side-swept fringe if you want to create a slimmer appearance.

Q: Can I wear a full fringe if I have a small forehead?

A: Yes, you can wear a full fringe if you have a small forehead. However, it is best to choose a wispy or layered fringe that falls above the eyebrows to create the illusion of a higher forehead.

Q: How often should I trim my full fringe?

A: You should trim your full fringe every 4-6 weeks to maintain its shape and length.

Hair Care

Here are some hair care tips for maintaining a full fringe:

  • Wash your fringe regularly with a mild shampoo
  • Blow dry your fringe with a round brush to create volume
  • Avoid using heavy styling products that can weigh down your fringe
  • Use a dry shampoo to refresh your fringe between washes
  • Avoid touching your fringe with dirty hands to prevent breakouts
