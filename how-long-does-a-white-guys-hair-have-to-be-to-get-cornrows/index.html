How Long Does A White Guy s Hair Have To Be To Get Cornrows

Written by Petter Jul 24, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Cornrows are a popular hairstyle that originated in Africa and have been adopted by many cultures around the world. They provide a unique and stylish way to manage and style hair. For white guys, cornrows can be a bit more challenging to achieve, but it is still possible. In this article, we will explore how long a white guy's hair needs to be to get cornrows, as well as provide tips on how to achieve this style.

Table of Contents

  • How to Get Cornrows on White Guys
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Maintaining Cornrows
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Cornrows

How to Get Cornrows on White Guys

In order to get cornrows, your hair needs to be long enough to braid. The general rule of thumb is that your hair should be at least 2-3 inches long. This will allow the braids to hold and not come undone. If your hair is shorter than this, it may be difficult to create clean and neat cornrows.

It is also important to note that the texture of your hair can affect how well cornrows hold. If your hair is naturally straight, it may be more difficult to create and maintain cornrows. However, if you have curly or wavy hair, it may be easier to achieve this style.

Another factor to consider when getting cornrows is the skill level of the person doing the braiding. Cornrows require a certain level of skill and precision, so it is important to find someone who is experienced in creating this style. You may want to ask for recommendations or look for a stylist who specializes in braiding and natural hair styles.

Step-by-Step Guide

If you're ready to get cornrows, here is a step-by-step guide to help you achieve this style:

  1. Start with clean, detangled hair. It is best to wash and condition your hair the day before getting cornrows.
  2. Section your hair into small, even parts using a comb. You may want to use clips to keep each section separate.
  3. Starting at the front of your head, take a small section of hair and divide it into three equal parts.
  4. Begin braiding your hair by crossing the right section over the middle section, then the left section over the new middle section. Repeat this process as you add more hair to each section.
  5. Continue braiding until you reach the end of your hair. Secure the braid with a small elastic band or hair tie.
  6. Repeat this process for each section of hair until your entire head is covered in cornrows.
  7. Once your cornrows are complete, you can style them as desired. You may want to leave them down or gather them into a bun or ponytail.

Tips for Maintaining Cornrows

Now that you have cornrows, here are some tips to help you maintain this style:

  • Wrap your hair at night with a silk or satin scarf to prevent frizz and protect your braids.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your scalp and hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using heavy hair products or oils that can weigh down your braids and cause them to come undone.
  • Be gentle when washing your hair, and avoid pulling or tugging on your braids.
  • Consider getting touch-ups every 2-4 weeks to keep your cornrows looking fresh.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about getting cornrows:

Can white guys get cornrows?

Yes, white guys can get cornrows. However, it may be more difficult to achieve this style depending on the texture and length of your hair.

How long does my hair need to be?

Your hair should be at least 2-3 inches long to get cornrows. This will allow the braids to hold and not come undone.

How long do cornrows last?

Cornrows can last anywhere from 2-8 weeks, depending on how well you maintain them and how quickly your hair grows.

Hair Care Tips for Cornrows

Here are some additional hair care tips for maintaining cornrows:

  • Avoid scratching your scalp, as this can cause irritation and damage to your braids.
  • Use a lightweight hair oil or serum to add shine and nourishment to your hair.
  • Avoid using heat tools or styling products that can damage your braids.
  • Be gentle when removing your braids, and use a detangling spray or conditioner to help ease the process.

With these tips and guidelines, you can achieve and maintain cornrows as a white guy. Remember to find a skilled stylist, take care of your hair, and enjoy your new look!
