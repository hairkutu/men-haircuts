How To Style Hair Growing Out From Pixie Cut

Description

So you've decided to grow out your pixie cut? Congratulations, you're on your way to longer locks! However, the growing out process can be frustrating when your hair is at that awkward in-between stage. Fear not, with the right styling techniques and a bit of patience, you can make the most of your growing out phase.

How to

The key to styling hair growing out from a pixie cut is to work with the texture you have. Embrace the natural wave or curl in your hair and experiment with different products and tools to enhance it. On the other hand, if your hair is straight, you can create volume and texture with the right styling techniques.

Step by step

1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair with products that are suitable for your hair type.

2. Towel-dry your hair and apply a heat protectant spray to protect your hair from damage caused by heat styling tools.

3. Blow-dry your hair using a round brush to create volume and texture. If your hair is naturally curly or wavy, use a diffuser attachment to enhance your natural texture.

4. Use a curling wand or flat iron to create loose waves or curls. Alternatively, you can use hot rollers to create a soft, bouncy look.

5. Finish off with a texturizing spray or sea salt spray to add volume and hold to your hair.

Tips

- Experiment with different parting and styling techniques to find what works best for your face shape and hair type.

- Use bobby pins, headbands, and hair accessories to add interest and variety to your hairstyles.

- Invest in a good quality hairbrush and comb to detangle your hair without causing breakage.

- Don't be afraid to try new products and tools to find what works best for your hair.

FAQ

Q: How long does it take to grow out a pixie cut?

A: It can take anywhere from six months to a year or more to grow out a pixie cut, depending on how fast your hair grows and how long you want it to be.

Q: Can I dye my hair while growing out a pixie cut?

A: Yes, you can dye your hair while growing out a pixie cut, but be sure to use a dye that is gentle and doesn't cause damage to your hair.

Hair Care

As your hair grows out, it's important to take good care of it to prevent breakage and promote healthy growth. Here are some tips:

- Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and promote healthy growth.

- Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish your hair and keep it soft and shiny.

- Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can cause damage to your hair.

- Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote healthy hair growth.

With these tips and techniques, you can make the most of your growing out phase and enjoy the journey to longer, healthier hair!
