How To Cut The Top Of Disconnected Undercut

Written by Luffy Jul 05, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're looking for a stylish and edgy haircut, a disconnected undercut might just be the right cut for you. This haircut features a shaved or closely-cropped undercut on the sides and back, while the top is left longer for contrast. In this article, we'll be focusing on how to cut the top of disconnected undercut.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to
  3. Step-by-Step
  4. Tips
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care

Description

The disconnected undercut has been a popular haircut for both men and women in recent years. This haircut features a dramatic contrast between the shaved or closely-cropped sides and back, and the longer hair on top. The top can be styled in a variety of ways, from slicked back to messy and textured.

How to

When it comes to cutting the top of a disconnected undercut, it's important to have the right tools on hand. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and possibly some thinning shears if you want to add texture to the hair.

Step-by-Step

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to cut the top of a disconnected undercut:

  1. Start by sectioning off the top of the hair with a comb.
  2. Use your scissors to trim the hair to the desired length. If you're not sure how much to cut, it's always better to start with less and trim more as needed.
  3. Use thinning shears to add texture to the hair, if desired.
  4. Blend the top with the sides using a comb and scissors.
  5. Style the hair as desired using a hair product like pomade or wax.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting the top of a disconnected undercut:

  • Start with clean, dry hair for the best results.
  • Use a comb to section off the hair and make sure you're only cutting the top.
  • Take your time and cut slowly to avoid making mistakes.
  • Use thinning shears sparingly to avoid creating choppy or uneven layers.
  • Experiment with different styles and products to find the look that works best for you.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting the top of a disconnected undercut:

How often should I get my disconnected undercut trimmed?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how short you want to keep the undercut. Some people get their hair trimmed every 4-6 weeks, while others can go longer between cuts.

Can I cut the top of a disconnected undercut myself?

It's possible to cut your own hair, but it can be difficult to do so evenly and accurately. It's recommended to have a professional stylist cut your hair, especially if you're going for a more complex style.

What products should I use to style my disconnected undercut?

It depends on the look you're going for. Pomade or wax can be used to create a slicked back or structured style, while a texturizing spray can be used to create a messy, textured look.

Hair Care

To keep your disconnected undercut looking its best, it's important to take good care of your hair. This includes washing it regularly with a nourishing shampoo and conditioner, using a heat protectant spray before styling with hot tools, and avoiding over-styling or using too many hair products.

In conclusion, cutting the top of a disconnected undercut can be a fun and creative way to change up your look. With the right tools, techniques, and products, you can achieve a stylish and edgy haircut that suits your personality and style.
