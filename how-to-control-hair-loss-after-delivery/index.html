How To Control Hair Loss After Delivery Tips And Tricks

Written by William Mar 15, 2023 Â· 8 min read

After giving birth, many women experience hair loss due to hormonal changes in the body. This can be a frustrating and stressful experience, but it is important to remember that it is a normal part of the postpartum period. In this article, we will discuss how to control hair loss after delivery with tips and tricks to help you manage this temporary condition.

Description

Postpartum hair loss, also known as postpartum alopecia, is a common condition that affects many women after giving birth. During pregnancy, hormonal changes can cause hair to remain in the growth phase, resulting in thicker and fuller hair. After delivery, hormones return to normal levels, which can cause hair to enter the shedding phase, resulting in hair loss. This condition usually begins around 3-6 months after delivery and can last up to a year.

How to Control Hair Loss After Delivery

While postpartum hair loss cannot be prevented, there are ways to manage and control it. Here are some tips and tricks:

1. Maintain a Healthy Diet

Eating a well-balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals can help promote healthy hair growth. Be sure to include foods high in iron, zinc, and biotin, such as leafy greens, nuts, and eggs.

2. Get Enough Sleep

Getting enough sleep is essential for overall health and can also help reduce stress, which can contribute to hair loss. Aim for 7-9 hours of sleep per night.

3. Manage Stress

Stress can contribute to hair loss, so it is important to find ways to manage stress levels. Practice relaxation techniques such as yoga, meditation, or deep breathing exercises.

4. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools such as flat irons and curling irons, as they can damage hair and contribute to breakage. Allow hair to air dry or use a low-heat setting on a hair dryer.

5. Use Gentle Hair Products

Choose gentle hair products that do not contain harsh chemicals or sulfates, which can strip hair of its natural oils and contribute to breakage. Look for products specifically designed for postpartum hair loss.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide to managing hair loss after delivery:

Step 1: Consult with Your Doctor

Before making any changes to your hair care routine, consult with your doctor to rule out any underlying medical conditions that may be contributing to hair loss.

Step 2: Maintain a Healthy Diet

Eat a well-balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote healthy hair growth.

Step 3: Get Enough Sleep

Aim for 7-9 hours of sleep per night to reduce stress levels and promote overall health.

Step 4: Manage Stress

Practice relaxation techniques such as yoga, meditation, or deep breathing exercises to manage stress levels.

Step 5: Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools that can damage hair and contribute to breakage.

Step 6: Use Gentle Hair Products

Choose gentle hair products specifically designed for postpartum hair loss.

Tips for Managing Hair Loss After Delivery

Here are some additional tips to help manage hair loss after delivery:

1. Be Gentle with Hair

Avoid pulling or tugging on hair, as it can contribute to breakage.

2. Try a Haircut

A shorter haircut can make hair appear fuller and can help manage hair loss.

3. Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

Avoid using a brush, which can pull on hair and contribute to breakage. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to gently detangle hair.

4. Consider Supplements

Talk to your doctor about taking supplements such as biotin, which can promote healthy hair growth.

FAQs

Q: Will my hair grow back after postpartum hair loss?

A: Yes, hair will eventually grow back after postpartum hair loss. It may take several months to a year for hair to return to its pre-pregnancy state.

Q: Is postpartum hair loss preventable?

A: No, postpartum hair loss cannot be prevented. It is a normal part of the postpartum period.

Q: Can breastfeeding contribute to postpartum hair loss?

A: Yes, hormonal changes associated with breastfeeding can contribute to postpartum hair loss.

Hair Care Tips for Postpartum Hair Loss

Here are some hair care tips specifically designed for postpartum hair loss:

1. Use a Volumizing Shampoo

A volumizing shampoo can make hair appear fuller and thicker.

2. Apply Hair Masks

Use hair masks to nourish and hydrate hair, which can help reduce breakage.

3. Massage Scalp

Massaging the scalp can help increase blood flow to hair follicles, promoting healthy hair growth.

4. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Avoid tight hairstyles such as braids and ponytails, which can pull on hair and contribute to breakage.

By following these tips and tricks, you can manage postpartum hair loss and promote healthy hair growth. Remember to be patient, as it may take several months for hair to return to its pre-pregnancy state.
