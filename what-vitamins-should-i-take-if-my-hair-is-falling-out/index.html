What Vitamins Should I Take If My Hair Is Falling Out

Written by Petter Jul 20, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you are experiencing hair loss, you are not alone. Millions of people around the world suffer from this condition, and it can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, stress, and poor nutrition. One way to combat hair loss is to make sure you are getting the right vitamins and minerals in your diet. In this article, we will discuss what vitamins you should take if your hair is falling out.

Vitamin A

Vitamin A is essential for healthy hair growth, as it helps to produce sebum, which moisturizes the scalp and keeps the hair healthy. However, it is important to get the right amount of vitamin A, as too much can actually cause hair loss. The recommended daily intake of vitamin A is 700-900 mcg for women and 900-1200 mcg for men.

Vitamin B Complex

The B vitamins are crucial for healthy hair growth, as they help to produce red blood cells, which carry oxygen and nutrients to the scalp and hair follicles. A deficiency in any of the B vitamins can lead to hair loss. The B vitamins include thiamine (B1), riboflavin (B2), niacin (B3), pantothenic acid (B5), pyridoxine (B6), biotin (B7), folic acid (B9), and cobalamin (B12).

Vitamin C

Vitamin C is an antioxidant that helps to protect the hair follicles from damage caused by free radicals. It also helps the body absorb iron, which is essential for healthy hair growth. The recommended daily intake of vitamin C is 75-90 mg for women and 90-120 mg for men.

Vitamin D

Vitamin D is essential for healthy hair growth, as it helps to regulate the growth and differentiation of hair follicles. It also helps to reduce inflammation, which can contribute to hair loss. The recommended daily intake of vitamin D is 15-20 mcg for adults.

Vitamin E

Vitamin E is an antioxidant that helps to protect the hair follicles from damage caused by free radicals. It also helps to improve blood circulation, which is essential for healthy hair growth. The recommended daily intake of vitamin E is 15 mg for adults.

Biotin

Biotin, also known as vitamin H, is essential for healthy hair growth, as it helps to produce keratin, which is a protein that makes up the hair, skin, and nails. A deficiency in biotin can lead to hair loss. The recommended daily intake of biotin is 30-100 mcg for adults.

Iron

Iron is essential for healthy hair growth, as it helps to carry oxygen to the hair follicles. A deficiency in iron can lead to hair loss. The recommended daily intake of iron is 18 mg for women and 8 mg for men.

Zinc

Zinc is essential for healthy hair growth, as it helps to regulate the production of oil in the scalp. It also helps to improve the immune system, which can contribute to healthy hair growth. The recommended daily intake of zinc is 8-11 mg for adults.

Omega-3

Omega-3 fatty acids are essential for healthy hair growth, as they help to reduce inflammation and improve blood circulation. They also help to moisturize the scalp and prevent dryness. The best sources of omega-3s are fatty fish, such as salmon, mackerel, and sardines.

Tips for Healthy Hair

While taking the right vitamins and minerals can help to promote healthy hair growth, there are other things you can do to keep your hair healthy:

  • Avoid harsh chemicals and heat styling
  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet
  • Stay hydrated
  • Get enough sleep
  • Manage stress

FAQ

Q: Can vitamins help with hair loss?

A: Yes, taking the right vitamins and minerals can help to promote healthy hair growth and prevent hair loss.

Q: What are the best vitamins for hair loss?

A: The best vitamins for hair loss are vitamin A, vitamin B complex, vitamin C, vitamin D, vitamin E, biotin, iron, zinc, and omega-3 fatty acids.

Q: Can too much vitamin A cause hair loss?

A: Yes, too much vitamin A can actually cause hair loss. It is important to get the right amount of vitamin A, which is 700-900 mcg for women and 900-1200 mcg for men.

Q: How long does it take for vitamins to help with hair loss?

A: It can take several months for vitamins to help with hair loss, as hair growth is a slow process. It is important to be patient and consistent with your vitamin regimen.

In conclusion, taking the right vitamins and minerals can help to promote healthy hair growth and prevent hair loss. Make sure you are getting the right amount of vitamin A, B complex, C, D, E, biotin, iron, zinc, and omega-3 fatty acids in your diet, and follow the tips for healthy hair to keep your locks looking their best.
