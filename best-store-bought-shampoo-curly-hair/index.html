Best Store Bought Shampoo For Curly Hair

Written by Luffy Jul 25, 2023 · 7 min read

As someone with curly hair, you know that finding the right shampoo can be a challenge. You want a product that cleanses your scalp and hair without stripping away natural oils or leaving your curls feeling dry and brittle. Fortunately, there are several great options for store-bought shampoos that are specifically designed for curly hair. In this article, we’ll take a closer look at some of the best store-bought shampoos for curly hair, how to use them, and tips for keeping your curls healthy and beautiful.

Description

Curly hair is unique in that it requires extra moisture to keep it healthy and defined. The best store-bought shampoos for curly hair are those that are specifically formulated to provide that extra moisture while still cleansing your scalp and hair. These shampoos often contain ingredients like coconut oil, shea butter, and argan oil, which help to nourish and hydrate your curls.

How to Choose the Right Shampoo for Curly Hair

When shopping for a shampoo for curly hair, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, look for a product that is sulfate-free. Sulfates are harsh detergents that can strip away natural oils and leave your curls feeling dry and frizzy. Instead, opt for shampoos that contain gentle cleansers like cocamidopropyl betaine or decyl glucoside.

You’ll also want to look for shampoos that contain moisturizing ingredients like coconut oil, shea butter, or argan oil. These ingredients help to nourish and hydrate your curls, leaving them looking soft, shiny, and defined.

Step-by-Step Guide to Washing Curly Hair

Washing curly hair can be a bit more involved than washing straight hair, but with the right technique, you can keep your curls looking healthy and defined. Here’s a step-by-step guide:

  1. Wet your hair thoroughly with warm water.
  2. Apply a quarter-sized amount of shampoo to your scalp and massage gently with your fingertips.
  3. Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water.
  4. Apply a quarter-sized amount of conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends.
  5. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair while the conditioner is still in.
  6. Rinse your hair thoroughly with cool water.
  7. Wrap your hair in a microfiber towel or cotton t-shirt to dry. Avoid using a regular towel, as the rough texture can cause frizz.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Curls

Here are a few tips for keeping your curls looking their best:

  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons or curling irons, as they can damage your hair and cause frizz.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s dry, as it can cause breakage and frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair when it’s wet.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to help define your curls and keep them looking hydrated and shiny.
  • Avoid using products that contain alcohol, as they can dry out your hair.

FAQs

Q: How often should I wash my curly hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and how much oil your scalp produces. Some people with curly hair can go several days between washes, while others may need to wash their hair every other day.

Q: Can I use a regular shampoo on curly hair?

A: It’s best to avoid using regular shampoos on curly hair, as they often contain sulfates and other harsh detergents that can strip away natural oils and leave your curls feeling dry and frizzy.

Q: Can I use a conditioner as a shampoo?

A: While some people with curly hair choose to co-wash (or wash their hair with conditioner instead of shampoo), it’s not recommended for everyone. Co-washing can be a great way to add extra moisture to your curls, but if you have an oily scalp, it may not be the best option for you.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

When it comes to caring for curly hair, it’s important to use products that are specifically formulated for your hair type. In addition to a good shampoo and conditioner, consider using a leave-in conditioner, curl cream, or hair oil to help keep your curls hydrated and defined. And don’t forget to trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and keep your curls looking their best!
