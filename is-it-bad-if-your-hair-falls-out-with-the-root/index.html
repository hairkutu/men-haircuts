Is It Bad If Your Hair Falls Out With The Root

Written by William Jul 11, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you've ever found a clump of hair in your brush or on your pillow, you may have wondered if it's normal for your hair to fall out with the root. In some cases, it's perfectly normal for hair to shed naturally. However, if you're losing a significant amount of hair with the root attached, it could be a sign of an underlying issue. In this article, we'll explore whether it's bad if your hair falls out with the root and what you can do about it.

FAQ

What does it mean if your hair falls out with the root?

If your hair falls out with the root attached, it means that the hair follicle has been destroyed or detached from the scalp. This can be a sign of an underlying issue that needs to be addressed.

Is it normal for hair to fall out with the root?

It's normal for a small amount of hair to shed naturally every day. However, if you're losing a significant amount of hair with the root attached, it could be a sign of an underlying issue.

What should I do if my hair is falling out with the root?

If you're losing a significant amount of hair with the root attached, it's important to see a doctor or dermatologist to determine the underlying cause of your hair loss.

Causes of Hair Loss with the Root

Hair loss with the root attached can be caused by a variety of factors, including:

  • Genetics: If hair loss runs in your family, you may be more likely to experience hair loss with the root attached.
  • Hormones: Hormonal changes can cause hair to shed more than usual, and in some cases, can cause hair loss with the root attached.
  • Stress: Extreme physical or emotional stress can cause hair to fall out with the root attached.
  • Nutrient deficiencies: A lack of vitamins and minerals in your diet can cause hair loss with the root attached.
  • Medical conditions: Medical conditions such as alopecia, thyroid disorders, and autoimmune diseases can cause hair loss with the root attached.
  • Medications: Certain medications, such as chemotherapy drugs, can cause hair loss with the root attached.

How to Determine if Your Hair Loss is Normal

If you're concerned about hair loss with the root attached, there are a few things you can do to determine if it's normal:

  • Count the hairs: Count how many hairs you're losing each day. On average, it's normal to lose 50-100 hairs per day.
  • Check the root: If you're losing a significant amount of hair with the root attached, it's a sign that the hair follicle has been destroyed or detached from the scalp. This could be a sign of an underlying issue.
  • Look for other symptoms: If you're experiencing other symptoms such as itching, redness, or scaling of the scalp, it's important to see a doctor or dermatologist to determine the underlying cause of your hair loss.

Tips for Preventing Hair Loss with the Root

If you're concerned about hair loss with the root attached, there are a few things you can do to prevent it:

  • Eat a healthy diet: Make sure you're getting enough vitamins and minerals in your diet, especially B vitamins, iron, and zinc.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles: Tight hairstyles such as braids, ponytails, and cornrows can pull on the hair and cause damage to the follicle.
  • Avoid heat styling: Heat styling tools such as flat irons and curling irons can damage the hair and cause it to fall out with the root attached.
  • Manage stress: Practice stress-management techniques such as meditation, yoga, or deep breathing to reduce stress and prevent hair loss.

Hair Care Tips for Healthy Hair

To keep your hair healthy and prevent hair loss with the root attached, follow these hair care tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly: Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo to remove dirt and oil.
  • Use conditioner: Use a conditioner to moisturize and protect your hair.
  • Avoid harsh chemicals: Avoid using harsh chemicals such as hair dyes and bleach, which can damage the hair and cause it to fall out with the root attached.
  • Protect your hair: Wear a hat or scarf to protect your hair from the sun and wind.

In conclusion, if you're losing a significant amount of hair with the root attached, it could be a sign of an underlying issue. It's important to see a doctor or dermatologist to determine the underlying cause of your hair loss. By following the tips in this article, you can prevent hair loss with the root attached and keep your hair healthy and strong.
