How To Keep Hair Nice After Sleeping Tips And Tricks

Written by Petter May 30, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Waking up with frizzy, tangled hair can be frustrating, especially when you have a busy day ahead. Fortunately, there are several ways to keep your hair looking great even after a night of tossing and turning. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks on how to keep hair nice after sleeping.

Table of Contents

  9. Use Dry Shampoo
  10. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Sleep on a Silk Pillowcase

A silk pillowcase can help prevent hair from tangling and breaking while you sleep. Unlike cotton, silk is a smooth and gentle fabric that won't rub against your hair, causing damage. Additionally, silk pillowcases can help retain moisture in your hair, preventing dryness and frizz.

Tie Your Hair Up

If you have long hair, tying it up in a loose bun or ponytail can help prevent tangles and knots. However, make sure to use a scrunchie or hair tie that won't cause damage or leave creases in your hair.

Use a Leave-In Conditioner

A leave-in conditioner can help keep your hair moisturized and tangle-free. Apply a small amount of leave-in conditioner to your hair before going to bed, focusing on the ends and any areas prone to tangles.

Braid Your Hair

Braiding your hair before going to bed can help prevent tangles and create soft, beachy waves. Consider trying a loose, messy braid or a fishtail braid for a more polished look.

Apply Hair Oil

Applying a small amount of hair oil to your ends can help prevent dryness and frizz. Coconut oil, argan oil, and jojoba oil are all great options for keeping hair moisturized and looking shiny.

Avoid Brushing Wet Hair

Wet hair is more prone to breakage and damage, so avoid brushing or combing your hair when it's wet. Instead, wait until your hair is dry or gently detangle it with a wide-tooth comb.

Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

A wide-tooth comb is a gentle way to detangle hair without causing damage. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up, using a light touch and taking your time.

Wrap Your Hair in a Scarf

Wrapping your hair in a silk scarf or bonnet can help protect it from rubbing against your pillowcase and causing damage. Additionally, a scarf can help retain moisture in your hair and prevent frizz.

Use Dry Shampoo

Dry shampoo can be a lifesaver when you don't have time to wash your hair. Apply a small amount to your roots before bed, and use a wide-tooth comb to distribute it evenly. This will help absorb excess oil and leave your hair looking fresh and voluminous in the morning.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Q: Can I sleep with wet hair?

A: Sleeping with wet hair can cause breakage and damage, so it's best to let your hair air dry or blow dry it before going to bed.

Q: How often should I wash my hair?

A: This can vary depending on your hair type and lifestyle, but most people should aim to wash their hair every 2-3 days. Over-washing can strip your hair of its natural oils, leading to dryness and damage.

Q: Can I use a regular cotton pillowcase?

A: While cotton pillowcases are common, they can cause hair to tangle and break. Consider switching to a silk or satin pillowcase for smoother, healthier hair.

By following these tips and tricks, you can keep your hair looking great even after a night of sleep. Whether you prefer to braid your hair, use a leave-in conditioner, or sleep on a silk pillowcase, there are plenty of ways to keep your hair looking its best.
