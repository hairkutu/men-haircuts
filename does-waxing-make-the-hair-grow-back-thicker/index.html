Does Waxing Make The Hair Grow Back Thicker

Description

Waxing is a popular hair removal method that involves removing hair from the root using a sticky wax. Waxing can be done on various parts of the body, including the legs, underarms, bikini area, and face. Many people prefer waxing to other hair removal methods because it can keep the skin smooth and hair-free for several weeks. However, there is a common myth that waxing can make the hair grow back thicker and darker. In this article, we will explore whether this myth is true or false.

How to Wax Your Hair

Before we dive into the question of whether waxing makes hair grow back thicker, let's first discuss how to wax your hair. Here are the steps:

Step 1: Choose the Right Wax

There are two types of wax: hard wax and soft wax. Hard wax is applied and removed without using strips, while soft wax requires strips to be applied and removed. Choose the wax that works best for the area you wish to wax.

Step 2: Prep Your Skin

Make sure your skin is clean and dry. You can also apply baby powder to the area to be waxed to help the wax adhere better.

Step 3: Apply the Wax

Using a spatula, apply the wax in the direction of hair growth. Make sure the wax is not too hot to avoid burns.

Step 4: Remove the Wax

If using hard wax, wait for the wax to cool and harden, then remove it using your fingers. If using soft wax, apply a strip on top of the wax and press it down. Then, quickly pull the strip in the opposite direction of hair growth.

Step-by-Step Guide to Waxing

Here is a more detailed step-by-step guide to waxing:

Step 1: Exfoliate

A day or two before waxing, exfoliate the area to be waxed. This will help remove dead skin cells and prevent ingrown hairs.

Step 2: Trim Hair

If the hair is too long, trim it to about 1/4 inch. If the hair is too short, the wax will not be able to grip it properly.

Step 3: Apply Wax

Heat the wax according to the instructions on the package. Test the temperature of the wax on your wrist before applying it to the area to be waxed. Apply the wax in the direction of hair growth.

Step 4: Apply Strip

If using soft wax, apply a strip on top of the wax and press it down. Smooth the strip in the direction of hair growth.

Step 5: Remove Strip

Hold the skin taut with one hand and quickly pull the strip in the opposite direction of hair growth with the other hand. Repeat until all the hair is removed.

Waxing Tips

Here are some tips to make your waxing experience more comfortable:

  • Take a pain reliever 30 minutes before waxing to reduce discomfort.
  • Apply a numbing cream to the area to be waxed.
  • Avoid waxing during your period, as your skin may be more sensitive.
  • Avoid sun exposure for 24 hours after waxing.

FAQs About Waxing

Does waxing make hair grow back thicker?

No, waxing does not make hair grow back thicker. In fact, over time, waxing can lead to finer and sparser hair growth.

How often should I wax?

It depends on how fast your hair grows. Generally, you can wax every 4-6 weeks.

Can I wax at home?

Yes, you can wax at home. However, if you are new to waxing, it may be best to go to a professional to avoid mistakes and injuries.

Hair Care After Waxing

After waxing, it is important to take care of your skin to prevent irritation and ingrown hairs. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid hot showers or baths for 24 hours after waxing.
  • Avoid tight clothing that may irritate the skin.
  • Apply a soothing lotion or oil to the area to moisturize the skin.
  • Exfoliate the area regularly to prevent ingrown hairs.

In conclusion, waxing does not make hair grow back thicker. It is a safe and effective hair removal method that can keep your skin smooth and hair-free for several weeks. By following the proper waxing technique and taking care of your skin after waxing, you can achieve the best results.
