How To Layer Medium Length Wavy Hair

Written by Frank Jul 09, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have medium length wavy hair, you know that it can be difficult to manage at times. One way to make it easier to style is by adding layers. Layers can add volume, movement, and texture to your hair. In this article, we will go over how to layer medium length wavy hair step by step. We will also provide tips and answer some frequently asked questions about hair care.

Table of Contents

  • How to Layer Medium Length Wavy Hair
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Step by Step

Step 1: Wash your hair

Before you start layering your hair, it's important to wash it. Use a shampoo and conditioner that are designed for wavy hair. This will help to keep your hair hydrated and prevent frizz.

Step 2: Towel dry your hair

After washing your hair, gently towel dry it. Avoid rubbing your hair with the towel, as this can cause frizz. Instead, gently squeeze out the excess water.

Step 3: Comb your hair

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair. Start at the ends and work your way up to the roots. This will prevent any tangles from getting worse.

Step 4: Section your hair

Section your hair into four parts: top, sides, and back. Use hair clips to keep each section separated.

Step 5: Start with the back section

Start with the back section of your hair. Take a small section of hair and hold it up. Use your fingers or a comb to determine how long you want the layers to be. Cut the hair at an angle, starting from the bottom and working your way up. Repeat this process for the rest of the back section.

Step 6: Move on to the side sections

Next, move on to the side sections. Take a small section of hair from the back of the side section and hold it up. Use your fingers or a comb to determine how long you want the layers to be. Cut the hair at an angle, starting from the bottom and working your way up. Repeat this process for the rest of the side sections.

Step 7: Finish with the top section

Finally, move on to the top section of your hair. Take a small section of hair from the back of the top section and hold it up. Use your fingers or a comb to determine how long you want the layers to be. Cut the hair at an angle, starting from the bottom and working your way up. Repeat this process for the rest of the top section.

Tips

  • Use sharp hair scissors to prevent split ends.
  • Start with a small amount of hair and work your way up to prevent cutting too much off.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet, as it can be difficult to see how much you're cutting.
  • Consider getting a professional haircut if you're unsure about layering your hair yourself.

FAQ

Can I layer my hair if it's not wavy?

Yes, you can layer your hair regardless of its texture. However, the layering may look different depending on the texture of your hair.

How often should I get my hair layered?

It's recommended to get your hair layered every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and style.

Will layering my hair make it shorter?

Yes, layering your hair will make it shorter. However, the amount of length you lose will depend on how many layers you add and how long you want them to be.

Hair Care

After layering your hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

  • Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Use a heat protectant before using any heat styling tools.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage.

By following these tips and the steps outlined in this article, you can successfully layer your medium length wavy hair. Remember to take care of your hair properly to keep it looking healthy and beautiful.
