What Age Should Hair Turn Grey

Written by Aprilia Mar 26, 2023 · 4 min read

As we age, our hair gradually turns grey due to the loss of pigments called melanin. Many people wonder what age they should expect their hair to turn grey. In this article, we will explore the factors that affect when hair turns grey and provide tips for caring for grey hair.

Factors That Affect When Hair Turns Grey

Genetics play a significant role in determining when hair turns grey. If your parents or grandparents turned grey at a young age, you are more likely to experience the same. However, there are other factors that can affect when hair turns grey, such as:

  • Stress
  • Diet
  • Smoking
  • Medical conditions

Stress can lead to premature greying of hair, and a diet lacking in certain nutrients can also contribute. Smoking has been linked to premature greying as well as hair loss. Certain medical conditions, such as anemia and thyroid problems, can also affect the pigmentation of hair.

How to Care for Grey Hair

Grey hair is often coarser and drier than pigmented hair, so it requires special care. Here are some tips for caring for grey hair:

  1. Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of natural oils.
  3. Use a wide-toothed comb to avoid pulling and breaking the hair.
  4. Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can further dry out the hair.
  5. Consider using a hair mask or oil treatment once a week to restore moisture.

Frequently Asked Questions

At What Age Does Hair Typically Turn Grey?

Hair typically starts to turn grey in your 30s or 40s, but it can happen earlier or later depending on genetics and other factors.

Can Stress Cause Grey Hair?

Yes, stress can lead to premature greying of hair. This is because stress can deplete the melanin-producing cells in hair follicles.

Is There a Way to Reverse Grey Hair?

While there is no way to reverse grey hair completely, there are products that can help restore some of the natural pigment to hair. These products typically contain natural ingredients such as henna or coffee.

Conclusion

While there is no set age when hair should turn grey, genetics and other factors can play a role. If you are experiencing premature greying, it is important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and moisturized. With the right care, grey hair can be just as beautiful as pigmented hair.
