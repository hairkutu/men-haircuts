How To Cut Your Own Hair In Steps A Guide For Beginners

Written by Aprilia Jun 02, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune on haircuts at the salon? Or perhaps, you just want to try something new and learn how to cut your own hair? Whatever your reason may be, cutting your own hair may seem daunting at first, but with the right tools, technique, and some practice, you can achieve a professional-looking haircut from the comfort of your own home. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps on how to cut your own hair in a relaxed English language.

How to Cut Your Own Hair in Steps

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting your own hair, it is essential to have the right tools. You will need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, hair clips, and a mirror. If you have longer hair, you may also need hair thinning scissors.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

It is always best to cut your hair when it is clean and dry. This makes it easier to see the length and shape of your hair.

Step 3: Decide on the Length and Style

Before you start cutting, decide on the length and style you want to achieve. You can do this by looking at pictures online or in magazines, or by asking for advice from friends and family.

Step 4: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into small sections using hair clips. This will make it easier to cut your hair evenly.

Step 5: Start Cutting

Start cutting your hair one section at a time. Use the comb to guide the hair and cut with the scissors. Remember to cut small sections at a time and check the length as you go.

Step 6: Thin Out Your Hair

If you have thicker hair, you may want to thin it out using hair thinning scissors. This will help to remove bulk and create a more natural-looking haircut.

Step 7: Blend Your Hair

Blend your hair by combing it down and using the scissors to cut any uneven strands. This will create a more polished and professional-looking haircut.

Step 8: Check Your Hair

Once you have finished cutting your hair, check it in the mirror for any uneven spots or areas that need touching up.

Step 9: Style Your Hair

Style your hair as desired. You can use a blow dryer, straightener, or curling iron to create different looks.

Step 10: Clean Up

After you have finished cutting your hair, clean up any stray hairs and put away your tools.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting your own hair:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up
  • Don't cut too much hair at once
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head
  • Be patient and take your time

FAQs

Can I Cut My Own Hair If I Have Curly Hair?

Yes, you can cut your own hair if you have curly hair. However, it is important to remember that curly hair tends to shrink when it dries, so be cautious when cutting and avoid cutting too much at once.

How Often Should I Cut My Own Hair?

This depends on your hair type and the style you are trying to achieve. Generally, it is recommended to cut your hair every 4-6 weeks to keep it healthy and maintain the shape.

Hair Care

After cutting your own hair, it is important to take care of it to maintain its health and shine. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Use a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends

With these steps, tips, and FAQs, you should now feel more confident in cutting your own hair. Remember to be patient, take your time, and practice, practice, practice. Who knows, you might just discover a hidden talent for hair cutting!
