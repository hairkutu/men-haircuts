How To Do A Straight Haircut By Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jul 23, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're looking to cut your own hair at home, you might be wondering if it's possible to achieve a straight haircut. The answer is yes! With the right tools and techniques, you can give yourself a professional-looking haircut without leaving your house. In this article, we'll guide you through the process of how to do a straight haircut by yourself, and provide tips on how to care for your hair afterwards.

Table of Contents

  1. Tools You'll Need
  2. Preparing Your Hair
  3. Sectioning Your Hair
  4. Cutting Your Hair
  5. Blending Your Hair
  6. Trimming Your Hair
  7. Tips for a Successful Haircut
  8. Frequently Asked Questions
  9. Hair Care After Your Haircut
  10. Conclusion

Tools You'll Need

Before you get started, you'll need to gather a few tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clippers (optional)

Make sure your scissors are sharp, as dull scissors can damage your hair and make it more difficult to cut. If you're using clippers, make sure you have the right guard length for the desired length of your hair.

Preparing Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will make it easier to cut and ensure that you have a clean, fresh canvas to work with. Once your hair is dry, comb it straight and smooth to get rid of any tangles or knots.

Sectioning Your Hair

Now it's time to section your hair. Use your comb to create a straight part down the middle of your head, from your forehead to the nape of your neck. Then, make another part from the top of one ear to the top of the other ear, dividing your hair into four sections.

Clip up three of the sections, leaving one section down at a time to cut. This will help you focus on one section at a time and ensure that you don't accidentally cut too much hair.

Cutting Your Hair

Start by cutting the first section of hair. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut straight across, following the length of the hair. Repeat this process for each section of hair, making sure to cut straight across and not at an angle.

If you're using clippers, start with a longer guard length and work your way down to a shorter length until you achieve the desired length. Use the same technique of holding the hair between your fingers and cutting straight across.

Blending Your Hair

Once you've cut all of your hair to the desired length, it's time to blend the sections together. This will ensure that your haircut looks even and seamless. Use your comb to comb the hair between the different sections together, and use scissors to trim any pieces that stick out.

Trimming Your Hair

Now it's time to trim any stray hairs or uneven pieces. Use your scissors to carefully trim any areas that need it, keeping in mind that it's always better to trim a little at a time than to cut too much.

Tips for a Successful Haircut

  • Start with longer hair and work your way down to a shorter length, as it's easier to cut more hair than it is to fix a haircut that's too short.
  • Take your time and don't rush the process. It's better to spend a little extra time getting it right than to have to live with a bad haircut.
  • Use a mirror to check your progress as you go. This will help you see any areas that need more attention.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I cut my hair wet or dry?

You can cut your hair wet or dry, but it's generally easier to cut when it's dry, as wet hair can be more difficult to control.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to get a haircut every 4-6 weeks to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

What do I do if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic! You can always trim a little more hair to fix the problem, or wait for it to grow out and try again.

Hair Care After Your Haircut

After your haircut, make sure to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid over-styling or using heat on your hair too often. You can also use a leave-in conditioner or oil to keep your hair hydrated and nourished.

Conclusion

With these tips and techniques, you can give yourself a straight haircut at home and save money on trips to the salon. Remember to take your time, use the right tools, and care for your hair afterwards to keep it looking its best.
