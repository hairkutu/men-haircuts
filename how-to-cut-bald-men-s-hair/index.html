How To Cut Bald Men s Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Jul 19, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you a barber or hairstylist who wants to learn how to cut bald men's hair? Or maybe you're a bald man looking to save some money and cut your own hair at home. Whatever your reason, this guide will give you all the information you need to give a great bald haircut. Keep reading for tips, tricks, and step-by-step instructions.

Description

Bald haircuts are a popular choice for men who want a low-maintenance look. With no hair to style or maintain, bald men can save time and money on hair products and salon visits. However, even bald heads need occasional trimming to keep the hairline looking neat and clean. Whether you're a professional barber or a DIY haircutter, this guide will show you how to cut bald men's hair like a pro.

How to Cut Bald Men's Hair

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You will need:

  • Clipper with a balding attachment
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Barber cape or towel
  • Hand mirror

Make sure your tools are clean and in good condition before you start.

Step 2: Prep the Hair and Scalp

Start by washing the hair and scalp with shampoo and warm water. This will remove any dirt, oil, or product buildup and make the hair easier to cut. Dry the hair with a clean towel, then comb it in the direction it naturally falls. Use a hand mirror to check the back of the head.

Step 3: Attach the Balding Clipper

Attach the balding clipper to your regular clipper. The balding attachment has no guard and will cut the hair very short. Start at the nape of the neck and move the clipper upward, against the direction of hair growth. Repeat this process all over the head, making sure to use a light touch and not apply too much pressure.

Step 4: Blend the Hairline

Once the hair is cut, use the scissors to trim any longer hairs around the hairline. Use the comb to lift the hair and cut it to the desired length. Blend the hairline by cutting in a diagonal line, following the natural curvature of the head. This will create a gradual transition from hair to skin.

Step 5: Clean Up

Use the clipper to clean up any stray hairs around the ears and neck. Use the scissors to trim any longer hairs on the top of the head. Brush off any loose hair with a towel, then use a hand mirror to check for any missed spots.

Tips

  • Use a light touch when using the clipper to avoid irritation or razor burn.
  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to the balding attachment to avoid cutting too much hair at once.
  • Use a handheld mirror to check the back of the head and make sure the cut is even.
  • Apply a moisturizer or aftershave lotion to the scalp after the cut to soothe any irritation.

FAQ

Can I cut my own bald hair?

Yes, with the right tools and technique, you can cut your own bald hair at home. However, if you're not comfortable with the process, it's best to visit a professional barber.

How often should I cut my bald hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how short you prefer it. Most bald men need a trim every 2-4 weeks to keep the hairline looking neat.

Hair Care

Even though bald hair requires minimal maintenance, it's still important to keep the scalp clean and moisturized. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner designed for sensitive skin, and avoid using harsh chemicals or styling products that can irritate the scalp. Apply a moisturizer or sunblock to the scalp when spending time outdoors, and see a dermatologist if you notice any unusual bumps or rashes.

Now that you know how to cut bald men's hair, you can add this skill to your hairstyling repertoire. Whether you're cutting your own hair or a client's, following these steps will ensure a clean, professional-looking haircut. Happy cutting!
