How To Cut Long Hair Yourself Straight A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Jul 23, 2023 · 8 min read

If you’re looking to save some money on your next haircut or just want to try cutting your long hair yourself, you’ve come to the right place. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the process of how to cut long hair yourself straight, from preparing your hair to the final trim. We’ll also provide some tips and tricks to help you achieve the perfect cut.

Description

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, especially if you have long hair. However, with the right tools and techniques, it can be done successfully. This guide will show you how to cut long hair yourself straight, without having to go to a salon.

How to Cut Long Hair Yourself Straight: Step by Step

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting your hair, you’ll need to gather the right tools. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Sectioning clips
  • Comb
  • Spray bottle filled with water

Make sure your scissors are sharp, as dull scissors can cause split ends and make your hair look uneven. Sectioning clips will help you divide your hair into manageable sections, while a comb will help you comb your hair straight. The spray bottle filled with water will help you dampen your hair, making it easier to cut.

Step 2: Prepare Your Hair

Before cutting your hair, you’ll need to prepare it. Start by washing your hair and letting it air dry. Once your hair is dry, use a comb to remove any tangles. Then, divide your hair into four sections: two in the front and two in the back. Use sectioning clips to keep each section in place.

Step 3: Start Cutting

Start with one of the sections in the back. Using your comb, comb your hair straight down. Hold your hair between your fingers and cut off the desired length. Repeat this process with the other sections in the back.

Once you’ve finished cutting the back sections, move on to the front sections. Comb your hair straight down, and hold it between your fingers. Cut off the desired length, being careful not to cut too much.

Step 4: Check for Evenness

After you’ve finished cutting your hair, it’s important to check for evenness. Comb your hair straight down and check to see if the length is the same on both sides. If it’s not, make any necessary adjustments.

Step 5: Final Trim

Once you’re happy with the length and evenness of your hair, it’s time for the final trim. Using your scissors, trim any flyaway hairs or uneven ends. Be careful not to cut too much, as this can ruin the shape of your cut.

Tips for Cutting Long Hair Yourself Straight

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect cut:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up. It’s easier to cut more hair than it is to grow it back.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head.
  • Don’t cut your hair when it’s wet, as wet hair can appear longer than it actually is.
  • Be patient and take your time. Cutting your hair yourself can take longer than going to a salon.

FAQ

Can I cut my own long hair straight?

Yes, you can cut your own long hair straight. However, it’s important to have the right tools and follow the proper techniques to achieve the best results.

How often should I cut my hair?

It’s recommended to cut your hair every six to eight weeks to prevent split ends and promote healthy hair growth.

What if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

If you make a mistake while cutting your hair, don’t panic. You can always go to a salon to have it fixed or wait for it to grow out.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it’s important to take care of it to promote healthy hair growth. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

Cutting your own long hair straight can be a fun and rewarding experience. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve the perfect cut without having to go to a salon. If you’re feeling nervous, start with small cuts and work your way up. Remember to be patient and take your time, and you’ll have beautiful, straight hair in no time.
