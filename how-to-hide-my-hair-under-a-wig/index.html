How To Hide Your Hair Under A Wig A Step By Step Guide

If you're new to wearing wigs, one of the biggest challenges can be figuring out how to hide your natural hair underneath. Whether you're wearing a wig for fashion or for medical reasons, a secure and natural-looking fit is key. Here's a step-by-step guide on how to hide your hair under a wig.

How to Hide Your Hair Under a Wig

When it comes to hiding your hair under a wig, there are a few different techniques you can use depending on the length and texture of your hair.

Braid or Twist Your Hair

For longer hair, braiding or twisting your hair is a great way to keep it flat and out of the way. You can either braid your hair in a traditional three-strand braid, or try a more complex style like a fishtail braid or Dutch braid. Twisting your hair is another option, and can be done in a simple twist or a more elaborate style like a French twist.

Use a Wig Cap

A wig cap is a thin, stretchy cap that you wear over your hair to keep it flat and smooth. Wig caps come in a variety of colors and materials, so you can choose one that matches your skin tone and feels comfortable on your head. Simply pull the wig cap over your head and tuck any loose strands of hair underneath.

Pin Your Hair Up

If your hair is shorter, you can try pinning it up using bobby pins or hair clips. Start by sectioning your hair into small sections and pinning each section up and away from your face. Once all of your hair is pinned up, you can put on your wig and adjust it as needed.

Step-by-Step Instructions

Now that you know the different techniques for hiding your hair under a wig, here's a step-by-step guide on how to do it:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. If your hair is dirty or oily, it will be harder to keep it flat and smooth under the wig.
  2. If your hair is long, braid or twist it into a style that will lay flat against your head. If your hair is short, pin it up using bobby pins or hair clips.
  3. Put on a wig cap. Stretch the wig cap over your head, making sure to cover all of your hair. Tuck any loose strands of hair underneath the wig cap.
  4. Put on your wig. Adjust the wig so that it fits snugly against your head, and use the combs or clips on the wig to secure it in place.
  5. Style your wig as desired. You can use a wig brush or comb to style your wig, or use heat tools like a curling iron or straightener. Just be sure to use a low heat setting and protect your wig from heat damage.

Tips for Hiding Your Hair Under a Wig

Here are a few additional tips to help you hide your hair under a wig:

  • Choose a wig that fits your head snugly. A loose wig can shift around and reveal your natural hair underneath.
  • If you have long hair, consider tucking it into a bun or ponytail before braiding or twisting it. This will make it easier to keep your hair flat and smooth.
  • Use a wig cap that matches your skin tone. This will help the wig look more natural and blend in with your scalp.
  • If you have a particularly thick or voluminous wig, you may want to use a few bobby pins to secure it in place.

FAQs About Hiding Your Hair Under a Wig

Do I need to wear a wig cap?

While you don't necessarily need to wear a wig cap, it can be helpful for keeping your hair flat and smooth under the wig. Plus, it can help prevent the wig from slipping or sliding around on your head.

Can I wear a wig if I have short hair?

Yes, you can definitely wear a wig if you have short hair. You may just need to pin your hair up more tightly or use a wig cap to keep it in place.

How can I keep my wig from slipping?

If your wig is slipping, you may need to adjust the placement of the combs or clips on the wig. You can also try using a wig grip or adhesive tape to help keep the wig in place.

Hair Care Tips for Wearing a Wig

Wearing a wig can be a great way to switch up your look, but it's important to take care of your natural hair and scalp as well. Here are a few tips:

  • Wash your wig regularly to keep it clean and free of buildup. Use a mild shampoo and conditioner designed for synthetic or human hair wigs.
  • Avoid using heat tools on your wig, as this can cause damage and shorten the lifespan of the wig.
  • Wear a wig cap to protect your natural hair and scalp from friction and rubbing.
  • Take breaks from wearing your wig to give your scalp a chance to breathe.

With these tips and techniques, you can confidently wear your wig without worrying about your natural hair showing through. Whether you're wearing a wig for fashion or for medical reasons, a little bit of preparation and care can go a long way.
