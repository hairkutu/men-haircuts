How To Fix Thick Short Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Frank May 11, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you struggling to manage your thick, short hair? Do you find it difficult to style, and often feel like it lacks volume or definition? If so, you're not alone. Many people with thick, short hair struggle to find a style that works for them. But don't worry, there are plenty of ways to fix this issue and achieve the hair of your dreams. In this article, we'll walk you through some simple steps to help you fix your thick, short hair once and for all.

Description

Thick hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it can look beautiful and voluminous, it can also be difficult to manage and style, especially if it's short. Thick hair can easily become frizzy, flat, and lifeless if not properly cared for. This is why it's essential to have a good hair care routine in place and to know how to style your hair in a way that suits your hair type.

How to Fix Thick Short Hair

Step 1: Choose the Right Haircut

The first step in fixing your thick, short hair is to choose the right haircut. Shorter haircuts tend to work better for thick hair, as they can help to reduce the weight and add more volume and definition. Consider getting a layered haircut, as this can help to create movement and texture in your hair. You can also opt for a bob, pixie, or a blunt cut, depending on your personal style and face shape.

Step 2: Use the Right Products

Using the right products is essential when it comes to fixing your thick, short hair. Look for products that are specifically designed for thick hair, such as volumizing shampoos and conditioners. You can also use styling products like mousse, gel, or hairspray to add more texture and hold to your hair. Just make sure not to overdo it, as too much product can weigh your hair down and make it look greasy.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair

Blow drying your hair can help to add volume and definition to your thick, short hair. Start by towel-drying your hair, then use a round brush to blow dry your hair in sections. Focus on the roots of your hair, as this is where you want to add the most volume. Use a cool shot at the end of your blow-dry to help set your style in place.

Step 4: Try Different Styling Techniques

Experiment with different styling techniques to find the one that works best for your hair type. You can try using a straightener or curling iron to add more texture and definition to your hair. You can also try braiding your hair overnight to create natural waves or using a hair diffuser to add more volume and bounce to your hair.

Tips for Managing Thick Short Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you manage your thick, short hair:

  • Don't wash your hair every day, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it look dull and lifeless.
  • Invest in a good quality hairbrush or comb to help detangle your hair without causing damage.
  • Avoid using hot tools like straighteners or curling irons too often, as this can cause heat damage to your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun, wind, and other elements by wearing a hat or using a hair serum with UV protection.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote healthy hair growth.
  • Get regular trims to help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

FAQs

Q: Is it better to have short or long hair for thick hair?

A: Shorter haircuts tend to work better for thick hair, as they can help to reduce the weight and add more volume and definition. However, you can still have long hair if you prefer, just make sure to get regular trims to keep it healthy and avoid split ends.

Q: How often should I wash my thick, short hair?

A: It's best to wash your hair every 2-3 days, as this can help to preserve your hair's natural oils and prevent it from becoming dry or brittle.

Hair Care for Thick Short Hair

Caring for your hair is essential if you want to achieve the best results. Here are some tips to help you care for your thick, short hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized and healthy.
  • Avoid using hot tools like straighteners or curling irons too often, as this can cause heat damage to your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun, wind, and other elements by wearing a hat or using a hair serum with UV protection.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage and damage to your hair.

By following these simple tips and steps, you can easily fix your thick, short hair and achieve the hair of your dreams. So, go ahead and try out these tips and see the difference for yourself!
