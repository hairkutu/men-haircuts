How To Fix Over Cut Bangs A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Jul 15, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you've ever had a haircut gone wrong, then you know the frustration of dealing with over cut bangs. Whether it was a DIY haircut or a salon mishap, having bangs that are too short or uneven can be a real headache. But don't worry, there are ways to fix over cut bangs without having to wait for them to grow out. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps to get your bangs looking great again.

Description

Over cut bangs can happen for a variety of reasons. Maybe you tried to trim your bangs yourself, or your stylist misunderstood how short you wanted them. Whatever the cause, the result is bangs that are too short or uneven, and can be difficult to style. But don't worry, with a little patience and some know-how, you can fix over cut bangs and get your hair looking great again.

How to Fix Over Cut Bangs: Step-by-Step

Step 1: Assess the Damage

The first step in fixing over cut bangs is to assess the damage. Take a close look at your bangs and determine how much needs to be fixed. If they are just slightly too short, you may be able to style them in a way that hides the mistake. If they are significantly shorter than you wanted, you'll need to take more drastic measures.

Step 2: Don't Panic

It can be easy to panic when you realize your bangs are too short, but try to stay calm. Remember, hair grows back! In the meantime, there are ways to style your bangs to make them look better or even hide the mistake altogether.

Step 3: Experiment with Styling

If your bangs are only slightly too short or uneven, you may be able to experiment with different styling techniques to make them look better. For example, you could try sweeping your bangs to the side or parting them in the middle. You could also add some volume by blow-drying them with a round brush.

Step 4: Trim the Rest of Your Hair

If your bangs are significantly shorter than you wanted, you may need to trim the rest of your hair to match. This can be a scary prospect, but if you're careful and take your time, you can do it yourself at home. Alternatively, you could go to a salon and ask them to fix the mistake.

Step 5: Invest in Hair Extensions

If you're not comfortable trimming the rest of your hair or you don't want to wait for your bangs to grow out, you could invest in hair extensions. There are clip-in extensions specifically designed for bangs that can add length and volume to your hair. Just make sure you choose extensions that match your natural hair color.

Tips for Fixing Over Cut Bangs

  • Don't try to fix over cut bangs immediately after the mistake. Wait a few days to see how they settle.
  • Be patient. Hair grows at an average rate of half an inch per month, so it may take a while for your bangs to grow out.
  • If you're going to trim the rest of your hair to match your bangs, make sure you have the right tools on hand, such as sharp scissors and a comb.
  • If you're not confident in your ability to fix over cut bangs yourself, don't hesitate to go to a salon and ask for help.

FAQs

Q: Can I fix over cut bangs myself?

A: It depends on how significant the mistake is. If your bangs are only slightly too short or uneven, you may be able to fix them yourself by experimenting with styling techniques. However, if they are significantly shorter than you wanted, you may need to trim the rest of your hair to match, which can be tricky to do yourself.

Q: How long does it take for hair to grow back?

A: Hair grows at an average rate of half an inch per month. So, if your bangs are only slightly too short, it may only take a few weeks for them to grow out. If they are significantly shorter than you wanted, it may take several months for them to reach the length you want.

Q: Can I wear a hat or headband to hide over cut bangs?

A: Yes, wearing a hat or headband can be a great way to hide over cut bangs while you wait for them to grow out. Just be sure not to wear them too tightly, as this can cause further damage to your hair.

Hair Care

While you're waiting for your bangs to grow out, it's important to take good care of your hair. This means using gentle shampoo and conditioner, avoiding heat styling tools as much as possible, and getting regular trims to keep your hair healthy. You could also try using hair masks or other treatments to keep your hair nourished and hydrated.

Conclusion

Fixing over cut bangs can be a frustrating experience, but with a little patience and some know-how, you can get your hair looking great again. Whether you experiment with styling, trim the rest of your hair to match, or invest in hair extensions, there are ways to fix over cut bangs and make them look great.
