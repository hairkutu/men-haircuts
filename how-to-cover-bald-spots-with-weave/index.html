How To Cover Bald Spots With Weave A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Frank May 13, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Dealing with bald spots can be frustrating and impact one's confidence. Thankfully, weaves are a great way to cover bald spots and achieve a full, voluminous look. In this article, we'll go over how to cover bald spots with weave, step by step. We'll also provide tips for maintaining your weave and answer some frequently asked questions.

How to Cover Bald Spots with Weave

Before we begin, it's important to note that you should always consult with a professional stylist before attempting to cover bald spots with weave. They will be able to assess the extent of the balding and recommend the best course of action.

Step 1: Choose the Right Weave

When covering bald spots, it's important to choose a weave that closely matches your natural hair texture and color. This will help to create a seamless look and prevent your weave from standing out.

Consider opting for a closure or frontal weave, as these provide more coverage and can be styled in various ways.

Step 2: Prep Your Hair

Before installing the weave, you'll need to prep your hair. This involves washing and conditioning your hair, as well as blow-drying it straight.

It's important to ensure that your hair is completely dry before proceeding with the installation, as moisture can cause your weave to loosen and become uncomfortable.

Step 3: Install the Weave

Once your hair is prepped, it's time to install the weave. This is where it's crucial to work with a professional stylist, as they will be able to create a customized installation that covers your bald spots and looks natural.

The stylist will likely braid your hair and then sew the weave onto the braids. They may also use adhesive to secure the weave in place.

Step 4: Style Your Weave

After the weave is installed, you can style it as desired. This may involve curling, straightening, or braiding the hair. Just be sure to avoid using excessive heat, as this can damage the weave and your natural hair.

Tips for Maintaining Your Weave

To ensure that your weave looks great and lasts as long as possible, follow these tips:

  • Avoid using heavy styling products, as these can weigh down the weave and make it look unnatural.
  • Sleep with a satin or silk pillowcase to prevent tangling and breakage.
  • Wash and condition your weave regularly, using products that are specifically formulated for weaves.
  • Avoid excessive heat styling, as this can damage the weave and your natural hair.

FAQ

How long does a weave typically last?

A weave can last anywhere from 4-8 weeks, depending on how well it's maintained and how quickly your natural hair grows.

Will a weave damage my natural hair?

A properly installed and maintained weave should not cause damage to your natural hair. However, if the weave is installed too tightly or left in for too long, it can cause breakage and damage.

Can I wash my weave?

Yes, you can and should wash your weave regularly to keep it looking and feeling fresh. Just be sure to use products that are specifically formulated for weaves and avoid rubbing or tugging on the hair.

Hair Care

To maintain the health of your natural hair, be sure to deep condition regularly and avoid using excessive heat or harsh chemicals.

Also, be gentle when detangling and avoid pulling or tugging on your hair. This can cause breakage and damage, which can lead to more bald spots.

Conclusion

Covering bald spots with weave can be a great way to achieve a full, voluminous look. Just be sure to work with a professional stylist, choose the right weave, and maintain it properly. By following these tips and taking care of your natural hair, you can achieve a beautiful, healthy head of hair.
