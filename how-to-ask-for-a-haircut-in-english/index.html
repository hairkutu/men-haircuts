How To Ask For A Haircut In English A Relaxed Guide

Written by Bardi Apr 04, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're in a foreign country and need a haircut, it can be intimidating to try and communicate what you want to the stylist. But fear not! In this guide, we'll show you how to ask for a haircut in English in a relaxed and easy-to-understand way. We'll cover everything from vocabulary to common phrases, so you can leave the salon with the perfect haircut.

Table of Contents:

  • Vocabulary
  • Common Phrases
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Communication
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care Tips

Vocabulary

Before we dive into the phrases you'll need to know, let's go over some key vocabulary words:

  • Barber - a person who cuts men's hair
  • Hairdresser/Stylist - a person who cuts women's hair
  • Salon - a place where you can get your hair cut
  • Trim - to cut a small amount of hair off the ends
  • Layer - to cut hair into different lengths
  • Buzz cut - a very short haircut
  • Crew cut - a short haircut where the hair is longer on top and shorter on the sides
  • Bang/fringe - the hair that falls over your forehead

Common Phrases

Now that you know some important vocabulary, let's move on to some common phrases you'll need to know:

  • "I'd like a haircut, please." - This is a simple and straightforward way to ask for a haircut.
  • "I want to keep the length, but just tidy it up a bit." - Use this phrase if you want to keep most of your hair, but just need a little maintenance.
  • "I'd like a trim, please." - This is a good phrase to use if you just want a small amount of hair cut off.
  • "Can you layer my hair?" - Use this phrase if you want your hair cut into different lengths.
  • "I want a buzz cut." - Use this phrase if you want a very short haircut.
  • "Can you give me a crew cut?" - Use this phrase if you want a short haircut where the hair is longer on top and shorter on the sides.
  • "Can you cut my bangs/fringe?" - Use this phrase if you want the hair on your forehead cut.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know the vocabulary and phrases, here's a step-by-step guide on how to ask for a haircut in English:

  1. Decide what type of haircut you want.
  2. Find a salon or barber shop.
  3. When you arrive, greet the stylist/barber with a friendly "hello" or "hi."
  4. Use one of the common phrases we listed above to ask for the type of haircut you want.
  5. If the stylist/barber doesn't understand, try rephrasing or using gestures to communicate.
  6. If the stylist/barber still doesn't understand, try using a picture on your phone or a magazine to show them what you want.
  7. Once the stylist/barber understands, sit back and relax while they cut your hair.
  8. If you're not happy with the haircut, politely ask if they can fix it.
  9. When they're finished, say "thank you" and pay for your haircut.
  10. Finally, if you're happy with the result, leave a tip for the stylist/barber.

Tips for Communication

Here are some tips to help you communicate effectively with your stylist/barber:

  • Bring a picture or use gestures to help communicate what you want.
  • Speak slowly and clearly.
  • If you're not sure how to say something, use a translation app on your phone.
  • Don't be afraid to ask the stylist/barber to repeat themselves if you don't understand.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about getting a haircut in English:

What do I do if the stylist/barber doesn't understand me?

If the stylist/barber doesn't understand what you're saying, try rephrasing or using gestures to communicate. You can also use a picture on your phone or a magazine to show them what you want.

How much should I tip the stylist/barber?

A good rule of thumb is to tip 15-20% of the cost of the haircut.

What if I'm not happy with the haircut?

If you're not happy with the result, politely ask if they can fix it. Most stylists/barbers will be happy to make adjustments to make sure you're satisfied.

Hair Care Tips

Now that you have your new haircut, here are some tips to keep your hair looking healthy:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using too many styling products, as they can weigh your hair down and make it look greasy.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to avoid breaking your hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking neat and healthy.

Now that you know how to ask for a haircut in English, you can confidently walk into any salon or barber shop and get the perfect haircut. Just remember to speak slowly and clearly, bring a picture or use gestures to communicate what you want, and don't be afraid to ask the stylist/barber to repeat themselves if you don't understand. Happy hair cutting!
