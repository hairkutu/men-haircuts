How To Put Up Long Thin Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jul 24, 2023 · 7 min read

Long thin hair can be a blessing and a curse. It can be beautiful and ethereal, but it can also be difficult to style. Putting up long thin hair can be especially challenging, as it tends to slip out of most hairstyles. In this guide, we’ll show you how to put up long thin hair in a way that will stay put all day.

Table of Contents

  • How to Put Up Long Thin Hair
  • Step-by-Step Instructions
  • Tips for Putting Up Long Thin Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Long Thin Hair

How to Put Up Long Thin Hair

Before we get started, it’s important to have the right tools on hand. You’ll need:

  • Bobby pins
  • Hair elastics
  • Texturizing spray
  • Hairspray

Now, let’s get started.

1. Start with Clean Hair

Wash your hair and make sure it’s completely dry before you start styling. Clean hair will help your style stay in place longer.

2. Add Texture

Long thin hair tends to be slippery, so you’ll want to add some texture to help it stay in place. Spray some texturizing spray throughout your hair and scrunch it with your fingers to create some volume and texture.

3. Choose Your Style

There are many ways to put up long thin hair, so choose a style that works for your hair type and the occasion. Some popular options include:

  • A messy bun
  • A low ponytail
  • A braided updo

Step-by-Step Instructions

1. Messy Bun

  1. Pull your hair back into a loose ponytail.
  2. Twist the ponytail and wrap it around the base to create a bun.
  3. Pin the bun in place with bobby pins. Leave some pieces of hair out around your face for a soft, romantic look.
  4. Spray with hairspray to keep the style in place.

2. Low Ponytail

  1. Brush your hair back and gather it into a low ponytail at the nape of your neck.
  2. Secure the ponytail with a hair elastic.
  3. Take a small section of hair from the underside of the ponytail and wrap it around the elastic to hide it.
  4. Pin the section of hair in place with a bobby pin.
  5. Spray with hairspray to keep the style in place.

3. Braided Updo

  1. Create a deep side part and braid a section of hair on the larger side of the part.
  2. Pin the braid in place with bobby pins at the nape of your neck.
  3. Take the rest of your hair and twist it into a low bun.
  4. Pin the bun in place with bobby pins.
  5. Spray with hairspray to keep the style in place.

Tips for Putting Up Long Thin Hair

  • Don’t be afraid to use a lot of bobby pins. The more pins you use, the more secure your style will be.
  • Use hairspray to keep your style in place, but don’t overdo it. Too much hairspray can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • If your hair is too thin to create a bun or ponytail, try using a hairpiece to add volume and length.

FAQs

1. How do I put up long thin hair without damaging it?

Use gentle hair elastics and avoid pulling your hair too tight. If you need to use bobby pins, be sure to use ones with smooth, rounded tips.

2. How do I keep my style in place all day?

Use plenty of bobby pins and hairspray, and avoid touching your hair too much throughout the day.

Hair Care for Long Thin Hair

Long thin hair can be delicate, so it’s important to take good care of it. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat tools on your hair too often, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Eat a healthy diet and stay hydrated to promote healthy hair growth.

With these tips and tricks, you’ll be able to put up your long thin hair in a variety of styles that will stay put all day.
