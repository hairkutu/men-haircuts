Does Shoulder Length Hair Suit Fat Faces

Description

If you have a round or chubby face, you may be wondering if shoulder length hair suits your features. While there's no one-size-fits-all answer to this question, there are some guidelines you can follow to determine if this hairstyle is right for you.

In general, shoulder length hair can help elongate the face and draw attention away from any fullness in the cheeks. However, you'll need to consider factors such as your hair texture, face shape, and personal style before making a decision.

How to Determine if Shoulder Length Hair Suits Fat Faces

The first step in determining if shoulder length hair is a good choice for your round face is to identify your face shape. Stand in front of a mirror and pull your hair back away from your face. Look at the shape of your features and compare them to the following:

  • Round face: cheeks are the widest part of the face and the length and width are nearly equal
  • Oval face: forehead is slightly wider than the chin and the face is longer than it is wide
  • Square face: forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are similar in width and the face is angular
  • Heart-shaped face: forehead is wider than the cheeks and chin, and the face is widest at the temples
  • Diamond-shaped face: widest part of the face is at the temples, and the chin and forehead are narrower

If you have a round face, you may want to consider a shoulder length hairstyle that incorporates layers and volume at the crown to create the illusion of length. If you have an oval or heart-shaped face, you can experiment with straight or curly shoulder length hair.

Step-by-Step Guide to Choosing Shoulder Length Hair for Fat Faces

If you're interested in trying a shoulder length hairstyle, follow these steps to find the perfect look for your face shape:

  1. Consult with a hairstylist who has experience working with round faces.
  2. Bring photos of hairstyles you like to your appointment.
  3. Consider adding layers to create the illusion of length.
  4. Avoid blunt cuts that can accentuate fullness in the cheeks.
  5. Experiment with bangs to see if they flatter your features.
  6. Try different styling techniques, such as curling or straightening, to see what works best for your hair type.
  7. Don't be afraid to make changes if the first hairstyle you try doesn't feel right.

Tips for Styling Shoulder Length Hair on Fat Faces

Once you've settled on a shoulder length hairstyle, use these tips to keep your hair looking its best:

  • Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add body to your hair.
  • Avoid heavy, oily products that can weigh your hair down.
  • Use a round brush to blow dry your hair and create volume at the crown.
  • Consider using a hair straightener or curling iron to add texture and definition to your hair.
  • Visit your hairstylist regularly to keep your hair healthy and well-maintained.

FAQs about Shoulder Length Hair on Fat Faces

Can I wear my hair in a middle part with a round face?

Yes, you can wear your hair in a middle part with a round face. However, a side part can be more flattering as it creates asymmetry and draws attention away from the roundness of the face.

Should I avoid bangs with a round face?

Bangs can be a great way to add interest and dimension to a shoulder length hairstyle. However, if you have a round face, you may want to avoid heavy or blunt bangs that can make your face appear fuller. Instead, opt for wispy or side-swept bangs that create a soft, feminine look.

Can I wear my hair curly with a round face?

Yes, curly hair can be a great way to add volume and texture to a shoulder length hairstyle on a round face. However, you'll want to avoid tight, corkscrew curls that can make your face appear smaller. Instead, opt for loose, beachy waves that frame your face and create a soft, romantic look.

Hair Care Tips for Shoulder Length Hair

No matter what your face shape, taking care of your shoulder length hair is essential for keeping it healthy and beautiful. Use these tips to keep your hair looking its best:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid stripping it of natural oils.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish your hair.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair every day, as this can cause damage and breakage.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a leave-in conditioner.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and maintain a healthy appearance.

With the right haircut and styling techniques, shoulder length hair can be a flattering and versatile option for fat faces. Whether you prefer straight, curly, or layered hair, the key is to choose a style that complements your unique features and personal style.
