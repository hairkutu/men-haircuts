What To Do For Really Frizzy Hair Tips How To And Hair Care

Written by Eveline Apr 07, 2023 · 8 min read

Do you have really frizzy hair that seems impossible to control? Frizzy hair can be caused by various factors, such as humidity, heat damage, or genetics. But don’t worry, there are several things that you can do to tame those frizzies and achieve smoother, more manageable hair. In this article, we’ll discuss some tips, how-to steps, and hair care advice to help you deal with frizzy hair.

Table of Contents

  • Tips for Taming Frizzy Hair
  • How to Tame Frizzy Hair
  • Hair Care for Frizzy Hair
  • FAQs about Frizzy Hair

Tips for Taming Frizzy Hair

Before we dive into the how-to steps, here are some tips that you can try to tame your frizzy hair:

  • Avoid washing your hair too frequently, as it can strip your hair of its natural oils that help keep it moisturized.
  • Use a hydrating shampoo and conditioner that are specifically formulated for frizzy hair.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools, such as flat irons and curling wands, as they can damage your hair and make it even frizzier.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or a detangling brush to gently comb your hair when it’s wet.
  • Avoid rubbing your hair vigorously with a towel to dry it, as this can cause friction and make your hair frizzier. Instead, use a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt to gently blot your hair.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or a hair oil to your hair to help moisturize and tame frizz.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain alcohol, as they can dry out your hair and make it more prone to frizz.
  • Wear a hat or a scarf when you’re outside on a humid day to help protect your hair from frizz-inducing moisture.

How to Tame Frizzy Hair

Now that you know some tips for preventing frizz, here’s how you can tame your frizzy hair:

Step 1: Start with clean, damp hair.

Wash your hair with a hydrating shampoo and conditioner that are specifically formulated for frizzy hair. After you’ve rinsed out the conditioner, gently squeeze out the excess water from your hair with a microfiber towel or an old t-shirt.

Step 2: Apply a hair serum or a hair oil.

Apply a hair serum or a hair oil to your hair, focusing on the ends and the mid-lengths. These products can help moisturize your hair and tame frizz. Be sure to choose a product that is lightweight and won’t weigh down your hair.

Step 3: Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.

Use a wide-tooth comb or a detangling brush to gently comb your hair, starting from the ends and working your way up to the roots. This can help prevent breakage and minimize frizz.

Step 4: Blow-dry your hair using a diffuser attachment.

Attach a diffuser to your blow dryer and use it to dry your hair. The diffuser can help distribute the heat more evenly and minimize frizz. Use a low or medium heat setting and avoid touching your hair with the diffuser too much.

Step 5: Finish with a hair spray or a hair cream.

Apply a hair spray or a hair cream to your hair to help set your style and tame frizz. Choose a product that is lightweight and won’t make your hair look greasy or weighed down.

Hair Care for Frizzy Hair

In addition to the tips and how-to steps above, here are some hair care practices that you can incorporate into your routine to help manage frizzy hair:

  • Deep condition your hair once a week to help replenish moisture and prevent breakage.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools too frequently, or use a heat protectant spray before using them.
  • Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends and promote healthy growth.
  • Avoid using hair products that contain sulfates, as they can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it more prone to frizz.
  • Avoid using hair elastics that can tug on your hair and cause breakage. Instead, use scrunchies or hair clips.

FAQs about Frizzy Hair

Q: Can I brush my frizzy hair?

A: Yes, you can brush your frizzy hair, but be sure to use a wide-tooth comb or a detangling brush to avoid causing breakage and more frizz.

Q: How often should I wash my frizzy hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and how much oil your scalp produces. Generally, you can wash your hair every 2-3 days or less frequently if your hair is particularly dry or damaged.

Q: Can I use a hair mask for frizzy hair?

A: Yes, using a hair mask once a week can help moisturize and tame frizz. Look for a hair mask that is specifically formulated for frizzy hair and follow the instructions on the packaging.

With these tips, how-to steps, and hair care advice, you can take control of your frizzy hair and achieve smoother, more manageable locks. Remember to be patient and consistent with your hair care routine, and don’t be afraid to experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for you.
