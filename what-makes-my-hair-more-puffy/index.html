What Makes My Hair More Puffy

Written by Frank May 04, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you struggling with puffy hair? Do you find yourself constantly trying to tame your hair but it just keeps getting bigger and bigger? Well, you’re not alone! Many people with various hair types experience hair puffiness, and it can be frustrating to deal with. In this article, we’ll discuss what makes your hair more puffy and provide tips on how to combat it.

Table of Contents:

  • Description

  • How to

  • Step by Step

  • Tips

  • FAQ

  • Hair Care

Description

Firstly, it’s important to understand what causes hair puffiness. Puffiness is usually caused by excessive moisture or humidity. When the hair is exposed to humidity, it absorbs moisture from the air, causing the hair shaft to swell. This leads to frizz, tangles, and overall puffiness.

Another factor that contributes to hair puffiness is the type of hair you have. If you have naturally curly or wavy hair, your hair is more prone to puffiness due to the shape of the hair follicles. Curly and wavy hair is more porous and absorbs more moisture, making it more prone to frizz and puffiness.

How to

The good news is that there are ways to combat hair puffiness. One way is to use anti-frizz and smoothing products. These products are designed to help smooth the hair cuticle and reduce frizz. Look for products that contain ingredients like argan oil, coconut oil, and shea butter, which are known for their hydrating and smoothing properties.

Another way to combat hair puffiness is to blow-dry your hair using a diffuser attachment. A diffuser helps distribute heat evenly, which can help reduce frizz and puffiness. You can also try air-drying your hair by plopping it. Plopping is a technique where you wrap your hair in a t-shirt or microfiber towel to help absorb excess moisture and reduce frizz.

Step by Step

If you’re not sure how to plop your hair, here’s a step-by-step guide:

  1. Wash and condition your hair as usual.
  2. Flip your head upside down and place a t-shirt or microfiber towel on a flat surface.
  3. Gently lower your hair onto the t-shirt, making sure your curls are in place.
  4. Wrap the t-shirt around your hair and tie it in a knot at the front of your head.
  5. Leave the t-shirt on for 10-20 minutes, or until your hair is partially dry.
  6. Remove the t-shirt and let your hair air dry or finish drying with a diffuser attachment.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help combat hair puffiness:

  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons, as they can damage the hair cuticle and lead to more frizz.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair, as brushing can lead to more frizz and puffiness.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can transfer oils and dirt from your hands onto your hair, leading to more frizz.
  • Consider getting a keratin treatment, which can help smooth the hair cuticle and reduce frizz and puffiness.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about hair puffiness:

Q: Can using too much product cause hair puffiness?

A: Yes, using too much product can weigh down the hair and make it look puffy. Use a dime-sized amount of product and distribute it evenly throughout your hair.

Q: Can humidity cause hair puffiness even if I have straight hair?

A: Yes, humidity can still cause hair puffiness even if you have straight hair. Straight hair is still susceptible to moisture absorption, which can lead to frizz and puffiness.

Hair Care

Lastly, taking care of your hair is essential in preventing hair puffiness. Make sure to use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner, as sulfates can strip the hair of its natural oils and lead to dryness and frizz. Additionally, avoid over-washing your hair and use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

By following these tips and taking care of your hair, you can combat hair puffiness and achieve smooth and manageable hair. Don’t let hair puffiness get in the way of feeling confident and beautiful!
