How To Cut Your Hair In Short Layers At Home

Written by William May 13, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're looking to switch up your hairstyle, cutting your hair in short layers is a great option. Not only does it add texture and volume, but it also gives your hair a modern and trendy look. However, going to a salon to get your hair cut can be expensive and time-consuming. That's why we've put together this guide on how to cut your hair in short layers at home. With a few simple tools and some basic knowledge, you can achieve the perfect short layered haircut from the comfort of your own home.

How to Cut Your Hair in Short Layers: Step by Step

Before you get started, it's important to gather your tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and some hair clips. Once you have everything you need, follow these steps:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will make it easier to cut and ensure that your hair is free from any tangles or knots. If you have curly hair, it's best to let it dry naturally to avoid any shrinkage.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using a comb, divide your hair into sections. Start by parting your hair down the middle and then dividing it into four equal sections - two in the front and two in the back. Secure each section with a hair clip.

Step 3: Cut the Bottom Layer

Start by cutting the bottom layer of your hair. To do this, take one of the back sections and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers and cut it straight across. Repeat this step for the other back section.

Step 4: Cut the Middle Layer

Once you've finished cutting the bottom layer, move on to the middle layer. Take one of the front sections and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers and cut it at a slight angle towards the back. This will create a subtle layering effect. Repeat this step for the other front section.

Step 5: Cut the Top Layer

Finally, it's time to cut the top layer. Take a small section of hair from the top of your head and comb it straight up. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers and cut it at a slight angle towards the back. This will create volume and texture. Repeat this step for the rest of the top layer.

Step 6: Check Your Layers

Once you've finished cutting your hair, it's important to check your layers. Take a comb and run it through your hair to make sure that your layers are even. If you notice any unevenness, simply trim the hair until it's even.

Tips for Cutting Your Hair in Short Layers

Cutting your hair in short layers can be a bit tricky, especially if you're doing it for the first time. Here are a few tips to help you achieve the perfect short layered haircut:

  • Always use sharp scissors to avoid split ends.
  • Make sure your hair is completely dry before cutting it.
  • Start by cutting a little bit of hair at a time to avoid cutting too much off.
  • When cutting the top layer, hold the hair straight up to create more volume.
  • Don't be afraid to ask for help if you're struggling to cut the back of your hair.

FAQs about Cutting Your Hair in Short Layers

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting your hair in short layers:

Can you cut your hair in short layers with curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your hair in short layers with curly hair. However, it's important to cut your hair when it's dry to avoid any shrinkage. It's also a good idea to use a diffuser when blow-drying your hair to enhance your curls.

How often should you cut your hair in short layers?

It's recommended to cut your hair in short layers every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and texture of your haircut.

Can you cut your hair in short layers if you have fine hair?

Yes, cutting your hair in short layers can actually make your hair look thicker and fuller. However, it's important to not cut too much hair off as this can make your hair look even thinner.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Hair in Short Layers

After cutting your hair in short layers, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain the shape and texture of your haircut. Here are some tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that's suitable for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain the shape of your haircut.

With these tips and tricks, you can achieve a beautiful short layered haircut from the comfort of your own home. Just remember to take your time and be patient, and you'll have a trendy and modern hairstyle in no time.
