How To Thin Long Hair With Thinning Shears

Written by Christine Apr 30, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you have long hair, you may find that it becomes heavy and difficult to manage. Thinning your hair can make it easier to style and give it a more natural look. Thinning shears are a great tool for achieving this. In this blog post, we will show you how to thin long hair with thinning shears.

Description

Thinning shears are scissors with teeth on one or both blades. These teeth create gaps in the hair when the scissors are closed, removing some of the bulk. Thinning shears can be used on all hair types, but they are particularly useful for thick, heavy hair.

How to Thinning Long Hair with Thinning Shears

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will make it easier to see where the bulk is and where you need to thin it out.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections using hair clips. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that you don't miss any areas.

Step 3: Use the Thinning Shears

Take one of the sections of hair and hold it straight up. Using the thinning shears, cut into the hair, starting at the roots and working your way down.

Make sure that you only cut a small amount of hair with each snip. You can always go back and thin more if you need to.

Step 4: Repeat on all Sections

Repeat this process on all sections of your hair until you have thinned it to your liking.

Tips

- Start with a small amount of hair and work your way up, so you don't thin too much at once.

- Use the thinning shears on the underneath layers of your hair, so the top layer still looks full.

- For a more natural look, thin out the ends of your hair, rather than the roots.

FAQ

Can I use regular scissors to thin my hair?

Regular scissors can be used to thin hair, but thinning shears are more effective.

How often should I thin my hair?

This depends on how quickly your hair grows and how thick it is. Generally, thinning your hair every 6-8 weeks is a good idea.

Hair Care

After thinning your hair, it's important to take care of it. Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.

Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair. If you do use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray beforehand.

Regular trims are also important to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends.

In conclusion, thinning shears are a great tool for thinning long hair. Follow these steps and tips to achieve a natural and manageable look.
