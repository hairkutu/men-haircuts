How To Cut A Layered Wavy Bob Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Jun 07, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of your long hair and looking for a new haircut that is trendy and easy to maintain? Look no further than the layered wavy bob! This hairstyle is perfect for those who want to add some volume and texture to their hair without sacrificing too much length. Here is a step-by-step guide on how to cut a layered wavy bob:

Description

The layered wavy bob is a popular hairstyle that features layers of hair that are cut to different lengths, creating volume and texture. The hair is also cut to a length that falls between the chin and the shoulders, giving the hair a bouncy and playful look. This hairstyle is perfect for those with wavy or curly hair, as the layers will enhance the natural texture of the hair.

How to Cut a Layered Wavy Bob

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You will need sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section off your hair.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Wash and dry your hair before you start cutting. This will give you a better idea of how much length you want to take off and will make it easier to see the natural texture of your hair.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four parts: the top, the sides, and the back. Use hair clips to hold each section in place.

Step 4: Cut the Back Section

Start with the back section of your hair. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Cut the hair at an angle, starting from the bottom and working your way up. Repeat this step for each section of hair in the back.

Step 5: Cut the Side Sections

Next, move on to the side sections of your hair. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Cut the hair at an angle, starting from the bottom and working your way up. Repeat this step for each section of hair on both sides.

Step 6: Cut the Top Section

Finally, move on to the top section of your hair. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Cut the hair at an angle, starting from the bottom and working your way up. Repeat this step for each section of hair on the top of your head.

Step 7: Blend the Layers

After you have cut all the sections of your hair, use a comb to blend the layers together. This will create a seamless and natural look.

Tips

  • Be patient and take your time. Cutting your own hair can be tricky, so don't rush the process.
  • Start with small sections of hair and cut less than you think you need. You can always go back and cut more if necessary.
  • If you have curly hair, consider cutting it when it is dry. This will give you a better idea of how the hair will look when it is styled.

FAQ

Can I cut a layered wavy bob if I have straight hair?

Yes, you can still cut a layered wavy bob if you have straight hair. However, you may need to use styling tools like a curling iron or a straightener to create the wavy texture.

How often should I cut my layered wavy bob?

You should cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain the shape and length of your layered wavy bob.

Hair Care

To keep your layered wavy bob looking healthy and shiny, make sure to use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. You can also use a hair mask once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair and cause split ends.

Cutting a layered wavy bob is a great way to update your look and add some volume and texture to your hair. Follow these simple steps, and you'll have a trendy new haircut in no time!
