What To Wear Running 65 Degrees A Guide To Comfortable And Stylish Running Outfits

Written by Oliver Jun 06, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Running is a great way to stay fit and healthy, but it's important to dress appropriately for the weather. When the temperature is around 65 degrees, you need to wear clothes that will keep you comfortable and dry. In this guide, we will show you what to wear running 65 degrees, so you can enjoy your workout without worrying about your clothes.

Description

Running in 65-degree weather can be tricky. It's not too hot, but it's not too cold either. You need to find the right balance between keeping yourself warm and not overheating. You also need to consider the humidity and wind, which can affect how you feel during your workout.

How to Dress for Running 65 Degrees

Here are some tips on what to wear running 65 degrees:

1. Wear Moisture-Wicking Clothes

Moisture-wicking clothes are designed to keep you dry by wicking sweat away from your body. They are made from materials like polyester, nylon, and spandex, which are breathable and quick-drying. This will help you stay comfortable and prevent chafing.

2. Wear Layers

When it's 65 degrees outside, you may feel chilly at the beginning of your run, but you'll warm up quickly. Wearing layers will help you regulate your body temperature. Start with a lightweight, long-sleeved shirt or a tank top and add a light jacket or a hoodie on top. You can always take off a layer if you get too warm.

3. Choose Breathable Fabrics

Fabrics like cotton and wool can trap sweat and make you feel uncomfortable. Instead, choose fabrics that are breathable, like mesh or bamboo. They will allow air to circulate and keep you cool.

4. Wear Proper Running Shoes

Your running shoes are the most important piece of equipment you need. Make sure you have shoes that fit well and provide proper support for your feet. Look for shoes that are lightweight and breathable, with good traction and cushioning.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on what to wear running 65 degrees:

Step 1: Check the Weather

Before you go for a run, check the weather forecast to see if it will be 65 degrees outside. If the temperature is going to be much hotter or colder, adjust your outfit accordingly.

Step 2: Choose Moisture-Wicking Clothes

Select a moisture-wicking shirt and shorts or leggings made from synthetic materials.

Step 3: Wear Layers

Start with a lightweight, long-sleeved shirt or a tank top and add a light jacket or hoodie on top.

Step 4: Choose Breathable Fabrics

Select fabrics like mesh or bamboo, which will allow air to circulate and keep you cool.

Step 5: Wear Proper Running Shoes

Make sure you have shoes that fit well and provide proper support for your feet.

Step 6: Accessorize

You may want to wear a hat or a headband to keep sweat out of your eyes or wear sunglasses to protect your eyes from the sun.

Tips for Running 65 Degrees

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when running in 65-degree weather:

1. Hydrate

Drink plenty of water before, during, and after your run to stay hydrated.

2. Use Sunscreen

Apply sunscreen to exposed skin to protect yourself from harmful UV rays.

3. Warm Up

Start your run with a warm-up to prevent injury and increase flexibility.

4. Cool Down

End your run with a cool-down to help your body recover.

FAQs

Q: What should I wear running when it's 65 degrees and raining?

A: Wear a waterproof jacket and pants to stay dry. Choose clothes made from synthetic materials that will dry quickly.

Q: Can I wear cotton clothes when running 65 degrees?

A: Cotton clothes can trap sweat and make you feel uncomfortable. It's best to wear clothes made from breathable and moisture-wicking fabrics.

Q: How do I know if my running shoes fit properly?

A: Your running shoes should fit snugly but not be too tight. Make sure there's enough room in the toe box and that your heel doesn't slip out of the shoe.

Hair Care

When running in 65-degree weather, it's essential to protect your hair from sweat and the elements. Here are some tips:

1. Tie Your Hair Back

Tie your hair back in a ponytail or a bun to keep it out of your face.

2. Use Hair Ties

Use hair ties that won't damage your hair or cause breakage.

3. Use Dry Shampoo

Use dry shampoo to absorb sweat and oil from your hair.

By following these tips on what to wear running 65 degrees, you'll be able to stay comfortable and stylish during your workout. Remember to stay hydrated, wear sunscreen, and warm up and cool down properly. With the right outfit and accessories, you'll be ready to hit the road and achieve your fitness goals.
