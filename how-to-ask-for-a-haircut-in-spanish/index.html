How to Ask for a Haircut in Spanish

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let's review some basic phrases and vocabulary for asking for a haircut in Spanish.

  • Quiero un corte de pelo. (I want a haircut.)
  • Quiero un corte de pelo corto/largo. (I want a short/long haircut.)
  • Quiero un corte de pelo con capas. (I want a layered haircut.)
  • Quiero un corte de pelo con flequillo. (I want a haircut with bangs.)
  • Quiero un corte de pelo con tijeras/navaja. (I want a haircut with scissors/razor.)
  • Quiero recortar las puntas. (I want to trim the ends.)

These phrases should cover the basics of what you need to communicate to your barber or hairdresser. But don't worry if you don't remember all of them - we'll go over them again in the step-by-step guide.

Tips for Getting the Haircut You Want

Here are some additional tips to help you get the haircut you want:

  • Bring a picture or use hand gestures to demonstrate what you want.
  • Use simple language and avoid complex vocabulary.
  • Be confident and clear in your communication.
  • Ask for recommendations if you're not sure what style would look best on you.
  • Consider scheduling your appointment for a time when the salon or barbershop is less busy, so you have more time to communicate with your stylist.

FAQs

What if I don't speak Spanish at all?

How much should I tip my barber or hairdresser?
