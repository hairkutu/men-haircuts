If you have wavy hair and are considering a short haircut, you're in luck! Short haircuts for wavy hair are all the rage in 2021, and there are plenty of styles to choose from. How to Choose the Right Short Haircut for Wavy Hair

When it comes to choosing a short haircut for wavy hair, there are a few factors to consider. The first is your face shape. Some short haircuts look better on certain face shapes than others. The second is the texture of your hair. Wavy hair can range from loose waves to tight curls, so you'll want to choose a haircut that works well with your specific texture.

Another factor to consider is your personal style. Do you prefer a classic, low-maintenance look, or are you more adventurous and willing to try something bold and edgy? Finally, think about how much time you're willing to spend styling your hair each day. Some short haircuts require more upkeep than others.

Short Bob

The short bob is a classic haircut that works well with wavy hair. This style is versatile and can be customized to suit your individual face shape and texture. A short bob that falls just above the chin can help to highlight your cheekbones and jawline, while a longer bob that falls just below the chin can help to elongate your face.

Pixie Cut

For those who are willing to go bold and edgy, a pixie cut can be a great option. This haircut is super short, with hair that's typically no longer than two inches in length. A pixie cut can help to showcase your natural waves, and it's a low-maintenance option that requires little styling.

Layered Bob

A layered bob is a great option for those with wavy hair, as it can help to add movement and texture to your locks. This style is especially flattering for those with a round or square face shape, as the layers can help to create the illusion of a slimmer face.

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Short Wavy Hair

Styling short wavy hair is easy and can be done in just a few minutes. Here's a step-by-step guide to styling your short wavy hair:

  1. Start with clean, towel-dried hair.
  2. Apply a small amount of styling product to your hair. This can be a mousse, gel, or cream.
  3. Using your fingers, scrunch your hair to enhance your natural waves.
  4. Blow dry your hair using a diffuser attachment. This will help to enhance your waves and prevent frizz.
  5. Once your hair is dry, use a curling iron to touch up any areas that need a little extra definition.
  6. Finish with a light hold hairspray to keep your style in place.

Tips for Maintaining Short Wavy Hair

Keeping your short wavy hair looking great requires a little bit of effort, but the result is well worth it. Here are some tips for maintaining your short wavy hair:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to maintain your haircut and prevent split ends.
  • Invest in a silk pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage while you sleep.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I get a short haircut if I have really curly hair?

Yes! Short haircuts can work well with all types of curly hair, from loose waves to tight coils. Just be sure to choose a haircut that works well with your specific texture.

How often should I wash my short wavy hair?

It depends on your hair type and personal preference. Some people prefer to wash their hair every day, while others only wash it once or twice a week. Experiment to find what works best for you.

Hair Care for Short Wavy Hair

Caring for your short wavy hair is important to keep it looking healthy and beautiful. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair when wet.
  • Avoid brushing your hair too much, as this can cause frizz.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair too often.

With the right short haircut and styling techniques, you can rock your wavy hair with confidence all year long. Whether you choose a classic bob or a bold pixie cut, be sure to take care of your hair to keep it looking and feeling its best.
