How To Cut Curly Hair To Get More Volume A Complete Guide

Written by Luffy Apr 08, 2023 · 9 min read

If you have curly hair, you know that getting volume can be a challenge. However, with the right haircut, you can achieve the full, bouncy curls of your dreams. In this guide, we’ll go over how to cut curly hair to get more volume, step by step. We’ll also provide tips and answer common questions about curly hair. Let’s get started!

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Curly Hair to Get More Volume
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Curly Hair
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

How to Cut Curly Hair to Get More Volume

The key to getting more volume in curly hair is to create layers. Layers help to distribute the weight of the hair, allowing it to bounce up and create more lift. However, it’s important to cut curly hair differently than straight hair. Here are the steps to follow:

1. Start with Dry Hair

Curly hair has a different texture when it’s wet, so it’s important to cut it when it’s dry. This will give you a more accurate idea of how the hair will look when it’s styled. Make sure to detangle the hair before you start cutting.

2. Determine the Length

Decide how much length you want to take off. It’s important to remember that curly hair will shrink up when it dries, so don’t cut too much off. Start with a small amount and work your way up.

3. Create Layers

The key to getting more volume is to create layers. Start at the bottom of the hair and work your way up, cutting small sections at a time. Make sure to cut at an angle to create a soft, natural look.

4. Check for Balance

Once you’ve created the layers, check to make sure they’re balanced. The hair should be longer in the front and shorter in the back, with the layers gradually getting shorter as they go up.

5. Refine the Shape

Once you’ve created the layers, refine the shape of the hair. Use scissors to cut any stray hairs and create a more polished look.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a more detailed step-by-step guide to cutting curly hair for more volume:

1. Detangle the Hair

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle the hair before you start cutting. This will make it easier to see the natural curl pattern and ensure a more even cut.

2. Section the Hair

Section the hair into four equal parts: top, bottom, left, and right. Use hair clips to secure each section.

3. Start with the Bottom Section

Start with the bottom section of hair. Take a small section of hair, about 1 inch wide, and twist it. This will help to define the natural curl pattern.

4. Cut at an Angle

Using scissors, cut the hair at an angle, starting at the bottom and working your way up. Cut about 1/4 to 1/2 inch off each section. Make sure to cut at an angle to create a soft, natural look.

5. Move to the Top Section

Once you’ve finished the bottom section, move on to the top section. Take a small section of hair, about 1 inch wide, and twist it. Cut at an angle, starting at the bottom and working your way up.

6. Cut the Side Sections

Repeat the same process on the left and right sections of hair. Make sure to cut at an angle and create layers that gradually get shorter as they go up.

7. Check for Balance

Once you’ve finished cutting all sections of hair, check to make sure the layers are balanced. The front should be longer than the back, with the layers gradually getting shorter as they go up.

8. Refine the Shape

Use scissors to cut any stray hairs and refine the shape of the hair. Make sure to create a soft, natural look.

Tips for Cutting Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when cutting curly hair:

  • Use sharp scissors to prevent split ends
  • Don’t cut too much off at once, as curly hair will shrink up when it dries
  • Cut the hair when it’s dry to get a more accurate idea of how it will look when styled
  • Create layers to distribute the weight of the hair and create more volume
  • Always cut at an angle to create a soft, natural look
  • Consult with a professional stylist if you’re unsure about cutting your own curly hair

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I cut my own curly hair?

Yes, you can cut your own curly hair. However, it’s important to follow the right steps and use sharp scissors to prevent split ends. If you’re unsure about cutting your own hair, consult with a professional stylist.

How often should I cut my curly hair?

It’s recommended that you cut your curly hair every 3-4 months to prevent split ends and promote healthy growth.

What’s the best way to style curly hair for more volume?

Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to enhance your natural curl pattern and create more volume. You can also use styling products like mousse or curl cream to add volume and definition.

Hair Care Tips for Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips for taking care of curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to prevent dryness
  • Deep condition once a week to keep hair hydrated
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as they can damage hair
  • Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz
  • Don’t brush curly hair, as it can disrupt the natural curl pattern

By following these tips and using the right techniques, you can achieve more volume in your curly hair. Remember to be patient and take your time when cutting your hair, and consult with a professional stylist if you’re unsure about the process.
