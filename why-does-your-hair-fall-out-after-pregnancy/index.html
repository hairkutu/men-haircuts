Why Does Your Hair Fall Out After Pregnancy

Written by Luffy Apr 28, 2023 Â· 8 min read

After pregnancy, many new mothers experience hair fall out. This can be a stressful and worrying time, especially if you don't understand why it's happening. In this article, we'll take a look at the reasons why your hair may be falling out after pregnancy and what you can do to manage it.

Table of Contents:

  • Nutrition

  • How to Manage Hair Fall Out

Hormonal Changes

During pregnancy, your hormones are in overdrive. This can lead to thicker, fuller hair. However, after giving birth, your hormone levels decrease rapidly. This can cause your hair to enter a resting phase, which leads to shedding. This shedding usually occurs around 3 months postpartum and can last up to a year.

Additionally, if you breastfeed, your hormone levels may stay elevated, which can delay hair fall out. However, once you stop breastfeeding, you may experience hair fall out as your hormone levels decrease.

Stress

Giving birth and caring for a newborn can be stressful. Stress can cause hair fall out by pushing hair follicles into a resting phase. This type of hair fall out usually occurs around 3 months after a stressful event.

Nutrition

Pregnancy and breastfeeding can take a toll on your body, especially if you're not getting the proper nutrition. A lack of vitamins and minerals, such as iron and biotin, can lead to hair fall out.

Postpartum Thyroiditis

Postpartum thyroiditis is a condition that affects some women after giving birth. It occurs when the immune system attacks the thyroid gland, which can lead to an overactive or underactive thyroid. Both of these conditions can cause hair fall out.

How to Manage Hair Fall Out

If you're experiencing hair fall out after pregnancy, there are several things you can do to manage it:

1. Be Gentle

Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, as this can cause more hair fall out. Use a wide-toothed comb and avoid tight hairstyles, such as braids and ponytails.

2. Use Gentle Hair Products

Use gentle shampoos and conditioners that are free from harsh chemicals, such as sulfates and parabens. These chemicals can strip your hair of its natural oils, which can lead to hair fall out.

3. Eat a Balanced Diet

Eat a diet that's rich in vitamins and minerals, such as iron, biotin, and vitamin D. These nutrients can help promote healthy hair growth.

Step by Step Tips

Here are some step by step tips for managing hair fall out:

1. Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner that's free from harsh chemicals. Rinse your hair well to remove any product buildup.

2. Step 2: Detangle Your Hair

Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair. Start at the ends and work your way up to the roots.

3. Step 3: Dry Your Hair

Pat your hair dry with a soft towel. Avoid rubbing your hair vigorously, as this can cause more hair fall out.

4. Step 4: Style Your Hair

Avoid using heat tools, such as straighteners and curling irons, as these can damage your hair. Instead, opt for gentle styling methods, such as air-drying or using rollers.

FAQ

Q: How long does hair fall out last after pregnancy?

A: Hair fall out usually occurs around 3 months postpartum and can last up to a year.

Q: Does breastfeeding cause hair fall out?

A: Breastfeeding may delay hair fall out, but once you stop breastfeeding, you may experience hair fall out as your hormone levels decrease.

Q: Can stress cause hair fall out?

A: Yes, stress can cause hair fall out by pushing hair follicles into a resting phase. This type of hair fall out usually occurs around 3 months after a stressful event.

Hair Care

Here are some tips for taking care of your hair:

1. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trimming your hair regularly can help prevent split ends, which can lead to hair fall out.

2. Avoid Harsh Chemicals

Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as bleach and hair dye. These chemicals can weaken your hair and cause it to fall out.

3. Protect Your Hair

Protect your hair from the sun, wind, and cold by wearing a hat or scarf.

In conclusion, hair fall out after pregnancy is a common occurrence that can be managed with the right care and attention. By being gentle with your hair, using gentle hair products, eating a balanced diet, and managing stress, you can help promote healthy hair growth and prevent further hair fall out.
