How To Thin Out Layered Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine May 17, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Layered hair can be a great way to add volume and texture to your hair, but sometimes it can become too thick and overwhelming. If you're looking for a way to thin out your layered hair, you've come to the right place. In this step-by-step guide, we'll show you how to thin out layered hair in a few easy steps.

How to Thin Out Layered Hair

Thinning out layered hair is a simple process that you can do at home with some basic tools. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and drying it completely. You want to work with clean, dry hair so that you can see the layers clearly.

Step 2: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Using your comb, divide your hair into sections. If you have long hair, you may want to divide it into four sections. If you have shorter hair, you may only need two sections. Use your hair clips to secure each section.

Step 3: Thin Out the Bottom Layer

Start with the bottom layer of your hair. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Use your scissors to cut vertically into the hair, making small snips. This will help to thin out the hair without changing the overall length.

Step 4: Move on to the Middle Layer

Once you've thinned out the bottom layer, move on to the middle layer. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Again, use your scissors to make small vertical snips. Be sure to blend the middle layer with the bottom layer for a natural look.

Step 5: Thin Out the Top Layer

Finally, move on to the top layer of your hair. Take small sections of hair and use your scissors to make vertical snips. Be sure to blend the top layer with the middle and bottom layers for a natural look.

Step 6: Check Your Progress

Once you've thinned out all of your layers, check your progress in the mirror. If you still feel like your hair is too thick, you can repeat the process on any sections that need more thinning.

Tips for Thinning Out Layered Hair

Thinning out layered hair is a simple process, but there are a few tips to keep in mind:

  • Always use sharp hair scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Start with small snips and work your way up. You can always thin out more, but you can't put hair back once it's cut.
  • Be patient and take your time. Rushing the process can lead to mistakes and uneven layers.

FAQs

Can I thin out my layered hair if I have curly hair?

Yes! Thinning out layered hair works for all hair types, including curly hair. Just be sure to cut the hair while it's dry so that you can see the layers clearly.

Will thinning out my layered hair damage it?

No, thinning out your layered hair should not damage it if done correctly. Just be sure to use sharp hair scissors and make small snips to avoid uneven cuts.

Hair Care After Thinning Out Layered Hair

After thinning out your layered hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends.

Thinning out layered hair is a simple process that you can do at home with some basic tools. Follow these steps and tips to achieve a natural, voluminous look that you'll love!
