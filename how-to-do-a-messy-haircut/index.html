How To Do A Messy Haircut A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jun 12, 2023 · 9 min read

Messy haircuts have become increasingly popular in recent years, as they provide a relaxed and effortless look. However, achieving this look can be tricky, especially if you’re doing it yourself. In this article, we will guide you through the process of doing a messy haircut, including tips, FAQs, and hair care.

Table of Contents

  • How to Do a Messy Haircut
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for a Perfect Messy Haircut
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Messy Haircuts

How to Do a Messy Haircut

Messy haircuts are all about creating a natural look that appears effortless. This style works best for those with medium to long hair who want a low-maintenance look. Here are the basic steps:

1. Prepare Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair. Towel dry your hair until it’s damp, and then comb it through to remove any tangles. If you have curly hair, apply some curl-defining cream or mousse to help define your curls.

2. Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into small sections using hair clips. This will help you focus on one section at a time and ensure that you don’t miss any spots. Start with the back sections first, and work your way towards the front.

3. Use Scissors to Cut

Using hair-cutting scissors, start by trimming the ends of each section. Don’t worry about making it perfect; messy haircuts are all about uneven ends. Make sure you cut at an angle to create a natural-looking finish.

4. Texturize Your Hair

Now it’s time to create some texture. Use thinning shears or a razor comb to create layers and texture throughout your hair. This will add volume and movement to your hair, giving you that messy, effortless look.

5. Style Your Hair

Once you’ve finished cutting and texturizing your hair, it’s time to style it. Messy haircuts work best with a tousled, bedhead look. Apply some texturizing spray or dry shampoo to add volume and texture to your hair. Use your fingers to scrunch your hair and create a messy, natural look.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a more detailed step-by-step guide to help you achieve a perfect messy haircut:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and a nourishing conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated.

Step 2: Towel Dry Your Hair

After washing your hair, use a towel to gently dry your hair until it’s damp. Avoid rubbing your hair with the towel, as this can cause damage and frizz.

Step 3: Comb Your Hair

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair. This will make it easier to section your hair and cut it evenly.

Step 4: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into small sections using hair clips. Start with the back sections first and work your way towards the front.

Step 5: Cut the Ends

Using hair-cutting scissors, trim the ends of each section. Aim for an uneven, textured look, rather than a blunt cut.

Step 6: Texturize Your Hair

Use thinning shears or a razor comb to create layers and texture throughout your hair. This will add volume and movement to your hair, giving you that messy, effortless look.

Step 7: Style Your Hair

Apply some texturizing spray or dry shampoo to add volume and texture to your hair. Use your fingers to scrunch your hair and create a messy, natural look.

Tips for a Perfect Messy Haircut

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect messy haircut:

  • Use hair-cutting scissors instead of regular scissors to avoid split ends.
  • Don’t cut your hair too short; messy haircuts work best with medium to long hair.
  • Use thinning shears or a razor comb to create layers and texture throughout your hair.
  • Don’t worry about making it perfect; messy haircuts are all about uneven ends and a natural look.
  • Use texturizing spray or dry shampoo to add volume and texture to your hair.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about messy haircuts:

What hair types work best for messy haircuts?

Messy haircuts work best for those with medium to long hair who want a low-maintenance look. However, anyone can try this style, regardless of their hair type.

How often should I get a messy haircut?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly it loses its shape. As a general rule, aim to get a haircut every 6-12 weeks to maintain your style.

Can I do a messy haircut at home?

Yes, you can do a messy haircut at home. However, it’s important to use the right tools and techniques to avoid damaging your hair. Follow our step-by-step guide for best results.

Hair Care for Messy Haircuts

Messy haircuts are low-maintenance, but they still require some care to keep them looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Use a nourishing conditioner to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Use a texturizing spray or dry shampoo to add volume and texture to your hair.
  • Get a haircut every 6-12 weeks to maintain your style.

With these tips and our step-by-step guide, you can achieve the perfect messy haircut. Remember, messy haircuts are all about creating a natural, effortless look, so don’t worry about making it perfect. Good luck!
