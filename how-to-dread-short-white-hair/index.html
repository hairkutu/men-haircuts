How To Dread Short White Hair A Step By Step Guide

Are you tired of your short white hair looking dull and lifeless? Have you ever considered dreading your hair? Dreading your hair can give it a new, exciting look, and contrary to popular belief, you can dread even short, white hair. In this article, we'll show you how to dread short white hair in a relaxed, easy-to-understand language.

How to Dread Short White Hair

Dreading short white hair requires a bit of patience and expertise. However, with the right guidance, you can dread your hair in no time. Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let's take a look at some basic things you need to know before dreading your hair.

Firstly, dreading your hair can be a bit painful, especially if you're doing it for the first time. The tightness of the knots that are used to create the dreads can cause your scalp to be a bit sore. Secondly, dreads require regular maintenance to keep them looking neat and tidy. You'll need to wash and retwist them regularly to prevent them from unraveling.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to dread short white hair:

  1. Start by washing your hair thoroughly to remove any dirt or residue that may be present. Use a clarifying shampoo to ensure that your hair is squeaky clean.
  2. Section your hair into small sections, about the size of a pencil. Use rubber bands to secure each section in place, leaving about an inch of hair at the roots.
  3. Apply a dread wax or gel to each section, starting from the roots and working your way down to the tips. Make sure that each section is well coated with the wax or gel.
  4. Using a comb, start backcombing each section. Hold the section taut with one hand and use the comb to backcomb the hair towards the roots. Repeat this process until the section starts to form a knot-like shape.
  5. Once the section has been backcombed, roll it between your palms to help it lock in place. This will also help to smooth out any bumps or lumps that may be present.
  6. Repeat the above steps for each section of hair until your entire head is covered in dreads.
  7. Once all the sections have been dreadlocked, use a hairdryer to dry your hair thoroughly. This will help to set the dreads in place.
  8. Finally, use a crochet hook to tidy up any loose hairs or stray strands that may be present.

Tips for Dreading Short White Hair

Here are some tips to help you get the best results when dreading short white hair:

  • Use a good quality dread wax or gel to help your hair lock in place.
  • Make sure that your hair is well moisturized before you start the dreading process. Dry hair can be brittle and prone to breakage.
  • Be patient. Dreading your hair can take several hours depending on the length of your hair.
  • Consider seeking professional help if you're not confident in your ability to dread your hair.

FAQs About Dreading Short White Hair

Can I dread short white hair?

Yes, you can dread even short, white hair. However, the process may take longer than dreading longer hair.

How long does it take to dread short white hair?

The time it takes to dread short white hair depends on the length of your hair and the thickness of the sections. It can take anywhere from a few hours to a few days to dread short hair.

Do I need to use wax or gel to dread my hair?

No, you don't have to use wax or gel to dread your hair. However, using a dread product can help to hold the knots in place and prevent them from unraveling.

Hair Care for Dreaded Short White Hair

After dreading your hair, it's essential to take good care of it to keep it looking neat and tidy. Here are some tips to help you care for your dreaded short white hair:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo to prevent buildup.
  • Retwist your dreads every few weeks to keep them looking neat and tidy.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products on your dreads as they can cause buildup and attract dirt.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your dreads as they can cause the knots to unravel.

Dreading short white hair can be a fun and exciting way to switch up your look. With the above guide, you can dread your hair in the comfort of your home. Remember to take good care of your dreads to keep them looking healthy and neat.
