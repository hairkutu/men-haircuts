How To Style A Pixie Cut At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jul 17, 2023 · 8 min read

If you recently got a pixie cut, you may be wondering how to style it at home. While it can be intimidating to style such a short haircut, with the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a variety of looks from sleek and polished to tousled and edgy. In this guide, we’ll walk you through the steps to style a pixie cut at home, share some tips and tricks, and answer some frequently asked questions. Let’s get started!

How to Style a Pixie Cut at Home

Before you begin styling your pixie cut, make sure your hair is clean and dry. You’ll also want to have the right tools on hand, including a comb, a round brush, a blow dryer, and styling products like hairspray, gel, or pomade. Here’s how to style a pixie cut at home:

Step 1: Start with a clean, dry pixie cut

Ensure your hair is completely dry and free of any tangles or knots. Starting with clean, dry hair will make it easier to style and help your hairstyle stay in place longer.

Step 2: Use a comb or brush to part your hair

Decide where you want your part to be and use a comb or brush to create a clean part. You can part your hair on the side, in the middle, or go for an edgier look with an asymmetrical part.

Step 3: Blow dry your hair

Using a round brush and blow dryer, start at the back of your head and work your way forward, using the brush to lift and add volume to your hair. Blow dry your hair in the direction you want it to lay. For a sleek look, blow dry your hair straight down. For a tousled, messy look, blow dry your hair in different directions.

Step 4: Apply styling products

Once your hair is dry, apply your desired styling products. Hairspray, gel, and pomade are all great options for holding your style in place. Use a small amount of product and work it through your hair with your fingers, focusing on the ends and any areas that need extra hold.

Step 5: Finish with a final touch

Once you’ve applied your styling products, use your fingers to shape your hair into your desired style. You can slick your hair back, tousle it for a messy look, or add some texture with a few curls. Finish with a final touch of hairspray or other styling product to hold your look in place.

Tips for Styling a Pixie Cut at Home

Styling a pixie cut can take some practice, but with these tips and tricks, you’ll be a pro in no time:

  • Invest in quality styling products: Good hairspray, gel, or pomade can make all the difference in holding your style in place.
  • Experiment with different partings: Switching up your part can completely change the look of your pixie cut.
  • Use a round brush for volume: A round brush can help add volume and lift to your hair, which is especially important for shorter styles.
  • Don’t be afraid to try new styles: Pixie cuts are versatile and can be styled in many different ways, so don’t be afraid to experiment with new looks.

FAQ About Styling a Pixie Cut at Home

How often should I style my pixie cut?

This depends on your hair type and the style you’re going for. Some people may need to style their pixie cut daily, while others can go a few days between styles. Experiment with different routines to find what works best for you and your hair.

Can I use a curling iron on my pixie cut?

Yes! A curling iron can be a great tool for adding texture and volume to your pixie cut. Just be sure to use a heat protectant spray and work in small sections to avoid burning your scalp.

Do I need to trim my pixie cut regularly?

Yes. Pixie cuts require regular trims to maintain their shape and prevent split ends. Aim to get a trim every 4-6 weeks to keep your pixie looking fresh.

Caring for Your Pixie Cut

While styling your pixie cut is important, so is taking care of it. Here are some tips for keeping your pixie looking healthy and vibrant:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and moisturized.
  • Avoid using too many styling products, which can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray and avoiding excessive blow drying or styling.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends.

With these tips and tricks, you’ll be able to style your pixie cut at home with ease. Remember to experiment with different looks and have fun with your new haircut!
