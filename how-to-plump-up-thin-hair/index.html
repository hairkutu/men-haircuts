How To Plump Up Thin Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Robby Mar 25, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have thin hair, you may feel like you're stuck with limp locks that lack volume and bounce. But fear not, there are ways to plump up your hair and give it the body and fullness you desire. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks for achieving thicker, more voluminous hair.

How to Plump Up Thin Hair

First and foremost, it's important to understand that thin hair can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, age, and certain health conditions. While you can't change your genes, there are steps you can take to improve the appearance of your hair.

Step 1: Choose the Right Shampoo and Conditioner

When it comes to plumping up thin hair, it's important to start with a good foundation. That means choosing a shampoo and conditioner that are specifically formulated for fine or thin hair. Look for products that contain ingredients like biotin, keratin, and collagen, which can help strengthen and thicken hair strands.

Step 2: Use a Volumizing Mousse

After washing and conditioning your hair, apply a volumizing mousse to your roots. This will help lift your hair and create the illusion of thicker, fuller locks. Be sure to distribute the mousse evenly through your hair, using a comb or your fingers to work it in.

Step 3: Blow Dry Your Hair Upside Down

One of the easiest ways to add volume to thin hair is by blow drying it upside down. This will help lift your roots and create a fuller, more voluminous look. Use a round brush to smooth your hair as you dry it, and be sure to focus on your roots to maximize volume.

Step 4: Try a Root Lifter

If you're still not getting the volume you want, try a root lifter. This is a product that you apply directly to your roots to give them an extra boost. Look for a root lifter that contains ingredients like hydrolyzed wheat protein, which can help strengthen and thicken hair strands.

Tips for Plumping Up Thin Hair

Here are some additional tips and tricks for achieving thicker, more voluminous hair:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, as brushes can pull and damage hair strands.
  • Avoid using heavy hair products like oils and serums, as these can weigh down your hair and make it look flat.
  • If you have long hair, consider getting layers to add movement and body.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons too often, as these can damage and weaken hair strands.
  • Eat a healthy diet that's rich in protein, vitamins, and minerals to support healthy hair growth.

Hair Care FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about plumping up thin hair:

What causes thin hair?

Thin hair can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, age, and certain health conditions. Hormonal changes, stress, and poor nutrition can also contribute to thinning hair.

Can I use hair extensions to plump up thin hair?

Yes, hair extensions can be a great way to add volume and length to thin hair. However, it's important to choose high-quality extensions that won't damage your natural hair.

How often should I wash my hair if it's thin?

If you have thin hair, you may not need to wash it every day. In fact, washing too often can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it look even thinner. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days, and use dry shampoo in between washes to freshen up your roots.

The Bottom Line

While you may not be able to change your genetics, there are steps you can take to achieve thicker, more voluminous hair. By choosing the right products, blow drying your hair upside down, and using a root lifter, you can plump up your thin hair and enjoy a fuller, more confident look.
