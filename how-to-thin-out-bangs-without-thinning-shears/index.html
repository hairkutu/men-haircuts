How To Thin Out Bangs Without Thinning Shears A Complete Guide

Written by Frank Jun 18, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Do you have bangs that are too thick and heavy for your liking? Thinning out your bangs can make a significant difference in the way your hair looks and feels. However, not everyone has access to thinning shears or wants to use them. Fortunately, there are other ways to thin out your bangs without thinning shears. In this article, we will show you how to thin out bangs without thinning shears.

How to Thin Out Bangs Without Thinning Shears

Here are some ways to thin out your bangs without using thinning shears:

1. Use a Razor

One of the easiest ways to thin out your bangs without thinning shears is to use a razor. A razor can help you create a softer, more textured look by removing some of the bulk from your hair. To use a razor, follow these steps:

Step 1: Comb your bangs forward and hold them between your fingers.

Step 2: Use a razor to cut small sections of hair at a time, starting from the middle of your bangs and working your way outwards.

Step 3: Be sure to use a sharp razor and work slowly to avoid cutting too much hair.

Step 4: Finish by combing your bangs to see the results.

2. Twist and Cut

Another way to thin out your bangs is to twist and cut them. This technique involves twisting small sections of hair and cutting them diagonally to create a more textured look. Here's how to do it:

Step 1: Comb your bangs forward and separate them into small sections.

Step 2: Twist each section of hair tightly.

Step 3: Use a pair of scissors to cut the twisted section diagonally.

Step 4: Repeat this process for each section of hair until you achieve the desired look.

3. Use Thinning Scissors

If you don't have thinning shears, you can try using thinning scissors instead. Thinning scissors have teeth that help to remove bulk from your hair without cutting it all off. Here's how to use them:

Step 1: Comb your bangs forward and hold them between your fingers.

Step 2: Use thinning scissors to cut small sections of hair at a time, starting from the middle of your bangs and working your way outwards.

Step 3: Be sure to work slowly and use a comb to blend the hair as you go.

Step 4: Finish by combing your bangs to see the results.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step by step guide on how to thin out bangs without thinning shears:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. This will make it easier to work with and ensure that your bangs are clean and free of any product buildup.

Step 2: Comb Your Bangs Forward

Comb your bangs forward and hold them between your fingers. This will help you see exactly how much hair you need to thin out.

Step 3: Use a Razor, Thinning Scissors or Twist and Cut

Choose a technique that you feel comfortable with and start thinning out your bangs. Be sure to work slowly and take your time to avoid cutting too much hair.

Step 4: Check the Results

Once you've finished thinning out your bangs, comb them to see the results. If you're happy with the way they look, you're done! If not, you can always go back and thin out more hair.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you thin out your bangs without thinning shears:

  • Use a sharp razor or scissors to avoid damaging your hair.
  • Work slowly and carefully to avoid cutting too much hair.
  • Use a comb to blend the hair as you go to create a more natural look.
  • Start with small sections of hair and thin out more as needed.
  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different techniques until you find one that works best for you.

FAQ

Can I use regular scissors to thin out my bangs?

Yes, you can use regular scissors to thin out your bangs. However, it's important to use a sharp pair of scissors and work slowly to avoid cutting too much hair.

How much hair should I thin out?

The amount of hair you should thin out will depend on your personal preference and the thickness of your hair. Start with small sections of hair and thin out more as needed until you achieve the desired look.

Will thinning out my bangs damage my hair?

Thinning out your bangs should not damage your hair as long as you use a sharp razor or scissors and work slowly. However, if you have damaged or fragile hair, it's best to consult with a professional stylist before attempting to thin out your bangs.

Hair Care

To keep your bangs looking their best, it's important to take good care of your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Use a heat protectant before using any hot styling tools.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking neat and healthy.
  • Eat a healthy diet and stay hydrated to promote healthy hair growth.

With these tips and techniques, you can easily thin out your bangs without thinning shears. Remember to work slowly and carefully, and don't be afraid to experiment with different techniques until you find one that works best for you. Happy styling!
