How To Cut Semi Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Jul 07, 2023 · 10 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune at the hair salon every time you need a trim or a new style? Cutting your own hair might seem daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be a fun and cost-effective way to maintain your semi curly locks. In this article, we’ll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut semi curly hair, along with tips, FAQs, and hair care advice.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You’ll Need
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Cutting Semi Curly Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips for Semi Curly Hair

How to Cut Semi Curly Hair

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let’s review the basics of cutting semi curly hair. Semi curly hair falls somewhere between straight and curly, and can have a variety of textures, thicknesses, and lengths. The key to a successful haircut is to work with the natural texture and shape of your hair, rather than trying to force it into a specific style.

When cutting semi curly hair, it’s important to use the right techniques, tools, and products. We’ll cover all of these in detail in the following sections, but here’s a quick overview:

  • Use sharp scissors or shears
  • Start with dry, detangled hair
  • Section your hair carefully
  • Trim small amounts at a time
  • Work with the natural shape of your hair
  • Use styling products to enhance your natural curls

Tools You’ll Need

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the right tools on hand. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Sharp scissors or shears
  • Wide-tooth comb
  • Clips or hair ties to section your hair
  • Optional: electric clippers or trimmers for shorter styles

It’s important to use sharp scissors or shears, as dull blades can damage your hair and create split ends. You can find hair cutting scissors at most beauty supply stores, or order them online. A wide-tooth comb will help you detangle your hair and separate it into sections, while clips or hair ties will keep your hair organized and out of the way.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now, let’s get into the nitty-gritty of cutting semi curly hair. Follow these steps for a successful DIY haircut:

  1. Start with dry, detangled hair. Brush or comb your hair thoroughly to remove any knots or tangles. Semi curly hair tends to shrink up when it’s wet, so cutting it while dry will give you a more accurate length.
  2. Section your hair carefully. Use clips or hair ties to divide your hair into four or more sections, depending on the thickness and length of your hair. Make sure each section is even and secure.
  3. Start with the back. Choose one of the back sections and comb it straight down. Hold the ends of your hair between your fingers, and trim off any split ends or damaged sections. Work your way up the section, trimming small amounts at a time. Repeat on the other back section.
  4. Move to the sides. Release one of the side sections and comb it straight down. Hold the ends of your hair between your fingers, and trim off any split ends or damaged sections. Work your way up the section, trimming small amounts at a time. Repeat on the other side section.
  5. Trim the top. Release the top section of your hair and comb it straight up. Hold the ends of your hair between your fingers, and trim off any split ends or damaged sections. Work your way across the top of your head, trimming small amounts at a time.
  6. Blend the layers. Once you’ve trimmed each section, use your scissors or shears to blend the layers together. Comb each section down and cut into the hair at a slight angle, creating a soft, feathered effect.
  7. Check for balance. Stand in front of a mirror and check for any uneven sections or choppy spots. Make small adjustments as needed.
  8. Style your hair. Once you’re happy with your new haircut, style your hair as desired. Semi curly hair looks best when it’s allowed to air dry, but you can also use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to enhance your natural curls.

Tips for Cutting Semi Curly Hair

Here are a few extra tips to keep in mind when cutting semi curly hair:

  • Trim small amounts at a time, rather than trying to cut off a lot of hair at once
  • Work with your hair’s natural shape and texture
  • If you’re cutting shorter hair, use electric clippers or trimmers to create a clean, even cut
  • Don’t be afraid to ask for help from a friend or family member
  • Invest in high-quality hair care products, such as a good leave-in conditioner or curl-enhancing cream

FAQs

Here are some common questions about cutting semi curly hair:

Q: How often should I cut my semi curly hair?

A: It depends on your hair type and growth rate, but most people need a trim every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends and maintain their style.

Q: Can I cut my semi curly hair while it’s wet?

A: We recommend cutting semi curly hair while it’s dry, as wet hair tends to shrink up and can result in a shorter cut than you intended.

Q: How can I create layers in my semi curly hair?

A: To create layers, simply cut into the hair at a slight angle, rather than trimming straight across. This will create a softer, more textured effect.

Hair Care Tips for Semi Curly Hair

Finally, here are some tips for keeping your semi curly hair healthy and beautiful:

  • Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo, as sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish your hair and prevent breakage
  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible, or use a heat protectant spray before using them
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protection spray
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and maintain your style

With these tips and techniques, you’ll be able to cut your semi curly hair with confidence and ease. Remember to take your time, work with your hair’s natural texture, and have fun experimenting with different styles!
