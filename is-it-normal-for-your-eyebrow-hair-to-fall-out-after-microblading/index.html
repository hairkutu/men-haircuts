Is It Normal For Your Eyebrow Hair To Fall Out After Microblading

Written by Robby Sep 11, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Microblading is a popular cosmetic procedure that involves tattooing small, hair-like strokes onto the eyebrows to create a fuller and more defined look. While microblading can provide long-lasting results, some people may experience eyebrow hair loss after the procedure. In this article, we will explore whether it is normal for your eyebrow hair to fall out after microblading and provide tips on how to care for your eyebrows post-treatment.

Table of Content

  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

After microblading, it is normal for some eyebrow hairs to fall out. This is because the microblading process involves creating small incisions in the skin to implant pigment. These incisions can damage existing eyebrow hairs, causing them to fall out. Additionally, the healing process can also cause some hairs to shed. However, this hair loss should be temporary, and new hairs should grow back in their place.

How to

If you experience eyebrow hair loss after microblading, there are several things you can do to promote hair growth and prevent further hair loss. Here are some tips:

1. Avoid Touching Your Eyebrows

Touching your eyebrows can transfer bacteria and oils from your hands to your skin, which can cause infections and irritation. Additionally, touching your eyebrows can cause the pigment to fade, leading to patchy and uneven results.

2. Keep Your Eyebrows Clean

Cleanse your eyebrows twice a day with a gentle cleanser to remove dirt, oil, and makeup. Avoid using harsh exfoliants or scrubs, as these can irritate the skin and cause further hair loss.

3. Apply a Soothing Balm

After cleansing your eyebrows, apply a soothing balm to the area to promote healing and prevent infection. Look for a balm that contains natural ingredients like aloe vera, chamomile, and calendula.

Step by step

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to care for your eyebrows after microblading:

Step 1: Avoid Touching Your Eyebrows

Keep your hands away from your eyebrows to prevent bacteria and oils from transferring to your skin.

Step 2: Cleanse Your Eyebrows Twice a Day

Use a gentle cleanser to remove dirt, oil, and makeup from your eyebrows.

Step 3: Apply a Soothing Balm

After cleansing, apply a soothing balm to your eyebrows to promote healing and prevent infection.

Step 4: Avoid Excessive Sweating

Avoid strenuous exercise and excessive sweating for at least a week after microblading to prevent the pigment from fading or spreading.

Step 5: Avoid Sun Exposure

Avoid direct sunlight and tanning beds for at least two weeks after microblading to prevent the pigment from fading.

Tips

Here are some additional tips on how to care for your eyebrows after microblading:

1. Avoid Swimming

Avoid swimming or soaking in water for at least a week after microblading to prevent infection.

2. Don't Pick at Your Eyebrows

Avoid picking or scratching at your eyebrows, as this can cause the pigment to fade and lead to scarring.

3. Be Patient

It can take several weeks for your eyebrows to fully heal and for the pigment to settle. Be patient and avoid touching or picking at your eyebrows during this time.

FAQ

Q: Is it normal for my eyebrows to scab after microblading?

A: Yes, it is normal for your eyebrows to scab after microblading. This is because the healing process involves the formation of scabs, which protect the skin and promote healing. However, it is important not to pick at the scabs, as this can cause the pigment to fade or lead to scarring.

Q: How long does it take for my eyebrows to heal after microblading?

A: It can take up to four weeks for your eyebrows to fully heal after microblading. During this time, you may experience some itching, flaking, and scabbing. It is important to follow the aftercare instructions provided by your microblading technician to ensure proper healing.

Hair Care

To promote hair growth and prevent further hair loss, consider incorporating these hair care tips into your routine:

1. Eat a Healthy Diet

Eating a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals can help promote hair growth and prevent hair loss. Focus on eating foods like leafy greens, fruits, nuts, and lean proteins.

2. Take Supplements

Consider taking supplements like biotin, vitamin D, and iron to promote hair growth and prevent hair loss.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid using heat styling tools like blow dryers, flat irons, and curling irons, as these can damage hair and lead to hair loss.

In conclusion, it is normal for your eyebrow hair to fall out after microblading. However, with proper care and patience, your eyebrows should heal and grow back in their place. Follow the aftercare instructions provided by your microblading technician, and consider incorporating hair care tips into your routine to promote hair growth and prevent further hair loss.
