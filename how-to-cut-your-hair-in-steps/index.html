How To Cut Your Hair In Steps A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Aug 27, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune on haircuts? Do you want to learn how to cut your hair in steps and save some money? Well, you've come to the right place. In this article, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to cut your hair at home. We'll also give you some tips and tricks to help you achieve the perfect cut. Let's get started!

How to Cut Your Hair in Steps

Before we start, you'll need a few tools to get the job done. Here's what you'll need:

  • Sharp scissors or hair cutting shears
  • Comb or brush
  • Hair clips or ties
  • Mirror
  • Spray bottle

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair and towel-drying it. Damp hair is easier to cut, and it'll give you a more accurate result.

Step 2: Comb and Section Your Hair

Comb your hair to remove any tangles and then section it into four parts. Use hair clips or ties to secure each section in place.

Step 3: Start with the Bottom

Start with the bottom section of your hair. Take a small section, comb it straight, and cut it to your desired length. Repeat this process for the entire section.

Step 4: Move to the Sides

After you finish the bottom section, move to the sides. Take a small section, comb it straight, and cut it to the same length as the bottom section. Repeat this process for both sides.

Step 5: Cut the Top Section

Finally, cut the top section of your hair. This part can be a bit tricky, so take your time. Comb the hair straight up and cut it to your desired length. Make sure to blend the top section with the sides and the bottom section.

Step 6: Check Your Hair

After you finish cutting your hair, check it in the mirror. Make any necessary adjustments to ensure that your hair is even and straight.

Step 7: Style Your Hair

Style your hair as desired. You can blow-dry it, straighten it, or curl it. Get creative!

Tips for Cutting Your Hair at Home

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect cut:

  • Invest in a good pair of hair cutting shears. Don't use regular scissors.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet. Wet hair shrinks when it dries, so you might end up cutting more than you intended.
  • Use a spray bottle to dampen your hair if it starts to dry out during the cutting process.
  • Take your time. Cutting your hair can be a time-consuming process, but it's worth it in the end.

FAQ About Cutting Your Hair at Home

Can I cut my hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can cut your curly hair at home. However, keep in mind that curly hair tends to bounce up when it dries, so cut less than you think you need to.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Can I cut my own bangs?

Yes, you can cut your own bangs. However, be careful not to cut them too short, as they tend to look shorter when they dry.

Hair Care After Cutting Your Hair

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its health and shine. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using hot tools such as blow dryers, curling irons, and straighteners frequently to prevent heat damage.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Use a hair mask or oil treatment once a week to nourish your hair.

Conclusion

Learning how to cut your hair in steps can save you time and money. With the right tools and techniques, you can achieve a salon-worthy cut at home. Remember to take your time, be patient, and follow our step-by-step guide. Happy cutting!
