How To Take Care Of Wavy Hair For Beginners A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Aprilia Jun 01, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Wavy hair can be a blessing and a curse. It can look effortlessly beautiful one day and frizzy the next. If you're new to having wavy hair, you might not know where to start with taking care of it. In this guide, we'll break down everything you need to know about how to take care of wavy hair for beginners.

Table of Contents

  • How to Take Care of Wavy Hair for Beginners
  • Step by Step
  • Tips

  • Hair Care Products for Wavy Hair

Description

Wavy hair is characterized by loose, flowing waves that can range from subtle to dramatic. It's a hair type that's often envied for its natural texture, but it can also be challenging to manage without the right care. Wavy hair tends to be more prone to frizz, dryness, and tangles than straight hair, so it requires a little extra TLC.

How to Take Care of Wavy Hair for Beginners

If you're new to having wavy hair, here are some tips to help you get started:

1. Use a gentle shampoo

Wavy hair can be prone to dryness, so it's important to use a gentle, moisturizing shampoo that won't strip your hair of its natural oils. Look for shampoos that are sulfate-free and contain ingredients like coconut oil or shea butter to help hydrate your hair.

2. Condition, condition, condition

Conditioning is crucial for wavy hair. It helps to detangle and soften your hair, making it easier to manage. Use a conditioner that's specifically designed for wavy hair, and focus on the mid-lengths and ends of your hair where it's most prone to dryness.

3. Don't skip the leave-in conditioner

A leave-in conditioner can work wonders for wavy hair. It helps to hydrate and define your curls, while also protecting your hair from heat damage. Apply a small amount of leave-in conditioner to damp hair before styling.

4. Use a diffuser

When blow-drying your hair, use a diffuser attachment to help enhance your waves. A diffuser helps to distribute heat evenly and prevent frizz, while also defining your curls.

5. Embrace air-drying

Air-drying is a great option for wavy hair. Simply scrunch your hair with a towel to remove excess water, apply your styling products, and let your hair dry naturally. This can help to enhance your waves and prevent frizz.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide to taking care of wavy hair:

1. Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo.

Massage the shampoo into your scalp and hair, and rinse thoroughly.

2. Apply conditioner.

Focus on the mid-lengths and ends of your hair, and leave the conditioner in for a few minutes before rinsing.

3. Apply leave-in conditioner.

Apply a small amount of leave-in conditioner to damp hair, focusing on the ends.

4. Style your hair.

Use a diffuser attachment when blow-drying your hair, or let it air-dry naturally.

5. Finish with a styling product.

Apply a styling product that's designed for wavy hair, like a curl cream or mousse, to enhance your waves.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for taking care of wavy hair:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair instead of a brush, which can cause frizz.
  • Avoid using hot tools like straighteners or curling irons too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • If you have naturally oily hair, focus on conditioning just the ends of your hair to avoid weighing it down.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help nourish your hair.

FAQ

What should I do if my wavy hair is frizzy?

If your wavy hair is prone to frizz, try using a smoothing serum or oil to help tame flyaways. You can also try using a microfiber towel to dry your hair, as it's gentler on your hair and can help prevent frizz.

How often should I wash my wavy hair?

It depends on your hair type and how prone it is to dryness. Some people with wavy hair can go several days between washes, while others may need to wash their hair every other day. Experiment with different washing schedules to find what works best for your hair.

Can I still straighten my wavy hair?

Yes, you can still straighten your wavy hair if you want to. Just be sure to use a heat protectant spray and not to straighten it too often to avoid damaging your hair.
