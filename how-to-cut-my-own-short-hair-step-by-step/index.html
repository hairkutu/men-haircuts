How To Cut My Own Short Hair Step By Step

Written by Luffy May 26, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're looking to save some money and take matters into your own hands, cutting your own short hair is a great way to start. With the right tools, technique, and a little bit of patience, you can achieve salon-worthy results from the comfort of your own home. Follow these steps to learn how to cut your own short hair step by step.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Preparation
  • Sectioning Your Hair
  • Cutting Your Hair
  • Texturizing Your Hair
  • Trimming Your Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care Tips

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the right tools. You'll need:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Clippers (optional)
  • Hair clips or elastics
  • Handheld mirror or mirror on a stand

Preparation

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure it's clean and dry. Wet hair can stretch and appear longer, so cutting it while it's wet can result in a shorter cut than you intended. Also, make sure you're wearing clothing that you don't mind getting hair on.

Sectioning Your Hair

Sectioning your hair is important to ensure an even and precise cut. Use a comb to create a middle part, then divide your hair into four equal sections: two in the front and two in the back. Secure each section with a clip or elastic.

Cutting Your Hair

Start with the front sections of your hair. Take a small section of hair from one side and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, then cut off the desired length. Repeat on the other side, making sure to cut evenly.

Move on to the back sections of your hair. Take a small section of hair from one side and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, then cut off the desired length. Repeat on the other side, making sure to cut evenly. Use a handheld mirror or mirror on a stand to check the back of your head.

Texturizing Your Hair

If you want to add texture to your hair, use scissors to cut into the ends of your hair at a 45-degree angle. This will create a choppy, textured look.

Trimming Your Hair

To trim your hair, comb it straight down and snip off any split ends. Work your way around your head, making sure to trim evenly.

FAQs

How often should I cut my short hair?

It's recommended to get a trim every 4-6 weeks to maintain your short hairstyle.

Can I cut my hair if it's curly?

Cutting curly hair can be a bit trickier, so it's best to leave it to a professional stylist.

What should I do if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

If you make a mistake, try not to panic. You can always try to fix it or wear your hair in a different style until it grows out. If it's a major mistake, consider going to a professional stylist to fix it.

Hair Care Tips

After cutting your hair, be sure to take care of it properly. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using heat styling tools too frequently. Consider getting a deep conditioning treatment every few weeks to keep your hair healthy and shiny.

Cutting your own short hair can be a bit intimidating, but with the right tools and technique, you can achieve great results. Remember to take your time, be patient, and don't be afraid to ask for help if you need it. Good luck!
