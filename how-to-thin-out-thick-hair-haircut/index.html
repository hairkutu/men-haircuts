How To Thin Out Thick Hair Haircut A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Aug 21, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Thick hair can be both a blessing and a curse. While it's great for adding volume and body to your hair, it can also be difficult to manage and style. If you're tired of dealing with thick hair, one solution is to get a haircut that thins it out. In this post, we'll show you how to thin out thick hair haircut, step by step.

How to Thin Out Thick Hair Haircut

Description

Thinning out your hair involves removing some of the bulk to make it more manageable. This can be done in a variety of ways, including using thinning shears, razor cutting, or texturizing scissors. The technique you choose will depend on your hair type and the look you're going for.

How to

Here's how to thin out thick hair haircut:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. This will help to remove any product buildup and make it easier to work with.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four parts, using clips or hair ties to keep them separate. This will make it easier to work with each section individually.

Step 3: Choose Your Technique

Choose the technique you want to use to thin out your hair. Thinning shears are a popular choice, as they can remove bulk without changing the length of your hair. Razor cutting can also be effective, but it's important to use a sharp razor and be careful not to overdo it. Texturizing scissors can create a softer, more natural look.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Start cutting your hair, working with one section at a time. Use your chosen technique to remove some of the bulk from each section, being careful not to remove too much. Remember that you can always remove more later, but you can't put it back!

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Once you've thinned out all four sections, blend the layers together using your scissors. This will create a more natural look and prevent any harsh lines.

Step 6: Style Your Hair

Style your hair as desired, using a styling product to add texture and hold. You may find that your hair is easier to manage and style now that it's been thinned out.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you thin out your hair successfully:

  • Start with small sections and work your way up to larger ones.
  • Be conservative with the amount of hair you remove.
  • Don't try to thin out your hair when it's wet, as it will look much shorter when it dries.
  • Consider going to a professional stylist for best results.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about thinning out hair:

Q: Will thinning out my hair make it look thinner?

A: Thinning out your hair can make it look less bulky and more manageable, but it won't necessarily make it look thinner. If you want to add volume to your hair, try using a volumizing shampoo or mousse instead.

Q: How often should I thin out my hair?

A: This will depend on your hair type and how quickly it grows, but most people can go 6-8 weeks between haircuts. If you find that your hair becomes difficult to manage before then, you may need to thin it out more frequently.

Hair Care

Thinning out your hair can be a great way to make it more manageable, but it's important to take care of it properly to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips for caring for your hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that's designed for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish your hair and keep it healthy.
  • Get regular haircuts to keep your hair looking its best.

Thinning out thick hair can be a great way to make it more manageable and easy to style. By following these steps and taking proper care of your hair, you can achieve a look that you love.
