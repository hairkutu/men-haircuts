What Causes Excessive Moulting In Dogs

Written by Robby Aug 13, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Description | How to | Step by step | Tips | FAQ | Hair Care

Description

Excessive moulting can be a real problem for dog owners, especially those who suffer from allergies. It can also be a sign of an underlying health issue. In this post, we will take a look at some of the causes of excessive moulting in dogs and what you can do to help reduce the amount of hair your dog sheds.

How to

If you are wondering what causes excessive moulting in dogs, there are several factors that can contribute to the problem. Some of the most common causes include:

  • Poor nutrition
  • Stress and anxiety
  • Hormonal imbalances
  • Allergies
  • Skin infections
  • Fleas and ticks

Poor Nutrition

Dogs that are not getting the right nutrients from their food can experience excessive moulting. Make sure your dog is getting a balanced diet that includes all the necessary vitamins and minerals.

Stress and Anxiety

Stress and anxiety can cause excessive moulting in dogs. If your dog is experiencing stress or anxiety, try to identify the cause and address it. You may want to consider consulting with a veterinarian or a professional dog trainer.

Hormonal Imbalances

Hormonal imbalances can also contribute to excessive moulting in dogs. If you suspect that your dog has a hormonal imbalance, consult with your veterinarian for testing and treatment options.

Allergies

Allergies can cause excessive moulting in dogs. If your dog is allergic to something in their environment or their food, it can cause them to shed more than usual. Consult with your veterinarian for testing and treatment options.

Skin Infections

Some dogs may experience excessive moulting due to skin infections. If your dog has a skin infection, it is important to treat it promptly to reduce the amount of hair they shed.

Fleas and Ticks

Fleas and ticks can cause excessive moulting in dogs. Make sure your dog is protected from these parasites with regular flea and tick prevention.

Step by step

If you want to reduce the amount of hair your dog sheds, here are some steps you can take:

  • Ensure your dog is getting a balanced diet
  • Reduce your dog's stress and anxiety
  • Treat any hormonal imbalances
  • Treat any allergies
  • Treat any skin infections
  • Protect your dog from fleas and ticks

Tips

Here are some additional tips to help reduce the amount of hair your dog sheds:

  • Regular grooming can help remove loose hair
  • Use a shedding blade or brush to help remove excess hair
  • Regular baths can help remove loose hair and reduce shedding
  • Use a high-quality dog shampoo and conditioner
  • Consider using a deshedding tool to help reduce shedding

FAQ

Q: How much shedding is normal for a dog?

A: Shedding is normal for all dogs. However, the amount of shedding can vary depending on the breed and individual dog.

Q: Can excessive moulting be a sign of an underlying health issue?

A: Yes, excessive moulting can be a sign of an underlying health issue. If you are concerned about the amount of hair your dog is shedding, consult with your veterinarian.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is important for reducing the amount of hair your dog sheds. Regular grooming, baths, and the use of quality hair care products can all help reduce shedding. Make sure to consult with your veterinarian for recommendations on the best hair care routine for your dog.
