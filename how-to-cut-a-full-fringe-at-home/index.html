How To Cut A Full Fringe At Home Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Jun 29, 2023 · 6 min read

Are you tired of your old hairstyle and want to switch it up? A full fringe might be the perfect solution for you! Cutting a full fringe at home can seem daunting, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done easily. In this article, we’ll walk you through the steps to cut a full fringe at home.

How to Cut a Full Fringe at Home

Before we dive into the steps, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Water spray bottle

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Wash and dry your hair thoroughly. Brush it out and separate the front section of your hair that you want to cut into a fringe. Use hair clips to keep the remaining hair out of the way.

Step 2: Determine the Length and Style

Decide on the length and style of your fringe. Do you want it to be straight across or have a side-swept look? Use a comb to section off the hair you want to cut and comb it straight down.

Step 3: Cut the Hair

Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, about an inch away from the desired length. Use sharp scissors to cut the hair straight across. If you want a side-swept look, cut the hair at a slight angle.

Step 4: Check the Length

Release the hair and check the length. If it’s too long, trim a little bit more, but be careful not to cut too much at once. You can always trim more later, but you can’t put hair back once it’s cut.

Step 5: Style Your Fringe

Use the comb to style your fringe the way you want it. If you want a straight fringe, comb it straight down. If you want a side-swept look, comb it to the side. Use a water spray bottle to dampen the hair and make it easier to style.

Tips for Cutting a Full Fringe at Home

  • Start with a longer length and gradually cut more if needed.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure a clean cut.
  • Don’t cut your fringe too short, as it will look uneven and take longer to grow out.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring and may cause strain on your arms.

FAQs

Q: How do I know if a full fringe will suit me?

A: The best way to determine if a full fringe will suit you is to try it out with a wig or hair extensions. If you’re happy with how it looks, go ahead and cut your hair.

Q: Can I cut my fringe when it’s wet?

A: It’s best to cut your fringe when it’s dry, as wet hair can appear longer than it actually is.

Hair Care After Cutting a Full Fringe

After cutting your full fringe, it’s important to take care of it to keep it looking its best. Trim it every few weeks to maintain the length, and use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep it healthy and shiny. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.

Now that you know how to cut a full fringe at home, you can experiment with different styles and lengths. Remember to take your time and be patient, and don’t be afraid to ask for help if you need it. Happy cutting!
