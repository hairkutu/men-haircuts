How To Cut Girl Hair At Home With Clippers A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel May 25, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're looking for a way to save time and money on haircuts for your daughter or yourself, cutting hair at home with clippers can be a great solution. With the right tools and techniques, you can create a professional-looking haircut in the comfort of your own home. In this guide, we'll cover everything you need to know about how to cut girl hair at home with clippers, including tips, tricks, and FAQs.

Table of Contents:

  2. Preparation
  3. Sectioning the Hair
  4. Cutting the Hair
  5. Blending the Hair
  6. Cleaning Up
  7. Finishing Touches
  8. Tips for Success
  9. FAQs
  10. Hair Care after Cutting

Tools You'll Need

Before you begin cutting hair with clippers, you'll need to gather a few essential tools:

  • Clippers with various guard sizes
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Water Spray Bottle
  • Hair clips or elastics
  • Towel or cape to protect clothing
  • Handheld mirror to check the back of the head

Make sure your clippers are sharp and well-maintained. If your blades are dull or dirty, they can pull or snag the hair, making it difficult to achieve a clean cut.

Preparation

Before you start cutting, make sure the hair is clean, dry, and free of tangles. Wet hair can be more difficult to cut, and tangles can create unevenness in the final result. It's also a good idea to have the person whose hair you're cutting wear a shirt that you don't mind getting hair on, or use a towel or cape to protect their clothing.

Sectioning the Hair

Next, you'll want to section the hair. This will make it easier to cut and ensure that you don't miss any areas. To do this, start by dividing the hair into four sections: top, sides, and back. Use hair clips or elastics to secure each section in place.

Cutting the Hair

Start with the longest guard size and work your way down to shorter sizes. Take small sections of hair and hold them between your fingers, pulling them away from the head at a 90-degree angle. Run the clippers over the hair, moving from the bottom up, in smooth, even strokes. Repeat this process with each section of hair, using shorter guard sizes as you move up the head.

Blending the Hair

Once you've cut the hair to the desired length, it's time to blend the sections together. Use scissors to trim any longer hairs that may be sticking out, and use the clippers without a guard to create a seamless transition between the different sections of hair.

Cleaning Up

After you've finished cutting the hair, use a comb to brush away any loose hairs. You can also use a handheld vacuum or a brush to clean up the area around you.

Finishing Touches

To finish the haircut, use scissors to trim any stray hairs around the ears and neck. You can also use the clippers to create a clean line around the edges of the hairline.

Tips for Success

  • Start with a longer guard size and work your way down to avoid cutting too much hair.
  • Use the comb to hold the hair in place and guide the clippers.
  • Take breaks if you or the person whose hair you're cutting becomes uncomfortable.
  • Don't rush the process. Take your time and check your work frequently.

FAQs

Can I cut my own hair with clippers?

Yes, you can cut your own hair with clippers, but it can be more challenging than cutting someone else's hair. You'll need to use a handheld mirror to check the back of your head and work carefully to avoid cutting too much hair.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't panic! You can always use scissors to trim any uneven areas or take a break and come back to it later.

How often should I cut my hair with clippers?

The frequency of haircuts will depend on the desired length and style, as well as the rate of hair growth. As a general rule, shorter hairstyles will need more frequent touch-ups than longer styles.

Hair Care after Cutting

After cutting the hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep the hair clean and moisturized, and avoid using heat styling tools too frequently. Regular trims every 4-6 weeks can help maintain the shape and length of the haircut.

With these tips and techniques, you can confidently cut girl hair at home with clippers. Remember to take your time and be patient, and don't be afraid to ask for help or advice from a professional stylist if you're unsure about anything.
