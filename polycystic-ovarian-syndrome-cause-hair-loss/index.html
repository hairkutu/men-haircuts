Polycystic Ovarian Syndrome Cause Hair Loss Everything You Need To Know

Written by Oliver Mar 15, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Polycystic ovarian syndrome (PCOS) is a hormonal disorder that affects many women. It occurs when the ovaries produce too much androgen (male hormones). This hormonal imbalance can lead to a range of symptoms, including irregular periods, weight gain, acne, and hair loss. In this article, we will focus on how PCOS can cause hair loss and what you can do about it.

What is PCOS?

PCOS is a condition that affects the ovaries and disrupts the normal menstrual cycle. Women with PCOS have multiple small cysts on their ovaries, which can cause the ovaries to produce too much androgen. This hormonal imbalance can lead to a range of symptoms, including:

  • Irregular periods or no periods at all
  • Weight gain or difficulty losing weight
  • Acne or oily skin
  • Hair loss or thinning hair

How Does PCOS Cause Hair Loss?

PCOS can cause hair loss in several ways. First, the excess androgen produced by the ovaries can lead to hair thinning or balding. Androgen-sensitive hair follicles on the scalp shrink and produce thinner, shorter hair. Over time, these follicles can stop producing hair altogether, leading to permanent baldness.

Second, high levels of insulin in women with PCOS can also contribute to hair loss. Insulin resistance is common in women with PCOS, which means their bodies have trouble processing insulin. This can lead to high levels of insulin in the blood, which can cause inflammation and damage to hair follicles.

How to Treat Hair Loss Caused by PCOS?

If you are experiencing hair loss or thinning hair due to PCOS, there are several things you can do to help promote hair growth and prevent further hair loss. Here are some tips:

1. Manage Your Hormonal Imbalance

One of the most effective ways to treat hair loss caused by PCOS is to manage your hormonal imbalance. This may involve taking birth control pills or other medications that can regulate your menstrual cycle and reduce androgen levels. Your doctor may also recommend anti-androgen drugs, which block the effects of androgen on the hair follicles.

2. Improve Your Diet

Eating a healthy diet can also help manage the symptoms of PCOS and promote hair growth. Focus on eating whole, nutrient-dense foods and avoiding processed foods and sugar. You may also want to consider taking supplements that can support healthy hair growth, such as biotin, zinc, and iron.

3. Reduce Inflammation

Inflammation can contribute to hair loss, so reducing inflammation in your body can help promote hair growth. This may involve taking anti-inflammatory supplements, such as fish oil or turmeric, or incorporating anti-inflammatory foods into your diet, such as leafy greens, berries, and fatty fish.

4. Practice Good Hair Care Habits

Finally, taking good care of your hair can help prevent further hair loss and promote healthy hair growth. Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair, and use gentle products that won't irritate your scalp. You may also want to consider using a hair growth serum or scalp treatment to stimulate hair growth.

FAQs

Can hair loss from PCOS be reversed?

Yes, hair loss caused by PCOS can be reversed with the right treatment. Managing your hormonal imbalance, improving your diet, and practicing good hair care habits can all help promote healthy hair growth.

What kind of doctor should I see for PCOS-related hair loss?

You should see a dermatologist or endocrinologist for PCOS-related hair loss. These specialists can help diagnose the cause of your hair loss and recommend appropriate treatment options.

Conclusion

PCOS can cause a range of symptoms, including hair loss or thinning hair. If you are experiencing hair loss due to PCOS, there are several things you can do to promote healthy hair growth and prevent further hair loss. Managing your hormonal imbalance, improving your diet, reducing inflammation, and practicing good hair care habits can all help promote healthy hair growth and restore your confidence.
