How To Be A Barber Uk A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Jun 17, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you interested in becoming a barber in the UK? If so, you’ve come to the right place. In this article, we’ll guide you through the process of becoming a qualified barber in the UK. We’ll cover everything from the qualifications you’ll need to the steps you’ll need to take to set up your own barber shop.

Description

Becoming a barber can be a rewarding career choice for those who are passionate about hair cutting and styling. Barbers provide grooming services such as haircuts, shaves, and beard trims to their clients. They also offer advice on hair care and styling products. In the UK, there is a growing demand for skilled barbers, so if you’re interested in this career path, now is a great time to get started.

How to Become a Barber UK

Step 1: Gain the Right Qualifications

The first step to becoming a barber in the UK is to gain the right qualifications. You will need to complete a Level 2 or Level 3 NVQ in Barbering. These courses cover a range of topics, from basic hair cutting and styling to more advanced techniques such as razor cutting and clipper work. You can find NVQ courses at local colleges or private training providers.

Step 2: Get Some Work Experience

Once you’ve gained your qualifications, it’s a good idea to get some work experience. This will help you to develop your skills and gain confidence in your abilities. You could look for work as an apprentice in a barber shop, or you could offer your services as a trainee barber to friends and family.

Step 3: Register with the National Hair & Beauty Federation

Once you’ve gained some work experience, you should consider registering with the National Hair & Beauty Federation (NHBF). This is the UK’s largest trade association for hairdressing, barbering and beauty salon owners. By joining the NHBF, you’ll gain access to a range of benefits, including legal advice, industry news and networking opportunities.

Step 4: Set Up Your Own Barber Shop

If you’re interested in setting up your own barber shop, there are a few things you’ll need to do. Firstly, you’ll need to find a suitable premises. This could be a shop on the high street or a unit in a shopping centre. You’ll also need to purchase equipment such as barber chairs, mirrors and hair cutting tools. Finally, you’ll need to market your business to attract customers.

Tips for Being a Successful Barber

Here are some tips to help you become a successful barber:

  • Be friendly and approachable with your clients
  • Listen carefully to your client’s needs and preferences
  • Keep up to date with the latest hair cutting and styling trends
  • Offer advice on hair care and styling products
  • Keep your barber shop clean and tidy

Frequently Asked Questions

What qualifications do I need to become a barber in the UK?

You will need to complete a Level 2 or Level 3 NVQ in Barbering.

How do I gain work experience as a barber?

You could look for work as an apprentice in a barber shop, or you could offer your services as a trainee barber to friends and family.

Do I need to register with any professional bodies?

You should consider registering with the National Hair & Beauty Federation (NHBF).

Hair Care Tips for Clients

Here are some hair care tips you can offer your clients:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a good quality shampoo
  • Use a conditioner to keep your hair soft and manageable
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, such as hair dryers and straighteners
  • Trim your hair regularly to keep it looking neat and tidy
  • Use hair styling products sparingly

Conclusion

Becoming a barber in the UK can be a rewarding and fulfilling career choice. By following the steps outlined in this article, you can gain the right qualifications, gain work experience, register with professional bodies and set up your own barber shop. By offering great customer service and hair care advice, you can build a loyal customer base and enjoy a successful career as a barber.
