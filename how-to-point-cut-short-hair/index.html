How To Point Cut Short Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Mar 31, 2023 · 6 min read

Are you tired of the same old short haircut with a blunt edge? Point cutting may be the solution you need. Point cutting is a technique that involves cutting the hair at an angle, creating a softer and more textured look. In this guide, we will show you how to point cut short hair in a relaxed and easy-to-understand language.

Description

Point cutting is a technique that creates a textured and natural-looking finish to haircuts. This technique involves taking small sections of hair and cutting into the ends at a 45-degree angle. Point cutting can be done on any hair type, but it is particularly effective on short haircuts. It can also be used to add movement and volume to longer hairstyles.

How to

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to point cut short hair:

Step 1: Prepare the Hair

Before you start point cutting, make sure the hair is clean and dry. Comb the hair to remove any tangles and divide it into small sections.

Step 2: Hold the Scissors Properly

Hold the scissors with your thumb and ring finger on the handles and your index and middle fingers on the blades. This grip will give you more control and precision when cutting.

Step 3: Take a Small Section of Hair

Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers. Make sure the hair is taut and has no bumps or kinks.

Step 4: Cut into the Hair

Using the point of the scissors, cut into the hair at a 45-degree angle. Start at the ends and work your way up, taking small snips.

Step 5: Blend the Sections

Repeat steps 3 and 4 on the next section of hair, making sure to blend the sections together.

Step 6: Check for Symmetry

After you have finished point cutting, check for symmetry. Make sure both sides of the hair are even and balanced.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you achieve the perfect point cut:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way up to larger sections. This will give you more control and precision.
  • Use sharp scissors to avoid damaging the hair.
  • Point cut in the direction of the hair growth for a more natural look.
  • Don't overdo it. Point cutting should create a soft and textured look, not a choppy or uneven finish.

FAQ

What is the difference between point cutting and blunt cutting?

Blunt cutting involves cutting the hair straight across, creating a sharp and defined edge. Point cutting involves cutting into the hair at an angle, creating a softer and more textured look.

Can point cutting be done on any hair type?

Yes, point cutting can be done on any hair type, but it is particularly effective on short haircuts.

Will point cutting damage my hair?

No, point cutting should not damage your hair as long as you use sharp scissors and don't overdo it.

Hair Care

After you have point cut your hair, make sure to take care of it properly. Use a shampoo and conditioner that is suitable for your hair type and avoid using heat styling tools too often. Trim your hair regularly to maintain the shape and texture of your point cut.

Point cutting short hair is a great way to add texture and movement to your haircut. With this step-by-step guide, you can achieve a soft and natural-looking finish to your hair. Remember to take care of your hair properly to maintain the shape and texture of your point cut.
