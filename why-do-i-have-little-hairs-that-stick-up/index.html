Why Do I Have Little Hairs That Stick Up

Written by Petter Aug 12, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Have you ever noticed those tiny, pesky hairs that seem to stand up and refuse to lay flat? These little hairs can be frustrating, especially when you're trying to achieve a smooth and sleek hairstyle. In this article, we'll discuss why these hairs exist, and what you can do to tame them.

Description

Little hairs that stick up are often referred to as "baby hairs." These hairs are usually shorter and finer than the rest of your hair, and they tend to grow around your hairline. Baby hairs can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, hair damage, and hormonal changes.

How to Tame Baby Hairs

If you're tired of dealing with unruly baby hairs, there are a few things you can do to tame them. Here are some tips:

1. Use a Toothbrush or Spoolie Brush

One of the easiest ways to tame baby hairs is to use a toothbrush or spoolie brush. Simply spray your brush with hairspray or apply a small amount of gel, and use it to gently smooth down your baby hairs.

2. Apply Hair Oil

If you're dealing with particularly stubborn baby hairs, try applying a small amount of hair oil. This will help to weigh down your hairs and keep them in place. You can also use a light hair wax or pomade for added hold.

3. Embrace Your Baby Hairs

Instead of trying to hide your baby hairs, embrace them! Baby hairs can actually add a cute and playful touch to your hairstyle. You can even use them to create a trendy "baby hair" look by slicking them back or creating a small "S" wave.

Step-by-Step Guide to Taming Baby Hairs

Here's a step-by-step guide to taming your baby hairs:

Step 1: Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Before you begin taming your baby hairs, make sure your hair is clean and dry. Wet hair can be more difficult to work with, and may not hold your styling products as well.

Step 2: Apply Styling Product

Next, apply a small amount of styling product to your baby hairs. You can use hairspray, gel, or hair wax, depending on your preference. Apply the product sparingly, as you don't want to weigh down your hair.

Step 3: Brush Your Baby Hairs

Using a toothbrush or spoolie brush, gently brush your baby hairs in the direction you want them to lay. Make sure to be gentle, as you don't want to create more flyaways or breakage.

Step 4: Smooth and Set Your Hairs

Once you've brushed your baby hairs, use your fingers to smooth them down and set them in place. You can use a bit of hairspray for added hold.

Tips for Dealing with Baby Hairs

Here are some additional tips for dealing with baby hairs:

1. Don't Overuse Heat Styling Tools

Overusing heat styling tools can damage your hair and cause more baby hairs to form. Try to limit your use of flat irons and curling wands, and always use a heat protectant spray.

2. Choose the Right Hair Products

Choosing the right hair products can make a big difference in the appearance of your baby hairs. Look for products that are lightweight and won't weigh down your hair.

3. Protect Your Hair at Night

Wearing a silk or satin scarf or bonnet at night can help to protect your hair and prevent new baby hairs from forming.

FAQs

Q: Are Baby Hairs a Sign of Hair Damage?

A: Not necessarily. While hair damage can contribute to the formation of baby hairs, they can also be a natural part of your hair growth cycle.

Q: Will Cutting My Baby Hairs Make Them Go Away?

A: No. Cutting your baby hairs may temporarily remove them, but they will grow back.

Q: Can I Use Hair Gel to Tame My Baby Hairs?

A: Yes. Hair gel can be a great option for taming baby hairs, as it provides a strong hold.

Hair Care for Baby Hairs

Finally, taking good care of your hair can help prevent the formation of new baby hairs. Here are some tips:

1. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles, such as braids and ponytails, can cause hair damage and lead to more baby hairs. Try to wear your hair down or in loose styles as often as possible.

2. Eat a Healthy Diet

Eating a healthy diet rich in vitamins and nutrients can help promote healthy hair growth and prevent breakage.

3. Stay Hydrated

Drinking plenty of water can also help keep your hair healthy and prevent breakage.

In conclusion, baby hairs can be a frustrating part of your hair routine, but with the right styling products and techniques, you can tame them and even embrace them as part of your unique style.
