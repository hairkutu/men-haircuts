Is It Haram To Get Cornrows

Written by Petter Aug 24, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you considering getting cornrows but wondering if it’s haram? It’s a common question among Muslims who want to follow Islamic guidelines in every aspect of their life. In this article, we’ll explore the topic of getting cornrows and answer some of the most frequently asked questions.

Table of Content

  • How to Get Cornrows
  • Step-by-Step Guide

  • FAQ
  • Hair Care for Cornrows

Description

Cornrows are a popular hairstyle that originated in Africa and have been worn by people of all races and cultures. The style involves braiding the hair tightly into small, straight rows that run from the front to the back of the head. Many people love cornrows because they are low-maintenance, long-lasting, and look great on both men and women.

However, some Muslims may wonder whether getting cornrows is haram (forbidden) according to Islamic teachings. The answer is not straightforward and depends on various factors, such as the intention behind getting the hairstyle, the cultural significance of cornrows, and the opinions of Islamic scholars.

How to Get Cornrows

If you’ve decided to get cornrows, there are a few things you should know before heading to the salon or braider:

  • Choose a stylist who has experience with cornrows and has a good reputation.
  • Make sure your hair is clean and free of any products before getting it braided.
  • Decide on the size and pattern of the cornrows you want. You can choose from straight or curved rows, zigzag lines, or intricate designs.
  • Be prepared to sit for several hours while your hair is braided. Cornrows can take anywhere from 2 to 6 hours depending on the complexity and length of your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here’s a step-by-step guide to getting cornrows:

  1. Wash and condition your hair a day or two before your appointment.
  2. Come to the salon with dry hair. Your stylist will start by parting your hair into sections using a comb.
  3. They will then begin braiding your hair from the front to the back using a three-strand technique.
  4. As they braid, they will add more hair from the section to each strand until the entire section is braided.
  5. They will continue this process until all the sections are braided into cornrows.
  6. Once all the cornrows are complete, your stylist may trim any loose ends or shape the hairstyle to your liking.
  7. You can add beads, shells, or other accessories to your cornrows for a unique look.
  8. Take care of your cornrows by washing your hair with a mild shampoo and conditioner every 1-2 weeks and moisturizing your scalp with natural oils.

Tips for Getting Cornrows

Here are some tips to make your cornrow experience more comfortable and enjoyable:

  • Bring a book, magazine, or music to pass the time while you’re getting your hair braided.
  • Eat a light meal beforehand, as you may be sitting for several hours.
  • Wear comfortable clothing and avoid wearing anything that may get in the way of your stylist.
  • Communicate with your stylist about your preferences and any concerns you may have.
  • Avoid getting your hair braided too tight, as it can cause hair loss and damage to the scalp.

FAQ

Is it haram to get cornrows?

There is no clear consensus among Islamic scholars on whether getting cornrows is haram. Some argue that it is permissible as long as it does not involve imitating non-Muslims or adopting their customs. Others believe that cornrows have cultural and religious significance in Africa and should not be worn by Muslims who do not belong to that culture.

Can I wear cornrows if I am a Muslim convert?

If you converted to Islam from a culture that traditionally wears cornrows, you may be allowed to continue wearing the hairstyle as part of your cultural identity. However, it is best to consult with a knowledgeable Islamic scholar to make sure you are not violating any Islamic guidelines.

Can I pray with cornrows?

Yes, you can pray with cornrows as long as your hair is clean and covered properly. You can wear a hijab or a headscarf to cover your hair during prayer.

Hair Care for Cornrows

Here are some tips for taking care of your hair while wearing cornrows:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a mild shampoo and conditioner to prevent buildup and keep your scalp clean.
  • Avoid using heavy oils and greases that can clog your pores and weigh down your hair.
  • Moisturize your scalp with natural oils like coconut oil, jojoba oil, or olive oil to prevent dryness and itching.
  • Avoid pulling or tugging on your cornrows, as it can cause breakage and damage to your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather by wearing a hat or scarf.

Overall, getting cornrows is a personal choice that depends on your cultural background, personal style, and Islamic beliefs. As long as you are respectful of Islamic guidelines and cultural traditions, you can wear cornrows with pride and confidence.
