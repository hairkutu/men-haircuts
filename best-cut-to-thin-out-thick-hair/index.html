Best Cut To Thin Out Thick Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 22, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Thick hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it can look beautiful and voluminous, it can also be a hassle to maintain and style. If you have thick hair and are looking to thin it out, getting the right haircut can make all the difference. In this article, we will discuss the best cut to thin out thick hair, how to achieve it, and how to take care of your hair after the cut.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Thin Out Thick Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Thinning Out Thick Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After the Cut

Description

The best cut to thin out thick hair is a layered haircut. Layering involves cutting the hair at different lengths to create movement and reduce bulk. This technique thins out the hair without sacrificing length, making it a great option for those who want to keep their long locks. Layering can also add volume and dimension to the hair, making it look more lively and bouncy.

How to Thin Out Thick Hair

Thinning out thick hair requires more than just getting a layered haircut. There are several techniques and tools that hair stylists use to achieve the desired look. Here are some of the most common methods:

Thinning Shears

Thinning shears are scissors that have teeth on one blade and a smooth edge on the other. When used on the hair, they remove some of the bulk while leaving the length intact. Thinning shears are a great option for those who want a subtle thinning effect.

Razoring

Razoring involves using a razor to cut the hair at an angle, creating a tapered effect. This technique can be used to thin out the hair and add texture. However, it should be done by a professional as it can damage the hair if not done properly.

Texturizing

Texturizing involves cutting the hair at different lengths to create a choppy, layered effect. This technique can be used to thin out the hair and add volume. However, it is not recommended for those with very curly or coarse hair as it can create frizz.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you know the different techniques for thinning out thick hair, here is a step-by-step guide to achieving the best cut:

Step 1: Consult with a Professional

Before getting a haircut, it is important to consult with a professional stylist. They can assess your hair type and recommend the best technique for thinning out your hair. They can also give you advice on how to style and care for your hair after the cut.

Step 2: Decide on the Length

Decide on the length you want to keep. Layering can be done on any length, but it is important to have a clear idea of how much length you want to lose.

Step 3: Choose the Technique

Based on your hair type and the desired effect, choose the technique that will work best for you. Thinning shears are the most subtle option, while texturizing can create a more dramatic effect.

Step 4: Cut the Hair

The stylist will cut the hair at different lengths to create layers. They will use the chosen technique to thin out the hair and reduce bulk. The stylist may also add some texture to the hair to create movement.

Step 5: Style the Hair

Once the cut is complete, the stylist will style the hair to show off the new layers. They may use a blow dryer, curling iron, or straightener to create the desired look. They may also recommend hair products to keep the hair healthy and styled.

Tips for Thinning Out Thick Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when thinning out thick hair:

  • Choose a stylist who has experience with layered haircuts
  • Be clear about the amount of length you want to lose
  • Communicate with the stylist throughout the process
  • Ask for advice on how to style and care for your hair after the cut

FAQs

Can thinning out thick hair damage it?

Thinning out thick hair should not damage it if done properly. However, using razors or texturizing techniques can cause damage if not done by a professional.

How often should I get a layered haircut?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly it loses its shape. As a general rule, it is recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to maintain the layers and shape.

Hair Care After the Cut

After getting a layered haircut, it is important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and styled. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid heat styling tools as much as possible
  • Use a hair mask once a week to nourish the hair
  • Trim the ends regularly to maintain the shape

Thick hair can be a hassle, but with the right cut, it can be transformed into a beautiful and manageable mane. By consulting with a professional stylist, choosing the right technique, and taking care of your hair after the cut, you can achieve the best cut to thin out thick hair.
