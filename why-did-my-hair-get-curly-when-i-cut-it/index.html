Why Did My Hair Get Curly When I Cut It

Written by Aprilia Apr 19, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you someone who has always had straight hair, but suddenly noticed that your hair became curly after getting a haircut? Don't worry, you are not alone. This can be a common occurrence, and there are a few reasons why your hair may have become curly after getting a trim. In this article, we will explore the reasons behind this phenomenon, and give you tips on how to care for your new curly locks.

Table of Contents

  • What Causes Hair to Become Curly After a Haircut?
  • How to Care for Your New Curly Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Curly Hair
  • Tips for Maintaining Healthy Curly Hair
  • FAQs

What Causes Hair to Become Curly After a Haircut?

There are a few reasons why your hair may have become curly after getting a haircut. One reason is that the weight of your hair may have contributed to it being straight. When you cut your hair, you remove some of the weight, which can cause your hair to spring up and become curly. Another reason is that curly hair may have been hiding underneath your straight hair all along. When you cut your hair, you may have removed some of the weight that was weighing down your curls, allowing them to bounce back.

Another reason why your hair may have become curly is due to the cutting technique used by your stylist. If your stylist used a razor or thinning shears to cut your hair, this can create texture and movement in your hair, making it appear curly. This is especially true for those with fine hair, as the razor or shears can create volume and body.

How to Care for Your New Curly Hair

Now that you have curly hair, it's essential to take care of it properly. Curly hair requires different care than straight hair, as it tends to be more fragile and prone to breakage. Here are some tips on how to care for your new curly locks:

  • Use sulfate-free shampoos and conditioners designed for curly hair
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as this can cause damage to your curls
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized
  • Avoid using products that contain alcohol, as this can dry out your hair

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Curly Hair

Styling curly hair can be challenging, but with the right techniques, you can achieve beautiful, defined curls. Here's a step-by-step guide on how to style your curly hair:

  1. Start with freshly washed hair, and apply a leave-in conditioner to keep your curls moisturized
  2. Divide your hair into sections, and apply a curl cream or gel to each section, starting from the ends and working your way up
  3. Use a diffuser attachment on your hairdryer to dry your hair, using a low heat and low speed setting
  4. Once your hair is dry, use your fingers to gently separate your curls and create volume
  5. Finish with a hairspray or oil to add shine and hold

Tips for Maintaining Healthy Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips for maintaining healthy curly hair:

  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of natural oils
  • Wear a satin or silk scarf or bonnet to bed to prevent frizz
  • Avoid using cotton towels to dry your hair, as this can cause frizz
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends

FAQs

Q: Is it normal for hair to become curly after a haircut?

A: Yes, it is normal for hair to become curly after a haircut. This is because the weight of your hair may have contributed to it being straight, and cutting it removes some of the weight, causing it to spring up and become curly.

Q: Can cutting my hair make it permanently curly?

A: No, cutting your hair cannot make it permanently curly. Your hair type and texture are determined by your genetics, and while cutting your hair can create the appearance of curls, it will not change your natural hair type.

Q: Do I need to use different products for curly hair?

A: Yes, curly hair requires different products than straight hair. Look for sulfate-free shampoos and conditioners designed for curly hair, as well as styling products like curl creams and gels.

Now that you know why your hair may have become curly after a haircut, and how to care for your new curls, you can embrace your new look with confidence. Remember to be patient and experiment with different styling techniques and products until you find what works best for your hair.
