How To Make Thick Hair Less Poofy Tips And Tricks

Written by Aprilia Mar 21, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you have thick hair, you've probably experienced the frustration of dealing with poofy, voluminous locks. But fear not, there are ways to tame your mane and make your hair more manageable. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks for how to make thick hair less poofy.

How to Make Thick Hair Less Poofy: Step by Step

Step 1: Get a Good Haircut

A good haircut can make a world of difference when it comes to managing thick, poofy hair. Look for a stylist who has experience working with thick hair and ask for a cut that will thin out your hair and add some layers for movement.

Step 2: Use the Right Shampoo and Conditioner

Thick hair tends to be dry, so it's important to use a hydrating shampoo and conditioner to keep your locks moisturized. Look for products that are specifically formulated for thick, coarse hair.

Step 3: Towel Dry Your Hair

Avoid using a blow dryer, as it can make your hair even more poofy. Instead, gently towel dry your hair by blotting it with a soft towel.

Step 4: Apply a Smoothing Serum

A smoothing serum can help tame frizz and make your hair more manageable. Apply a pea-sized amount of serum to your hands and work it through your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up.

Step 5: Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

A wide-tooth comb is gentler on your hair than a brush and can help detangle your locks without adding extra volume. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up, being careful not to tug or pull.

Step 6: Blow Dry Your Hair

If you must use a blow dryer, use a diffuser attachment and set it to the lowest heat setting. Hold the dryer about six inches away from your hair and use a gentle, downward motion to dry your locks.

Tips for Managing Thick Hair

In addition to the steps listed above, here are some tips for managing thick, poofy hair:

  • Avoid using too much product, as it can weigh down your hair and make it look even more poofy.
  • Don't shampoo your hair every day, as this can strip your locks of natural oils and make your hair even drier.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid touching your hair too much throughout the day, as this can cause frizz and make your hair more poofy.

Frequently Asked Questions

What causes thick hair to be poofy?

Thick hair is often prone to being poofy because it has a lot of volume and can be difficult to manage. Dryness and humidity can also contribute to poofiness.

Can I use a straightening iron to make my hair less poofy?

While a straightening iron can help smooth out your hair, it can also cause damage and make your hair even more dry and frizzy. Use it sparingly and make sure to use a heat protectant spray.

What should I do if my hair is still poofy after following these steps?

If your hair is still poofy after following these steps, try using a hair mask or consulting with a stylist for more personalized advice.

Hair Care for Thick, Poofy Hair

Taking care of thick, poofy hair can be a challenge, but with the right products and techniques, you can keep your locks looking healthy and beautiful. Remember to use a hydrating shampoo and conditioner, towel dry your hair, and use a smoothing serum to help tame frizz. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your locks, and avoid using too much product or touching your hair too much throughout the day. With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to make your thick hair less poofy and more manageable in no time.
