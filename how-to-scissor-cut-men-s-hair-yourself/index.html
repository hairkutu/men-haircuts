How To Scissor Cut Men s Hair Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jul 12, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Welcome to our guide on how to scissor cut men's hair yourself. Whether you're looking to save money, learn a new skill, or just want to try something new, cutting your own hair can be a fun and rewarding experience. In this article, we'll walk you through the process of cutting men's hair with scissors, from gathering your tools to finishing your cut. Let's get started!

Table of Contents

  1. Tools You'll Need
  2. Prepping Your Hair and Tools
  3. Sectioning Your Hair
  4. The Cut
  5. Clean Up and Finishing Touches
  6. Tips for Success
  7. FAQs
  8. Hair Care After Your Cut

Tools You'll Need

Before you get started, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Scissors: Use hair-cutting scissors, which are sharper and more precise than regular scissors.
  • Comb: A wide-toothed comb is ideal for sectioning and detangling hair.
  • Clippers (optional): Clippers can be used to trim hair around the ears and neck.
  • Clip or hair tie: Use a clip or hair tie to keep hair out of the way while you work.
  • Cape or towel: To keep hair off your clothes.

Prepping Your Hair and Tools

Before you begin cutting, make sure your hair and tools are prepped:

  • Wash and dry your hair: This will make it easier to cut and give you a better idea of how your hair falls naturally.
  • Sharpen your scissors: If your scissors are dull, they won't cut as cleanly and can damage your hair.
  • Clean your tools: Wipe down your scissors and clippers with a disinfectant to prevent the spread of germs.
  • Set up your workspace: Choose a well-lit area with a mirror and a comfortable chair. Cover surfaces with a cape or towel to catch hair clippings.

Sectioning Your Hair

Sectioning your hair before you cut it ensures a more even cut:

  1. Start by parting your hair down the middle.
  2. Section off the top half of your hair with a clip or hair tie.
  3. Divide the bottom section into two or three more sections, depending on the thickness of your hair.
  4. Clip each section out of the way until you're ready to cut it.

The Cut

Now it's time to start cutting:

  1. Start with the bottom section of hair, unclipping one section at a time.
  2. Comb through the hair to remove any tangles.
  3. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut off the desired length, using the comb as a guide.
  4. Work your way up through each section of hair, cutting it to the desired length.
  5. Check your work in the mirror to make sure the length is even on both sides.

Clean Up and Finishing Touches

Once you've finished cutting, it's time to clean up and add some finishing touches:

  1. Trim around the ears and neck with clippers, if desired.
  2. Remove any hair clippings from your skin and clothes.
  3. Style your hair as desired.

Tips for Success

Here are some tips to help you succeed at cutting your own hair:

  • Start with small cuts: It's easier to take off more hair later than to try to fix a mistake.
  • Go slow: Take your time and make sure you're cutting evenly on both sides.
  • Use the comb as a guide: This will help you maintain an even length.
  • Check your work: Use a mirror to make sure you haven't missed any spots.

FAQs

Here are some common questions about cutting men's hair with scissors:

Q: How do I cut the back of my hair?
A: Use a handheld mirror to see the back of your head while you cut. Q: Can I use regular scissors to cut my hair?
A: It's not recommended, as regular scissors can damage your hair and make it harder to cut. Q: Can I cut my hair wet or dry?
A: It's best to cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can stretch and make it harder to cut evenly.

Hair Care After Your Cut

Now that you've successfully cut your own hair, it's important to take care of it:

  • Wash and condition your hair regularly.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Trim your hair every 4-8 weeks to maintain the length.
  • If you have any concerns about your hair or scalp, consult a professional stylist.

That's it! We hope this guide has been helpful in teaching you how to scissor cut men's hair yourself. Remember, practice makes perfect, so don't be afraid to try again if you don't get it right the first time. Happy cutting!
