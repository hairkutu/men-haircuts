How to Find Your Natural Hair Type

Your natural hair type is determined by the shape and texture of your hair strands. There are four main hair types, which are:

  • Type 1 â Straight
  • Type 2 â Wavy
  • Type 3 â Curly
  • Type 4 â Coily/Kinky

To identify your natural hair type, you need to examine the shape and pattern of your hair strands. You can do this by following these simple steps:

Step-by-Step Instructions

  1. Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup, dirt, or oil.
  2. Allow your hair to air dry or use a microfiber towel to blot out the excess water.
  3. Section your hair into four parts using hair clips or bands.
  4. Examine each section of your hair and check the shape and pattern of your strands.
  5. Type 1 hair is straight and has no curls or waves. The hair strands are often smooth and shiny.
  6. Type 2 hair is wavy and has an "S" shape pattern. The waves can range from loose to tight.
  7. Type 3 hair is curly and has a defined "S" or "Z" shape pattern. The curls can range from loose to tight.
  8. Type 4 hair is coily/kinky and has a tight "Z" or "S" shape pattern. The hair strands are often densely packed together.
  9. Once you have determined your hair type, you can use this information to find the right hair care routine, products, and styling techniques that work best for you.

Tips for Identifying Your Hair Type

Here are some additional tips to help you identify your natural hair type:

  • Use a magnifying glass to examine your hair strands more closely.
  • Observe how your hair behaves when wet and dry.
  • Ask a hair stylist or a friend with similar hair type to help you identify your hair type.
  • Take hair quizzes online to get a better understanding of your hair type.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about identifying natural hair types:

1. Can your hair type change over time?

Yes, your hair type can change over time due to hormonal changes, age, or hair damage.

2. Can you have more than one hair type?

Yes, it's possible to have a mix of two or more hair types on your head, such as wavy and curly hair.

3. Do hair types affect hair growth?

No, hair types do not affect hair growth. Hair growth is determined by genetics, diet, and overall health.

Hair Care for Different Hair Types

Once you have identified your natural hair type, you can use this information to find the right hair care routine, products, and styling techniques that work best for you.

  • Type 1 hair needs gentle cleansing and lightweight products to prevent oil buildup.
  • Type 2 hair needs hydration and light hold products to enhance the waves.
  • Type 3 hair needs moisture and definition products to enhance the curls.
  • Type 4 hair needs moisture and sealant products to prevent dryness and breakage.

Overall, identifying your natural hair type is the first step in achieving healthy and beautiful hair. Use the tips and instructions provided in this article to determine your hair type and tailor your hair care routine accordingly. With the right products and techniques, you can embrace your natural hair and feel confident in your own skin.
