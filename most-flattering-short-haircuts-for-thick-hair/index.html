Most Flattering Short Haircuts For Thick Hair

Written by William Mar 19, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Thick hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it provides plenty of volume and texture, it can also be difficult to manage and style. Short haircuts are a great way to tame thick hair and make it easier to manage. In this article, we'll explore the most flattering short haircuts for thick hair, along with tips on how to style and care for your new 'do.

Table of Contents

  • Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Short Haircuts for Thick Hair
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Short Haircut for Thick Hair
  • FAQs About Short Haircuts for Thick Hair
  • Hair Care Tips for Thick Hair

How to Choose the Right Short Haircut for Your Thick Hair

When it comes to short haircuts for thick hair, there are a lot of options to choose from. Here are a few things to consider when selecting the right haircut for you:

Face Shape

Consider your face shape when choosing a short haircut. If you have a round face, go for a choppy pixie cut or a layered bob. If you have an oval face, try a blunt bob or a short shag. If you have a square face, go for a side-swept bang or a choppy bob.

Hair Texture

Consider your hair texture when selecting a short haircut. If you have fine hair, opt for a blunt bob or a pixie cut with choppy layers. If you have curly hair, try a short shag or a layered bob. If you have straight hair, go for a sleek, blunt bob or a textured pixie cut.

Lifestyle

Finally, consider your lifestyle when selecting a short haircut. If you're low maintenance, go for a choppy bob or a pixie cut with textured layers. If you're high maintenance, try a sleek, blunt bob or a short shag with lots of layers.

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Short Haircuts for Thick Hair

Now that you've selected the perfect short haircut for your thick hair, it's time to learn how to style it. Here's a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Wash and Condition

Start by washing and conditioning your hair. Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to enhance your hair's natural texture.

Step 2: Towel Dry

Gently towel dry your hair, being careful not to rub it too harshly. You want to preserve as much of your hair's natural texture as possible.

Step 3: Apply Product

Apply a styling product to your hair. If you have fine hair, go for a mousse or a volumizing spray. If you have curly hair, use a curl-enhancing cream or gel. If you have straight hair, use a smoothing serum or cream.

Step 4: Blow Dry

Blow dry your hair using a round brush. Use the brush to lift your roots and add volume to your hair. If you have curly hair, use a diffuser attachment to enhance your curls.

Step 5: Style

Style your hair using a flat iron or curling iron. If you have a pixie cut, use a flat iron to add texture and volume. If you have a bob or a shag, use a curling iron to create loose waves.

Tips for Maintaining Your Short Haircut for Thick Hair

Now that you've styled your short haircut, it's important to maintain it. Here are a few tips:

Trim Regularly

Short haircuts require regular trims to maintain their shape. Visit your stylist every 4-6 weeks to keep your haircut looking fresh.

Use Heat Protectant

When using heat styling tools, always use a heat protectant to prevent damage to your hair.

Avoid Overwashing

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, leading to dryness and damage. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days.

FAQs About Short Haircuts for Thick Hair

What short haircut is best for thick hair?

The best short haircut for thick hair depends on your face shape, hair texture, and lifestyle. Some popular options include the pixie cut, the bob, and the shag.

How do I style short haircuts for thick hair?

To style short haircuts for thick hair, start by washing and conditioning your hair. Apply a styling product, then blow dry and style using a flat iron or curling iron.

How often should I trim my short haircut?

You should trim your short haircut every 4-6 weeks to maintain its shape and prevent split ends.

Hair Care Tips for Thick Hair

Thick hair requires special care to keep it healthy and manageable. Here are a few tips:

Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

When detangling your hair, use a wide-tooth comb to prevent breakage and damage.

Avoid Overbrushing

Brushing your hair too often can lead to breakage and damage. Aim to brush your hair once or twice a day.

Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Once a week, use a deep conditioning treatment to keep your hair soft, shiny, and healthy.

Short haircuts are a great way to tame thick hair and make it easier to manage. Whether you opt for a pixie cut, a bob, or a shag, there's a short haircut that's perfect for you. By following these styling and hair care tips, you can keep your short haircut looking fresh and fabulous.
