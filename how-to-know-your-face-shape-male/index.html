How To Know Your Face Shape Male A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Bardi May 30, 2023 · 10 min read

If you're a male looking to figure out your face shape, you're in the right place. Knowing your face shape is key to picking the right hairstyle, beard style, and overall grooming routine. In this article, we'll go over the different face shapes for men and how to determine which one you have.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Determine Your Face Shape
  2. Different Face Shapes for Men
  3. Tips for Styling Based on Your Face Shape
  4. FAQs About Face Shapes for Men
  5. How to Take Care of Your Hair and Skin Based on Your Face Shape

How to Determine Your Face Shape

The first step to figuring out your face shape is to pull your hair back and stand in front of a mirror. You want to be able to see the shape of your face and hairline clearly. Here are the steps to determine your face shape:

Step 1: Measure Your Forehead

Using a tape measure or ruler, measure the distance across your forehead from one eyebrow arch to the other. Write down your measurement in inches.

Step 2: Measure Your Cheekbones

Measure the distance across your cheekbones from the outer corner of one eye to the outer corner of the other eye. Write down your measurement in inches.

Step 3: Measure Your Jawline

Measure the distance from the tip of your chin to the point below your ear where your jawbone starts to angle upwards. Double this measurement to get your jawline length.

Step 4: Measure Your Face Height

Measure the distance from the center of your hairline to the tip of your chin. Write down your measurement in inches.

Step 5: Determine Your Face Shape

Now that you have your measurements, you can determine your face shape using the following guidelines:

  • Oval: Your face height is greater than your cheekbones, and your forehead is greater than your jawline.
  • Round: Your face height, cheekbones, and jawline are all similar in measurement.
  • Square: Your forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are all similar in measurement.
  • Heart: Your forehead is wider than your cheekbones and jawline, and your face tapers to a point at your chin.
  • Diamond: Your cheekbones are wider than your forehead and jawline, and your face tapers to a point at your forehead and chin.
  • Triangle: Your jawline is wider than your forehead and cheekbones.

Once you've determined your face shape, you can move on to finding the best hairstyles and grooming routines to suit your unique features.

Different Face Shapes for Men

Let's take a closer look at the different face shapes for men and what they entail:

Oval Face Shape

Oval face shapes are considered the most versatile, as they have balanced proportions and can pull off a variety of hairstyles and grooming routines. If you have an oval face shape, consider yourself lucky!

Round Face Shape

Round face shapes have a soft, circular appearance with full cheeks and a rounded jawline. To balance out the roundness, opt for hairstyles with height on top and shorter sides.

Square Face Shape

Square face shapes have a strong, angular appearance with a prominent jawline and forehead. To soften the angles, go for hairstyles with longer layers and textured tops.

Heart Face Shape

Heart face shapes have a wider forehead and a pointed chin. To balance out the proportions, go for hairstyles with volume on top and shorter sides.

Diamond Face Shape

Diamond face shapes have a narrow forehead and jawline with wider cheekbones. To enhance the cheekbones, try hairstyles with volume on top and shorter sides.

Triangle Face Shape

Triangle face shapes have a wider jawline than forehead and cheekbones. To balance out the proportions, go for hairstyles with volume on top and shorter sides.

Tips for Styling Based on Your Face Shape

Now that you know your face shape, it's time to pick the right hairstyle and grooming routine. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • For oval face shapes, try a side part or undercut.
  • For round face shapes, try a messy quiff or textured crop.
  • For square face shapes, try a side-swept fringe or messy top.
  • For heart face shapes, try a slicked-back look or messy fringe.
  • For diamond face shapes, try a messy fringe or textured top.
  • For triangle face shapes, try a messy top or textured crop.

Remember, these are just guidelines – feel free to experiment with different styles and see what works best for you!

FAQs About Face Shapes for Men

What if my face shape doesn't fit into any of these categories?

It's possible that you have a combination of face shapes or a unique shape that doesn't fit into any of these categories. In that case, focus on the features you want to enhance or balance out and choose a hairstyle and grooming routine that works for you.

Do face shapes change over time?

While your bone structure doesn't change, your face shape can appear different depending on factors like weight loss or gain, aging, and changes in hairstyle or beard style.

Can I change my face shape with surgery?

While there are surgical procedures that can alter the appearance of your face, we don't recommend going that route just to fit into a certain face shape category. Embrace your unique features and work with what you have!

How to Take Care of Your Hair and Skin Based on Your Face Shape

Now that you know your face shape and the best hairstyles and grooming routines to suit it, it's important to take care of your hair and skin. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Choose hair products based on your hair type and the hairstyle you want to achieve.
  • Moisturize your skin to keep it hydrated and healthy.
  • Use sunscreen to protect your skin from sun damage.
  • Choose skincare products based on your skin type and concerns.

By taking care of your hair and skin, you'll look and feel your best – no matter what your face shape is!

Knowing your face shape is the key to picking the right hairstyle, beard style, and grooming routine. By following these tips and guidelines, you'll be able to find the perfect look to suit your unique features. Happy grooming!
