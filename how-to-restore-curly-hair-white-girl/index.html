How To Restore Curly Hair For White Girls A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Jun 24, 2023 Â· 11 min read

Are you a white girl with curly hair that has lost its bounce and shine? Don't worry, you're not alone! Many women with naturally curly hair struggle to maintain its health and beauty, especially when it has been chemically treated or styled with heat tools. In this blog post, we'll show you how to restore your curly hair to its former glory. From understanding your hair type to developing a healthy hair care routine, we've got you covered!

Understanding Your Hair Type

Before you begin restoring your curly hair, it's important to understand your hair type. This will help you choose the right products and styling techniques for your hair. There are generally four types of curly hair:

Type 2: Wavy Hair

Wavy hair has a loose S-shaped pattern and is prone to frizz. It's the easiest type of curly hair to style and manage.

Type 3: Curly Hair

Curly hair has a tighter S-shaped pattern and can be prone to dryness and frizz. It requires more moisture and gentle handling to maintain its shape.

Type 4: Coily Hair

Coily hair has a tight, springy curl pattern and is prone to dryness and breakage. It requires the most moisture and gentle handling to prevent damage.

Type 4a: Tightly Coiled Hair

Tightly coiled hair has a small, springy curl pattern and is the most fragile type of curly hair. It requires the most moisture and gentle handling to prevent damage.

Developing a Healthy Hair Care Routine

Now that you understand your hair type, it's time to develop a healthy hair care routine. This should include the following steps:

Step 1: Determine how often you should wash your hair.

Curly hair tends to be dry, so you don't want to over-wash it. Depending on your hair type, you may only need to wash your hair once or twice a week. If you have very dry hair, you may only need to wash it once every two weeks.

Step 2: Choose a sulfate-free shampoo.

Sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils, so it's important to choose a sulfate-free shampoo. Look for a shampoo that is specifically designed for curly hair.

Step 3: Deep condition your hair once a week.

Deep conditioning your hair once a week will help to restore moisture and prevent breakage. Look for a deep conditioner that is specifically designed for curly hair.

Step 4: Avoid using heat tools.

Heat tools can damage your hair and cause it to become dry and brittle. If you must use heat, use a heat protectant spray and keep the temperature low.

Washing Your Curly Hair

When washing your curly hair, follow these tips:

Tip 1: Wet your hair thoroughly.

Before applying shampoo, wet your hair thoroughly with warm water. This will help to open up your hair cuticles and allow the shampoo to penetrate your hair more effectively.

Tip 2: Apply shampoo to your scalp.

Apply shampoo to your scalp and massage it in gently with your fingertips. Don't scrub your hair or pile it on top of your head, as this can cause tangling and breakage.

Tip 3: Rinse your hair thoroughly.

Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water, making sure to remove all of the shampoo. Don't rub your hair with a towel, as this can cause frizz and breakage.

Conditioning Your Curly Hair

After shampooing, it's time to condition your hair. Follow these tips:

Tip 1: Apply conditioner to the ends of your hair.

Apply conditioner to the ends of your hair first, as this is where your hair is most damaged and dry. Work the conditioner up to your roots, but don't apply it to your scalp.

Tip 2: Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair while the conditioner is still in your hair. Start at the ends and work your way up to the roots.

Tip 3: Rinse your hair thoroughly.

Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water, making sure to remove all of the conditioner. Don't rub your hair with a towel, as this can cause frizz and breakage.

Deep Conditioning Your Curly Hair

Deep conditioning your hair once a week will help to restore moisture and prevent breakage. Follow these tips:

Tip 1: Apply deep conditioner to your hair.

Apply deep conditioner to your hair, focusing on the ends. If your hair is very dry, you can apply deep conditioner to your entire head.

Tip 2: Cover your hair with a plastic cap.

Cover your hair with a plastic cap and let the deep conditioner sit for 30 minutes to an hour. You can also use a hair dryer or sit under a hooded dryer to help the conditioner penetrate your hair more effectively.

Tip 3: Rinse your hair thoroughly.

Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water, making sure to remove all of the deep conditioner. Don't rub your hair with a towel, as this can cause frizz and breakage.

Drying Your Curly Hair

When drying your curly hair, follow these tips:

Tip 1: Use a microfiber towel or t-shirt.

Use a microfiber towel or t-shirt to gently blot your hair dry. Don't rub your hair with a towel, as this can cause frizz and breakage.

Tip 2: Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer.

Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your hair on a low heat setting. This will help to maintain your hair's natural curl pattern.

Tip 3: Air dry your hair if possible.

If possible, let your hair air dry. This will help to prevent frizz and maintain your hair's natural curl pattern.

Styling Your Curly Hair

When styling your curly hair, follow these tips:

Tip 1: Use a leave-in conditioner or curl enhancer.

Use a leave-in conditioner or curl enhancer to help define your curls and prevent frizz.

Tip 2: Don't touch your hair while it's drying.

Don't touch your hair while it's drying, as this can cause frizz and disrupt your hair's natural curl pattern.

Tip 3: Use a silk or satin pillowcase.

Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage while you sleep.

Tips for Restoring Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips for restoring your curly hair:

Tip 1: Get regular trims.

Getting regular trims will help to prevent split ends and breakage.

Tip 2: Avoid brushing your hair.

Avoid brushing your hair, as this can cause frizz and disrupt your hair's natural curl pattern.

Tip 3: Protect your hair from the sun.

Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protectant spray.

FAQs

Q: Can I restore my curly hair if it's been chemically treated?

A: Yes, you can restore your curly hair
