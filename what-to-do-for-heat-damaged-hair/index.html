What To Do For Heat Damaged Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Eveline Jun 16, 2023 · 7 min read

Heat styling tools like blow dryers, flat irons, and curling irons can help us achieve our desired hairstyles, but they can also cause heat damage to our hair. Heat damage can leave our hair looking dull, frizzy, and lifeless. So, what can you do to repair heat damaged hair? In this blog post, we’ll share some tips and tricks to help you restore your hair’s health and vitality.

How to Identify Heat Damaged Hair

Before we dive into how to fix heat damaged hair, let’s first talk about how to identify it. Heat damaged hair is often dry, brittle, and prone to breakage. It may also have split ends and a rough texture, and it may appear dull and lifeless. If you notice these signs, it’s likely that your hair has been damaged by heat styling tools.

Step-by-Step Guide to Repairing Heat Damaged Hair

Step 1: Cut Off the Damaged Ends

The first step to repairing heat damaged hair is to get rid of the damaged ends. You can do this by trimming your hair or getting a haircut. This will help prevent further damage and promote healthy hair growth.

Step 2: Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

After cutting off the damaged ends, you’ll want to focus on nourishing and hydrating your hair. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help repair and restore your hair’s health. Look for products that contain ingredients like keratin, argan oil, and avocado oil, which are known for their restorative properties.

Step 3: Avoid Heat Styling Tools

If your hair has been damaged by heat styling tools, it’s important to give it a break from them. Avoid using blow dryers, flat irons, and curling irons and let your hair air dry instead. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

Step 4: Use a Leave-In Conditioner

Another way to nourish and hydrate your hair is to use a leave-in conditioner. This will help keep your hair moisturized throughout the day and prevent further damage. Look for a lightweight leave-in conditioner that won’t weigh your hair down.

Tips for Preventing Heat Damage

Prevention is key when it comes to heat damaged hair. Here are some tips to help you avoid heat damage in the first place:

  • Use heat styling tools on low or medium heat settings
  • Always use a heat protectant spray
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on wet hair
  • Limit your use of heat styling tools to once or twice a week
  • Consider using alternative styling methods, such as braids or twists

FAQs About Heat Damaged Hair

Can heat damaged hair be repaired?

Yes, heat damaged hair can be repaired. By following the steps outlined in this blog post, you can help restore your hair’s health and vitality.

How long does it take to repair heat damaged hair?

The time it takes to repair heat damaged hair will vary depending on the extent of the damage. It may take several weeks or months to see significant improvement, so be patient and consistent with your hair care routine.

Can heat damaged hair be prevented?

Yes, heat damaged hair can be prevented by following the tips outlined in this blog post. By using heat styling tools on low or medium heat settings, using a heat protectant spray, and limiting your use of heat styling tools, you can help prevent heat damage.

Hair Care Tips for Healthy Hair

Aside from avoiding heat damage, there are other things you can do to promote healthy hair. Here are some tips:

  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals
  • Drink plenty of water to stay hydrated
  • Avoid harsh chemical treatments, such as perms and relaxers
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s wet, as it’s more prone to breakage

By following these tips and tricks, you can help repair and prevent heat damaged hair, and promote healthy hair growth. Remember to be patient and consistent with your hair care routine, and you’ll be rewarded with healthy, beautiful hair.
