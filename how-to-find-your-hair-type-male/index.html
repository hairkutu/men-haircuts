How To Find Your Hair Type Male A Comprehensive Guide

Written by William Aug 22, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you a male who is struggling to find the right hair products for your hair type? Do you often wonder what type of hair you have and what products will work best for you? Look no further! In this article, we will guide you through the process of finding your hair type, providing step-by-step instructions, tips, and FAQs.

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Determine Your Hair Texture
  • Step 2: Identify Your Hair Density
  • Step 3: Assess Your Hair Porosity
  • Tips for Caring for Your Hair Type
  • FAQs

Step 1: Determine Your Hair Texture

The first step in finding your hair type is to determine your hair texture. Hair texture refers to the thickness or diameter of each individual hair strand. There are three main types of hair texture: fine, medium, and coarse. To determine your hair texture, take a single strand of hair and place it between your thumb and index finger:

  • If you can barely feel the strand or it is difficult to see, you have fine hair.
  • If you can feel the strand but it is not too thick or too thin, you have medium hair.
  • If you can easily feel the strand and it is thick, you have coarse hair.

Step 2: Identify Your Hair Density

The second step in finding your hair type is to identify your hair density. Hair density refers to the number of hair strands on your scalp. There are three main types of hair density: low, medium, and high. To identify your hair density, separate your hair into sections and look at your scalp:

  • If you can easily see your scalp, you have low hair density.
  • If you can see some scalp but not all, you have medium hair density.
  • If you cannot see your scalp, you have high hair density.

Step 3: Assess Your Hair Porosity

The final step in finding your hair type is to assess your hair porosity. Hair porosity refers to how easily your hair absorbs and retains moisture. There are three main types of hair porosity: low, medium, and high. To assess your hair porosity, take a single strand of hair and place it in a glass of water:

  • If your hair sinks to the bottom of the glass, you have high porosity hair.
  • If your hair floats in the middle of the glass, you have medium porosity hair.
  • If your hair floats on top of the water, you have low porosity hair.

Tips for Caring for Your Hair Type

Now that you have determined your hair type, it is important to know how to care for it. Here are some tips:

  • If you have fine hair, use lightweight products to avoid weighing down your hair.
  • If you have medium hair, use products that provide volume and texture.
  • If you have coarse hair, use products that provide moisture and control frizz.
  • If you have low hair density, avoid heavy products that can weigh down your hair.
  • If you have high hair density, use products that provide volume and texture.
  • If you have high porosity hair, use products that provide moisture and seal the cuticle.
  • If you have low porosity hair, use products that are lightweight and can penetrate the hair shaft.

FAQs

1. Can I have more than one hair type?

Yes, it is possible to have more than one hair type. For example, you can have medium hair texture, low hair density, and high porosity.

2. How often should I wash my hair?

It is recommended to wash your hair 2-3 times a week, depending on your hair type and lifestyle.

3. Can I change my hair type?

No, your hair type is determined by genetics and cannot be changed. However, you can improve the health and appearance of your hair through proper care and maintenance.

4. What is the best way to detangle my hair?

Use a wide-tooth comb or a brush specifically designed for detangling. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots, being gentle and avoiding pulling or tugging.

5. How can I prevent hair breakage?

Avoid using heat tools too often, use a heat protectant when using heat tools, avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair, and use a deep conditioner regularly.


Now that you know how to find your hair type male, you can confidently choose the right hair products and care for your hair properly. Remember to always listen to your hair and adjust your routine as needed. Happy hair care!
