How To Use Thinning Shears Women s Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Aug 10, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're looking to add some texture and volume to your hair, thinning shears can be a great tool. However, many women are intimidated by these specialized scissors and unsure of how to use them properly. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process of using thinning shears on women's hair, from selecting the right shears to finishing your new look.

  • Preparing Your Hair

Selecting the Right Thinning Shears

The first step in using thinning shears on your hair is selecting the right type. There are many different types of thinning shears on the market, so it's important to choose the one that's best for your hair type and desired look.

When selecting thinning shears, consider the following:

  • The number of teeth on the shears
  • The size of the teeth on the shears
  • The length of the blades
  • The material of the blades

Generally, the more teeth on the shears, the less hair will be removed with each cut. If you have thick hair, you may want to choose shears with more teeth. Additionally, smaller teeth will remove less hair than larger teeth. The length of the blades and the material of the blades can also affect the performance of the shears.

Preparing Your Hair

Before you begin using thinning shears, it's important to prepare your hair properly. Start by washing your hair and drying it thoroughly. You may also want to apply a heat protectant product if you plan on using heat styling tools.

Next, comb your hair to remove any tangles and knots. If you have particularly thick or long hair, you may want to consider sectioning your hair.

Sectioning Your Hair

Sectioning your hair can make the thinning process easier and more efficient. To section your hair, use clips or hair ties to divide your hair into smaller sections. Begin by sectioning your hair into two or three large sections, then divide each section into smaller subsections as needed.

Thinning Your Hair

To thin your hair using thinning shears, begin by holding the shears parallel to your hair with the teeth facing outward. Take a small section of hair and hold it between your fingers, then use the shears to cut into the hair at a slight angle.

Repeat this process with small sections of hair, working your way around your head. Be careful not to cut too much hair at once, as this can result in an uneven or choppy look.

Finishing Your New Look

Once you've finished thinning your hair, you can style it as desired. Thinned hair tends to have more volume and texture, so you may want to try a new style or experiment with different products.

Tips for Using Thinning Shears on Women's Hair

Here are a few additional tips to keep in mind when using thinning shears on women's hair:

  • Start with small sections of hair and work your way around your head
  • Be careful not to cut too much hair at once
  • Use the right type of thinning shears for your hair type
  • Consider sectioning your hair to make the process easier
  • Experiment with different styles and products to make the most of your new look

FAQs About Thinning Shears

What are thinning shears?

Thinning shears are specialized scissors designed to remove excess hair from the hair shaft. They feature teeth on one or both blades, which help to thin out the hair without creating a blunt or choppy look.

Can thinning shears be used on all hair types?

Thinning shears can be used on most hair types, but they may not be suitable for very fine or fragile hair. It's important to choose the right type of shears for your hair type and desired look.

How often should I use thinning shears on my hair?

The frequency with which you use thinning shears on your hair will depend on your individual hair type and growth rate. As a general rule, it's best to use thinning shears every 6-8 weeks to maintain your desired look.

Hair Care After Using Thinning Shears

After using thinning shears on your hair, it's important to care for your hair properly to maintain its health and appearance. Be sure to use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and consider using a hair mask or deep conditioning treatment once a week.

Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair and reduce its volume and texture. If you do use heat styling tools, be sure to apply a heat protectant product first.

With these tips and techniques, you can use thinning shears to add volume and texture to your hair and create a new look that's uniquely your own.
