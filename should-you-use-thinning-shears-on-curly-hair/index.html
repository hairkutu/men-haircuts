Should You Use Thinning Shears On Curly Hair

Written by Eveline Mar 21, 2023 Â· 7 min read

When it comes to curly hair, there are a lot of mixed opinions on whether or not you should use thinning shears. Some people swear by them, while others avoid them at all costs. In this article, we will explore the pros and cons of using thinning shears on curly hair and help you decide whether or not they are right for you.

Description

Thinning shears are a type of scissors that have teeth cut into one or both blades. They are designed to remove some of the hair's thickness without altering the overall shape of the cut. This can be beneficial for people with thick hair who want to reduce weight and volume without sacrificing length.

However, when it comes to curly hair, things get a bit more complicated. Curly hair has a tendency to frizz and become uncontrollable when it is cut with thinning shears. This is because the shears can disrupt the natural curl pattern and create uneven layers.

How to Use Thinning Shears on Curly Hair

If you do decide to use thinning shears on your curly hair, there are a few things you should keep in mind:

Step 1: Choose the Right Shears

Not all thinning shears are created equal. You will need to find a pair that is specifically designed for curly hair. Look for shears with wide teeth that are spaced far apart. This will help prevent the shears from disrupting your natural curl pattern.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Before you start cutting, you will need to section your hair. This will help you see where you need to thin it out. Use clips to separate your hair into manageable sections.

Step 3: Cut with Caution

When you start cutting, use the shears sparingly. It's better to take off too little than too much. Only cut a small amount of hair at a time, and avoid cutting too close to the scalp.

Tips for Using Thinning Shears on Curly Hair

Here are a few additional tips to keep in mind when using thinning shears on curly hair:

  • Make sure your hair is dry before you start cutting. Wet hair can stretch and create an uneven cut.
  • Use a comb to help guide the shears through your hair.
  • Don't cut too much at once. It's better to take your time and make several passes than to cut too much at once.
  • Be mindful of the length of your hair. Thinning shears can make your hair appear shorter than it actually is.

FAQ

Q: Can thinning shears damage curly hair?

A: Yes, if not used properly, thinning shears can damage curly hair. They can disrupt the natural curl pattern and create uneven layers.

Q: How often should I use thinning shears on curly hair?

A: It's best to use thinning shears sparingly on curly hair. You should only use them when your hair starts to feel too thick and heavy.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Whether or not you decide to use thinning shears on your curly hair, there are a few things you should keep in mind when it comes to hair care:

  • Use a wide-toothed comb or a detangling brush to avoid pulling and tugging on your curls.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling irons, as they can damage your hair and disrupt your natural curl pattern.
  • Use a deep conditioner once a week to help moisturize and nourish your curls.
  • Avoid using harsh shampoos that can strip your hair of its natural oils.

Conclusion

So, should you use thinning shears on curly hair? The answer is that it depends. While thinning shears can be beneficial for people with thick hair who want to reduce weight and volume, they can be damaging to curly hair if not used properly. If you do decide to use thinning shears on your curly hair, make sure to use a pair that is specifically designed for curly hair, section your hair before cutting, and cut with caution. And remember, hair care is just as important as the cut itself, so make sure to take good care of your curls!
