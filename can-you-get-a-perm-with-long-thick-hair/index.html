Can You Get A Perm With Long Thick Hair

Written by Frank Jun 18, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have long, thick hair and have been considering getting a perm, you may be wondering if it's possible. The short answer is yes, you can get a perm with long thick hair, but there are a few things you should know before taking the plunge. In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know about getting a perm with long thick hair, including how to prepare, what to expect, and how to care for your new curls.

Description

Perms are a popular way to add curls or waves to straight hair. The process involves applying a chemical solution to the hair, which breaks down the hair's natural bonds and allows it to be reshaped. The hair is then wrapped around perm rods and another chemical solution is applied to set the curls. Perms can be a great way to add volume and texture to flat or lifeless hair, but they can also be damaging if not done properly.

How to

If you have long thick hair and are considering a perm, there are a few things you can do to prepare. First, make sure your hair is in good condition. If your hair is damaged or brittle, a perm can make things worse. Consider getting a deep conditioning treatment or trim before your perm to ensure your hair is healthy.

You should also talk to your stylist about the type of curl you want. There are a variety of perm rod sizes available, and the size of the rod will determine the size of the curl. If you want loose, beachy waves, for example, you'll want to use larger rods. If you want tight, spiral curls, smaller rods will be necessary.

On the day of your perm, your stylist will start by washing your hair and applying the perm solution. They will then wrap your hair around the perm rods and apply the setting solution. The entire process can take a few hours, depending on the length and thickness of your hair. Once the setting solution is applied, you'll need to sit with the perm rods in your hair for a period of time, usually around 20-30 minutes.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step breakdown of what to expect during a perm:

  1. Wash your hair to remove any buildup or styling products.
  2. Apply the perm solution to your hair, making sure to saturate each section.
  3. Wrap your hair around perm rods, starting at the nape of your neck and working your way up.
  4. Apply the setting solution to your hair and let it sit for the recommended time.
  5. Remove the perm rods and rinse your hair thoroughly.
  6. Apply a neutralizer to your hair to stop the perming process.
  7. Rinse your hair again and apply a deep conditioning treatment.
  8. Dry and style your new curls.

Tips

Here are a few tips to help you get the best results from your perm:

  • Don't wash your hair for at least 48 hours after your perm. This will give the curls time to set.
  • Avoid using heat on your hair for the first few days after your perm. Heat can cause the curls to fall out.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair after your perm. A brush can disrupt the curls.
  • Consider using a curl-enhancing product to help define and hold your curls.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about getting a perm with long thick hair:

Will a perm damage my hair?

A perm can be damaging if not done properly. Make sure you go to a stylist who is experienced in perming long thick hair, and make sure your hair is in good condition before your perm.

How long will my perm last?

A perm can last anywhere from a few months to a year, depending on how fast your hair grows and how well you take care of your curls.

Can I color my hair after getting a perm?

You should wait at least two weeks before coloring your hair after getting a perm. The chemicals in hair dye can be harsh on freshly permed hair.

Hair Care

To keep your perm looking great, it's important to take good care of your hair. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat tools on your hair, as they can cause the curls to fall out.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to help define and hold your curls.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

In conclusion, getting a perm with long thick hair is definitely possible, but it's important to take the time to prepare and find a stylist who is experienced in perming long hair. With the right care and maintenance, you can enjoy beautiful, bouncy curls for months to come.
