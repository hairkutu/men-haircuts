How To Cut Long Side Swept Bangs Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Jun 21, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of having to go to the salon every time you need to trim your side swept bangs? Well, we've got some good news for you! With a few simple steps, you can easily cut your long side swept bangs yourself at home. In this article, we'll show you how to do it and provide you with some tips and FAQs to help you achieve the perfect look.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Long Side Swept Bangs Yourself
  • Step-by-Step Guide

How to Cut Long Side Swept Bangs Yourself

Before we get started, it's important to have the right tools. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and some clips to hold your hair in place. It's also a good idea to have a reference photo of the style you're trying to achieve.

Now, let's get down to business. Here's a simple guide to cutting long side swept bangs yourself:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start by washing and drying your hair as you normally would. This will help you see the natural fall of your bangs.
  2. Part your hair down the middle and comb your bangs straight down.
  3. Take a small section of your bangs and twist it away from your face. This will create a natural separation between your bangs and the rest of your hair.
  4. Using your scissors, snip the ends of the twisted section. Be sure to cut at an angle, following the natural curve of your head.
  5. Release the twisted section and comb it back into place. Check to see if it's the right length and adjust as needed.
  6. Repeat steps 3-5 on the other side, making sure to match the length of your bangs.
  7. Blend the ends of your bangs with the rest of your hair by snipping into the ends at an angle. This will create a seamless transition.
  8. Style your bangs as desired.

And that's it! You've just cut your own long side swept bangs.

Tips for Cutting Long Side Swept Bangs

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind:

  • Start with small snips and work your way up to the desired length. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once it's been cut.
  • Don't pull your hair too tightly when cutting. This can cause your bangs to be shorter than you intended.
  • If you're unsure about cutting your own bangs, practice on a small section first.
  • Remember that hair shrinks when it dries, so cut your bangs a little longer than you want them to be.

FAQs About Cutting Long Side Swept Bangs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting long side swept bangs:

  • Can I cut my bangs when they're wet? It's best to cut your bangs when they're dry, as wet hair can be misleading and cause you to cut too much.
  • What if I mess up? Don't worry! Hair grows back, and there are always ways to fix a bad cut. If you're really unhappy with your bangs, visit a professional stylist for help.
  • How often should I trim my bangs? This can vary depending on how fast your hair grows and how long you want your bangs to be. As a general rule, trim your bangs every 4-6 weeks.

Hair Care After Cutting Long Side Swept Bangs

Now that you've cut your bangs, it's important to take care of them properly. Here are some tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy.
  • Avoid using heat tools on your bangs, as this can cause damage and split ends.
  • Try different styling products to see what works best for your hair type.
  • Visit a professional stylist for regular trims to keep your bangs looking their best.

With these tips and tricks, you can confidently cut your own long side swept bangs at home. Happy snipping!
