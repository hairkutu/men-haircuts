How To Cover Bald Head After Chemo Tips And Tricks

Written by Aprilia Sep 04, 2023 · 9 min read

Cancer treatment can be one of the most challenging experiences for anyone. Chemotherapy, in particular, can be very hard on the body and often results in hair loss. While baldness is a common side effect of chemo, it can be difficult to come to terms with for many patients. Fortunately, there are many ways to cover a bald head after chemo. In this article, we’ll discuss the best tips and tricks for concealing baldness and feeling confident after treatment.

How to Cover Bald Head After Chemo

The first step in covering a bald head after chemo is to choose the right head covering. There are several options available, including:

1. Hats and Caps

Hats and caps are a popular choice for covering baldness after chemo. They are available in many styles, colors, and materials, making it easy to find one that suits your preferences. Baseball caps, beanies, and sun hats are all great options to consider.

2. Scarves and Head Wraps

Scarves and head wraps are another popular choice for covering a bald head after chemo. They are available in many materials, colors, and patterns, making it easy to find one that suits your style. You can wear them in many different ways, from simple knots to more elaborate styles.

3. Wigs

Wigs are another option for covering a bald head after chemo. They are available in many styles and colors, making it easy to find one that suits your preferences. Wigs are available in both synthetic and human hair options, with the latter being the more expensive choice.

Step-by-Step Guide on How to Cover Bald Head After Chemo

Follow these simple steps to cover your bald head after chemo:

Step 1: Choose a Head Covering

Choose a head covering that suits your style and preferences. Consider factors such as material, color, and style.

Step 2: Tie a Scarf or Wrap

If you choose to wear a scarf or head wrap, learn how to tie it in different styles. There are many tutorials available online that can help you learn how to tie a scarf or wrap in different ways.

Step 3: Wear a Hat or Cap

If you choose to wear a hat or cap, find one that fits your head comfortably. You can wear it with the bill facing forward or backward, depending on your preference.

Step 4: Put on a Wig

If you choose to wear a wig, put it on carefully to avoid damaging it. Make sure it fits your head comfortably and looks natural.

Tips for Covering Bald Head After Chemo

Here are some tips to help you cover your bald head after chemo:

1. Choose Comfortable Materials

Choose head coverings that are made from comfortable materials, such as cotton or bamboo. These materials will be gentle on your scalp and won’t cause irritation or discomfort.

2. Experiment with Different Styles

Try different styles of head coverings to find one that suits your preferences. Experiment with different colors, patterns, and materials until you find one that you feel comfortable wearing.

3. Use Accessories

Use accessories like earrings, necklaces, and scarves to add some flair to your head covering. This will help you feel more confident and stylish.

4. Be Confident

Remember that baldness is a temporary side effect of chemo. Be confident in yourself and your journey, and don’t be afraid to show off your bald head if you feel comfortable doing so.

FAQs About Covering Bald Head After Chemo

Q: Is it necessary to cover my bald head after chemo?

A: No, it is not necessary to cover your bald head after chemo. It is a personal choice and depends on your comfort level.

Q: How can I make my head covering stay in place?

A: Use bobby pins or hair clips to secure your head covering in place. You can also use a hairband or scarf to keep it in place.

Q: How often should I wash my wig?

A: It is recommended to wash your wig every 6-8 wears or as needed. Use a wig shampoo and conditioner to keep it clean and in good condition.

Hair Care After Chemo

After chemo, it is important to take care of your hair and scalp. Here are some tips:

1. Be Gentle

Be gentle with your hair and scalp when washing and brushing. Use a wide-tooth comb to avoid pulling or tugging.

2. Use Mild Shampoos

Use a mild shampoo and conditioner to avoid drying out your hair and scalp. Look for products that are free of sulfates and harsh chemicals.

3. Avoid Heat Styling

Avoid heat styling tools like curling irons and straighteners, as they can damage your hair. Allow your hair to air dry or use a low heat setting on your blow dryer.

4. Eat a Healthy Diet

Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth. Foods like leafy greens, nuts, and eggs are all great choices.

Remember, covering a bald head after chemo is a personal choice. Whether you choose to wear a hat, scarf, or wig, the most important thing is to feel comfortable and confident in your own skin. With these tips and tricks, you can find a head covering that suits your style and preferences and feel confident after treatment.
