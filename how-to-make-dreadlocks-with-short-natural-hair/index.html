How To Make Dreadlocks With Short Natural Hair

Written by William May 18, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of the same old hairstyle? Do you want to try something new and exciting? Why not try dreadlocks? Dreadlocks are a great way to add some personality and style to your hair. In this blog post, we will show you how to make dreadlocks with short natural hair. Follow these steps and you'll have a brand new hairstyle in no time!

Description:

Dreadlocks are a type of hairstyle that involves twisting and matting sections of hair to create a rope-like appearance. This style is popular among people of all ages and cultures. Dreadlocks can be made with any hair type, including short natural hair. In this blog post, we will show you how to make dreadlocks with short natural hair using a simple and easy method.

How to:

Step 1: Wash your hair

Before you start making your dreadlocks, wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo. This will remove any product buildup and make your hair easier to work with. Rinse your hair thoroughly and towel dry.

Step 2: Section your hair

Using a comb, section your hair into small, even sections. The size of the sections will depend on how thick you want your dreadlocks to be. Secure each section with a hair tie or clip.

Step 3: Apply dread wax

Apply a small amount of dread wax to each section of hair. Dread wax helps to hold the hair in place and encourage the locking process. Be careful not to use too much wax, as this can cause buildup and make your hair look greasy.

Step 4: Twist your hair

Starting at the roots, twist each section of hair in a clockwise direction. Continue twisting until the entire section is tightly coiled. Repeat this process for each section of hair.

Step 5: Palm roll your hair

Once you have twisted all of your hair, use the palms of your hands to roll each section back and forth. This will help to smooth out any bumps and encourage the hair to lock together.

Step 6: Let your hair dry

Allow your hair to air dry or use a hair dryer on a low heat setting. Do not use a high heat setting, as this can damage your hair and undo all of your hard work.

Step 7: Maintain your dreadlocks

To maintain your dreadlocks, you will need to re-twist your hair every few weeks. You can also use a dreadlock maintenance spray to help keep your hair looking neat and tidy.

Tips:

  • Be patient. It can take several months for your dreadlocks to fully mature.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can cause buildup in your hair.
  • Wear a scarf or wrap your hair at night to prevent your dreadlocks from unraveling.
  • Experiment with different styles and accessories to make your dreadlocks truly unique.

FAQ:

Q: Can I make dreadlocks with short natural hair?

A: Yes, you can make dreadlocks with any hair type, including short natural hair.

Q: How long does it take for dreadlocks to form?

A: It can take several months for your dreadlocks to fully mature.

Q: Do I need to use dread wax?

A: No, you do not need to use dread wax. However, it can be helpful in holding your hair in place and encouraging the locking process.

Hair Care:

To keep your dreadlocks looking their best, it's important to take care of your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a clarifying shampoo to remove buildup.
  • Avoid using heavy oils or products that can cause buildup in your hair.
  • Use a dreadlock maintenance spray to help keep your hair looking neat and tidy.
  • Wear a scarf or wrap your hair at night to prevent your dreadlocks from unraveling.

Now that you know how to make dreadlocks with short natural hair, it's time to get started! Follow these steps and you'll have a brand new hairstyle in no time. Remember to be patient and take care of your hair to keep your dreadlocks looking their best.
