How To Fix Thick Hair Tips Tricks And Hair Care

Written by William Sep 06, 2023 · 6 min read

Thick hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it can make your hair look full and healthy, it can also be difficult to manage and style. If you're struggling to tame your thick locks, don't worry – there are plenty of ways to fix thick hair and make it more manageable.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Fix Thick Hair
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Managing Thick Hair
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care for Thick Hair

How to Fix Thick Hair

Fixing thick hair starts with finding the right haircut for your hair type. While many people with thick hair opt for long, flowing locks, a shorter cut with layers can help reduce bulk and make your hair more manageable. A good hairstylist can help you find the perfect cut for your hair type and texture.

Another way to fix thick hair is to use the right products. Look for products specifically designed for thick hair, such as volumizing shampoos and conditioners. These products can help add volume and reduce frizz. Avoid heavy, oily products that can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to fixing thick hair:

  1. Start by finding the right haircut for your hair type. A shorter cut with layers can help reduce bulk and make your hair more manageable.
  2. Use the right products for your hair type. Look for volumizing shampoos and conditioners designed for thick hair.
  3. Avoid heavy, oily products that can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  4. Blow dry your hair with a round brush to add volume and reduce frizz.
  5. If you need to straighten your hair, use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage.
  6. Use a light-hold hairspray or styling cream to keep your hair in place.
  7. If you're still struggling to manage your thick hair, consider getting a keratin treatment or other hair smoothing treatment.

Tips for Managing Thick Hair

Here are some additional tips for managing thick hair:

  • Don't wash your hair every day. Washing too often can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it more difficult to manage. Aim to wash every 2-3 days.
  • Avoid brushing your hair too often, as this can lead to breakage and frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.
  • Incorporate regular hair masks into your hair care routine. These can help keep your hair hydrated and reduce frizz.
  • Consider getting regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about fixing thick hair:

Q: Can I thin out my thick hair?

A: Yes, a hairstylist can use thinning shears or a razor to remove some of the bulk from your hair.

Q: Can I use a straightening iron on thick hair?

A: Yes, but make sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage.

Q: Should I use a heavy conditioner on my thick hair?

A: No, a heavy conditioner can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy. Look for a lightweight conditioner designed for thick hair.

Hair Care for Thick Hair

In addition to the tips above, there are some other things you can do to care for your thick hair:

  • Avoid using hot tools too often, as this can lead to damage and breakage.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair and lead to breakage.
  • Incorporate regular scalp massages into your hair care routine to promote healthy hair growth.

With the right haircut, products, and hair care routine, you can fix thick hair and make it more manageable. Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles and products until you find what works best for you.
