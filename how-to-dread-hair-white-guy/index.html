How To Dread Hair White Guy A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy May 07, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're a white guy looking to dread your hair, you may be wondering where to start. Dreading hair requires patience and dedication, but the end result is worth it. In this guide, we'll go over the steps you need to take to dread your hair as a white guy.

Description

Dreadlocks are a popular hairstyle that have been around for centuries. They are created by matting hair together and allowing it to lock and form into dreadlocks. Dreads can be worn by anyone, regardless of their hair type or ethnicity. However, white guys may need to take some extra steps to get their hair to dread properly.

How to Dread Hair White Guy: Step by Step

Step 1: Stop Cutting Your Hair

The first step to dreading your hair is to stop cutting it. Dreads need length to form properly, so let your hair grow out. This may take a few months, depending on how short your hair is currently.

Step 2: Clean Your Hair

Before you start dreading your hair, make sure it's clean. Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any products or buildup. This will help your hair lock together better.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Next, section your hair into small, even sections. You can use a comb to create precise sections or just use your fingers to separate your hair. The size of your sections will determine the size of your dreads, so choose your section size accordingly.

Step 4: Backcomb Your Hair

Take one of your sections and backcomb it. Start at the root and comb towards the ends. This will create tangles and knots in your hair, which is what will eventually form into dreads.

Step 5: Twist Your Hair

After you've backcombed your hair, twist it. Twisting your hair will help the knots form together and begin to lock. You can use a crochet hook or your fingers to twist your hair.

Step 6: Repeat

Repeat steps 4 and 5 for each section of your hair. Take your time and be patient. Dreading your hair can take several hours or even days, depending on the length and thickness of your hair.

Step 7: Maintain Your Dreads

Once your hair is dreaded, you'll need to maintain your dreads to keep them looking good. This includes washing your hair regularly, separating any knots that may be trying to merge together, and palm rolling your dreads to keep them tight.

Tips for Dreading Hair as a White Guy

Here are some additional tips to help you dread your hair as a white guy:

  • Use a residue-free shampoo to avoid buildup
  • Avoid using conditioner, as it can loosen your dreads
  • Be patient - dreading your hair takes time
  • Use a crochet hook or interlocking tool to tighten loose dreads
  • Wear a shower cap or wrap your dreads in a towel when showering to avoid getting them wet

FAQ

How long does it take to dread hair?

Dreading hair can take anywhere from a few hours to several days, depending on the length and thickness of your hair. Be patient and take your time to ensure your dreads form properly.

Can I wash my dreads?

Yes, you can wash your dreads. In fact, it's important to wash them regularly to keep them clean and healthy. Use a residue-free shampoo and avoid using conditioner, as it can loosen your dreads.

Can I dread my hair if it's thin or fine?

Yes, you can still dread your hair if it's thin or fine. However, you may need to use a different method to create your dreads, such as the twist and rip method or the backcombing method combined with wax. Talk to a professional stylist for advice on the best method for your hair type.

Hair Care for Dreads

Keeping your dreads healthy and looking good requires some hair care. Here are some tips to care for your dreads:

  • Wash your dreads regularly with a residue-free shampoo
  • Avoid using conditioner, as it can loosen your dreads
  • Keep your dreads dry - wear a shower cap or wrap your dreads in a towel when showering
  • Separate any knots that may be trying to merge together to avoid congoing
  • Palm roll your dreads regularly to keep them tight

With these tips and steps, you should be able to successfully dread your hair as a white guy. Remember to be patient and take your time, and your dreads will look great in no time.
