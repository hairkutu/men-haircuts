What Is A Soft Undercut Haircut

Written by Frank Aug 08, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Soft undercut haircut is a trendy hairstyle that has been gaining popularity in recent years. It is a type of haircut that features short hair on the sides and back, and long hair on top. The hair on top is usually cut in layers, which gives it a textured and voluminous look. The soft undercut is a versatile hairstyle that can be styled in many different ways, making it a favorite among men and women alike.

Description of a Soft Undercut Haircut

A soft undercut haircut is a type of haircut where the hair is cut shorter on the sides and back, while the hair on the top is left longer. The hair on top is usually cut in layers, which gives it a textured and voluminous look. The haircut is called a "soft" undercut because there is no harsh line between the short hair on the sides and back and the longer hair on top. This creates a seamless transition between the short and long hair, giving the haircut a natural and effortless look.

How to Get a Soft Undercut Haircut

If you want to get a soft undercut haircut, you should go to a professional hairstylist who has experience in cutting this type of haircut. When you get to the salon, you should bring a picture of the haircut you want to show the hairstylist. This will help the hairstylist understand what you want and give you the haircut you desire.

The hairstylist will start by cutting your hair shorter on the sides and back. They will then cut the hair on top in layers, which will give it a textured and voluminous look. The hairstylist will also blend the hair on the sides and back with the hair on top, creating a seamless transition between the short and long hair.

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling a Soft Undercut Haircut

Styling a soft undercut haircut is easy and can be done in many different ways. Here is a step-by-step guide to styling a soft undercut haircut:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

The first step to styling a soft undercut haircut is to wash your hair. Use a shampoo and conditioner that is suitable for your hair type.

Step 2: Towel Dry Your Hair

After washing your hair, towel dry it until it is damp. Do not rub your hair vigorously with the towel, as this can damage your hair.

Step 3: Apply Styling Product

Apply a styling product to your damp hair. You can use a mousse, gel, or pomade, depending on the look you want to achieve. Work the product through your hair evenly.

Step 4: Blow Dry Your Hair

Using a blow dryer, dry your hair while using your fingers to style it. Use a round brush to create volume at the top of your head.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

Once your hair is dry, style it in the way you want. You can use your fingers to create texture or a comb to create a more polished look.

Tips for Maintaining a Soft Undercut Haircut

Here are some tips for maintaining a soft undercut haircut:

  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape of the haircut.
  • Use a styling product that is suitable for your hair type.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can damage it.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and other environmental factors.

FAQs About Soft Undercut Haircuts

What is the difference between a soft undercut and a regular undercut?

The main difference between a soft undercut and a regular undercut is the presence of a seamless transition between the short hair on the sides and back and the longer hair on top. A regular undercut has a more noticeable line between the short and long hair.

What face shape is best for a soft undercut?

A soft undercut can work on many different face shapes. However, it is best suited for people with oval or heart-shaped faces.

Can women get a soft undercut?

Yes, women can get a soft undercut. In fact, it is a popular hairstyle for women who want a trendy and low-maintenance haircut.

Hair Care for Soft Undercut Haircuts

To take care of your soft undercut haircut, you should use a shampoo and conditioner that is suitable for your hair type. You should also use a styling product that is suitable for your hair type and avoid using too much heat on your hair. Protect your hair from the sun and other environmental factors, and get regular trims to maintain the shape of the haircut.
