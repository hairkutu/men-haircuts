How Long Do Dreads Grow In A Month

Written by Bardi Aug 30, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're thinking about getting dreadlocks or already have them, you may be wondering how long they'll grow in a month. The answer to this question depends on various factors, including your hair type, maintenance routine, and overall health. In this article, we'll explore everything you need to know about dreadlock growth and how to take care of your hair to promote healthy growth.

Table of Contents

  • How Long Do Dreads Grow in a Month?

  • Tips for Promoting Healthy Dreadlock Growth

How to Start Dreadlocks

Before we dive into the topic of dreadlock growth, let's first discuss how to start dreadlocks. There are several methods for starting dreadlocks, including the backcombing method, the twist and rip method, and the freeform method.

The backcombing method involves teasing small sections of hair towards the scalp using a fine-tooth comb. This method can be time-consuming and may require the help of a professional stylist.

The twist and rip method involves twisting small sections of hair and then pulling them apart to create dreadlocks. This method is less time-consuming than backcombing and can be done at home with the right tools.

The freeform method involves letting your hair naturally "loc" over time without any manipulation. This method is the most low-maintenance but can take the longest to achieve fully-formed dreadlocks.

How Long Do Dreads Grow in a Month?

Now, let's get to the main question: how long do dreads grow in a month? On average, dreadlocks can grow between 1/4 to 1/2 inch per month. However, this rate of growth can vary depending on several factors.

Factors That Affect Dreadlock Growth

Several factors can affect how long your dreadlocks grow in a month, including:

  • Hair Type: People with coarser, thicker hair may experience faster dreadlock growth than those with finer hair.
  • Maintenance Routine: Regularly washing and maintaining your dreadlocks can help promote healthy growth.
  • Overall Health: Your overall health and diet can also affect hair growth. Eating a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals can promote healthy hair growth.

Tips for Promoting Healthy Dreadlock Growth

If you want to promote healthy dreadlock growth, here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo to prevent buildup.
  • Avoid using heavy products or oils that can weigh down your hair and slow down growth.
  • Massage your scalp regularly to promote blood flow and stimulate hair growth.
  • Eat a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals, including B vitamins, vitamin D, and iron.

How to Maintain Your Dreadlocks

Maintaining your dreadlocks is essential for promoting healthy growth and preventing breakage. Here are some tips for maintaining your dreadlocks:

  • Don't twist or pull your dreadlocks too tightly, as this can cause breakage and slow down growth.
  • Use a crochet hook or interlocking tool to tighten loose hairs and prevent frizz.
  • Be gentle when washing your dreadlocks, and avoid scrubbing too hard or using hot water.
  • Protect your dreadlocks while sleeping by wearing a silk or satin scarf.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I wash my dreadlocks?

You should wash your dreadlocks at least once a week to prevent buildup and promote healthy growth.

Can I color my dreadlocks?

Yes, you can color your dreadlocks, but it's important to use a dye that's safe for dreadlocks and won't cause buildup or damage.

Can I swim with dreadlocks?

Yes, you can swim with dreadlocks, but it's important to rinse your hair thoroughly with clean water after swimming to prevent chlorine buildup.

Final Thoughts

In conclusion, dreadlocks can grow between 1/4 to 1/2 inch per month on average, but this rate of growth can vary depending on various factors. By following a healthy maintenance routine and taking care of your overall health, you can promote healthy dreadlock growth and achieve the length you desire.
