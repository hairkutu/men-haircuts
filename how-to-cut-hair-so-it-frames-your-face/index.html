How To Cut Hair So It Frames Your Face A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Apr 08, 2023 · 6 min read

Are you tired of your hair falling flat around your face? Do you want to create a more flattering and voluminous look? Cutting your hair so it frames your face is a great way to achieve this. In this article, we'll show you how to cut your hair to frame your face in a relaxed English language, complete with tips, FAQs, and hair care advice.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Cut Hair So It Frames Your Face
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Cutting Hair to Frame Your Face
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care Advice

How to Cut Hair So It Frames Your Face

The first step to cutting your hair so it frames your face is to determine your face shape. This will help you decide which style will best flatter your features. The most common face shapes are:

  • Oval
  • Round
  • Square
  • Heart-shaped
  • Diamond-shaped

Once you have determined your face shape, you can choose a hairstyle that will frame your face in a flattering way. This may involve layering your hair, creating bangs, or cutting your hair to a specific length.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to cutting your hair so it frames your face:

  1. Wash and dry your hair.
  2. Comb your hair straight down and create a center part.
  3. Section your hair into four parts (two in front and two in back).
  4. Starting with the front sections, comb your hair forward and hold it between your fingers.
  5. Cut the hair to the desired length, using the front sections as a guide for the rest of your hair.
  6. Move to the back sections and repeat the process, using the front sections as a guide.
  7. Check your work in the mirror, making any necessary adjustments.
  8. Style your hair as desired.
  9. Trim your hair every six to eight weeks to maintain the style.

Tips for Cutting Hair to Frame Your Face

Here are some tips to keep in mind when cutting your hair to frame your face:

  • Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends.
  • Don't cut too much at once – it's better to take off less and make adjustments as needed.
  • Start with dry hair, as wet hair can be misleading.
  • If you have curly hair, cut it when it's dry and in its natural form.
  • Experiment with different styles to find the one that works best for you.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting hair to frame your face:

Is it better to cut hair when it's wet or dry?

It's generally better to cut hair when it's dry, as wet hair can be misleading and may result in a shorter cut than intended.

How often should I trim my hair?

It's recommended to trim your hair every six to eight weeks to maintain the style and keep split ends at bay.

What if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

If you make a mistake while cutting your hair, don't panic. You can always make adjustments or visit a professional stylist for help.

Hair Care Advice

Cutting your hair to frame your face is just the first step – it's important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some basic hair care tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner suited to your hair type.
  • Don't wash your hair every day, as this can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep split ends at bay.
  • Eat a healthy diet and stay hydrated to promote hair growth.

Now that you know how to cut your hair to frame your face, you can experiment with different styles and find the one that works best for you. Remember to take good care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and beautiful.
