Is It Normal For My Dog To Lose A Lot Of Hair

Written by Eveline May 04, 2023 Â· 7 min read

As pet owners, we always want our furry friends to look their best. A shiny, lustrous coat is a sign of good health and vitality. But what if you notice that your dog is losing a lot of hair? Is this normal, or should you be concerned? In this article, we'll explore the reasons why dogs lose hair, when it's normal, and when you should seek veterinary care.

Why Do Dogs Lose Hair?

There are several reasons why your dog might be losing hair:

1. Shedding

Most dogs shed their coats twice a year, in the spring and fall. During this time, they'll lose a lot of hair as their bodies prepare for the change in seasons. This is a normal process, and you shouldn't be too concerned if you notice a lot of hair around your home.

2. Allergies

Dogs can be allergic to a wide range of things, including certain foods, pollen, dust mites, and flea bites. Allergies can cause your dog to scratch and bite at their skin, leading to hair loss. If you notice that your dog is scratching a lot or has red, inflamed skin, it's important to see your veterinarian.

3. Infections

Infections can also cause hair loss in dogs. Bacterial and fungal infections can cause skin irritation and hair loss, as can parasitic infections like mange. If you notice that your dog has scaly, flaky skin or is losing hair in patches, it's important to see your veterinarian.

When is it Normal for Dogs to Lose Hair?

As we mentioned earlier, shedding is a normal process for dogs. If your dog is shedding a lot, especially during the spring and fall, it's probably nothing to worry about. However, if you notice that your dog is losing hair outside of shedding season, or if the hair loss is accompanied by other symptoms like itching or redness, it's important to see your veterinarian.

How to Care for Your Dog's Coat

There are several things you can do to help keep your dog's coat healthy:

1. Brush your dog regularly

Regular brushing helps remove loose hair and distribute natural oils throughout your dog's coat. This can help keep their coat shiny and healthy.

2. Feed your dog a healthy diet

A nutritious diet can help keep your dog's coat healthy from the inside out. Make sure your dog's food is high-quality and contains all the essential nutrients they need.

3. Bathe your dog as needed

Dogs don't need to be bathed as often as humans do, but it's important to keep them clean. Use a gentle dog shampoo and avoid over-bathing, as this can strip their coat of natural oils.

FAQ About Dogs Losing Hair

Q: Is it normal for dogs to lose hair in the summer?

A: Yes, dogs can lose hair in the summer as part of their shedding process. However, if you notice excessive hair loss or other symptoms, it's important to see your veterinarian.

Q: Can stress cause hair loss in dogs?

A: Yes, stress can cause hair loss in dogs. If you've recently moved, added a new pet to the household, or experienced other changes, your dog may be experiencing stress.

Q: Can I use human shampoo on my dog?

A: No, you should never use human shampoo on your dog. Human shampoos are formulated for human hair, which has a different pH balance than dog hair. This can cause skin irritation and other problems.

Conclusion

In most cases, it's normal for dogs to lose hair as part of the shedding process. However, if you notice excessive hair loss or other symptoms, it's important to see your veterinarian. By taking good care of your dog's coat, you can help keep them healthy and happy for years to come.
