How To Flatten Thick Short Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Eveline Jun 08, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you struggling with thick short hair that seems impossible to flatten? Do you find yourself spending hours on end trying to tame your unruly locks? If so, you’ve come to the right place. In this article, we’ll be sharing some tips and tricks on how to successfully flatten thick short hair. From understanding hair types to recommended hair care routines, we’ve got you covered.

Description

Flattening thick short hair can be a challenge, especially if you’re unsure of where to start. But with the right techniques, products, and tools, you can achieve the sleek, straight look you desire. In this article, we’ll be breaking down the steps you need to take to get there.

How To

Step 1: Determine Your Hair Type

Before you can begin to flatten your hair, it’s important to understand your hair type. This will help you choose the right products and tools to achieve the look you want. Is your hair naturally curly or straight? Is it fine or thick? Understanding your hair type will make all the difference in achieving perfectly flat hair.

Step 2: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup or residue. This will help your hair look smoother and more manageable. Rinse thoroughly and follow up with a conditioner to help detangle your hair and add moisture.

Step 3: Use a Heat Protectant

Before using any heat tools on your hair, it’s important to use a heat protectant. This will help prevent damage and breakage, which can lead to frizz and flyaways. Apply the heat protectant evenly throughout your hair, paying special attention to the ends.

Step 4: Blow Dry Your Hair

Using a hair dryer, start blow drying your hair from the roots to the ends. Use a round brush to help smooth out your hair as you go. Make sure to dry your hair completely before moving on to the next step.

Step 5: Straighten Your Hair

Using a flat iron, start straightening your hair in small sections. Make sure to clamp down firmly and pull the iron through your hair slowly. Repeat this process until your entire head of hair is straight.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when trying to flatten thick short hair:

  • Use a high-quality flat iron with adjustable heat settings
  • Always use a heat protectant
  • Blow dry your hair in sections to make it easier to manage
  • Don’t forget to use a hair serum or oil to add shine and reduce frizz
  • Use a boar bristle brush to help smooth out your hair

FAQ

Q: Can I flatten my hair without a flat iron?

A: While a flat iron is the most effective tool for flattening hair, you can also achieve similar results with a blow dryer and round brush. Simply blow dry your hair in sections, using the round brush to smooth out your hair as you go.

Q: Can I use a flat iron on wet hair?

A: No, it’s not recommended to use a flat iron on wet hair. Wet hair is more prone to damage and breakage, so it’s important to dry your hair completely before using any heat tools.

Q: How often should I flatten my hair?

A: It’s not recommended to flat iron your hair every day, as this can lead to damage and breakage. Try to limit flat ironing to once or twice a week, and use a heat protectant every time.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential for achieving and maintaining flat hair. Here are some tips to keep your hair looking its best:

  • Use a clarifying shampoo once a week to remove buildup
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized
  • Avoid using heat tools every day
  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as bleach or hair dye

Conclusion

Flattening thick short hair may seem like a daunting task, but with the right techniques and products, it’s definitely achievable. Remember to take care of your hair, use a heat protectant, and limit your use of heat tools to prevent damage and breakage. And most importantly, have fun experimenting with different styles!
