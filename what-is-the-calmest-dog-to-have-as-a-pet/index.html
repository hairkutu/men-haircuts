What Is The Calmest Dog To Have As A Pet

Written by Petter Apr 21, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you looking for a furry companion that won’t leave you feeling stressed or anxious? If so, you may be wondering what is the calmest dog to have as a pet. In this article, we’ll explore some of the most relaxed and easy-going dog breeds out there. We’ll also provide tips on how to care for your new pup and keep them happy and healthy.

Description

When it comes to finding a calm dog breed, there are a few factors to consider. First, you’ll want to look for breeds that are known for their laid-back personalities. Second, you’ll want to consider the dog’s energy level and exercise needs. While some dogs may be naturally calm, they may still require plenty of exercise and stimulation to keep them happy.

Some of the calmest dog breeds include:

  • English Bulldog
  • Basset Hound
  • Cavalier King Charles Spaniel
  • Greyhound
  • Newfoundland
  • Great Dane
  • Bernese Mountain Dog
  • Shih Tzu

Of course, every dog is different, so it’s important to spend time with individual dogs before making a decision. You may also want to consider adopting an adult dog, as their personalities are typically more established than puppies.

How to Choose a Calm Dog

When choosing a calm dog breed, there are a few things to keep in mind:

  • Consider your lifestyle: If you don’t have a lot of time for exercise, a high-energy dog may not be the best fit.
  • Think about your living situation: Some dogs, like Great Danes, may require more space than others.
  • Research breed-specific health issues: Some breeds may be more prone to certain health issues, so it’s important to be aware of these before bringing a dog home.
  • Visit a reputable breeder or shelter: A good breeder or shelter should be able to provide information about the dog’s temperament and energy level.

Step by Step: Bringing Your Calm Dog Home

Once you’ve found the perfect calm dog for your lifestyle, it’s important to make their transition to their new home as smooth as possible. Here are some steps to follow:

  1. Prepare your home: Make sure your home is safe for your new dog. This may include things like securing loose wires and removing toxic plants.
  2. Stock up on supplies: You’ll need things like food, water bowls, a bed, and toys.
  3. Introduce your dog to their new home slowly: Allow your dog to explore their new space at their own pace.
  4. Establish a routine: Dogs thrive on routine, so try to establish a consistent schedule for feeding, exercise, and playtime.
  5. Train your dog: Even calm dogs need basic training to ensure they’re well-behaved and obedient.

Tips for Keeping Your Calm Dog Happy and Healthy

Once your new dog has settled into their new home, it’s important to keep them healthy and happy. Here are some tips:

  • Provide plenty of exercise: While calm dogs may not need as much exercise as high-energy dogs, they still need daily walks and playtime.
  • Feed a healthy diet: A balanced diet is important for your dog’s overall health.
  • Keep up with veterinary check-ups: Regular check-ups can help catch any health issues early.
  • Provide mental stimulation: Even calm dogs need mental stimulation to prevent boredom.
  • Groom regularly: Depending on the breed, your dog may require regular grooming to keep their coat healthy and free of mats.

FAQ: What is the Calmest Dog to Have as a Pet?

What is the most relaxed dog breed?

Some of the most relaxed dog breeds include English Bulldogs, Basset Hounds, and Cavalier King Charles Spaniels.

Do all calm dogs require less exercise?

No, every dog is different. While some calm dogs may require less exercise than high-energy dogs, they still need daily walks and playtime to stay healthy and happy.

Can calm dogs still be trained?

Yes, even calm dogs need basic training to ensure they’re well-behaved and obedient.

Hair Care for Your Calm Dog

Depending on the breed, your calm dog may require regular grooming to keep their coat healthy and free of mats. Here are some tips:

  • Brush your dog’s coat regularly: This can help prevent mats and tangles.
  • Bathe your dog as needed: Depending on the breed, your dog may need regular baths to keep their coat clean.
  • Trim your dog’s nails: Long nails can be uncomfortable for your dog and may cause issues with mobility.
  • Clean your dog’s ears: Regular ear cleaning can help prevent infections.

With a little research and preparation, you can find the perfect calm dog for your lifestyle. Whether you’re looking for a lazy couch companion or a calm dog to take on leisurely walks, there’s a breed out there for everyone.
