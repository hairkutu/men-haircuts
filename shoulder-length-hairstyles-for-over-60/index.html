Shoulder Length Hairstyles For Over 60 A Guide To Flattering And Easy To Manage Hair

Written by Frank Sep 10, 2023 Â· 9 min read

As we age, our hair often becomes thinner, drier, and more difficult to manage. While some women may choose to cut their hair short to avoid dealing with these issues, others prefer to maintain a longer length for the sake of variety and versatility. If you're over 60 and looking for a shoulder length hairstyle that's both flattering and easy to care for, this guide is for you.

  • Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Shoulder Length Hair

How to Choose the Perfect Shoulder Length Hairstyle

When it comes to choosing the right shoulder length hairstyle, there are a few factors to consider:

Face Shape

Just like with any hairstyle, the shape of your face will play a role in determining which shoulder length cut will be most flattering. An oval face shape is considered the most versatile, as it can pull off almost any style. If you have a round face, you may want to opt for a layered cut that adds volume and height. Square faces can benefit from softer, more rounded styles, while those with heart-shaped faces may want to consider a side-swept fringe or layers that frame the face.

Hair Texture

If your hair is naturally straight, a blunt shoulder length cut may be the way to go. If you have wavy or curly hair, you may want to opt for layers that will help to control frizz and add definition to your curls. If your hair is thinning or fine, consider a layered cut that adds volume and texture.

Lifestyle

Finally, consider your lifestyle when choosing a shoulder length hairstyle. If you're an active person who likes to exercise or spend time outdoors, you may want a low-maintenance style that can be easily tied back or tucked behind your ears. If you have a more formal or professional lifestyle, you may want a sleek and polished shoulder length cut that can be easily styled for any occasion.

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Shoulder Length Hair

Once you've chosen the perfect shoulder length hairstyle, it's important to know how to style it. Here's a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Start with clean, towel-dried hair

Before you start styling your hair, make sure it's clean and towel-dried. Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair and remove any knots or tangles.

Step 2: Apply a styling product

Apply a styling product like mousse or styling cream to your hair to add volume and texture. Be sure to work the product through your hair evenly, from roots to ends.

Step 3: Blow-dry your hair

Using a round brush, blow-dry your hair in sections. Focus on lifting the roots to add volume and smooth out any frizz or flyaways.

Step 4: Style your hair as desired

Once your hair is dry, you can style it as desired. You may want to add some loose curls or waves with a curling iron, or simply leave it straight and sleek.

Tips for Maintaining Healthy and Beautiful Hair

Here are some tips to help you keep your shoulder length hair looking its best:

Tip 1: Get regular trims

Regular trims will help to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

Tip 2: Use a deep conditioning treatment

Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy. Look for products that contain nourishing ingredients like argan oil, coconut oil, or shea butter.

Tip 3: Protect your hair from heat damage

When using heat styling tools like curling irons or straighteners, be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage to your hair.

FAQ About Shoulder Length Hairstyles for Over 60

Q: Can I still wear my hair long after 60?

A: Absolutely! There's no age limit on long hair. However, some women find that as they age, their hair becomes thinner and more difficult to manage, and may choose to cut it shorter for ease of styling.

Q: Are bangs a good idea for women over 60?

A: Bangs can be a great way to update your look and add some interest to your hairstyle. However, if you have fine or thinning hair, you may want to avoid heavy, blunt bangs that can be difficult to style.

Q: Can I still color my hair after 60?

A: Absolutely! There's no age limit on hair color. However, if you're concerned about damage or breakage, consider opting for a semi-permanent or temporary color that's gentler on your hair.

Hair Care Tips for Older Women

Here are some additional hair care tips for older women:

Tip 1: Use a gentle shampoo

As we age, our scalp may become more sensitive. Look for a gentle shampoo that's free of sulfates and other harsh chemicals.

Tip 2: Avoid over-washing your hair

Washing your hair too frequently can strip it of its natural oils, leaving it dry and brittle. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days.

Tip 3: Protect your hair from the sun

Just like your skin, your hair can be damaged by the sun's UV rays. Wear a hat or use a hair product with UV protection when spending time outdoors.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to find the perfect shoulder length hairstyle for your over 60 lifestyle and keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful for years to come.
