How To Layer Medium Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Jul 28, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're looking to switch up your medium-length hair without sacrificing length, layering is a great option. Layers can add texture, volume, and movement to your hair, making it look fresh and modern. While getting a professional haircut is always recommended, it's possible to layer medium hair at home with the right tools and techniques. In this blog post, we'll go over how to layer medium hair at home, including tips and tricks for achieving a salon-worthy result.

Description: What is Layering?

Layering is a haircutting technique where different lengths of hair are cut throughout the hair, creating depth and texture. Layers can be short or long and can be added to any hair length, including medium hair. Layering is a great way to add volume and movement to hair, making it look less flat and more dynamic. When done correctly, layering can accentuate your face shape and features, making it a popular choice for many people.

How to: Layering Medium Hair at Home

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to layer medium hair at home:

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start layering your hair, make sure you have the right tools on hand. You'll need a pair of sharp scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section your hair. It's also a good idea to have a mirror and good lighting so you can see what you're doing.

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair as you normally would. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start layering, as wet hair can be more difficult to cut evenly.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Using your comb, section your hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back. Clip each section so it's out of the way while you work on the others.

Step 4: Start Layering

Take a section of hair from the back and comb it straight down. Hold the scissors at a slight angle and cut into the hair, starting at the length you want the shortest layer to be. Work your way down the section, cutting at the same angle until you reach the length you want the longest layer to be. Repeat this process on the other sections of hair, making sure to cut evenly and regularly.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Once you've cut all the layers, use your scissors to blend them together. Take small sections of hair and gently cut into them, creating a seamless transition between layers.

Step 6: Check Your Work

Once you've finished layering, check your work in the mirror. Make sure the layers are even and balanced, and adjust as needed.

Tips for Layering Medium Hair at Home

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results when layering medium hair at home:

  • Start with small sections of hair and cut slowly to avoid making mistakes
  • Keep your scissors sharp for a cleaner cut
  • Don't cut too much hair at once - it's better to cut a little at a time and build up the layers gradually
  • When blending the layers, use your fingers to feel for any unevenness and cut accordingly

FAQs: Layering Medium Hair at Home

Here are some frequently asked questions about layering medium hair at home:

Q: Can I layer my hair if it's curly?

A: Yes, you can layer curly hair! Just make sure you cut the layers when the hair is dry and in its natural state, as curly hair can shrink when it dries.

Q: How often should I layer my hair?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly you want to change up your look. On average, people get their hair layered every 6-8 weeks.

Hair Care Tips for Layered Hair

Now that you've layered your hair, it's important to take good care of it to keep it looking its best. Here are some hair care tips for layered hair:

  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and hydrated
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair and make it look frizzy
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape and health of your layers
  • Consider using a hair mask or treatment once a week to keep your hair nourished and shiny

Layering medium hair at home can be a fun and easy way to switch up your look. By following these steps and tips, you can achieve a salon-worthy result without leaving your house. Just remember to take your time, be patient, and have fun!
