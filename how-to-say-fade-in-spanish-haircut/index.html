How To Say Fade In Spanish Haircut In Relaxed English Language

Written by Frank Aug 15, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Are you planning to get a fade haircut in a Spanish-speaking country but don't know how to say it in Spanish? In this post, we'll teach you how to say "fade" in Spanish and provide you with some tips on how to communicate your desired haircut with your barber or stylist. Let's get started!

How to Say Fade in Spanish?

The word "fade" in Spanish is "degradado". You can pronounce it as "deh-grah-dah-doh". It's the most common term used by Spanish-speaking barbers and hairstylists to describe this type of haircut. However, some Spanish-speaking countries might use different terms, so it's best to research the local slang before visiting a barbershop.

How to Communicate Your Desired Haircut?

It's essential to communicate your desired haircut clearly to your barber or stylist, especially if there's a language barrier. Here are some tips on how to do it:

1. Show Pictures

One of the best ways to communicate your desired haircut is by showing pictures. You can bring a photo of the haircut you want or search for it on your phone. This will give your barber or stylist a clear idea of what you're looking for.

2. Use Hand Gestures

If you don't have a photo, you can use hand gestures to describe the haircut. For example, you can use your fingers to show how short you want the sides or how long you want the top. You can also use facial expressions to show the style you're going for.

3. Use Simple Language

Using simple language is crucial when communicating with someone who doesn't speak your language fluently. Try to use basic words and avoid using slang or technical terms. Stick to simple sentences like "short on the sides and long on the top" or "fade on the sides and textured on the top".

Step by Step Guide for Getting a Fade Haircut

Now that you know how to say "fade" in Spanish and how to communicate your desired haircut let's go through the step-by-step process of getting a fade haircut:

1. Choose the Right Barber or Stylist

Choosing the right barber or stylist is essential for getting the perfect fade haircut. Look for someone who has experience with fades and has good reviews from previous clients.

2. Communicate Your Desired Haircut

Once you've found the right barber or stylist, communicate your desired haircut clearly. Use the tips we've provided above to ensure that there's no miscommunication.

3. Prepare Your Hair

Before your barber or stylist starts cutting your hair, make sure it's clean and dry. Wash your hair the day before and avoid using any styling products on the day of your appointment.

4. Start with the Sides

The barber or stylist will start with the sides, using clippers to create a gradual fade from short to long. They'll use different guard sizes to achieve the desired length.

5. Work on the Top

Once the sides are done, the barber or stylist will work on the top, using scissors to cut and shape the hair. They'll follow your desired style and texture.

6. Blend the Hair

Finally, the barber or stylist will blend the hair on the sides and the top, making sure that there are no harsh lines or transitions.

7. Style the Hair

After the haircut, the barber or stylist will style your hair using the products you prefer. They'll show you how to maintain your new haircut at home.

Tips for Maintaining Your Fade Haircut

Now that you have your new fade haircut, you'll want to maintain it properly. Here are some tips to help you:

1. Schedule Regular Haircuts

A fade haircut requires regular maintenance to keep it looking fresh. Schedule your next haircut every 4-6 weeks.

2. Use the Right Products

Use hair products that suit your hair type and hairstyle. Ask your barber or stylist for recommendations.

3. Wash and Condition Your Hair Regularly

Wash and condition your hair regularly to keep it healthy and clean. Use a mild shampoo and conditioner that doesn't strip your hair of its natural oils.

4. Protect Your Hair from Sun and Heat

Protect your hair from the sun and heat by wearing a hat or using a heat protectant spray when styling your hair with hot tools.

FAQ

1. Is "degradado" the only term used for "fade" in Spanish?

No, there might be different terms used in different Spanish-speaking countries. It's best to research the local slang before visiting a barbershop.

2. Can I get a fade haircut with curly hair?

Yes, a fade haircut can be done on curly hair. Your barber or stylist will adjust the technique and guard sizes to suit your hair type.

3. How often should I wash my hair after getting a fade haircut?

You should wash your hair every 2-3 days to keep it clean and healthy. Avoid washing it every day as it can strip your hair of its natural oils.

We hope this post has helped you learn how to say "fade" in Spanish and communicate your desired haircut with your barber or stylist. Remember to follow the tips for maintaining your fade haircut to keep it looking fresh and stylish. Happy grooming!
