How To Put Lister Clipper Blades On A Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Apr 23, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you're a horse owner or groomer, you know the importance of keeping your clipper blades sharp and in good condition. Lister clipper blades are a popular choice for many, but putting them on properly can be a bit tricky. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps of how to put Lister clipper blades on so you can get back to grooming in no time.

Description

Clipper blades are an essential tool for horse grooming, but they dull over time and need to be replaced. Lister clipper blades are a popular choice for many horse owners and groomers due to their durability and quality. However, replacing the blades can be a bit of a challenge if you're not familiar with the process. This guide will show you how to put Lister clipper blades on in a few simple steps.

How To: Put Lister Clipper Blades On

Step 1: Turn off the Clipper

Before attempting to put the new Lister clipper blade on, make sure the clipper is turned off and unplugged. This will help prevent any accidents or injuries while handling the blade.

Step 2: Remove the Old Blade

Using a screwdriver, remove the screws that hold the old blade in place. Once the screws are removed, carefully lift the old blade off the clipper. Be sure to set it aside in a safe place, as it can be reused or sent in for sharpening.

Step 3: Clean the Clipper

With the old blade removed, take a moment to clean the clipper. Use a soft brush to remove any hair or debris that may have accumulated on the clipper. This will help ensure that the new blade goes on smoothly and stays in place during use.

Step 4: Attach the New Blade

Take the new Lister clipper blade and align the screw holes with those on the clipper. Gently press the blade onto the clipper, making sure it is lined up properly. Once the blade is in place, use the screwdriver to tighten the screws securely.

Step 5: Test the Blade

Before using the clipper with the new blade, it's important to test it to make sure it is working properly. Turn the clipper on and let it run for a few seconds. If the blade is properly aligned and secured, it should run smoothly without any wobbling or shaking.

Tips for Putting Lister Clipper Blades On

Tip 1: Always make sure the clipper is turned off and unplugged before attempting to put the blade on or take it off.

Tip 2: If you're having trouble aligning the new blade with the clipper, try wiggling it a bit to find the proper position.

Tip 3: Be sure to tighten the screws securely to prevent the blade from coming loose during use.

FAQ

Q: Can I reuse my old Lister clipper blade?

A: Yes, you can reuse your old Lister clipper blade or send it in for sharpening.

Q: Do I need any special tools to put the Lister clipper blade on?

A: No, all you need is a screwdriver to remove the old blade and attach the new one.

Hair Care

Properly maintaining your clipper blades is essential for keeping your horse's coat looking its best. Regularly cleaning and oiling the blades will help prolong their lifespan and ensure they are working properly. In addition to replacing the blades when necessary, it's also important to keep them sharp to prevent any pulling or tugging on the horse's coat.

By following these simple steps for how to put Lister clipper blades on, you'll be able to keep your clipper in top condition and your horse looking its best.
