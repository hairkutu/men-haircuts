How to Care for Permed Hair After Shower

Perming your hair can be a great way to add volume, curls and texture. However, if you donât take care of your permed hair properly, it can become dry, brittle, and prone to breakage. Here's how to care for permed hair after shower:

1. Use a Wide-Toothed Comb

After washing your hair, use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair. This helps prevent breakage and damage to your permed hair. Start at the ends of your hair and work your way up to the roots, gently combing through any knots or tangles.

2. Don't Rub Your Hair with a Towel

When drying your hair, avoid rubbing it with a towel. Instead, gently squeeze out the excess water and pat your hair dry with a soft towel. Rubbing your hair with a towel can cause breakage and damage to your permed hair.

3. Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

After showering, apply a leave-in conditioner to help hydrate and protect your permed hair. This helps prevent breakage and damage to your hair. Apply the leave-in conditioner to the ends of your hair, working your way up to the roots.

4. Use a Heat Protectant

If you plan on using heat styling tools, such as a curling iron or straightener, make sure to use a heat protectant. This helps protect your permed hair from heat damage and prevents breakage.

Step-by-Step Guide to Caring for Permed Hair After a Shower

Here's a step-by-step guide to caring for permed hair after a shower:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Make sure to rinse your hair thoroughly to remove any product buildup.

Step 2: Detangle Your Hair

Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots.

Step 3: Pat Your Hair Dry

After showering, gently squeeze out the excess water and pat your hair dry with a soft towel.

Step 4: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to help hydrate and protect your permed hair.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

If you plan on using heat styling tools, make sure to use a heat protectant. Style your hair as desired.

Step 6: Touch Up Your Curls

If your curls need a touch-up, use a curling iron or rollers to define your curls.

Tips for Maintaining Permed Hair

Here are some tips for maintaining permed hair:

1. Don't Over-Wash Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, making it dry and brittle. Try to wash your hair every 2-3 days.

2. Avoid Heat Styling Tools

Heat styling tools can cause heat damage and breakage to your permed hair. Try to avoid using them as much as possible.

3. Use a Wide-Toothed Comb

Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair instead of a brush. This helps prevent breakage and damage to your permed hair.

4. Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trimming your hair regularly helps to prevent split ends and breakage. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

Frequently Asked Questions About Permed Hair

1. Can I Color My Permed Hair?

Yes, you can color your permed hair. However, it's best to wait at least 2 weeks after getting a perm before coloring your hair to prevent damage.

2. How Long Does a Perm Last?

A perm can last anywhere from 3-6 months, depending on your hair type and how well you take care of your permed hair.

3. Can I Perm Dyed Hair?

Yes, you can perm dyed hair. However, it's best to wait at least 2 weeks after coloring your hair to get a perm.

Essential Hair Care Products for Permed Hair

Here are some essential hair care products for permed hair:

1. Shampoo and Conditioner

Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to protect your permed hair.

2. Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to help hydrate and protect your permed hair.

3. Heat Protectant

Use a heat protectant to prevent heat damage and breakage when using heat styling tools.

4. Wide-Toothed Comb

Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair and prevent breakage.

5. Curling Iron or Rollers

Use a curling iron or rollers to define your curls and touch up your permed hair.

In conclusion, taking care of permed hair requires a good hair care routine and the use of essential hair care products. By following the tips and tricks outlined in this article, you can maintain healthy and beautiful permed hair.
