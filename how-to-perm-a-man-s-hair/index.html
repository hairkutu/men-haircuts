How To Perm A Man s Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Jul 25, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you a man looking to change up your hairstyle? Have you considered getting a perm? Perms can add volume, texture, and body to your hair, giving you a whole new look. In this guide, we'll show you how to perm a man's hair in a few easy steps.

Table of Contents

  • How to Perm a Man's Hair
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Perming Men's Hair
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Perming

How to Perm a Man's Hair

Perming a man's hair is similar to perming a woman's hair. The only difference is that men's hair is typically shorter, so the perm rods used will be smaller. Before perming your hair, you should decide what type of perm you want. There are different types of perms, such as spiral perms, body perms, and spot perms.

Types of Perms for Men

  • Spiral Perms: These perms create tight, springy curls that wrap around the entire length of the hair.
  • Body Perms: These perms create loose, beachy waves that add volume and texture to the hair.
  • Spot Perms: These perms are used to add curls to specific areas of the hair, such as the bangs or crown.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you've decided what type of perm you want, let's get started!

  1. Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup. Dry your hair completely.
  2. Apply a protective cream around your hairline, ears, and neck to prevent chemical burns.
  3. Divide your hair into sections using clips or hair ties. The size of the sections will depend on the size of the perm rods you're using.
  4. Apply the perm solution to each section of hair, making sure to saturate it completely.
  5. Wrap each section of hair around a perm rod, starting from the ends and rolling it up to the scalp. Secure the rod in place with a clip.
  6. Apply a neutralizer to each perm rod, making sure to saturate it completely. Leave the neutralizer on for the recommended time.
  7. Remove the perm rods from your hair and rinse with warm water. Do not shampoo your hair at this stage.
  8. Apply a conditioner to your hair and leave it on for a few minutes before rinsing with cool water.
  9. Dry your hair with a towel and then style it as desired.

Tips for Perming Men's Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when perming men's hair:

  • Consult with a professional hairstylist before attempting to perm your hair at home.
  • Use smaller perm rods for shorter hair.
  • Do not wash your hair for at least 48 hours after getting a perm.
  • Avoid using harsh shampoos or styling products that can damage your perm.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your perm hydrated and healthy.

FAQs

Can men get perms?

Yes, men can get perms. Perms are a great way to add volume, texture, and body to men's hair.

How long do perms last?

Perms can last anywhere from 2 to 6 months, depending on the type of perm and how well you take care of it.

Are perms damaging to hair?

Perms can be damaging to hair if not done properly or if the hair is already damaged. It's important to consult with a professional hairstylist before getting a perm.

Hair Care After Perming

After getting a perm, it's important to take care of your hair to maintain its health and longevity. Here are some tips for hair care after perming:

  • Avoid washing your hair for at least 48 hours after getting a perm.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent damage to your perm.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons or curling irons, which can damage your perm.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to keep your perm hydrated and healthy.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

Now that you know how to perm a man's hair, you can get the hairstyle you've always wanted. Just remember to consult with a professional hairstylist before attempting to perm your hair at home, and take care of your hair after perming to maintain its health and longevity.
