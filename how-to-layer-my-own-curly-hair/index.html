How To Layer My Own Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Jul 18, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you tired of your curly hair looking flat and lifeless? Layers can add volume and definition to your curls! But going to a hair salon can be expensive, and you might not want to risk cutting your own hair. Don't worry, with this step by step guide, you can learn how to layer your own curly hair in the comfort of your own home. Here's what you need to know:

How to Layer My Own Curly Hair

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

The first step to layering your curly hair is to wash and condition it. Make sure to use products that are specifically designed for curly hair. These products will help to define your curls and make them easier to work with.

Step 2: Detangle Your Hair

Next, you'll want to detangle your hair. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to gently work through any knots or tangles. It's important to be gentle with your hair, as curly hair can be prone to breakage.

Step 3: Divide Your Hair into Sections

Once your hair is detangled, divide it into sections. You can use hair clips or hair ties to keep each section separate. This will make it easier to layer your hair evenly.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Now it's time to start cutting! Begin with the bottom section of your hair. Use sharp hair cutting scissors and cut small sections at a time. It's important to cut straight across and not at an angle, as this can create uneven layers.

Step 5: Move to the Next Section

Once you've finished the bottom section, move on to the next one. It's important to cut each section evenly, so take your time and be patient.

Step 6: Cut the Top Layer

Finally, it's time to cut the top layer of your hair. This layer should be shorter than the bottom layers, as this will help to create volume and definition. Again, take your time and cut small sections at a time.

Tips for Layering Your Curly Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when layering your curly hair:

  • Use sharp hair cutting scissors. Dull scissors can create split ends and damage your hair.
  • Don't cut too much at once. It's better to cut small sections at a time to ensure even layers.
  • Consider cutting your hair when it's dry, as this can help you see the layers more clearly.
  • Experiment with different layering techniques to find what works best for you.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about layering curly hair:

Can I layer my own curly hair?

Yes, you can! With the right tools and techniques, you can layer your own curly hair at home.

How often should I layer my curly hair?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly you want to change your look. Some people layer their hair every few months, while others do it once a year.

Will layering my curly hair damage it?

No, layering your curly hair shouldn't damage it. However, using dull scissors or cutting too much at once can create split ends and damage your hair.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Now that you know how to layer your own curly hair, it's important to take care of it properly. Here are some tips:

  • Use products specifically designed for curly hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as these can damage your hair.
  • Detangle your hair gently with a wide-tooth comb or your fingers.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.

With these tips and techniques, you can have healthy, defined, and layered curly hair. Happy styling!
