How Do You Treat Male Pattern Baldness

Written by Eveline Jul 19, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Male pattern baldness affects a large percentage of men worldwide. It is a condition that can cause men to lose their hair gradually, leading to baldness. If you're experiencing male pattern baldness, you're not alone. There are several ways to treat this condition, depending on the severity of your hair loss.

Description

Male pattern baldness is a common condition that is characterized by hair loss on the scalp. It is also known as androgenetic alopecia and is caused by a combination of genetic and hormonal factors. The condition usually starts with a receding hairline, followed by thinning of the hair on the crown and temples of the head. This can eventually lead to complete baldness in some men.

How to Treat Male Pattern Baldness

There are several ways to treat male pattern baldness, depending on the severity of your hair loss. Here are some of the most common treatments:

Medications

There are two FDA-approved medications that can help treat male pattern baldness: finasteride and minoxidil. Finasteride is a prescription medication that works by blocking the hormone that causes hair loss. Minoxidil is an over-the-counter medication that is applied topically to the scalp and works by increasing blood flow to the hair follicles.

Hair Transplant Surgery

Hair transplant surgery involves taking hair from a donor area and transplanting it onto the balding area of the scalp. This procedure is typically done under local anesthesia and can take several hours to complete.

Low-Level Laser Therapy

Low-level laser therapy (LLLT) is a non-invasive treatment that involves using laser light to stimulate hair growth. This treatment is typically done in a clinic and can take several sessions to see results.

Step by Step Guide

If you're experiencing male pattern baldness, here are some steps you can take to treat it:

  1. Consult with your doctor to determine the severity of your hair loss and the best course of treatment.
  2. If prescribed medication, take it as directed by your doctor.
  3. If considering hair transplant surgery, research and choose a reputable surgeon.
  4. If undergoing LLLT, schedule regular sessions with a licensed provider.
  5. Be patient and consistent with your treatment plan, as it may take several months to see results.

Tips to Prevent Male Pattern Baldness

While male pattern baldness is largely genetic, there are some tips you can follow to prevent or delay its onset:

  • Maintain a healthy diet and exercise regularly to promote overall health.
  • Avoid smoking and excessive alcohol consumption, as they can contribute to hair loss.
  • Use gentle hair care products and avoid harsh chemicals and heat styling tools.
  • Protect your scalp from the sun by wearing a hat or using a sunscreen specifically designed for the scalp.

FAQs

Q: Is male pattern baldness reversible?

A: While there is no cure for male pattern baldness, there are treatments available that can help slow or stop its progression.

Q: Will wearing a hat cause male pattern baldness?

A: No, wearing a hat will not cause male pattern baldness. However, wearing a tight-fitting hat for extended periods of time can cause hair breakage and damage.

Q: Can stress cause male pattern baldness?

A: While stress can contribute to hair loss, it is not a direct cause of male pattern baldness.

Hair Care for Male Pattern Baldness

If you're experiencing male pattern baldness, it's important to take care of the hair you do have. Here are some tips for hair care:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid further damage to your hair.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals and heat styling tools, as they can cause further damage to your hair.
  • Consider using a volumizing or thickening product to give the appearance of fuller hair.
  • Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair, and avoid pulling or tugging on it.

Male pattern baldness can be a frustrating condition to deal with, but there are several treatments available to help slow or stop its progression. If you're experiencing hair loss, consult with your doctor to determine the best course of treatment for you.
