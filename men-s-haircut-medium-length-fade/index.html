Description

The medium length fade has been a popular men's haircut for years, and it's not hard to see why. This style is versatile and can be adapted to suit a wide range of face shapes and hair types. It's also a low-maintenance option that can easily transition from a casual daytime look to a more formal evening style.

The medium length fade typically involves shorter hair on the sides and back, gradually fading into longer hair on top. This creates a clean and polished look that's perfect for guys who want to keep their hair looking neat and tidy without too much fuss.

How to Get a Men's Haircut Medium Length Fade

If you're interested in trying out the medium length fade for yourself, the first step is to find a reputable barber who specializes in this type of cut. They'll be able to assess your hair type and face shape to determine the best way to achieve the look you're after.

When you sit down in the barber's chair, be sure to communicate your preferences clearly. If you have a specific style in mind, bring in a photo to show your barber. They may also ask you questions about how you typically style your hair and what products you use so they can tailor the cut to your needs.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to getting a men's haircut medium length fade:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Your barber may shampoo your hair if necessary.
  2. Choose the length you want on top. Most medium length fades will leave around 2-3 inches of hair on top.
  3. Decide on the type of fade you want. A low fade will have more hair left on the sides and back, while a high fade will have less.
  4. Your barber will use clippers to create the fade, starting with a longer guard and gradually working down to a shorter one.
  5. The sides and back of your hair will be cut shorter, gradually blending into the longer hair on top.
  6. Your barber will finish the cut by trimming the hair on top to your desired length and style.
  7. You may choose to have your hair styled with product or left natural.

Tips for Maintaining Your Medium Length Fade

Once you've got your medium length fade, it's important to take care of it to keep it looking great. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair regularly to keep it clean and healthy.
  • Use a high-quality shampoo and conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • If you're using a hair dryer, use a low heat setting and don't hold the dryer too close to your hair.
  • Visit your barber regularly for touch-ups and maintenance.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about the men's haircut medium length fade:

Is the medium length fade suitable for all hair types?

Yes, the medium length fade is a versatile style that can work for most hair types. However, your barber may need to make some adjustments to the cut to suit your specific hair type and texture.

How often should I get my medium length fade trimmed?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how neat you want to keep the style. Most guys opt for a trim every 4-6 weeks to keep the look fresh.

Can I style my medium length fade in different ways?

Yes, the medium length fade is a versatile style that can be styled in a variety of ways. Some popular options include slicking the hair back, creating a messy tousled look, or parting the hair to one side.

Hair Care for Your Medium Length Fade

Here are some additional tips for taking care of your medium length fade:

  • Avoid using hot tools like straighteners or curling irons, as these can damage your hair.
  • Use a heat protectant spray if you do need to use hot tools.
  • Avoid brushing your hair too vigorously, as this can cause breakage and damage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair instead.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip away natural oils and cause dryness.

With the right care and styling, your men's haircut medium length fade can look great for weeks to come.
