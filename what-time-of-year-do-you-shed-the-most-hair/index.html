What Time Of Year Do You Shed The Most Hair

Written by Eveline Aug 29, 2023 Â· 6 min read

If you have been noticing an increase in the amount of hair you shed, you might be wondering if it is normal or if there is a reason for it. One question that many people have is, "what time of year do you shed the most hair?" The answer to this question may surprise you!

Description

Hair shedding is a natural process that occurs in all humans. It is estimated that we shed anywhere from 50 to 100 hairs per day. However, during certain times of the year, this shedding can increase. For many people, the most significant increase in shedding occurs during the fall months.

How to Determine Hair Shedding

If you are concerned about excessive hair shedding, there are a few ways you can determine if your shedding is normal or if there is an underlying issue. One way is to simply count the number of hairs you shed per day. If you are shedding more than 100 hairs per day, you may want to consult with a dermatologist to determine the cause.

Another way to determine if your shedding is normal is to pay attention to the thickness of your ponytail. If you notice that your ponytail is getting thinner, this could be a sign of excessive shedding.

Step-by-Step Guide to Managing Excessive Hair Shedding

If you are experiencing excessive hair shedding, there are a few steps you can take to manage it:

  1. Consult with a dermatologist to rule out any underlying medical conditions.
  2. Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair, such as braids or ponytails.
  3. Be gentle when brushing or combing your hair.
  4. Avoid using heat styling tools, such as flat irons or curling irons.
  5. Eat a healthy diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals.

Tips for Reducing Hair Shedding

In addition to the steps outlined above, there are a few tips you can follow to reduce hair shedding:

  • Avoid over-washing your hair, as this can strip it of natural oils.
  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair, rather than a brush.
  • Consider using a silk pillowcase, as this can reduce friction and prevent hair breakage.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair, such as bleach or hair dyes.

FAQ

Q: Is it normal to shed more hair during certain times of the year?

A: Yes, it is normal to shed more hair during the fall months. This is due to a natural shedding cycle that occurs in many mammals.

Q: Can stress cause hair shedding?

A: Yes, stress can cause hair shedding. If you are experiencing excessive shedding, it is important to manage your stress levels.

Hair Care for Healthy Hair

In addition to managing hair shedding, it is important to take good care of your hair to keep it healthy. Some tips for healthy hair include:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using hot water on your hair, as this can strip it of natural oils.
  • Use a heat protectant before using heat styling tools.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.
  • Eat a healthy diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals.

By following these tips, you can keep your hair healthy and reduce the amount of shedding you experience.
