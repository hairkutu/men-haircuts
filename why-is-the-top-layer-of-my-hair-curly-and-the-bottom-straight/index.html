Why Is The Top Layer Of My Hair Curly And The Bottom Straight

Written by Aprilia Sep 10, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you one of those people who have the top layer of your hair curly while the bottom layer is straight? If yes, then you are not alone. This phenomenon is common among people with all hair types - whether curly, wavy, or straight. In this article, we will explore the reasons behind it and provide you with some tips on how to embrace your hair texture.

Description

The top layer of your hair may be curly while the bottom is straight due to a variety of reasons. It could be due to genetics, environmental factors, or the way you style your hair. Some people have naturally curly hair, while others have straight hair. The way your hair grows and the texture of your hair can also play a role in this.

How to Determine Your Hair Type?

The first step to understanding why the top layer of your hair is curly and the bottom is straight is to determine your hair type. There are four main types of hair - straight, wavy, curly, and coily. Your hair type is determined by the shape of your hair follicle, which is genetically determined.

Straight Hair

Straight hair is characterized by a smooth, shiny texture with no curl or wave. People with straight hair often have a hard time holding a curl or wave in their hair.

Wavy Hair

Wavy hair is characterized by a slight curl or wave that is most pronounced at the ends of the hair. People with wavy hair often have a hard time controlling frizz.

Curly Hair

Curly hair is characterized by a well-defined curl pattern that can range from loose to tight. People with curly hair often struggle with dryness and frizz.

Coily Hair

Coily hair is characterized by a tight, kinky curl pattern. People with coily hair often struggle with shrinkage and dryness.

Step by Step

If you are unsure of your hair type, here are some steps to help you determine it:

  1. Wash your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Towel-dry your hair gently.
  3. Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair.
  4. Allow your hair to air-dry.
  5. Observe the shape of your curls or waves.

Tips

Now that you know your hair type, here are some tips to help you embrace your natural hair texture:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner that is designed for your hair type.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to help reduce frizz and add moisture.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it is dry - this can cause breakage and frizz.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it is wet.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair - this can damage your hair and cause frizz.
  • Embrace your natural hair texture and experiment with different hairstyles that work for your hair type.

FAQ

Q: Can I change the texture of my hair?

A: You cannot change the texture of your hair permanently. However, you can style your hair in a way that makes it appear straighter or curlier.

Q: Is it normal to have different hair textures on different parts of my head?

A: Yes, it is normal to have different hair textures on different parts of your head. This is due to the way your hair grows and the shape of your hair follicles.

Q: Can I use a straightener to straighten the top layer of my hair?

A: Yes, you can use a straightener to straighten the top layer of your hair. However, be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage to your hair.

Hair Care

No matter what your hair type, proper hair care is essential to maintaining healthy, beautiful hair. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair:

  • Wash your hair no more than twice a week to prevent stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner that is designed for your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it is wet.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to help reduce frizz and add moisture.
  • Avoid using hair ties with metal - this can cause breakage and damage to your hair.

In conclusion, the top layer of your hair may be curly while the bottom is straight due to a variety of reasons, including genetics, environmental factors, and the way you style your hair. By understanding your hair type and embracing your natural hair texture, you can achieve healthy, beautiful hair.
