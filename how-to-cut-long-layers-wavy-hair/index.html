How To Cut Long Layers Wavy Hair

Written by Luffy May 25, 2023 · 6 min read

If you have long, wavy hair, you know that it can be difficult to manage. One way to make it easier to style is by cutting in long layers. This technique will give your hair movement and volume, making it look more stylish and modern. In this article, we’ll show you how to cut long layers wavy hair step by step, as well as provide tips on how to care for your hair afterward.

Table of Contents

  • How to Cut Long Layers Wavy Hair
  • Step by Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

How to Cut Long Layers Wavy Hair

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure it’s clean and dry. This will help you see the natural shape of your hair and make it easier to cut.

To begin, separate your hair into two sections, top and bottom. Use a hair clip to secure the top section out of the way.

Next, take a small section of hair from the bottom section and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers and slide your fingers down to the desired length. This will be your guide for cutting the rest of your hair.

Use scissors to cut the hair at the guide length. Make sure to cut straight across and not at an angle.

Continue cutting small sections of hair using the guide length as your reference. As you move up the hair, use the previous section as your guide to cut the next section.

Once you’ve finished cutting the bottom section, unclip the top section and repeat the process.

Step by Step

1. Wash and dry your hair

2. Separate your hair into top and bottom sections

3. Take a small section of hair from the bottom section

4. Comb the hair straight down

5. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers

6. Slide your fingers down to the desired length

7. Cut the hair at the guide length

8. Continue cutting small sections of hair

9. Use the previous section as your guide

10. Repeat the process on the top section

Tips

- Use sharp scissors to avoid split ends

- Cut hair in a well-lit area

- Don’t cut too much hair at once

- Take breaks if your hands get tired

- Don’t cut your hair when it’s wet, as it will appear shorter when it dries

FAQ

Q: Can I cut my own hair?

A: Yes, you can cut your own hair, but it’s recommended to have a professional do it.

Q: How often should I cut my hair?

A: It’s recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to maintain its shape and health.

Q: Will cutting my hair make it grow faster?

A: No, cutting your hair doesn’t affect its rate of growth.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it’s important to take care of it to maintain its health and style.

- Use a shampoo and conditioner specifically designed for wavy hair to avoid frizz

- Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle wet hair

- Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible

- Use a hair mask once a week to moisturize your hair

- Trim your hair regularly to avoid split ends

By following these steps, you’ll be able to cut long layers wavy hair at home and achieve a stylish, modern look.
