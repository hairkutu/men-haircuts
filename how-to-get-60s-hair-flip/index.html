How To Get 60S Hair Flip A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Jul 11, 2023 · 6 min read

Are you looking to channel your inner Brigitte Bardot or Twiggy with a 60s hair flip? This iconic hairstyle is still popular today, and it’s easy to see why. It’s fun, flirty, and adds a touch of retro glamour to any look. In this guide, we’ll show you how to achieve the perfect 60s hair flip in relaxed English language.

Description

The 60s hair flip is a hairstyle that has been around for decades. It’s a classic look that involves flipping the ends of your hair outwards, creating a bouncy and voluminous effect. This style is perfect for those who want to add a touch of vintage flair to their look.

How to

Here’s how to get the perfect 60s hair flip:

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair.

Wash your hair and blow-dry it until it’s completely dry. If you have naturally curly or wavy hair, you may want to straighten it first to make it easier to style.

Step 2: Section your hair.

Section your hair into two parts, with a part in the middle. Clip one side out of the way.

Step 3: Curl the ends.

Using a curling iron or hot rollers, curl the ends of your hair outwards. Make sure to curl away from your face for a more authentic 60s look. Repeat on the other side.

Step 4: Tease your hair.

Using a fine-toothed comb, tease the hair at the crown of your head for added volume. Be careful not to tease too much, as this can damage your hair.

Step 5: Flip your hair.

Using a brush or your fingers, gently flip your hair outwards. You can also use a hair spray or mousse to hold the style in place.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you get the perfect 60s hair flip:

  • Use a heat protectant on your hair before using hot tools.
  • Don’t be afraid to tease your hair for added volume.
  • Use a light hairspray or mousse to hold the style in place.
  • Experiment with different partings and hair accessories to make the look your own.

FAQ

What hair length is best for a 60s hair flip?

Any hair length can work for a 60s hair flip, but longer hair may require more styling to achieve the look.

Can I use a hair straightener instead of a curling iron?

Yes, you can use a hair straightener to create the curls, but you may need to twist the straightener slightly to achieve the desired shape.

How do I maintain my 60s hair flip?

To maintain your 60s hair flip, use a hair spray or mousse to hold the style in place. You may also want to touch up the curls with a curling iron or straightener as needed.

Hair Care

To keep your hair healthy and looking its best, make sure to use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner, and avoid using hot tools too often. Regular trims can also help prevent split ends and keep your hair looking fresh.

With these tips and tricks, you’ll be able to rock a 60s hair flip with confidence. Whether you’re dressing up for a special occasion or just want to add some retro flair to your everyday look, this hairstyle is sure to turn heads.
