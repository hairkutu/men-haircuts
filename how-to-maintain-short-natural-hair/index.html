How To Maintain Short Natural Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by Bardi Sep 08, 2023 · 6 min read

If you have short natural hair, you know how important it is to maintain your hair’s health and beauty. Whether you wear your hair in a TWA (teeny weeny afro) or in longer styles, there are some specific tips and tricks you can follow to keep your hair looking its best. In this article, we’ll explore how to maintain short natural hair, including a step-by-step guide, tips for hair care, and answers to frequently asked questions.

Table of Contents:

  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Hair Care
  • FAQs

Step-by-Step Guide

1. Wash Your Hair Regularly

Washing your hair regularly helps remove dirt, oil, and product buildup, which can weigh your hair down and make it look dull. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that won’t strip your hair of its natural oils. You can also try co-washing, which involves using a conditioner instead of shampoo to clean your hair.

2. Moisturize Your Hair

Short natural hair tends to be dry, so it’s important to keep it moisturized. Use a leave-in conditioner or moisturizer to keep your hair soft and hydrated. You can also use natural oils like olive oil, coconut oil, or jojoba oil to seal in moisture.

3. Style Your Hair Carefully

Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons or curling irons, as they can damage your hair. Instead, try air-drying your hair or using a diffuser on a low heat setting. You can also experiment with different styles like twist-outs, braid-outs, or wash-and-gos.

4. Protect Your Hair at Night

Use a satin or silk scarf or bonnet to protect your hair at night. This will help prevent breakage and keep your hair looking its best. You can also try sleeping on a satin or silk pillowcase.

Tips for Hair Care

1. Use Products Designed for Natural Hair

When choosing hair care products, look for products that are specifically designed for natural hair. These products are formulated to work with your hair’s unique texture and needs.

2. Be Gentle When Detangling Your Hair

Detangling your hair can be a challenge, especially if your hair is prone to tangles. Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to gently detangle your hair, starting at the ends and working your way up.

3. Protect Your Hair from the Sun

The sun can be damaging to your hair, causing it to become dry and brittle. Wear a hat or use a hair product with SPF to protect your hair from the sun’s rays.

FAQs

1. How often should I wash my short natural hair?

You should aim to wash your hair once a week, or every two weeks if your hair is particularly dry.

2. Should I use a leave-in conditioner or a moisturizer?

You can use either a leave-in conditioner or a moisturizer, depending on your hair’s needs. Leave-in conditioners are great for adding moisture and detangling your hair, while moisturizers are designed to keep your hair soft and hydrated.

3. How can I prevent breakage?

To prevent breakage, be gentle when detangling your hair, avoid using heat styling tools, and protect your hair at night with a satin or silk scarf or bonnet.

By following these tips and tricks, you can maintain your short natural hair and keep it looking its best. Remember to be patient and experiment with different styles to find what works best for you. With a little bit of care and attention, you can have healthy, beautiful hair that you’re proud to show off.
