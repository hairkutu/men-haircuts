How To Style Fringe Tips And Tricks For A Chic Look

Written by Bardi Apr 21, 2023 · 9 min read

If you’re looking for a new hairstyle that is easy to maintain and looks great, then fringe might be the perfect choice for you. Fringe is a versatile hairstyle that can be worn in many different ways, from a long, wispy fringe to a short, blunt fringe. In this article, we will show you how to style fringe in different ways, and provide you with tips and tricks to make sure your fringe always looks chic and on-trend.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Style Fringe
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips and Tricks
  • Frequently Asked Questions
  • Hair Care Tips

How to Style Fringe

Styling fringe can be a bit tricky, but with the right techniques and tools, you can achieve a beautiful and polished look. Here are some popular ways to style fringe:

1. Blunt Fringe

A blunt fringe is a classic style that never goes out of fashion. To achieve this look, ask your hairdresser to cut your fringe straight across, just above your eyebrows. To style, simply blow dry your fringe straight down, using a round brush to smooth out any kinks.

2. Side-Swept Fringe

A side-swept fringe is a more relaxed and casual look that is perfect for everyday wear. To achieve this style, ask your hairdresser to cut your fringe at an angle, starting from the outer corner of your eye and ending at the middle of your eyebrow. To style, simply blow dry your fringe to the side with a round brush, and use a bit of hairspray to keep it in place.

3. Wispy Fringe

A wispy fringe is a softer and more feminine look that is perfect for those who don't want a heavy fringe. To achieve this style, ask your hairdresser to cut your fringe in a choppy and textured way, leaving some longer pieces at the sides. To style, simply blow dry your fringe straight down, using your fingers to tousle the ends for a natural and effortless look.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to style fringe:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair to make sure it's clean and free of tangles.

Step 2: Towel Dry Your Hair

Gently towel dry your hair to remove excess water, but don't rub your hair too hard as this can cause damage and frizz.

Step 3: Apply Heat Protection Spray

Apply a heat protection spray to your hair to protect it from heat damage when blow drying.

Step 4: Blow Dry Your Hair

Blow dry your hair using a round brush to smooth out any kinks and create volume at the roots. Use your fingers to tousle your fringe for a more natural look.

Step 5: Style Your Fringe

Style your fringe according to the desired look you want to achieve. For a blunt fringe, blow dry it straight down. For a side-swept fringe, blow dry it to the side. For a wispy fringe, use your fingers to tousle the ends for a natural and effortless look.

Step 6: Finish with Hairspray

Finish your hairstyle with a bit of hairspray to keep your fringe in place and add shine to your hair.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips and tricks to help you style your fringe:

  • Use a round brush to create volume and smooth out any kinks.
  • Blow dry your fringe in the opposite direction to create more volume.
  • Use a bit of hairspray to keep your fringe in place and add shine to your hair.
  • If you have a cowlick, blow dry your fringe in the opposite direction of your natural hair growth to tame it.
  • If you have greasy hair, avoid touching your fringe too much as this can make it look greasier.

Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I trim my fringe?

It's best to trim your fringe every 4-6 weeks to keep it looking neat and polished.

Is fringe suitable for all face shapes?

Yes, fringe can suit all face shapes, but it's important to choose the right type of fringe to suit your face shape. Consult with your hairdresser to find the perfect style for you.

Can I style my fringe with a flat iron?

Yes, you can style your fringe with a flat iron, but make sure to use a heat protection spray to protect your hair from heat damage.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some hair care tips to keep your fringe looking healthy and beautiful:

  • Wash your hair regularly to keep it clean and free of dirt and oil.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can damage your hair and cause split ends.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and keep it looking neat and polished.

Now that you know how to style fringe, it's time to give it a try and see how it can transform your look. Whether you opt for a blunt fringe, side-swept fringe, or wispy fringe, with the right techniques and tools, you can achieve a chic and on-trend hairstyle that is easy to maintain and looks great.
