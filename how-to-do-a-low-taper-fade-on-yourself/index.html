How To Do A Low Taper Fade On Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Apr 12, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you're looking for a fresh and stylish haircut that's easy to maintain, a low taper fade might be just what you need. This popular haircut style involves gradually fading the hair from longer lengths at the top to shorter lengths at the back and sides, creating a clean and sharp look. The best part? You can do it yourself with just a few tools and some patience. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to achieve a perfect low taper fade on yourself.

Description of a Low Taper Fade

A low taper fade involves cutting the hair at the back and sides of the head gradually shorter, starting from a longer length at the top. The hair is blended seamlessly from one length to another, resulting in a clean and polished look. The fade usually starts around the ear and gradually fades down to the neckline, with the hair getting shorter and shorter as it goes down. The end result is a stylish and trendy look that works for all hair types and face shapes.

How to Do a Low Taper Fade on Yourself: Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the tools you'll need. You'll need a pair of clippers, scissors, a comb, and a mirror. It's also a good idea to have a spray bottle filled with water to keep your hair damp as you cut.

Step 2: Start with the Top

Begin by cutting the hair at the top to your desired length. Use scissors to trim the hair evenly across, making sure it's not too short or too long. Once you've achieved the desired length, use clippers to trim the hair around the edges, leaving the top untouched.

Step 3: Start the Fade

Next, start the fade by using clippers to trim the hair around the sides and back of the head. Start with a higher guard and work your way down to a lower guard, making sure to blend the hair seamlessly as you go. Use a comb to guide the clippers and create a smooth transition between the different lengths.

Step 4: Blend the Hair

Once you've achieved the desired length, use scissors to blend the hair around the edges and create a seamless transition. Work in small sections, cutting the hair slightly shorter as you move towards the neckline. Use the comb to guide the scissors and make sure the hair is even and blended.

Step 5: Clean Up the Neckline

Use clippers to trim the hair at the neckline, creating a clean and sharp line. Use the comb as a guide to create a straight line across the neck, and make sure the hair is evenly trimmed.

Step 6: Check Your Work

Once you've finished cutting, use a mirror to check your work and make any final adjustments. Make sure the hair is even and blended, and that the neckline is clean and sharp.

Tips for a Perfect Low Taper Fade

Here are some tips to help you achieve a perfect low taper fade:

  • Start with a higher guard and work your way down gradually to achieve a seamless transition
  • Use a comb to guide the clippers and scissors and create a smooth and even transition
  • Take your time and make small adjustments as you go
  • Use a mirror to check your work and make any final adjustments
  • Ask a friend or family member for help if you're not confident doing it yourself

FAQs About Low Taper Fade

Here are some frequently asked questions about low taper fade:

What is the difference between a low taper fade and a high taper fade?

A low taper fade starts around the ear and gradually fades down to the neckline, while a high taper fade starts higher up on the head and fades down to the neckline. A low taper fade is more subtle and conservative, while a high taper fade is more dramatic and bold.

What hair types work best for a low taper fade?

A low taper fade works for all hair types, but it's particularly popular with curly and wavy hair. The fade helps to define the curls and create a polished and stylish look.

How often should I get a low taper fade?

It's recommended to get a low taper fade every 2-4 weeks to maintain the look and keep the hair neat and tidy.

Hair Care Tips for Low Taper Fade

Here are some tips to help you take care of your low taper fade:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, such as blow dryers or straighteners
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized and healthy
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain the shape and length
  • Avoid harsh chemicals and treatments that can damage your hair

With these tips and our step-by-step guide, you'll be able to achieve a perfect low taper fade on yourself. Whether you're looking for a subtle and polished look or a bold and trendy style, the low taper fade is sure to impress.
