Is It Normal For A Baby To Lose Hair

Written by Robby May 18, 2023 · 7 min read

If you’re a new parent, you might be worried to see your baby losing hair. Don’t panic, it’s normal for babies to lose hair during their first six months. By the time your baby is a year old, they will have a head full of hair. In this blog post, we will answer all your questions about baby hair loss and provide tips on how to take care of your baby’s hair.

Table of Content

Why Do Babies Lose Hair?

It’s normal for babies to lose hair because of hormonal changes. During pregnancy, the mother’s hormones pass to the baby through the placenta, which stimulates hair growth. After birth, the baby’s hormones change, which can cause hair loss. Additionally, the baby’s scalp is very delicate and sensitive, so any friction or pressure can also cause hair loss.

When Does Baby Hair Loss Occur?

Baby hair loss typically occurs between the first six weeks and six months of life. It’s most common for babies to lose hair on the top and sides of their head, leaving hair around the back and neck. Some babies may also lose hair on their eyebrows and eyelashes.

How Much Hair Does a Baby Lose?

Every baby is different, but it’s normal for a baby to lose up to 50% of their hair during their first six months. Some babies may lose more or less hair, and the hair loss may be more noticeable on babies with longer hair.

When Does Baby Hair Regrow?

Baby hair usually starts to regrow around three months after the hair loss. It’s important to note that the regrowth may be a different color or texture than the baby’s original hair. The new hair may also grow in unevenly, leaving the baby with a patchy look for a while.

Tips for Taking Care of Your Baby's Hair

Here are some tips for taking care of your baby’s hair:

  • Be gentle when washing your baby’s hair. Use a mild shampoo and avoid scrubbing too hard.
  • Don’t use adult hair products on your baby’s hair. They can be too harsh and cause irritation.
  • Use a soft brush or comb to gently detangle your baby’s hair.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on the hair and cause breakage.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take for a baby's hair to grow back?

It takes about six months for a baby’s hair to grow back after the hair loss.

Can cradle cap cause hair loss?

Cradle cap, a common condition where the baby’s scalp becomes scaly and flaky, can cause hair loss. However, the hair loss is temporary and usually grows back once the cradle cap is treated.

When should I be concerned about my baby's hair loss?

If your baby is losing hair after six months or if you notice bald patches, it’s a good idea to talk to your pediatrician. They can rule out any underlying medical conditions that may be causing the hair loss.

Can I use oils on my baby's hair?

It’s best to avoid using oils on your baby’s hair, as they can be difficult to wash out and may cause irritation. Stick to a mild shampoo and gentle brushing.

Can I prevent my baby from losing hair?

Unfortunately, you can’t prevent your baby from losing hair. It’s a natural process that most babies go through. The good news is that the hair loss is temporary, and your baby will have a full head of hair by their first birthday.

Remember, baby hair loss is normal, and there’s no need to worry. Just be gentle with your baby’s hair and enjoy watching it grow back.
