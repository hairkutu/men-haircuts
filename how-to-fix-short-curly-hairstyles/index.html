How To Fix Short Curly Hairstyles

Written by Eveline Jun 25, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Short curly hairstyles can be both fun and challenging to manage. While they offer a lot of versatility and styling options, they can also be prone to frizz and tangling. Fortunately, there are several ways to fix short curly hairstyles and keep them looking their best. In this article, we'll go over some tips and tricks for fixing short curly hair, as well as some hair care advice to keep your curls healthy and vibrant.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Fix Short Curly Hairstyles
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Maintaining Short Curly Hair
  4. FAQ
  5. Hair Care for Short Curly Hair

How to Fix Short Curly Hairstyles

One of the best ways to fix short curly hairstyles is to use the right styling products. Look for products specifically designed for curly hair, such as curl enhancing creams, gels, and mousses. These products can help define your curls and reduce frizz.

Another way to fix short curly hair is to use a diffuser when blow-drying. A diffuser is an attachment for your hair dryer that helps distribute the heat evenly and reduce frizz. To use a diffuser, simply attach it to your hair dryer and use it on a low heat setting, scrunching your curls as you dry.

If you're looking for a quick fix, you can also try using a curling iron or wand to touch up any areas that need some extra definition. Just be sure to use a heat protectant spray and avoid applying too much heat to your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide for fixing short curly hairstyles:

  1. Start by washing your hair with a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner. Sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils, which can lead to frizz and dryness.
  2. Apply a curl enhancing cream or mousse to damp hair, using your fingers to distribute it evenly throughout your curls.
  3. Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer and dry your hair on a low heat setting, scrunching your curls as you go.
  4. If you need to touch up any areas, use a curling iron or wand on a low heat setting.
  5. Finish with a lightweight hairspray or anti-frizz serum to keep your curls in place.

Tips for Maintaining Short Curly Hair

Here are some tips for maintaining short curly hair:

  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can lead to frizz and breakage. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your curls when they're wet.
  • Protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray before blow-drying or using hot tools.
  • Try not to wash your hair too often, as this can strip it of its natural oils. Instead, opt for a co-wash or use a dry shampoo in between washes.
  • Get regular trims to keep your curls looking their best and prevent split ends.

FAQ

Can I straighten my short curly hair?

Yes, you can straighten your short curly hair using a flat iron or straightening brush. Just be sure to use a heat protectant spray and avoid applying too much heat to your hair.

How often should I wash my short curly hair?

It depends on your hair type and personal preference, but generally, you should aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days. If your hair is very dry or prone to frizz, you may want to wash it less frequently.

What should I do if my curls are frizzy?

If your curls are frizzy, try using a curl enhancing cream or mousse to define them. You can also use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to reduce frizz. Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can lead to more frizz.

Hair Care for Short Curly Hair

Here are some tips for taking care of your short curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using hot tools too often, as this can lead to heat damage and breakage.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it hydrated and healthy.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a swim cap.

By following these tips and tricks, you can fix your short curly hair and keep it looking healthy and beautiful. Remember to use the right products, avoid heat damage, and take good care of your curls!
