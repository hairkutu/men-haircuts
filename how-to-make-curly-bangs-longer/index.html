How To Make Curly Bangs Longer

Written by Frank Apr 13, 2023 · 8 min read

If you have curly bangs, you may be wondering how to make them longer without cutting them. Fortunately, there are several ways to achieve this look without sacrificing your curls. In this blog post, we will discuss the steps you can take to make your curly bangs longer while maintaining their natural texture.

Description

Curly bangs can be a great way to add some flair to your hairstyle. However, if you are looking to change up your look, you may want to make your bangs longer. This can be done without cutting them by using a few techniques that will help stretch out your curls.

How to

Here are the steps you can take to make your curly bangs longer:

1. Use a Diffuser

Using a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer can help to stretch out your curls and make them longer. Simply apply a heat protectant to your hair, then use the diffuser on a low heat setting to dry your bangs while scrunching them with your hands. This will help to elongate your curls and make them appear longer.

2. Braid Your Bangs

Braiding your bangs while they are wet can help to stretch them out and make them longer. Simply divide your bangs into three sections and braid them tightly. Allow the braids to dry completely, then undo them and gently comb out your curls with your fingers. This will give you longer, more elongated curls.

3. Use a Curling Iron

If you have a curling iron, you can use it to make your curly bangs longer. Simply wrap your bangs around the curling iron barrel and hold them in place for a few seconds. When you release the curl, it will be longer and more defined.

Step by Step

Here is a step-by-step guide to making your curly bangs longer:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. This will help to hydrate your curls and make them more pliable.

Step 2: Apply a Heat Protectant

Apply a heat protectant to your hair to prevent damage from heat styling tools.

Step 3: Use a Diffuser

Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your bangs while scrunching them with your hands. This will help to elongate your curls and make them appear longer.

Step 4: Braid Your Bangs

Braid your bangs tightly while they are wet, then allow them to dry completely. Gently comb out your curls with your fingers to create longer, more elongated curls.

Step 5: Use a Curling Iron

Wrap your bangs around a curling iron barrel and hold them in place for a few seconds. When you release the curl, it will be longer and more defined.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when making your curly bangs longer:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your curls instead of a brush, which can cause breakage.
  • Avoid using too much product, which can weigh down your curls and make them appear shorter.
  • Experiment with different techniques to find the one that works best for your hair type and texture.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about making curly bangs longer:

Can I make my bangs longer without cutting them?

Yes, you can use techniques like diffusing, braiding, and curling to stretch out your curls and make your bangs appear longer.

Will heat styling damage my curls?

Heat styling can damage your curls if you don’t use a heat protectant and if you use too much heat. Make sure to use a low heat setting and a heat protectant to protect your curls from damage.

How often should I wash my hair?

Curly hair tends to be drier than other hair types, so you may not need to wash it as often. Aim to wash your hair once or twice a week, or as needed.

Hair Care

When it comes to caring for your curly bangs, it’s important to use products that are designed for your hair type. Look for products that cater to curly hair, such as moisturizing shampoos and conditioners, leave-in conditioners, and curl-defining creams. Avoid using products that contain alcohol, which can dry out your hair and make it frizzy.

Additionally, make sure to protect your hair from heat styling tools by using a heat protectant and avoiding excessive heat. Finally, consider getting regular trims to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends.
