How To Stop Hair Fall After Pregnancy A Complete Guide

Written by Petter Aug 27, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you are a new mother and experiencing hair fall after pregnancy, you are not alone. It is a common problem that affects many women, but the good news is that there are ways to prevent and stop hair loss. In this article, we will discuss the causes of hair fall after pregnancy, how to prevent it, and some useful tips for hair care.

Table of Contents

  • Causes of Hair Fall After Pregnancy
  • Prevention of Hair Fall After Pregnancy
  • Tips for Hair Care
  • FAQs

Causes of Hair Fall After Pregnancy

Hormonal changes are the primary cause of hair fall after pregnancy. During pregnancy, the increased level of estrogen hormone prevents hair loss, and most women experience thick and healthy hair. However, after delivery, the estrogen level drops, and the hair follicles enter a resting phase, leading to hair fall. Other causes of hair fall after pregnancy include stress, lack of sleep, poor nutrition, and genetics.

Prevention of Hair Fall After Pregnancy

Although it is difficult to prevent hair fall entirely, there are several ways to minimize it. Here are some useful tips:

Eat a Balanced Diet

Eating a balanced diet is crucial for healthy hair growth. Include foods rich in vitamins, minerals, and protein, such as leafy greens, nuts, eggs, and fish. Avoid processed and junk food, as they are high in sugar and unhealthy fats.

Stay Hydrated

Drinking enough water is essential for overall health, including hair growth. It keeps the scalp hydrated and prevents dryness and itchiness. Aim to drink at least eight glasses of water daily.

Take Supplements

If your diet is lacking in essential nutrients, consider taking supplements. Biotin, iron, and zinc are essential for healthy hair growth. Consult your doctor before taking any supplements.

Manage Stress

Stress is a significant contributor to hair fall. Practice stress-management techniques, such as yoga, meditation, or deep breathing. Get enough sleep and take breaks when needed.

Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling wands, can damage hair and lead to breakage. Avoid using them as much as possible, and if you must, use a heat protectant spray.

Tips for Hair Care

In addition to the above prevention tips, here are some useful hair care tips:

Brush Gently

Brushing your hair too harshly can cause breakage and hair fall. Use a wide-toothed comb or a soft-bristled brush, and be gentle when detangling your hair.

Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles, such as braids, buns, and ponytails, can pull on the hair and cause breakage. Opt for loose hairstyles and avoid pulling your hair too tight.

Use a Mild Shampoo

Choose a mild shampoo that is free of harsh chemicals, such as sulfates and parabens. These chemicals can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause damage.

Condition Regularly

Conditioning your hair regularly helps to keep it hydrated and prevent breakage. Use a conditioner that is suitable for your hair type, and leave it on for a few minutes before rinsing.

FAQs

Q: How long does hair fall after pregnancy last?

A: Hair fall after pregnancy usually peaks at around three to four months postpartum and can last up to a year. However, it varies for each woman.

Q: Can breastfeeding cause hair fall?

A: Yes, breastfeeding can cause hair fall due to hormonal changes. However, it is usually temporary and resolves on its own.

Q: Is hair fall after pregnancy permanent?

A: No, hair fall after pregnancy is usually temporary and resolves on its own within a year.

In conclusion, hair fall after pregnancy is a common problem that can be prevented and minimized with proper hair care and a healthy lifestyle. Follow the prevention tips and hair care tips mentioned in this article, and consult your doctor if you experience excessive hair fall or have any concerns.
