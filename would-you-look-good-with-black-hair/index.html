Would You Look Good With Black Hair Find Out Now

Written by Bardi May 08, 2023 · 9 min read

Are you considering dyeing your hair black but unsure if it would suit you? We understand it can be daunting to make such a drastic change to your appearance. That’s why we’ve created this guide to help you determine whether black hair would look good on you or not. In this article, we will explore the different factors that influence how black hair will look on you, how to dye your hair black at home, and how to take care of your hair after the dyeing process.

How to Determine if Black Hair Would Suit You

There are various factors that determine whether black hair will look good on you or not. The most important ones are your skin tone, eye color, and natural hair color. Here’s how to determine whether black hair would suit you:

Skin Tone

Your skin tone is the most important factor to consider when choosing a hair color. If you have warm undertones, such as yellow or golden, black hair can look great on you. If you have cool undertones, such as pink or blue, black hair can make you look washed out. If you have a neutral skin tone, you can pull off black hair without any issues.

Eye Color

Your eye color can also influence how black hair will look on you. If you have dark eyes, black hair can make them look even more intense. If you have light eyes, such as blue or green, black hair can create a beautiful contrast with your eye color.

Natural Hair Color

If you have naturally dark hair, black hair can be a great choice for you. If you have light hair, such as blonde or light brown, black hair can be too harsh and drastic of a change.

How to Dye Your Hair Black at Home

If you’ve determined that black hair would suit you, the next step is to dye your hair black. Here’s how to do it at home:

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Before dyeing your hair, it’s important to prepare it. Wash your hair with a clarifying shampoo to remove any buildup and oils. Dry your hair completely with a towel.

Step 2: Mix the Dye

Follow the instructions on the hair dye box to mix the dye. Wear gloves to protect your hands from staining.

Step 3: Apply the Dye

Using a hair dye brush, apply the dye to your hair from the roots to the tips. Make sure to cover all the hair evenly. Leave the dye on for the amount of time specified on the box.

Step 4: Rinse and Condition

Rinse the dye out of your hair with lukewarm water. Apply the conditioner that came with the dye to your hair and leave it on for a few minutes. Rinse your hair with cold water to seal the cuticles and enhance shine.

Tips for Maintaining Black Hair

After dyeing your hair black, it’s important to take care of it to maintain its color and health. Here are some tips:

Use Color-Safe Products

Use shampoos and conditioners that are specifically designed for color-treated hair. These products will help maintain the vibrancy of your black hair.

Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools can damage your hair and cause your black hair to fade quickly. Avoid using them as much as possible, or use a heat protectant spray if you need to use them.

Protect Your Hair from the Sun

The sun can cause your black hair to fade and become dull. Wear a hat or use a UV-protectant spray when spending time outdoors.

FAQ

Can I dye my hair black if I have light hair?

You can, but it’s important to consider whether black hair would suit your skin tone and eye color first. If you have warm undertones and dark eyes, black hair can look great on you.

How long does black hair dye last?

Black hair dye can last up to 6 weeks, depending on how well you take care of your hair. Using color-safe products and avoiding heat styling can help prolong the life of your black hair dye.

Can I dye my hair black at home?

Yes, you can. Follow the instructions on the hair dye box carefully and wear gloves to protect your hands from staining.

Hair Care for Black Hair

Black hair requires special attention to keep it healthy and shiny. Here are some tips:

Moisturize Your Hair

Black hair can become dry and brittle if it doesn’t receive enough moisture. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated.

Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles, such as braids and ponytails, can cause tension on your hair and lead to breakage. Avoid them as much as possible.

Trim Your Hair Regularly

Trimming your hair regularly will help prevent split ends and breakage. Aim to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks.

Now that you know whether black hair would suit you, how to dye your hair black at home, and how to take care of your black hair, you’re ready to make the change! Remember to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and vibrant.
