Short Natural Hairstyles With Thin Edges

Written by William May 21, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you dealing with thinning edges and looking for the perfect short natural hairstyle to rock? Look no further! In this article, we will explore various short natural hairstyles that are perfect for those with thin edges.

Description

Thinning edges can be caused by a variety of factors such as genetics, tight hairstyles, and improper hair care. It is important to avoid hairstyles that put too much tension on the hairline and to take care of the hair properly to prevent further thinning.

How to

When choosing a short natural hairstyle, it is important to keep in mind that some styles may put too much tension on the hairline, leading to further thinning. Opt for styles that are low maintenance and do not require too much manipulation.

One great style for those with thin edges is the tapered cut. This style involves cutting the hair short on the sides and back while leaving the top longer. This style is low maintenance and does not require much manipulation, making it perfect for those with thinning edges.

Step by Step

1. Start by sectioning the hair into four parts: two on the sides and two in the back.

2. Use clippers to cut the hair on the sides and back short.

3. Leave the hair on top longer and style as desired.

Tips

When styling your hair, be sure to use products that are gentle on the hair and do not cause further thinning. Avoid using tight headbands or hair ties that put too much tension on the hairline.

It is also important to maintain a healthy hair care routine. Be sure to moisturize the hair regularly and avoid using heat styling tools too often.

FAQ

What hairstyles should I avoid if I have thinning edges?

You should avoid hairstyles that put too much tension on the hairline such as tight braids, weaves, and ponytails.

Can I still wear my hair in a ponytail if I have thinning edges?

If you have thinning edges, it is best to avoid wearing your hair in a ponytail as this can further thin the hairline. Opt for looser styles such as a low bun or a half-up, half-down style.

Hair Care

To prevent further thinning of the hairline, it is important to maintain a healthy hair care routine. This includes regular washing and conditioning of the hair, moisturizing the hair with a leave-in conditioner, and avoiding heat styling tools too often.

You can also use hair growth products such as oils and serums to promote hair growth and strengthen the hair.

With the right hairstyle and hair care routine, those with thinning edges can still rock a beautiful short natural hairstyle with confidence.
