How To Cut A Haircut Fade Step By Step Guide

Written by Aprilia Jul 10, 2023 · 7 min read

Cutting a haircut fade can be tricky, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done with ease. In this post, we’ll walk you through the steps on how to cut a haircut fade, from the tools required to the finishing touches. So, let's get started!

Description

A fade haircut is a popular hairstyle that involves gradually cutting the hair shorter from the top of the head down to the sides and back. This creates a fade effect that blends the hair from long to short. It's a versatile haircut that can be tailored to suit different hair types and lengths, making it a popular choice among men and women alike.

How to Cut a Haircut Fade

Before we begin, you'll need to gather the following tools:

  • Clippers
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Haircut cape
  • Haircutting spray bottle

Step 1: Choose the Right Clipper Guard

The first step is to choose the right clipper guard for the fade haircut. The clipper guard determines how short the hair will be cut. Start with a larger clipper guard number, such as a #4, and work your way down to smaller guard numbers as you progress to the sides and back.

Step 2: Cut the Top of the Hair

Using scissors, cut the top of the hair to the desired length. Make sure to leave enough length to blend with the sides and back. Use a comb to part the hair and cut it in sections, making sure to blend the hair as you go.

Step 3: Start Cutting the Sides and Back

Using the chosen clipper guard, start cutting the sides and back of the hair. Begin at the bottom and work your way up, gradually decreasing the clipper guard number as you go. Blend the hair as you move up, using a comb to guide the clipper and create a smooth transition from long to short hair.

Step 4: Blend the Top and Sides

Use scissors to blend the top and sides of the hair, creating a smooth transition. Take small sections of hair and cut at an angle to blend the hair together. Make sure not to cut too much, as this can create an uneven look.

Step 5: Define the Hairline

Use clippers to define the hairline around the ears and neck, making sure to create a clean, even line. Use scissors to trim any stray hairs and create a polished look.

Step 6: Finishing Touches

Finally, use a comb and scissors to make any final adjustments and create a polished, professional look. Spray the hair lightly with a haircutting spray bottle to set the style and you're done!

Tips

  • Take your time – cutting a fade takes patience and practice.
  • Make sure to blend the hair as you go to create a natural, seamless look.
  • Use scissors to create a polished, professional finish.
  • Take breaks as needed to avoid fatigue and ensure precision.

FAQ

Q: Can I cut a fade with just clippers?

A: While it's possible to cut a fade with just clippers, using scissors allows for more precision and control, resulting in a more polished finish.

Q: What length should I leave the top of my hair?

A: The length of the top of the hair depends on personal preference and hair type. A good rule of thumb is to leave at least 1-2 inches of hair on top to blend with the sides and back.

Q: How often should I get a haircut to maintain a fade?

A: It's recommended to get a haircut every 4-6 weeks to maintain a fade and keep the hair looking neat and polished.

Hair Care

Maintaining a fade requires regular hair care. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep the hair clean and moisturized. Trim any stray hairs as needed and visit a professional stylist regularly to maintain the look.
