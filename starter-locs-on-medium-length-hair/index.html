Starter Locs On Medium Length Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Aprilia Aug 01, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you considering getting starter locs on your medium length hair? This guide will provide you with all the information you need to know before taking the plunge. From what starter locs are to the step-by-step process of getting them, we've got you covered. Let's dive in!

Description

Starter locs are the beginning stages of the dreadlocking process. They are created by twisting or braiding your hair until it locks up and forms into a dread. Starter locs are an excellent option for those who want to begin their loc journey but are not ready to commit to fully formed locs.

How to

The process of getting starter locs on medium length hair is relatively simple. However, it is essential to go through the process with a professional stylist who has experience in creating starter locs. Here is a step-by-step guide on how to get starter locs:

Step 1: Consultation

Before starting the process, it is essential to have a consultation with a professional stylist. During this consultation, your stylist will assess your hair and determine if starter locs are a good fit for your hair type and texture. They will also discuss the process and answer any questions you may have.

Step 2: Wash and Condition

It is crucial to have clean hair before the process begins. Your stylist will wash and condition your hair to ensure it is free of any product buildup or debris that could interfere with the locking process.

Step 3: Parting

Your stylist will part your hair into small sections, usually with a rat-tail comb. The size of the sections depends on your hair's thickness and texture.

Step 4: Twisting or Braiding

After parting your hair, your stylist will begin twisting or braiding each section. They will use a locking gel or wax to help the hair lock up and form into a dread. The twisting or braiding process can take several hours, depending on the length and thickness of your hair.

Step 5: Palm Roll

Once your hair has been twisted or braided, your stylist will palm roll each section to encourage the hair to lock up and form into a dread. This process involves rolling the hair between your palms to create a smooth, cylindrical shape.

Step 6: Maintenance

After your starter locs have been created, it is essential to maintain them regularly. This involves regular washing, conditioning, and retwisting or palm rolling to keep them looking neat and tidy.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when getting starter locs:

  • Be patient: It can take several months for your starter locs to fully form into mature locs.
  • Keep them clean: Regular washing and conditioning are essential for keeping your starter locs healthy and clean.
  • Use the right products: It is essential to use products specifically designed for locs to avoid buildup and damage.
  • Be gentle: Avoid pulling or tugging on your starter locs, as this can cause breakage and damage.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about starter locs:

How long does it take for starter locs to form?

It can take several months for starter locs to fully form into mature locs. However, the process can vary depending on your hair type and texture.

Can I wash my hair with starter locs?

Yes! Regular washing and conditioning are essential for keeping your starter locs clean and healthy.

Do I need to use a specific product for my starter locs?

Yes, it is essential to use products specifically designed for locs to avoid buildup and damage.

Hair Care

Maintaining your starter locs is essential to the health and longevity of your locs. Here are some tips for caring for your starter locs:

  • Wash and condition regularly
  • Avoid using heavy oils and products
  • Retwist or palm roll regularly to keep them looking neat
  • Avoid using heat on your locs
  • Be gentle when styling

Starter locs on medium length hair are a great way to begin your loc journey. With proper care and maintenance, your starter locs will transform into beautiful, mature locs over time. Remember to be patient, gentle, and use the right products to keep them healthy and looking their best.
