What Haircut Would Look Good On Me

Written by Eveline Mar 29, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of your current hairstyle and looking for a change? Choosing a new haircut can be daunting, but with the right guidance, you can find a style that perfectly suits your face shape, hair type, and personality. In this blog post, we'll help you determine what haircut would look good on you, with step-by-step instructions and helpful tips. Let's get started!

Description

Before we delve into the details, let's define what we mean by a good haircut. A good haircut is one that flatters your features, enhances your natural beauty, and reflects your personal style. It should be easy to maintain, versatile enough to wear for different occasions, and make you feel confident and comfortable.

How to

The first step in finding the perfect haircut is to determine your face shape. This will help you choose a style that accentuates your best features and balances out any asymmetry. There are six basic face shapes: oval, round, square, heart, diamond, and long. To find out your face shape, pull your hair back from your face and take a close-up photo of yourself, or ask a friend to help you. Then, compare your face to the following descriptions:

Oval: Your face is longer than it is wide, with a gently curved jawline and forehead.

Round: Your face is almost as wide as it is long, with a soft, circular hairline and full cheeks.

Square: Your face is as wide as it is long, with a strong, angular jawline and forehead.

Heart: Your face is wider at the forehead and temples, with a pointed chin.

Diamond: Your face is widest at the cheekbones, with a narrow forehead and chin.

Long: Your face is longer than it is wide, with a straight hairline and chin.

Step-by-Step Guide

Once you've determined your face shape, you can start looking for hairstyles that suit you. Here's a step-by-step guide to finding the right haircut:

  1. Research hairstyles that flatter your face shape. You can use online tools, hairstyle magazines, or consult a hairstylist for inspiration.
  2. Consider your hair type, texture, and color. Not all hairstyles work with all hair types, so make sure you choose a style that suits your hair's natural characteristics.
  3. Think about your lifestyle and personal style. Do you prefer a low-maintenance or high-maintenance haircut? Do you want to make a bold statement or keep it simple and classic?
  4. Book a consultation with a hairstylist. This is especially important if you're making a drastic change or trying a new style. A hairstylist can give you advice on what will work best for your face shape and hair type, as well as show you how to style your new haircut.
  5. Take care of your hair. Once you've got your new haircut, make sure you take care of it properly. Use high-quality hair products, get regular trims, and protect your hair from heat damage.

Tips

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when choosing a new haircut:

  • Don't be afraid to experiment. If you're unsure about a new style, try it out with a wig or hair extensions first.
  • Consider your skin tone. Certain hair colors can complement or clash with your skin tone, so choose wisely.
  • Be realistic about your hair's limitations. If you have thin or fine hair, a voluminous hairstyle may not be achievable without extensions or products.
  • Be open to feedback. Your hairstylist may have suggestions or modifications that can improve the final result.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about choosing a new haircut:

Q: Will a short haircut suit me?

A: It depends on your face shape, hair type, and personal style. Short haircuts can be flattering on many people, but they require more maintenance than longer styles.

Q: Can I pull off a bob haircut?

A: Bobs can be versatile and flattering, but they require a bit of styling to maintain their shape. Talk to your hairstylist about whether a bob would suit your face shape and hair type.

Q: What haircut is best for curly hair?

A: Curly hair can work with a variety of haircuts, but it's important to choose a style that doesn't add too much weight or volume. Layers and tapered cuts can help define curls and reduce frizz.

Hair Care

Once you've got your new haircut, make sure you take care of your hair to keep it looking its best. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid heat styling too often. Get regular trims to maintain your haircut's shape, and use hair products that suit your hair type and styling needs.

Overall, finding the right haircut takes some research and experimentation, but with the right guidance, you can discover a style that enhances your natural beauty and makes you feel confident and stylish. Happy hairstyling!
