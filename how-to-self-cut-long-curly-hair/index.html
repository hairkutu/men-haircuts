How To Self Cut Long Curly Hair

Written by Daniel Aug 22, 2023 · 7 min read

If you have long curly hair, it can be a challenge to keep it trimmed and looking its best. Going to a salon can be expensive, especially if you need frequent trims. But did you know that you can cut your own long curly hair at home? With the right tools and a little bit of practice, you can save money and keep your curls looking fabulous. In this article, we’ll give you step-by-step instructions on how to self cut long curly hair.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Self Cut Long Curly Hair
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Cutting Long Curly Hair
  4. Frequently Asked Questions
  5. Hair Care Tips for Long Curly Hair

How to Self Cut Long Curly Hair

Before you get started, you’ll need a few tools. Here’s what you’ll need:

  • Sharp hair cutting scissors
  • A comb
  • A mirror
  • A spray bottle filled with water

Now that you have your tools, it’s time to get started.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Start by washing and conditioning your hair as you normally would. Once you’ve rinsed out the conditioner, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.

Step 2: Towel dry your hair and let it air dry until it’s about 80% dry. You don’t want to start cutting your hair when it’s soaking wet, as this can lead to uneven cuts.

Step 3: Divide your hair into sections using your comb. Start by parting your hair down the middle, and then create two sections on either side of your head.

Step 4: Take one section of hair and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your index and middle fingers, and slide your fingers down to where you want to make the cut. Use your scissors to trim the hair, making sure to cut straight across.

Step 5: Repeat this process on the other sections of your hair, working your way around your head. Make sure to cut each section of hair to the same length.

Step 6: Once you’ve finished cutting your hair, use your comb to check for any uneven spots. If you notice any areas that need a little more trimming, go back and touch them up.

Step 7: Style your hair as you normally would, and enjoy your freshly trimmed curls!

Tips for Cutting Long Curly Hair

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when cutting your own long curly hair:

  • Start with small cuts. It’s better to take off too little at first, and then go back and take off more if needed.
  • Make sure your scissors are sharp. Dull scissors can lead to split ends and uneven cuts.
  • Don’t cut your hair when it’s wet. Wet hair can be deceiving, and you may end up cutting off more than you intended.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your hair can be tiring, so don’t be afraid to take a break and come back to it later.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I cut my own curly hair?

A: Yes, you can! With the right tools and a little bit of practice, cutting your own curly hair can be a great way to save money and keep your curls looking great.

Q: How often should I cut my curly hair?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly it becomes damaged. Generally, it’s a good idea to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to keep it healthy and prevent split ends.

Q: What if I mess up?

A: Don’t worry! Hair grows back, and mistakes can be fixed. If you’re really unhappy with your cut, you can always go to a salon and have it fixed.

Hair Care Tips for Long Curly Hair

Now that you know how to cut your own long curly hair, here are a few tips for keeping it healthy and looking great:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and frizz.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s dry, as this can lead to breakage and frizz. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair when it’s wet.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools as much as possible, as they can damage your hair and cause it to become dry and brittle.
  • Deep condition your hair once a week to keep it moisturized and healthy.

By following these tips and using the right tools, you can keep your long curly hair looking great all year round.
