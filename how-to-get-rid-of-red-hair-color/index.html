How To Get Rid Of Red Hair Color A Comprehensive Guide

Written by William May 19, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you've recently dyed your hair red and are regretting the decision, don't worry. There are plenty of ways to get rid of red hair color without causing damage to your hair. In this article, we'll be discussing some tips, tricks, and methods for removing red hair dye. So, let's begin!

Table of Contents

  • How to Get Rid of Red Hair Color
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Removing Red Hair Dye
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Removing Red Hair Color

How to Get Rid of Red Hair Color

Before we dive into the methods for removing red hair dye, it's important to understand that the process can take time and patience. The longer you've had the red hair color, the more difficult it will be to remove. However, there are still several options available to you.

The most common methods for removing red hair color include:

  • Bleaching
  • Color-removing products
  • Natural remedies

Bleaching

Bleaching is a popular method for removing hair dye, but it can also be damaging to your hair. It's important to note that bleaching will only work on permanent hair dye and not on semi-permanent or temporary hair color. If you decide to bleach your hair, it's recommended that you go to a professional hairdresser. They will be able to assess your hair type and ensure that the bleaching process is done safely.

Color-removing products

Color-removing products are designed to strip the color from your hair without using bleach. They work by breaking down the dye molecules and washing them out of your hair. There are many products available on the market, but it's important to choose one that is specifically designed for red hair color. Some popular brands include Color Oops and Joico Color Intensity Eraser.

Natural remedies

If you're looking for a more natural approach to removing red hair dye, there are several options available. These include:

  • Vinegar
  • Baking soda
  • Lemon juice
  • Anti-dandruff shampoo

It's important to note that these remedies may not be as effective as bleaching or color-removing products, but they are a gentler alternative.

Step-by-Step Guide

Now that you understand the different methods for removing red hair color, let's dive into a step-by-step guide:

  1. Choose a method for removing the red hair dye (bleaching, color-removing product, or natural remedy).
  2. Do a patch test before applying any product to your hair. This will help ensure that you're not allergic to the product and that it won't cause any damage to your hair.
  3. Apply the product to your hair according to the instructions on the packaging.
  4. Leave the product on your hair for the recommended amount of time.
  5. Rinse your hair thoroughly with warm water.
  6. Apply a deep conditioning treatment to your hair to help restore moisture.
  7. If necessary, repeat the process until the red hair color has been removed.

Tips for Removing Red Hair Dye

Here are some additional tips to keep in mind when removing red hair dye:

  • Use a clarifying shampoo before applying any product to your hair. This will help remove any buildup and make the product more effective.
  • Be patient. Removing red hair dye can take time, especially if you've had the color for a long time.
  • Avoid using heat tools on your hair while removing the red hair dye. Heat can cause further damage to your hair.
  • Follow the instructions on the product packaging carefully. Using too much product or leaving it on for too long can cause damage to your hair.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about removing red hair dye:

Can I remove red hair dye without bleach?

Yes, there are several methods for removing red hair dye without using bleach, including color-removing products and natural remedies.

Will removing red hair dye damage my hair?

It depends on the method you choose and how well you take care of your hair afterward. Bleaching can be damaging to your hair, but color-removing products and natural remedies are typically gentler.

How long will it take to remove red hair dye?

The length of time it takes to remove red hair dye depends on the method you choose and how long you've had the color. It can take anywhere from a few days to a few weeks to completely remove the color.

Hair Care After Removing Red Hair Color

Once you've successfully removed the red hair dye, it's important to take care of your hair to ensure that it stays healthy and strong. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid using heat tools on your hair as much as possible.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to help restore moisture to your hair.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or dyes on your hair.

By following these tips, you can help ensure that your hair stays healthy and strong after removing the red hair dye.

Conclusion

Removing red hair color can be a daunting task, but with the right method and a little patience, it's possible to achieve the desired result. Whether you choose to use bleach, color-removing products, or natural remedies, be sure to follow the instructions carefully and take care of your hair afterward. With a little TLC, your hair will be back to its natural color in no time.
