How To Make A Man Bun With Curly Hair A Step By Step Guide

If you have curly hair and want to achieve the trendy man bun look, you may be wondering if it's even possible. The good news is that not only is it possible, but it's also relatively easy to do! In this guide, we'll take you through the steps to create a man bun with curly hair, as well as provide tips and hair care advice to keep your curls looking their best.

How to Make a Man Bun with Curly Hair

Making a man bun with curly hair is similar to making one with straight hair, but there are a few key differences to keep in mind. Here are the basic steps:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start by washing and conditioning your hair. Curly hair tends to be dry, so it's essential to keep it moisturized.
  2. Towel-dry your hair until it's damp but not dripping wet. Avoid rubbing your hair with the towel, as this can cause frizz.
  3. Apply a curl-defining product to your hair, such as a curl cream or gel. This will help your curls hold their shape and prevent frizz.
  4. Using a wide-tooth comb or your fingers, comb your hair back away from your face. Gather your hair into a ponytail at the crown of your head, leaving out any shorter layers or bangs.
  5. Twist the ponytail around itself to create a bun shape. Secure the bun with a hair tie or elastic band. If your hair is particularly thick or heavy, you may need to use bobby pins to keep the bun in place.
  6. Use your fingers to gently pull at the bun to create a slightly messy, relaxed look. You can also pull out a few strands around your face to frame it.
  7. Finish with a light hold hairspray to keep everything in place.

Tips for Making a Man Bun with Curly Hair

Here are a few additional tips to help you make the perfect man bun with curly hair:

  • Don't try to make your hair too slick or tight. Curly hair naturally has more volume and texture, and trying to fight against that can lead to frizz and breakage.
  • Experiment with different products to find what works best for your hair type. Some curl creams may be too heavy or greasy, while others may not provide enough hold.
  • Consider getting a trim every few months to keep your curls healthy and prevent split ends.
  • If you're struggling to get your hair into a ponytail, try using a hair tie and a couple of bobby pins to secure it in place.
  • Remember that a slightly messy, undone look is part of the appeal of the man bun. Don't worry too much about getting it perfect!

FAQ

Here are answers to some common questions about making a man bun with curly hair:

  • Can I make a man bun with short curly hair? It may be more difficult to create a full bun with shorter hair, but you can still gather your hair into a small ponytail at the back of your head and secure it with a hair tie.
  • Do I need to use a hair tie to make a man bun? While a hair tie or elastic band is the most common way to secure a man bun, you can also use bobby pins or hair clips if your hair is too short for a ponytail.
  • How do I prevent frizz in my curly hair? Regular conditioning, using a curl-defining product, and avoiding heat styling can all help reduce frizz in curly hair.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Curly hair requires a bit of extra care to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Wash your hair no more than every other day to avoid stripping it of its natural oils.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner designed for curly hair.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause frizz. Instead, detangle it gently with a wide-tooth comb or your fingers when it's wet.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to your hair after washing to help define your curls and keep them moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like blow dryers and straighteners, as these can damage curly hair. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray first and keep the temperature low.

With these tips and techniques, you'll be able to rock a stylish man bun with your curly hair in no time. Remember to embrace your natural texture and have fun with it!
