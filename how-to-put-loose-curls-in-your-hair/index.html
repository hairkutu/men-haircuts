How To Put Loose Curls In Your Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Jun 03, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're looking to add a little extra oomph to your hair, loose curls are a great way to do it. Not only are they stylish and versatile, but they're also easy to achieve with the right technique. In this post, we'll walk you through the steps of putting loose curls in your hair, from prepping your hair to finishing off your style. Let's get started!

How to Prep Your Hair for Loose Curls

Before you start curling your hair, it's important to prep it properly. Here's what you'll need to do:

1. Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start with clean, dry hair. Wash and condition your hair as you normally would, using products that are suited to your hair type.

2. Apply a Heat Protectant

Using a heat protectant spray or cream is essential to keep your hair from getting damaged from the heat of the curling iron. Apply the heat protectant to your hair, making sure to distribute it evenly.

3. Blow Dry Your Hair

Blow dry your hair until it's completely dry. You can either use a hair dryer or let your hair air dry. If you're using a hair dryer, use a round brush to give your hair some volume.

How to Put Loose Curls in Your Hair

Now that your hair is prepped and ready to go, it's time to start curling. Here's how to do it:

1. Section Your Hair

Section your hair into manageable pieces. Start with the bottom layer and work your way up. Use hair clips to keep the rest of your hair out of the way.

2. Choose Your Curling Iron

Choose a curling iron with a barrel size that's suited to the type of curls you want. For loose curls, use a larger barrel size.

3. Curl Your Hair

Take a small section of hair and wrap it around the curling iron, starting at the root and working your way down to the ends. Hold the curling iron in place for a few seconds, then release the hair. Repeat this process until all of your hair is curled.

4. Brush Out Your Curls

Once your curls have cooled down, brush them out gently with a paddle brush. This will give your curls a more natural, loose look.

Tips for Maintaining Your Loose Curls

Now that you've achieved the perfect loose curls, you'll want to make sure they last as long as possible. Here are some tips for maintaining your curls:

1. Use Hair Spray

Use a light hold hair spray to keep your curls in place. Spray it over your hair, making sure to keep the spray nozzle at least 6 inches away from your head.

2. Don't Touch Your Hair Too Much

Avoid touching your hair too much, as this can cause your curls to fall out. If you need to fix your hair, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to gently reshape the curls.

3. Sleep on a Silk Pillowcase

Sleeping on a silk pillowcase can help prevent your curls from getting frizzy or tangled overnight. It's also a great way to keep your hair looking smooth and shiny.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about putting loose curls in your hair:

1. How long do loose curls last?

Loose curls can last anywhere from a few hours to a few days, depending on your hair type and the products you use. To make your curls last longer, use a light hold hair spray and avoid touching your hair too much.

2. Can I put loose curls in short hair?

Yes, you can definitely put loose curls in short hair. Just make sure to choose a curling iron with a smaller barrel size.

3. Can I put loose curls in wet hair?

No, it's not recommended to put loose curls in wet hair. Make sure your hair is completely dry before you start curling it.

Hair Care for Loose Curls

Finally, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and shiny. Here are some tips for caring for your hair:

1. Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Once a week, use a deep conditioning treatment to give your hair some extra moisture. Apply the treatment to your hair and leave it on for 10-15 minutes before rinsing it out.

2. Avoid Heat Styling Too Often

Try to avoid heat styling your hair too often, as this can cause damage and breakage. If you do need to use heat styling tools, make sure to use a heat protectant and keep the temperature on the lower side.

3. Get Regular Trims

Getting regular trims can help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends from forming. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

By following these tips and techniques, you'll be able to put loose curls in your hair like a pro. Happy styling!
