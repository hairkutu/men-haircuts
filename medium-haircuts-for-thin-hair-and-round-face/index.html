How to Choose the Right Medium Haircut

Choosing the right haircut is essential when it comes to enhancing your features and highlighting your best attributes. For those with thin hair and a round face, there are a few key things to keep in mind when selecting a medium haircut.

Consider Your Face Shape

When selecting a medium haircut, it's important to consider your facial structure. If you have a round face, you'll want to choose a haircut that adds height and elongates your face. Layers and volume are your friends!

Think About Your Hair Texture

Thin hair can be challenging to style, but with the right cut, you can add volume and texture. Opt for a haircut that adds layers and dimension to your hair to create the illusion of thickness.

Don't Be Afraid of Bangs

Bangs are a great way to add texture and dimension to your hair. If you have a round face, opt for side-swept bangs that graze your cheekbones. This will help to elongate your face and create a more angular look.

Step-by-Step Guide to Achieving the Perfect Cut

Now that you know what to consider when selecting a medium haircut, it's time to learn how to achieve the perfect cut. Follow these simple steps to get the look you want:

Step 1: Consult with a Professional

Before you start cutting, it's important to consult with a professional hairstylist. They can help you select the right cut for your face shape and hair type.

Step 2: Wash Your Hair

Wash your hair and towel dry it before you start cutting. This will make it easier to see the natural fall of your hair and ensure an even cut.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Section your hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back. Use hair clips to secure each section and keep them out of the way.

Step 4: Cut the Back

Starting with the back section, use your scissors to create long layers. Start at the bottom of your hair and work your way up, cutting small sections at a time.

Step 5: Cut the Front

Move on to the front sections of your hair. Use your scissors to create face-framing layers that graze your cheekbones. Be sure to keep the layers longer towards the front of your face.

Step 6: Blend the Layers

Use your scissors to blend the layers together, creating a seamless look.

Step 7: Add Bangs (Optional)

If you want to add bangs, now is the time to do so. Use your scissors to create side-swept bangs that graze your cheekbones.

Tips for Styling Medium Haircuts for Thin Hair and Round Face

Now that you've achieved the perfect cut, it's time to learn how to style your new look. Here are some tips to keep in mind:

Tip 1: Use a Volumizing Shampoo and Conditioner

Thin hair can be challenging to style, but using a volumizing shampoo and conditioner can help add texture and body to your hair.

Tip 2: Blow Dry Your Hair Upside Down

Blow drying your hair upside down can help create volume and texture. Use a round brush to add shape and movement to your hair.

Tip 3: Use a Texturizing Spray

A texturizing spray can help add dimension and texture to your hair. Apply it to the roots of your hair and work your way down.

Tip 4: Experiment with Different Styles

Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles. Try wearing your hair up in a messy bun or half-up, half-down. Play around with different braids and twists to find a style that works for you.

FAQs

What is the best medium haircut for thin hair and round face?

The best medium haircut for thin hair and round face is one that adds height and dimension to your hair. Layers and volume are your friends, so opt for a cut that incorporates both.

Can I add bangs to my medium haircut?

Absolutely! Bangs can help add texture and dimension to your hair. Opt for side-swept bangs that graze your cheekbones to create a more angular look.

How can I add volume to my thin hair?

There are several ways to add volume to thin hair. Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner, blow dry your hair upside down, and use a texturizing spray to add dimension.

Hair Care Tips for Thin Hair and Round Face

Tip 1: Avoid Heat Styling Tools

Heat styling tools can damage your hair and make it appear even thinner. Opt for air drying your hair or using a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer.

Tip 2: Limit Hair Washing

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, making it appear even thinner. Aim to wash your hair every other day.

Tip 3: Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

A wide-tooth comb is gentler on your hair than a brush and can help prevent breakage.

Tip 4: Protect Your Hair from the Sun

UV rays can damage your hair and make it appear thinner. Wear a hat or use a leave-in conditioner with SPF to protect your hair from the sun.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to achieve the perfect medium haircut for thin hair and round face in no time. Remember to consult with a professional hairstylist and take care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and vibrant.
