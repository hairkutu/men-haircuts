Why Is Curly Hair Considered Unprofessional

Written by Eveline Aug 20, 2023 Â· 9 min read

Curly hair has been a topic of debate in professional settings for years. Many people believe that having curly hair can make someone appear unprofessional. In this article, we will explore the reasons why curly hair is deemed unprofessional and provide tips on how to maintain curly hair in a professional setting.

Reasons Why Curly Hair is Considered Unprofessional

There are several reasons why curly hair is considered unprofessional:

1. It is seen as unkempt

Curly hair is often associated with a messy, disheveled look. This can make it appear as though the person has not put effort into their appearance, which can be seen as unprofessional in certain settings.

2. It can be distracting

Curly hair can be quite eye-catching, especially if it is styled in a bold, voluminous way. In a professional setting, this can be distracting to colleagues or clients, which can be seen as unprofessional.

3. It can be difficult to manage

Curly hair can be more difficult to manage than straight hair. This can make it appear as though the person is not in control of their appearance, which can be seen as unprofessional.

How to Maintain Curly Hair in a Professional Setting

Just because curly hair is often seen as unprofessional doesn't mean that you have to straighten it every day. Here are some tips on how to maintain curly hair in a professional setting:

1. Find a style that works for you

Not all curly hair is the same, so it's important to find a style that works for your specific hair type. This may take some trial and error, but once you find a style that works for you, it will be much easier to maintain.

2. Use the right products

Curly hair requires different products than straight hair. Look for products that are specifically designed for curly hair, such as curl-enhancing shampoos and conditioners, and styling products like curl creams and gels.

3. Keep it under control

While bold, voluminous curls may be fun for a night out, they may not be appropriate for a professional setting. Keep your curls under control by using a diffuser when blow-drying, and avoiding excessive teasing or backcombing.

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Curly Hair Professionally

Here is a step-by-step guide to styling curly hair for a professional setting:

1. Wash your hair

Start by washing your hair with a curl-enhancing shampoo and conditioner. Be sure to rinse thoroughly to avoid any product buildup.

2. Apply styling product

Apply a curl cream or gel to damp hair, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends. Be sure to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair.

3. Diffuse your hair

Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair. This will help to enhance your curls without causing frizz.

4. Style as desired

Once your hair is dry, style it as desired. You may want to pull it back into a neat ponytail or bun, or leave it down with a few face-framing curls.

Tips for Keeping Curly Hair Sleek and Professional

Here are some additional tips for keeping curly hair looking sleek and professional:

1. Use a silk scarf

Wrap your hair in a silk scarf at night to help prevent frizz and maintain your curls' shape.

2. Avoid excessive heat

Avoid using excessive heat on your hair, as this can cause damage and frizz. If you must use heat, be sure to use a heat protectant spray first.

3. Trim regularly

Regular trims are important for maintaining healthy, sleek curls. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

FAQs About Curly Hair in Professional Settings

1. Can I wear my hair down in a professional setting if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can wear your hair down in a professional setting if you have curly hair. Just be sure to keep it under control and avoid excessive volume or teasing.

2. Is it unprofessional to have brightly colored or dyed curly hair?

In some professional settings, brightly colored or dyed curly hair may be seen as unprofessional. However, in more creative or artistic fields, it may be more acceptable.

Essential Hair Care for Curly Hair

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining healthy, sleek curls. Here are some tips for caring for curly hair:

1. Use a sulfate-free shampoo

Sulfates can be harsh on curly hair, so look for a shampoo that is sulfate-free.

2. Condition regularly

Curly hair tends to be dry, so be sure to condition regularly to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

3. Avoid brushing

Brushing curly hair can cause frizz and breakage. Instead, use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair.

4. Protect your hair from the sun

UV rays can be damaging to your hair, so be sure to protect your curls by wearing a hat or using a hair product with UV protection.

By following these tips, you can maintain healthy, sleek curls that are appropriate for any professional setting.
