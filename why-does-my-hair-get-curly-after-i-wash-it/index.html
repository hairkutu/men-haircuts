Why Does My Hair Get Curly After I Wash It

Written by Petter May 09, 2023 · 8 min read

Curly hair can be a blessing and a curse. On one hand, it’s voluminous and full of texture. On the other hand, it can be difficult to manage and style. If you’ve ever wondered why your hair gets curly after you wash it, you’re not alone. In this article, we’ll explore the science behind curly hair and provide tips for managing it.

The Science Behind Curly Hair

Curly hair is caused by the shape of the hair follicle. Straight hair follicles are round, while curly hair follicles are oval or elliptical. The shape of the hair follicle determines the shape of the hair shaft. Straight hair shafts are round, while curly hair shafts are twisted or bent. This is why curly hair appears more textured and voluminous than straight hair.

When you wash your hair, you’re introducing moisture to the hair shaft. This moisture can cause the hair shaft to expand, which can make curly hair appear even curlier. Additionally, the weight of wet hair can cause the hair to stretch, which can also contribute to the appearance of curlier hair.

How to Manage Curly Hair

Managing curly hair can be a challenge, but it’s not impossible. The key is to find a hair care routine that works for your specific hair type and texture. Here are some tips for managing curly hair:

1. Use a sulfate-free shampoo

Sulfates can strip the natural oils from your hair, leaving it dry and frizzy. Look for a sulfate-free shampoo that’s designed for curly hair.

2. Condition regularly

Curly hair tends to be dry, so it’s important to condition regularly. Look for a conditioner that’s designed for curly hair and leave it on for at least 5 minutes before rinsing.

3. Use a wide-toothed comb

Curly hair is more prone to tangling, so use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair when it’s wet. Start at the ends and work your way up to the roots.

4. Avoid heat styling

Heat styling can damage curly hair, so avoid using a blow dryer, flat iron, or curling iron as much as possible. If you must use heat, use a heat protectant spray and keep the heat on a low setting.

Step-by-Step Guide to Managing Curly Hair

Here’s a step-by-step guide to managing curly hair:

1. Shampoo and condition your hair

Use a sulfate-free shampoo and a conditioner designed for curly hair. Leave the conditioner on for at least 5 minutes before rinsing.

2. Gently pat your hair dry with a towel

Don’t rub your hair with a towel, as this can cause frizz. Instead, gently pat your hair dry.

3. Apply a leave-in conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair to help detangle and moisturize your curls.

4. Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair

Start at the ends and work your way up to the roots.

5. Let your hair air dry

Air drying is the best way to avoid frizz and damage from heat styling.

Tips for Managing Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips for managing curly hair:

  • Don’t brush your hair when it’s dry, as this can cause frizz.
  • Use a diffuser attachment on your blow dryer to dry your hair.
  • Invest in a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz while you sleep.
  • Try a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why does my hair get curly after I wash it?

When you wash your hair, you’re introducing moisture to the hair shaft. This moisture can cause the hair shaft to expand, which can make curly hair appear even curlier.

Can I straighten my curly hair?

Yes, but be careful not to damage your hair with heat styling. Use a heat protectant spray and keep the heat on a low setting.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Here are some additional tips for caring for curly hair:

  • Avoid harsh chemicals and treatments, as they can damage curly hair.
  • Don’t wash your hair every day, as this can strip the natural oils from your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s dry, as this can cause frizz.

In conclusion, curly hair can be a challenge to manage, but with the right hair care routine, it can be beautiful and full of texture. Use sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner, detangle with a wide-toothed comb, and avoid heat styling as much as possible. With these tips, you’ll be able to manage your curly hair like a pro.
