Difference Between Light Thinning And Progressed Thinning Hair

Written by Frank May 10, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you experiencing hair loss but unsure if it is light thinning or progressed thinning? Knowing the difference between the two can help you determine the best hair care routine and treatments for your specific hair needs. In this article, we will discuss the differences between light thinning and progressed thinning hair, the causes of hair thinning, and tips on how to care for your thinning hair.

Table of Contents

  • Light Thinning Hair
  • Progressed Thinning Hair
  • Causes of Hair Thinning
  • Hair Care Tips for Thinning Hair
  • FAQs

Light Thinning Hair

Light thinning hair is the initial stage of hair loss. It is characterized by a gradual thinning of the hair strands, and you may notice more hair shedding than usual. This stage of thinning is typically reversible with proper hair care and treatment.

With light thinning hair, you may notice that your hair is not as full or voluminous as it once was. However, you may not notice any bald spots or significant hair loss. In most cases, light thinning hair is not noticeable to others and can be easily managed with the right hair care routine.

Progressed Thinning Hair

Progressed thinning hair is the advanced stage of hair loss. At this stage, the hair strands become much thinner, and you may notice bald spots or patches on your scalp. This type of hair loss may be caused by genetics, hormonal changes, or health conditions such as alopecia.

Unlike light thinning hair, progressed thinning hair is noticeable to others and can have a significant impact on your self-esteem. If you are experiencing progressed thinning hair, it is important to seek professional help to determine the best course of treatment.

Causes of Hair Thinning

There are several causes of hair thinning, including:

  • Genetics
  • Hormonal changes
  • Stress
  • Dietary deficiencies
  • Medical conditions
  • Medications

Understanding the cause of your hair thinning can help you determine the best course of treatment.

Hair Care Tips for Thinning Hair

If you are experiencing light thinning hair, there are several things you can do to care for your hair and prevent further thinning:

  • Use a gentle, sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using heat styling tools
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair
  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet
  • Take hair growth supplements
  • Massage your scalp regularly to stimulate hair growth

If you are experiencing progressed thinning hair, it is important to seek professional help from a dermatologist or hair loss specialist. They may recommend treatments such as hair transplants, scalp reduction surgery, or medication to slow down the hair loss process.

FAQs

Can hair thinning be reversed?

Light thinning hair can be reversed with proper hair care and treatment. However, progressed thinning hair may be more difficult to reverse and may require professional treatment.

Will wearing a hat make my hair thin?

No, wearing a hat will not make your hair thin. However, wearing a tight hat or helmet for an extended period may cause hair breakage or damage.

Does stress cause hair thinning?

Yes, stress can cause hair thinning. When you are under stress, your body produces a hormone called cortisol, which can lead to hair loss.

What vitamins are good for hair growth?

Some vitamins that are good for hair growth include biotin, vitamin D, and iron. You can also take hair growth supplements that contain these vitamins.


In conclusion, knowing the difference between light thinning and progressed thinning hair is essential in determining the best hair care routine and treatments for your specific hair needs. Remember to seek professional help if you are experiencing progressed thinning hair and to take good care of your hair to prevent further thinning.
