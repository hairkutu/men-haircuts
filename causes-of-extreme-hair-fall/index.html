Causes Of Extreme Hair Fall Understanding The Reasons Behind Hair Loss

Are you experiencing excessive hair fall and wondering what could be the reason behind it? Hair fall can be caused by several factors, from genetics to lifestyle habits. In this article, we will discuss the different causes of extreme hair fall and how to prevent it.

Table of Contents

  • Genetics
  • Stress
  • Hormonal Changes
  • Nutritional Deficiencies
  • Medical Conditions
  • Styling and Chemical Treatments
  • Medications
  • Age
  • Pregnancy and Childbirth
  • Thyroid Disorders

Genetics

One of the primary causes of hair fall is genetics. If your family has a history of hair loss or baldness, you are more likely to experience it too. Male-pattern baldness is the most common type of hair loss in men, while women usually experience thinning of hair all over the scalp.

Stress

Stress is another significant factor that can lead to hair fall. When you are stressed, your body produces a hormone called cortisol, which can interfere with hair growth. Furthermore, stress can cause scalp inflammation, leading to hair fall. Therefore, it is essential to manage your stress levels through relaxation techniques, exercise, and meditation.

Hormonal Changes

Hormonal changes can also lead to hair fall. During menopause, women experience a decline in estrogen levels, which can result in hair thinning and loss. Similarly, hormonal imbalances in both men and women can lead to hair loss. For instance, an overactive or underactive thyroid gland can cause hair loss.

Nutritional Deficiencies

Nutritional deficiencies can also cause hair fall. A lack of essential nutrients like vitamins, minerals, and proteins can affect hair growth and lead to hair loss. Therefore, it is crucial to maintain a healthy and balanced diet that includes foods rich in nutrients like iron, zinc, and biotin.

Medical Conditions

Several medical conditions can cause hair fall. For instance, autoimmune diseases like lupus and alopecia areata can cause hair loss. Additionally, scalp infections and skin disorders like psoriasis and eczema can also lead to hair fall. Therefore, it is essential to consult a doctor if you are experiencing excessive hair fall.

Styling and Chemical Treatments

Styling and chemical treatments can also lead to hair fall. Excessive use of heat styling tools like curling irons and flat irons can damage hair and lead to breakage. Additionally, chemical treatments like hair dyeing, perming, and straightening can weaken hair and cause it to fall out. Therefore, it is essential to limit the use of styling tools and chemical treatments.

Medications

Several medications can cause hair fall as a side effect. For instance, chemotherapy drugs used to treat cancer can lead to hair loss. Similarly, medications used to treat depression, high blood pressure, and heart disease can also cause hair fall. Therefore, it is essential to consult a doctor before starting any new medication.

Age

Age is another significant factor that can lead to hair fall. As you age, your hair follicles shrink and produce thinner hair strands. Additionally, the production of natural oils that keep hair healthy decreases with age, leading to hair fall.

Pregnancy and Childbirth

Pregnancy and childbirth can also lead to hair fall. During pregnancy, hormonal changes can lead to increased hair growth. However, after childbirth, the hormonal changes can cause hair to fall out. Therefore, it is essential to take care of your hair during this period and consult a doctor if you experience excessive hair fall.

Thyroid Disorders

Thyroid disorders like hypothyroidism and hyperthyroidism can also lead to hair fall. These conditions can disrupt the production of hormones that regulate hair growth, leading to hair loss. Therefore, it is essential to consult a doctor if you suspect you have a thyroid disorder.

Conclusion

Excessive hair fall can be caused by several factors, from genetics to lifestyle habits. Therefore, it is crucial to identify the underlying cause of hair fall and take steps to prevent it. This can include maintaining a healthy and balanced diet, managing stress levels, limiting the use of styling tools and chemical treatments, and consulting a doctor if you experience excessive hair fall.
