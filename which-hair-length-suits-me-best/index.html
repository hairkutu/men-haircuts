Which Hair Length Suits Me Best A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Aprilia Apr 23, 2023 · 9 min read

Choosing the right hair length can be a daunting task, especially if you’re not sure what suits you best. With so many options available, it’s important to consider factors such as face shape, hair texture, and personal style. In this article, we’ll guide you through the process of finding the perfect hair length for you.

  • Hair Texture and Length
  • Personal Style
  • Hair Length Options
  • How to Get the Look

  • Final Thoughts

How to Determine Your Face Shape

The first step in finding the right hair length is to determine your face shape. This will help you identify which features to highlight and which to downplay. There are six main face shapes: oval, round, square, heart, diamond, and oblong.

To determine your face shape, stand in front of a mirror and pull your hair back. Use a measuring tape to take the following measurements:

  • Forehead: measure from the highest point of one eyebrow arch to the other
  • Cheekbones: measure across your cheekbones, starting and ending at the pointiest part below the outer corner of each eye
  • Jawline: measure from the tip of your chin to the point below your ear where your jaw angles upwards. Then multiply that number by two to get the full length of your jawline.
  • Face length: measure from the center of your hairline to the tip of your chin

Once you have these measurements, compare them to the guidelines below:

  • Oval: forehead is slightly wider than the jawline and the face gently tapers towards the chin
  • Round: cheekbones and face length have a similar measurement and the face is widest at the cheeks
  • Square: all measurements are fairly similar and the jawline is sharp
  • Heart: forehead is wider than the cheeks and the face gently tapers towards the chin
  • Diamond: cheekbones are the widest part of the face and the forehead and jawline are narrow
  • Oblong: face length is the greatest measurement and the forehead, cheekbones, and jawline are similar in size

Once you have determined your face shape, you can start considering which hair length will suit you best.

Hair Texture and Length

Another important factor to consider when choosing a hair length is your hair texture. Hair texture refers to the thickness and coarseness of your hair.

If you have fine hair, shorter lengths tend to work better. This is because longer hair can weigh fine hair down, making it look limp and lifeless. On the other hand, if you have thick or coarse hair, longer lengths can help to balance out the volume.

Curly hair also has its own set of rules when it comes to length. Curly hair tends to look shorter when it’s dry, so it’s important to take this into consideration when choosing a length.

Personal Style

Your personal style is another important factor to consider when choosing a hair length. If you prefer a more natural, low-maintenance look, shorter lengths may be the best option. On the other hand, if you enjoy experimenting with different styles and have the time to dedicate to styling, longer lengths may be a better fit.

It’s also important to consider your profession and lifestyle. If you work in a conservative environment, a shorter, more polished look may be best. If you lead an active lifestyle, a shorter, low-maintenance look may be more practical.

Hair Length Options

Now that you’ve considered your face shape, hair texture, and personal style, it’s time to explore your hair length options. Here are some of the most popular hair lengths:

  • Short: above the chin
  • Medium: between the chin and shoulders
  • Long: below the shoulders
  • Extra-long: waist-length or longer

Within each of these lengths, there are also variations that can be tailored to your personal style. For example, a short bob can be customized with bangs or layers, while long hair can be styled in loose waves or sleek and straight.

How to Get the Look

Once you’ve chosen your ideal hair length, it’s important to work with a stylist to achieve the look you want. Bring in photos of styles you like and be sure to communicate your preferences clearly.

It’s also important to consider the upkeep required for your chosen length. Shorter lengths may require more frequent trims to maintain their shape, while longer lengths may require more conditioning and detangling.

Tips for Maintaining Your Hair Length

Regardless of which length you choose, there are a few tips you can follow to keep your hair healthy and looking its best:

  • Use a heat protectant when using hot styling tools
  • Invest in a good quality shampoo and conditioner
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends
  • Avoid over-styling or over-processing your hair

FAQ

Q: Will a certain hair length make me look younger or older?

A: There’s no one-size-fits-all answer to this question, as it depends on a variety of factors. However, in general, shorter hair tends to be more youthful, while longer hair can be more mature.

Q: Can hair length affect my face shape?

A: Yes, hair length can make a big difference in how your face shape is perceived. For example, longer hair can elongate a round face, while a shorter cut can help to balance out a square face.

Final Thoughts

Choosing the right hair length is all about finding a style that complements your natural features and personal style. By considering factors such as face shape, hair texture, and lifestyle, you can find a length that makes you feel confident and beautiful. Remember to work with a stylist to achieve the look you want and to maintain your hair’s health with regular trims and proper care.
