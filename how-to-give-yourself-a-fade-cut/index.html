How To Give Yourself A Fade Cut A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine Apr 21, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of spending money on haircuts every month? Are you looking for a way to give yourself a fresh, new look? If so, a fade cut may be the perfect solution. In this guide, we'll show you how to give yourself a fade cut from the comfort of your own home.

Table of Contents

  1. Introduction
  2. How to Give Yourself a Fade Cut
  3. Step-by-Step Guide
  4. Tips for a Perfect Fade Cut
  5. FAQs

Introduction

A fade cut is a popular hairstyle that involves cutting the hair short on the sides and back, while leaving the hair on top longer. This creates a gradual fade from short to long hair, giving the hair a modern and stylish look. While many people prefer to get their fade cut done by a professional barber or stylist, it is possible to give yourself a fade cut at home with a little practice and patience. In this guide, we'll show you how to give yourself a fade cut using clippers and scissors.

How to Give Yourself a Fade Cut

Before we get started, it's important to note that giving yourself a fade cut can be challenging, especially if you've never done it before. However, with a little practice and patience, you can achieve a great-looking fade cut that will save you time and money in the long run. Here's what you'll need:

  • Clippers
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Haircutting cape or towel
  • Handheld mirror
  • Styling products (optional)

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these steps to give yourself a fade cut:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Use a comb to part your hair where you normally wear it.
  2. Use the clippers to trim the hair on the sides and back of your head. Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter guard for a gradual fade. Use the handheld mirror to check your progress and make sure you're cutting evenly.
  3. Blend the hair on the sides and back into the hair on top using the scissors. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut at an angle to create a seamless transition.
  4. Trim the hair on top to your desired length using the scissors. You can also use the clippers with a longer guard to trim the hair on top.
  5. Use the scissors to trim any stray or uneven hairs.
  6. Blend the hair around your ears and neckline using the clippers and scissors.
  7. Clean up any loose hairs with a brush or towel.
  8. Style your hair using your favorite products (optional).

Tips for a Perfect Fade Cut

Here are some tips to help you achieve a great-looking fade cut:

  • Practice makes perfect. Don't expect to get it right on your first try.
  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter guard for a gradual fade.
  • Use the handheld mirror to check your progress and make sure you're cutting evenly.
  • Take your time and don't rush the process.
  • Ask a friend or family member to help if you're having trouble.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about giving yourself a fade cut:

  • Can I give myself a fade cut if I've never done it before?
  • Yes, but it may take some practice and patience. Be prepared to make mistakes and learn from them.
  • What tools do I need to give myself a fade cut?
  • You'll need clippers, scissors, a comb, a haircutting cape or towel, a handheld mirror, and styling products (optional).
  • Can I give myself a fade cut with just clippers?
  • Yes, but it may be more difficult to achieve a seamless transition without using scissors.
  • How often should I give myself a fade cut?
  • It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly the fade grows out. Most people get a fade cut every 2-4 weeks.

Hair Care Tips

Here are some tips to keep your fade cut looking great:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, such as blow dryers and straighteners.
  • Use styling products sparingly to avoid buildup and greasiness.
  • Visit a professional barber or stylist every few months to keep your fade cut looking fresh.
  • Enjoy your new, stylish look!
