How To Do Natural Curls For Black Hair A Complete Guide

Written by Robby Mar 20, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of using heat styling tools to achieve curls in your black hair? Well, the good news is that you don't have to! In fact, there are many ways to achieve natural curls without damaging your hair. In this blog post, we will guide you through the process of achieving natural curls for black hair.

Table of Contents

  • How To
  • Step By Step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

How To

Before we dive into the methods of achieving natural curls, it's important to understand that not all curl patterns are the same. There are different types of black hair, and each hair type requires different techniques to achieve natural curls. It's important to identify your hair type before attempting any of these methods.

Once you've identified your hair type, you can start experimenting with different methods to achieve natural curls. Here are some of the most popular methods:

Method 1: Twist Out

The twist out method involves dividing your hair into sections and twisting each section tightly. After leaving the twists in overnight, you can unravel them to reveal natural curls. This method works best on hair that is at least shoulder length.

Method 2: Bantu Knots

Bantu knots involve twisting your hair into small knots and leaving them in overnight. When you unravel the knots, you'll have natural curls. This method works best on hair that is at least chin length.

Method 3: Flexi Rods

Flexi rods are bendable rods that can be used to create natural curls. To use flexi rods, you'll need to wrap your hair around the rods and leave them in overnight. This method works on all hair lengths.

Step By Step

Now that you know the different methods for achieving natural curls, let's go through the step-by-step process of each method:

Step 1: Wash and Condition Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair. This will help to remove any product buildup and prepare your hair for styling.

Step 2: Apply Styling Product

Apply a styling product to your hair. This can be a curl defining cream, gel, or mousse. Be sure to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair.

Step 3: Divide Your Hair Into Sections

Divide your hair into sections. The size of the sections will depend on the length of your hair.

Step 4: Twist, Knot, or Wrap Your Hair

Depending on the method you've chosen, twist, knot, or wrap your hair. Be sure to wrap each section tightly and evenly.

Step 5: Leave Your Hair to Set Overnight

Leave your hair to set overnight. This will give your hair enough time to form natural curls.

Step 6: Unravel Your Hair

In the morning, unravel your hair to reveal your natural curls.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you achieve the best results when doing natural curls for black hair:

  • Use a wide-toothed comb or your fingers to detangle your hair before styling.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to your hair before applying styling product.
  • Try different methods to see which one works best for your hair type.
  • Use a satin or silk pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage while you sleep.

FAQ

Q: How long will my natural curls last?

A: The length of time your natural curls will last depends on your hair type and the method you used. On average, natural curls can last anywhere from 2-5 days.

Q: Can I use heat styling tools to enhance my natural curls?

A: While it's not recommended to use heat styling tools on a regular basis, you can use them occasionally to enhance your natural curls. Just be sure to use a heat protectant spray before using any heat styling tools.

Q: How often should I do natural curls?

A: You can do natural curls as often as you like. However, it's important to give your hair a break in between styling to prevent damage.

Hair Care

It's important to take care of your hair before and after styling to prevent damage. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to prevent stripping your hair of natural oils.
  • Deep condition your hair at least once a week to keep it moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools on a regular basis.
  • Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends.

Now that you know how to do natural curls for black hair, you can experiment with different methods to find the one that works best for you. Remember to take care of your hair before and after styling to prevent damage.
