How To Style Fine Thick Hair A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Petter Jun 02, 2023 · 11 min read

If you have fine thick hair, you know the struggle of finding the right hairstyle that flatters your face shape and looks voluminous. It can be challenging to find the perfect balance between volume and texture, but fear not – we’ve got you covered. In this guide, we’ll go through everything you need to know about styling fine thick hair, including step-by-step tutorials, tips, and frequently asked questions. So, let’s get started!

Description

Fine thick hair is a hair type that has a small strand diameter but a lot of hair on the scalp. It’s often confused with coarse hair, but the two are entirely different. Fine thick hair is prone to breakage, and it can be challenging to manage without the right styling techniques. However, with the right styling tools and products, you can create a voluminous and textured look that will make heads turn.

How to Style Fine Thick Hair

Styling fine thick hair can be overwhelming, but it doesn’t have to be. With the right tools and techniques, you can create a stylish and voluminous look that flatters your face shape. Here are some tips on how to style fine thick hair:

1. Start with clean hair

Before you start styling your hair, make sure it’s clean and free of any product buildup. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any residue from your hair, and follow up with a nourishing conditioner to keep your strands hydrated and healthy.

2. Use the right styling products

When styling fine thick hair, it’s essential to use the right products to create volume and texture. Use a volumizing mousse or spray to add volume to your roots, and follow up with a texturizing spray or dry shampoo to create texture and definition.

3. Blow-dry your hair

Blow-drying your hair is an excellent way to create volume and texture. Use a round brush to lift your roots and create volume, and use a diffuser attachment to dry your hair without causing damage. Make sure to use a heat protectant spray to protect your hair from heat damage.

4. Experiment with different hairstyles

There are countless hairstyles that flatter fine thick hair, so don’t be afraid to experiment. From bouncy curls to sleek ponytails, the possibilities are endless. Just make sure to use the right products and tools to create your desired look.

Step-by-Step Guide to Styling Fine Thick Hair

Now that you know the basics of styling fine thick hair, let’s go through a step-by-step guide to creating a voluminous and textured look:

Step 1: Start with clean hair

As mentioned earlier, it’s essential to start with clean hair to achieve the best results. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup, and follow up with a nourishing conditioner to keep your strands hydrated.

Step 2: Apply a volumizing mousse

Apply a volumizing mousse to your hair, focusing on your roots. Use your fingers to massage the product into your scalp, and make sure it’s evenly distributed throughout your hair.

Step 3: Blow-dry your hair

Blow-dry your hair using a round brush to create volume and texture. Start at your roots and work your way down to the ends, using the brush to lift your roots and create volume. Use a diffuser attachment to dry your hair without causing damage.

Step 4: Add texture with a texturizing spray

Once your hair is dry, add texture and definition with a texturizing spray or dry shampoo. Spray the product onto your roots and use your fingers to massage it into your scalp. This will create volume and texture, making your hair look fuller and more voluminous.

Step 5: Finish with a hairspray

Finish your look with a light-hold hairspray to keep your hair in place. Spray the product onto your hair, focusing on your roots and the ends, and use your fingers to shape your hair into your desired style.

Tips for Styling Fine Thick Hair

Styling fine thick hair can be challenging, but with these tips, you’ll be able to create a voluminous and textured look in no time:

1. Use a clarifying shampoo once a week

Using a clarifying shampoo once a week will remove any product buildup from your hair, allowing your strands to breathe and promoting healthy hair growth.

2. Invest in quality styling tools

Investing in quality styling tools, such as a good blow dryer and round brush, will make a significant difference in the health and appearance of your hair.

3. Don’t wash your hair too often

Washing your hair too often can strip your strands of their natural oils, causing your hair to become dry and brittle. Try to wash your hair every other day or every two days, depending on your hair type.

FAQ

1. Can I use a flat iron on my fine thick hair?

Yes, you can use a flat iron on your fine thick hair, but make sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent heat damage. Use the lowest heat setting possible and avoid using the flat iron on wet hair.

2. How often should I cut my hair?

It’s recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks to promote healthy hair growth and prevent split ends.

3. How can I prevent my fine thick hair from becoming greasy?

To prevent your fine thick hair from becoming greasy, avoid using heavy styling products and wash your hair every other day or every two days. You can also use a dry shampoo to absorb excess oil and add volume to your roots.

Hair Care for Fine Thick Hair

Finally, taking care of your hair is crucial to maintaining healthy and voluminous strands. Here are some tips for hair care for fine thick hair:

1. Use a nourishing shampoo and conditioner

Using a nourishing shampoo and conditioner will keep your strands hydrated and healthy. Look for products that contain natural ingredients such as coconut oil, argan oil, and aloe vera.

2. Avoid using hot tools too often

Using hot tools too often can cause heat damage, leading to dry and brittle hair. Try to limit your use of hot tools and use a heat protectant spray when necessary.

3. Protect your hair from the sun

Exposure to the sun’s UV rays can cause your hair to become dry and damaged. Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a hair product that contains UV protection.

Conclusion

Styling fine thick hair can be challenging, but with the right tools and techniques, you can create a voluminous and textured look that flatters your face shape. Remember to start with clean hair, use the right products, and experiment with different hairstyles. With these tips and tricks, you’ll be able to achieve a stunning look that will make heads turn.
