Does Waxing Back Hair Make It Grow Thicker

Written by Luffy Jun 14, 2023 · 7 min read

Waxing is a popular hair removal method that has been used for centuries. It is a great way to remove unwanted hair from different parts of the body. However, one question that often comes up is whether waxing back hair makes it grow thicker. In this article, we will explore this question and give you all the information you need to know about waxing back hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Wax Back Hair
  • Step by Step Guide
  • Tips for Waxing Back Hair
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care after Waxing

Description

Waxing back hair involves applying warm wax to the back and then pulling it off with a cloth strip. This method removes hair from the root and leaves the skin smooth and hair-free for a longer period than shaving. However, some people believe that waxing back hair makes it grow thicker and darker, which is a common misconception.

How to Wax Back Hair

Before waxing back hair, make sure the hair is long enough for the wax to grip onto. Usually, the hair should be at least ¼ inch long. If the hair is too short, the wax won't be able to grab onto it, and the hair won't be removed.

Next, clean the back area thoroughly and apply talcum powder to make sure the skin is dry. Then, apply the warm wax to the back in sections using a wooden spatula. Make sure the wax is applied in the direction of hair growth.

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to wax back hair:

  1. Clean the back area thoroughly and dry it with a towel.
  2. Apply talcum powder to the back to ensure the skin is dry.
  3. Heat the wax in a wax warmer or microwave according to the instructions on the package.
  4. Use a wooden spatula to apply the wax in sections, following the direction of hair growth.
  5. Press a cloth strip onto the wax and smooth it down with your hand.
  6. Hold the skin taut with one hand and use the other hand to pull the cloth strip off in the opposite direction of hair growth.
  7. Repeat the process until all the hair has been removed.
  8. Apply a cooling gel or lotion to soothe the skin and reduce redness.

Tips for Waxing Back Hair

Here are some tips to ensure a successful and pain-free waxing experience:

  • Use a good quality wax that is designed for back hair removal.
  • Apply the wax in sections to avoid applying too much or too little wax.
  • Hold the skin taut when pulling off the wax strip to reduce pain and ensure all the hair is removed.
  • Apply a cooling gel or lotion to soothe the skin after waxing.
  • Avoid sun exposure for at least 24 hours after waxing to prevent skin irritation.

FAQ

Does waxing back hair make it grow thicker?

No, waxing back hair does not make it grow thicker. Waxing removes hair from the root, which can make the hair grow back softer and finer over time.

How long does waxing back hair last?

Waxing back hair can last up to 6 weeks, depending on how fast the hair grows back.

Is waxing back hair painful?

Waxing can be painful, especially on sensitive areas like the back. However, the pain is usually brief and can be reduced by holding the skin taut when pulling off the wax strip.

Hair Care after Waxing

After waxing back hair, it's important to take good care of the skin to prevent irritation and ingrown hairs. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid hot showers or baths for at least 24 hours after waxing.
  • Avoid wearing tight clothing that rubs against the skin.
  • Exfoliate the skin regularly to prevent ingrown hairs.
  • Apply a moisturizer to keep the skin hydrated.

In conclusion, waxing back hair does not make it grow thicker. Waxing is a safe and effective method of removing unwanted hair from the back, and with proper care, the skin can be kept smooth and hair-free for several weeks.
