What Does A Stacked Haircut Look Like

Written by Aprilia Aug 07, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you looking for a new hairstyle but not sure what to choose? A stacked haircut may be just what you need. This trendy cut has been popular for years and looks great on all hair types. In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know about what a stacked haircut looks like, how to get it, and how to care for it.

Description

A stacked haircut is a layered cut that creates volume and movement in your hair. The layers are cut shorter in the back and gradually get longer towards the front. This creates a "stacked" effect that adds dimension and texture to your hair. The back of the hair is usually cut to just below the nape of the neck, while the front can be cut to chin length or longer.

The stacked haircut is also known as an inverted bob, as the hair is shorter in the back and longer in the front. It's a versatile style that can be worn straight, curled, or in loose waves.

How to Get a Stacked Haircut

If you're interested in getting a stacked haircut, it's important to find a hairstylist who has experience with this style. They will be able to create the perfect layers and angles to suit your face shape and hair type.

During your appointment, your stylist will start by washing and drying your hair. They will then section your hair and cut the layers at an angle, creating the stacked effect. Once the cut is complete, they may add some additional layers or texture using thinning shears or a razor.

After your haircut, your stylist may recommend some styling products to help you recreate the look at home. These could include volumizing mousse, texturizing spray, or a heat protectant spray if you plan to use heat styling tools.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide on how to get a stacked haircut:

Step 1: Choose a hairstylist

Find a hairstylist who has experience with stacked haircuts. Look at their portfolio to see if they have done this style before and read reviews from previous customers.

Step 2: Wash and dry your hair

Before your appointment, make sure your hair is clean and dry. Your stylist will wash and dry your hair again before starting the cut.

Step 3: Section your hair

Your stylist will section your hair and clip it up, starting at the back of your head.

Step 4: Cut the layers

Your stylist will cut the layers at an angle, starting with the shortest layer at the back of your head. They will gradually cut longer layers towards the front, creating the stacked effect.

Step 5: Add texture

If necessary, your stylist may add some additional layers or texture using thinning shears or a razor.

Step 6: Style your hair

Your stylist may recommend some styling products to help you recreate the look at home. Style your hair as desired using a blow dryer, straightener, or curling iron.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you maintain your stacked haircut:

  • Use a volumizing mousse to add volume to your roots
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair to prevent damage
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape of your cut
  • Use a texturizing spray to add texture and movement to your hair
  • Consider getting highlights or lowlights to add dimension to your hair

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about stacked haircuts:

How long does a stacked haircut last?

A stacked haircut typically lasts 6-8 weeks before it starts to lose its shape. It's important to get regular trims to maintain the shape of the cut.

Can a stacked haircut work for all hair types?

Yes, a stacked haircut can work for all hair types. Your stylist will be able to adapt the cut to suit your hair texture and face shape.

Can I style my stacked haircut in different ways?

Yes, a stacked haircut is versatile and can be styled in many different ways. You can wear it straight, curled, or in loose waves.

Hair Care

Here are some tips for taking care of your stacked haircut:

  • Wash your hair every 2-3 days to prevent it from becoming too oily
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent damage to your hair
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair to prevent damage
  • Use a heat protectant spray if you plan to use heat styling tools
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape of your cut

Now that you know what a stacked haircut looks like, you can decide if this style is right for you. Remember to find a hairstylist who has experience with this cut and to take good care of your hair to keep it looking great.
