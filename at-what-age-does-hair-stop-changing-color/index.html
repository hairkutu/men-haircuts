At What Age Does Hair Stop Changing Color

Written by Daniel Jun 24, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Have you ever wondered at what age hair stops changing color? As we age, many changes happen in our bodies, and our hair is no exception. This article will explain the science behind hair color changes, how to care for your hair as it ages, and when you can expect your hair to stop changing color.

Table of Contents

  • The Science Behind Hair Color Changes
  • How Aging Affects Hair Color
  • When Hair Stops Changing Color
  • Hair Care Tips for Aging Hair
  • FAQs

The Science Behind Hair Color Changes

Hair color is determined by the amount and type of melanin present in the hair shaft. Melanin is a pigment that gives skin, hair, and eyes their color, and there are two types of melanin: eumelanin and pheomelanin. Eumelanin is responsible for brown and black hair, while pheomelanin is responsible for red and blonde hair.

As we age, our bodies produce less melanin, and the melanocytes (cells that produce melanin) in our hair follicles start to die. This results in less pigment being deposited in the hair shaft, which causes our hair to turn gray or white.

How Aging Affects Hair Color

Most people start to notice gray hairs in their 30s or 40s, but the age at which hair starts to change color varies from person to person. Genetics play a large role in when and how much gray hair a person will have.

Other factors that can affect when hair starts to change color include stress, illness, and certain medications. These can cause premature graying or accelerate the graying process.

When Hair Stops Changing Color

While hair can continue to gray or turn white as we age, there is no specific age at which hair stops changing color. Some people continue to have pigment in their hair well into their 80s or 90s, while others may go completely gray in their 50s or 60s.

However, it is safe to say that by the time a person reaches their 70s or 80s, their hair is unlikely to continue changing color.

Hair Care Tips for Aging Hair

As we age, our hair becomes thinner, drier, and more brittle. It is important to take care of your hair to keep it healthy and looking its best.

Here are some tips for caring for aging hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that is formulated for aging hair.
  • Avoid using hot styling tools, which can damage hair and make it more brittle.
  • Consider using a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to help nourish and strengthen hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protective spray.

FAQs

Can stress cause hair to change color?

While stress can cause hair loss, it is unlikely to cause hair to change color. However, stress can cause premature graying in some people.

Is it safe to dye gray hair?

Yes, it is safe to dye gray hair. However, be sure to use a gentle, ammonia-free hair dye to avoid further damage to aging hair.

Can diet affect hair color?

While diet can affect the health of your hair, it is unlikely to affect hair color.

Now that you know at what age hair stops changing color, you can take steps to care for your aging hair and keep it looking its best.
