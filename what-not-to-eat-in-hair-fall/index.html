What Not To Eat In Hair Fall A Complete Guide

Written by Bardi Apr 02, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Are you experiencing hair fall and wondering what could be the cause? While there could be many factors, your diet plays a significant role in maintaining healthy hair. In this article, we will discuss what foods to avoid to prevent hair fall.

Table of Contents:

  1. Processed Foods
  2. Sugar
  3. Alcohol
  4. Caffeine
  5. Dairy Products
  6. Fried Foods
  7. Refined Grains
  8. Salty Foods
  9. Spicy Foods
  10. High Protein Diets

Processed Foods

Processed foods are high in preservatives, artificial flavors, and chemicals that can damage your hair. These foods also lack essential nutrients that are required for healthy hair growth. Avoid packaged and processed foods like chips, cookies, and instant noodles.

Sugar

Consuming excessive sugar can lead to insulin resistance, which can cause hair fall. High sugar intake also leads to inflammation, which can damage hair follicles. Avoid sugary drinks like soda and packaged fruit juices, as well as candies and desserts.

Alcohol

Drinking alcohol in excess can lead to dehydration, which can cause hair fall. Alcohol also reduces the absorption of essential vitamins and minerals required for healthy hair growth. Keep your alcohol consumption in check and drink plenty of water to keep your hair hydrated.

Caffeine

Excessive caffeine intake can lead to dehydration, which can cause hair fall. Caffeine also reduces the absorption of essential vitamins and minerals required for healthy hair growth. Limit your caffeine intake by avoiding coffee, tea, and energy drinks.

Dairy Products

Dairy products like milk, cheese, and butter are high in saturated fats, which can cause hair fall. These products also contain hormones that can disrupt the natural balance of your body, leading to hair fall. Choose plant-based alternatives like almond milk and tofu instead.

Fried Foods

Fried foods like french fries and fried chicken are high in unhealthy fats that can clog your hair follicles, leading to hair fall. These foods also lack essential nutrients that are required for healthy hair growth. Choose grilled or baked alternatives instead.

Refined Grains

Refined grains like white bread and pasta lack essential nutrients that are required for healthy hair growth. These foods also have a high glycemic index, which can cause insulin resistance and hair fall. Choose whole grains like brown rice and quinoa instead.

Salty Foods

Consuming excessive salt can lead to dehydration, which can cause hair fall. Salt also disrupts the natural balance of your body, leading to hair fall. Avoid salty snacks like chips and salted nuts, as well as processed foods that are high in sodium.

Spicy Foods

Spicy foods like hot peppers and chili powder can cause inflammation, which can damage hair follicles and lead to hair fall. These foods also increase blood circulation, which can lead to excess oil production in your scalp and cause dandruff. Avoid spicy foods or consume them in moderation.

High Protein Diets

Consuming excessive protein can lead to an imbalance of amino acids, which can cause hair fall. High protein diets also lack essential nutrients like vitamins and minerals that are required for healthy hair growth. Balance your protein intake by including plant-based proteins like legumes and nuts in your diet.

Final Thoughts

Avoiding these foods will not only prevent hair fall but also promote overall health. A healthy diet rich in essential nutrients like vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants is crucial for healthy hair growth. Along with a healthy diet, make sure to take care of your hair by using gentle hair products and avoiding excessive heat styling.
