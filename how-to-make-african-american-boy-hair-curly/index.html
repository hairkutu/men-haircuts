How To Make African American Boy Hair Curly

Written by Christine Apr 11, 2023 · 7 min read

Are you tired of your son's straight hair? Do you want to add some curls to his style? In this article, we will guide you on how to make African American boy hair curly without damaging his hair. We will also provide tips on how to maintain his new curly hairstyle. Let's get started!

How to

Making African American boy hair curly is not an easy task, but it is achievable with the right products and techniques. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Wash His Hair

Begin by washing your son's hair with a sulfate-free shampoo. Sulfates can strip the hair of its natural oils, leaving it dry and brittle. Use warm water to open up the hair cuticles and allow the shampoo to penetrate the hair shaft. Rinse thoroughly with cool water to seal the cuticles.

Step 2: Apply a Leave-In Conditioner

Apply a leave-in conditioner to your son's damp hair. This will help to detangle his hair and prepare it for the next step. Use a wide-tooth comb to distribute the conditioner evenly through his hair.

Step 3: Apply a Curl Enhancing Product

Apply a curl enhancing product to his hair, such as a curl cream, mousse, or gel. This will define his curls and add some bounce to his hair. Apply the product evenly throughout his hair, starting from the roots to the tips.

Step 4: Use a Diffuser

Use a diffuser attachment on your hair dryer to dry your son's hair. A diffuser will help to distribute the heat evenly and prevent frizz. Start with low heat and speed settings, and gradually increase them as needed.

Step 5: Style His Hair

Once his hair is dry, use your fingers to separate his curls and style his hair as desired. You can use a hair pick to add more volume to his hair.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind when making African American boy hair curly:

  • Use a deep conditioner once a week to keep his hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage his hair.
  • Protect his hair at night by using a satin or silk pillowcase or a hair bonnet.
  • Avoid using too much product, as it can weigh down his curls and make them look greasy.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about making African American boy hair curly:

Can I use a perm to make my son's hair curly?

While a perm can make hair curly, it can also damage the hair and cause breakage. We recommend using natural methods, such as the ones outlined in this article, to make his hair curly.

How often should I wash my son's hair?

African American hair is prone to dryness, so it's best to wash it once a week or every two weeks, depending on his hair type and lifestyle.

Can I use oils on my son's hair?

Yes, you can use oils on his hair, such as coconut oil or jojoba oil, to keep his hair moisturized and shiny. However, be mindful of how much oil you use, as too much can weigh down his curls.

Hair Care

Maintaining your son's new curly hairstyle requires proper hair care. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness.
  • Apply a leave-in conditioner to his hair after every wash.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle his hair, starting from the tips and working your way up.
  • Trim his hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, and use a heat protectant spray before using them.

Congratulations, you now know how to make African American boy hair curly! With the right products and techniques, you can add some curls to your son's hair and give him a new look. Remember to take care of his hair to keep it healthy and strong.
