How Do You Know If Curtain Bangs Will Look Good On You

Written by Aprilia Apr 02, 2023 Â· 11 min read

Are you thinking of getting curtain bangs but not sure if they will suit your face shape and hair type? Curtain bangs, also known as Bardot bangs or fringe, are a popular trend in the hairstyling world. They are versatile, low-maintenance, and can flatter most face shapes. However, not everyone can pull off this style. In this article, we will guide you on how to know if curtain bangs will look good on you.

Face Shape

The first thing to consider is your face shape. Curtain bangs can suit most face shapes, but some styles work better for certain shapes. For example, if you have a round face, you may want to opt for longer and more textured bangs to elongate your face. If you have a heart-shaped face, you can go for shorter and wispy bangs to balance your features. If you have a square face, you can try side-swept bangs to soften your jawline.

Hair Type

The second thing to consider is your hair type. Curtain bangs work best on hair that is straight or slightly wavy. If you have curly or kinky hair, you may struggle to style your bangs and keep them in place. You may also need to trim your bangs more frequently to maintain their shape. If you have fine hair, you can add volume to your bangs by blow-drying them with a round brush. If you have thick hair, you can thin out your bangs with a razor or scissors.

Forehead Size

The third thing to consider is your forehead size. Curtain bangs can either highlight or hide your forehead, depending on their length and thickness. If you have a small forehead, you can go for shorter and thinner bangs to create the illusion of more space. If you have a large forehead, you can go for longer and thicker bangs to cover it up. However, be careful not to go too thick or heavy, as it can weigh down your face and make it look boxy.

Personal Style

The fourth thing to consider is your personal style. Curtain bangs can be styled in various ways, from sleek and polished to messy and bohemian. You should choose a style that complements your personality, wardrobe, and lifestyle. If you prefer a minimalistic and elegant look, you can go for a straight and blunt cut. If you prefer a casual and carefree look, you can go for a tousled and layered cut. If you prefer a retro and glamorous look, you can go for a side-swept and voluminous cut.

Professional Advice

The fifth thing to consider is professional advice. If you are still unsure whether curtain bangs will suit you, you can consult a hairstylist or a beauty expert. They can assess your hair texture, face shape, and personal style and recommend the best bangs for you. They can also show you different styling techniques and products to maintain your bangs. Be open to their suggestions and ask questions if you have any doubts.

How to Get Curtain Bangs

Assuming you have decided to get curtain bangs, the next step is to find a reputable salon or barber shop that specializes in this style. You can ask for recommendations from friends, family, or online forums. You can also check the salon's portfolio and reviews to see if they have experience with curtain bangs. Once you have found a suitable salon, you can book an appointment and prepare for the cut.

Step-by-Step Guide

The hairstylist will start by sectioning your hair and separating the bangs from the rest of the hair. They will then comb the bangs downward and trim them to the desired length. They may use scissors, a razor, or a combination of both, depending on the texture and thickness of your hair. They may also add layers or texture to the bangs to create movement and volume. Once the bangs are cut, they will style them according to your preference and hair type. They may use a blow dryer, a round brush, a straightener, or a curling iron to achieve the desired look. They may also recommend products such as hairspray, mousse, or serum to maintain your bangs.

Tips for Curtain Bangs

Here are some tips to keep in mind when styling and maintaining your curtain bangs:

  • Use a heat protectant spray before using hot tools to prevent damage to your hair.
  • Blow-dry your bangs with a round brush to create volume and shape.
  • Curl your bangs outward or inward to add dimension and movement.
  • Trim your bangs regularly to avoid split ends and maintain their shape.
  • Use dry shampoo to refresh your bangs between washes and absorb excess oil.
  • Experiment with different parting styles and placement of your bangs to find the most flattering look.

Curtain Bangs FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about curtain bangs:

  • Q: Are curtain bangs high maintenance?
  • A: Not necessarily. Curtain bangs can be low maintenance if you choose the right length and style for your hair type and face shape. They may require more frequent trims and styling if you have thick or curly hair.
  • Q: Can I still wear my hair up with curtain bangs?
  • A: Yes, you can. Curtain bangs can be versatile and blend in with different hairstyles, such as ponytails, buns, and braids.
  • Q: How do I know if I need to trim my curtain bangs?
  • A: If your bangs start to cover your eyes, look uneven, or lose their shape, it's time for a trim. You can either do it yourself with scissors or visit a hairstylist for a professional cut.

Hair Care for Curtain Bangs

Here are some hair care tips specifically for curtain bangs:

  • Wash your bangs separately from the rest of your hair to avoid tangling and frizz.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and damage.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products such as gel or wax, as they can weigh down your hair and make it greasy.
  • Protect your bangs from humidity and rain by using a hair spray or a scarf.
  • Consider investing in a silk pillowcase to reduce friction and prevent breakage.

By following these guidelines, you can determine whether curtain bangs will look good on you and how to maintain them. Remember to choose a style that suits your face shape, hair type, personal style, and professional advice. With the right cut and care, you can rock this trendy and chic hairstyle.
