How To Fix 360 Lace Frontal Wig

Are you struggling with how to fix your 360 lace frontal wig? Look no further! In this article, we will cover step-by-step instructions on how to fix your wig, tips to make the process easier, frequently asked questions, and hair care advice. Follow along to have your wig looking like new in no time!

Step-by-Step Instructions

Fixing your 360 lace frontal wig may seem daunting, but it's actually quite simple. Follow these steps to have your wig looking like new:

Step 1: Prepare Your Wig

Before you start fixing your wig, you need to prepare it. Detangle any knots or snarls in the hair using a wide-tooth comb. Gently wash the wig with lukewarm water and shampoo, then rinse thoroughly. Blot the wig gently with a towel to remove excess water.

Step 2: Adjust the Wig Cap

Adjust the wig cap to fit your head properly. Make sure it's snug but not too tight. Use the adjustable straps at the back of the wig to fit it securely on your head.

Step 3: Cut the Lace

Cut the lace around the perimeter of the wig. Be careful not to cut too close to the hairline, as this can cause the wig to shed. Leave a small amount of lace to blend with your skin.

Step 4: Apply Adhesive

Apply a thin layer of adhesive around the perimeter of your hairline. Use a brush or your fingers to spread it evenly. Wait a few seconds for the adhesive to become tacky.

Step 5: Place the Wig

Place the wig on your head, starting from the front and working your way back. Gently press the wig onto your scalp to secure it in place. Use a comb to blend the wig with your natural hair.

Step 6: Style Your Wig

Style your wig as desired. You can use heat tools, such as a curling iron or flat iron, to create curls or straighten the hair. Be sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage to the hair.

Tips for Fixing Your Wig

Here are some tips to make fixing your 360 lace frontal wig easier:

  • Use a wig stand or mannequin head to help you style your wig.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to protect your wig while you sleep.
  • Don't use too much adhesive, as this can cause the wig to slide or become uncomfortable.
  • Be gentle when detangling the hair, as excessive pulling can cause shedding or damage.
  • Experiment with different styles to find what works best for you.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some frequently asked questions about fixing a 360 lace frontal wig:

How often should I wash my wig?

It's recommended to wash your wig every 6-8 wears, or as needed if it becomes dirty or oily.

Can I use regular shampoo on my wig?

No, it's best to use a shampoo specifically formulated for wigs. Regular shampoo can be too harsh and cause damage to the hair.

How long will my wig last?

The lifespan of a wig depends on how often it's worn and how well it's cared for. With proper care, a wig can last up to a year or more.

Hair Care Advice

Proper hair care is essential for maintaining the quality and longevity of your wig. Here are some tips:

  • Use a wig brush or wide-tooth comb to detangle the hair.
  • Store your wig on a wig stand or mannequin head to maintain its shape.
  • Avoid using heat tools on high heat settings, as this can damage the hair.
  • Use a wig conditioner to keep the hair soft and manageable.
  • Avoid exposing your wig to sunlight or heat sources, as this can cause fading or damage.

Following these tips and steps will ensure that you can fix your 360 lace frontal wig with ease. With proper care, your wig will look beautiful and natural for a long time. Happy fixing!
