How To Refurbish An Old Wig Tips And Tricks

Written by Frank Jun 05, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Welcome to our guide on how to refurbish an old wig! If you've been looking for ways to breathe new life into your favorite wig, you've come to the right place. In this article, we'll be sharing some simple tips and tricks that you can use to give your old wig a makeover. From cleaning and detangling to restyling and cutting, we've got you covered. So, let's get started!

How to Refurbish an Old Wig

If you're wondering how to refurbish an old wig, the first thing you need to do is assess its condition. Is it tangled? Does it have frizz or flyaways? Has it lost its shape? Once you've identified the issues, you can start to tackle them one by one.

Step 1: Cleaning

The first step in refurbishing an old wig is to clean it. Use a wig brush or comb to gently remove any tangles, then fill a sink or basin with cool water and add a small amount of wig shampoo. Swish the wig around in the water, then rinse it thoroughly. Don't rub or twist the wig, as this can cause damage. Instead, gently squeeze out any excess water and pat the wig dry with a towel.

Step 2: Detangling

Once your wig is clean, the next step is to detangle it. Use a wide-toothed comb or wig brush to gently work through any knots or tangles. Start at the ends of the hair and work your way up to the roots, taking care not to pull too hard or use too much force. If you encounter a particularly stubborn knot, try using a detangling spray or conditioner to help loosen it.

Step 3: Restyling

If your wig has lost its shape or style, you can easily restyle it using a wig stand, wig clips, and a blow dryer. Start by placing the wig on the stand and securing it with clips. Then, use a blow dryer on a low heat setting to smooth out the hair and create your desired style. You can also use a curling iron or flat iron to add curls or waves.

Step 4: Cutting

If your wig is too long or has uneven layers, you may want to consider cutting it. However, if you're not confident in your cutting skills, it's best to have a professional stylist do it for you. They can help you achieve the perfect length and shape for your face and style.

Tips for Maintaining Your Refurbished Wig

Now that you've refurbished your old wig, it's important to take good care of it to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Store your wig on a wig stand or in a wig box to help maintain its shape.
  • Avoid exposing your wig to heat sources such as ovens or fireplaces.
  • Don't wear your wig while sleeping, as this can cause tangles and damage.
  • Use only wig-specific products when cleaning and styling your wig.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about refurbishing old wigs:

Can I dye my wig?

In most cases, it's not recommended to dye a wig, as the color may not take evenly and could damage the hair fibers. If you're looking for a new color, it's best to purchase a wig in that shade.

How often should I clean my wig?

It's recommended to clean your wig after 8-10 wears, or whenever it starts to look dirty or oily.

Can I use regular hair products on my wig?

No, it's best to use only wig-specific products on your wig, as regular hair products can be too harsh and damaging.

Hair Care

Remember, taking good care of your wig is essential to keeping it looking great. Be sure to follow the tips and tricks we've shared in this article, and don't hesitate to seek the advice of a professional stylist if you need help. With a little TLC, your old wig can look as good as new!
