How to Keep Hair from Falling Out after Bleaching - Hair Care Tips

If you've recently bleached your hair, you may be wondering how to prevent hair from falling out. Bleaching can damage hair, making it more prone to breakage and hair loss. However, there are steps you can take to keep your hair healthy and prevent hair loss after bleaching.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Keep Hair from Falling Out after Bleaching
  2. Step by Step Guide
  3. Tips to Keep Hair Healthy after Bleaching
  4. FAQs about Hair Loss after Bleaching
  5. Hair Care Routine for Bleached Hair

Step by Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to keep hair from falling out after bleaching:

1. Deep Condition your Hair Regularly

Bleaching strips the hair of its natural oils, making it dry and brittle. To keep your hair healthy, deep condition your hair at least once a week. Use a hair mask or hot oil treatment to nourish your hair and restore its moisture.

2. Avoid using Heat Styling Tools

Heat styling tools like blow dryers, flat irons, and curling irons can further damage bleached hair. If possible, air-dry your hair and avoid using heat styling tools. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray to minimize damage.

3. Use a Wide-tooth Comb

Bleached hair is fragile and prone to breakage. To prevent hair loss, use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair. Start from the ends and gently work your way up to the roots.

4. Trim your Hair Regularly

Bleached hair is more prone to split ends and breakage. To keep your hair healthy, trim your hair regularly. Aim to trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends from traveling up the hair shaft.

Tips to Keep Hair Healthy after Bleaching

Here are some additional tips to keep your hair healthy after bleaching:

1. Avoid Over-washing your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, making it dry and brittle. Aim to wash your hair every 2-3 days and use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner.

2. Protect your Hair from the Sun

The sun's UV rays can damage bleached hair, making it more prone to breakage and hair loss. Wear a hat or use a hair sunscreen to protect your hair from the sun.

3. Eat a Healthy Diet

A healthy diet rich in vitamins and nutrients can improve hair health and prevent hair loss. Eat foods high in protein, iron, and vitamin C to nourish your hair from the inside out.

FAQs about Hair Loss after Bleaching

1. Is it normal for hair to fall out after bleaching?

Yes, it is normal for hair to fall out after bleaching due to the damage caused to the hair shaft. However, proper hair care can minimize hair loss.

2. How long does it take for hair to stop falling out after bleaching?

It can take several weeks or months for the hair to stop falling out after bleaching. It is important to take proper care of your hair to prevent further hair loss.

3. Can hair grow back after falling out from bleaching?

Yes, hair can grow back after falling out from bleaching. However, it may take several months for the hair to grow back fully. Proper hair care can promote hair growth.

Hair Care Routine for Bleached Hair

Here is a hair care routine for bleached hair:

1. Deep Condition your Hair once a week

Use a hair mask or hot oil treatment to restore moisture to your hair.

2. Use a Sulfate-free Shampoo and Conditioner

Sulfates can strip the hair of its natural oils, making it dry and brittle. Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy.

3. Avoid Heat Styling Tools

Avoid using blow dryers, flat irons, and curling irons to minimize damage to your hair.

4. Protect your Hair from the Sun

Wear a hat or use a hair sunscreen to protect your hair from the sun's UV rays.

5. Trim your Hair Regularly

Trim your hair every 6-8 weeks to prevent split ends from traveling up the hair shaft.

By following these tips and taking proper care of your hair, you can prevent hair loss after bleaching and keep your hair healthy and beautiful.
