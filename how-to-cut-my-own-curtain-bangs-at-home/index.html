How To Cut My Own Curtain Bangs At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi May 05, 2023 · 7 min read
If you're looking to mix up your hairstyle and want to try a new look, cutting your own curtain bangs at home is a great option. Curtain bangs are a popular trend that can frame your face and add some dimension to your hair. It may seem daunting to cut your own bangs, but with the right tools and technique, it can be done easily. In this article, we will walk you through the steps of cutting your own curtain bangs at home.

Description

Curtain bangs are a style of bangs that are parted in the middle, framing your face like curtains. They can be a great way to add some interest to a simple hairstyle or to switch up your look without making a drastic change. Cutting your own curtain bangs at home can save you time and money, and it can be a fun project to try out.

How to Cut My Own Curtain Bangs at Home

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting your bangs, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You will need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section off your hair. It's also a good idea to have a spray bottle of water to keep your hair damp as you cut.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Start by sectioning off your hair. Use the comb to create a middle part down the center of your head, separating your hair into two sections. Clip the rest of your hair back so it's out of the way.

Step 3: Measure and Cut

Next, take a small section of hair from the front of one side of your head. Hold the hair between your fingers and measure how long you want your bangs to be. It's best to start longer and gradually cut more if needed.

Step 4: Cut at an Angle

Using the hair cutting scissors, cut the hair at an angle. Start at the middle of the section and cut downwards towards the ends. This will create a soft, feathered effect.

Step 5: Repeat on the Other Side

Repeat steps 3 and 4 on the other side of your head, making sure the bangs are even in length and thickness.

Step 6: Check Your Work

Once you've cut both sides, check your work in the mirror. If you need to make any adjustments, do so carefully and slowly.

Step 7: Style Your Bangs

Now that your bangs are cut, style them to your liking. You can blow dry them straight or use a curling iron to add some waves.

Tips

- Start with longer bangs and gradually cut more if needed - Cut at an angle for a soft, feathered effect - Take your time and make small adjustments as needed - Don't cut too much at once – you can always cut more later

FAQ

Can I cut my own curtain bangs if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can! Just make sure to cut your bangs while your hair is dry and in its natural state, so you can see how they will fall.

What if I mess up my bangs?

If you make a mistake while cutting your bangs, don't panic. You can always take them to a stylist to fix or adjust them.

Hair Care

To keep your curtain bangs looking their best, make sure to trim them every few weeks to maintain their shape. You can also use a small amount of hair oil or serum to keep them smooth and shiny. And don't forget to protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray when styling with hot tools. Cutting your own curtain bangs at home can be a fun and rewarding experience. Just remember to take your time, use the right tools, and make small adjustments as needed. With a little practice, you'll have perfect curtain bangs in no time!
