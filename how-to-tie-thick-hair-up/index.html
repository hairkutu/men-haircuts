How To Tie Thick Hair Up A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Jul 21, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Thick hair can be a blessing and a curse. On one hand, you have a lot of hair to work with and can create many different styles. On the other hand, it can be challenging to tie thick hair up, especially if you want it to stay in place all day. In this article, we will show you how to tie thick hair up in a way that is both stylish and practical.

Table of Contents:

  • How to Tie Thick Hair Up
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips for Tying Thick Hair Up
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Thick Hair

How to Tie Thick Hair Up

Before we dive into the step-by-step guide, let's go over some basics. First, you'll need to gather a few supplies:

  • Hair ties
  • Bobby pins
  • Hair spray

Next, decide on the style you want. There are many ways to tie thick hair up, including:

  • Ponytail
  • Bun
  • Braids
  • Half-up, half-down

Once you've decided on a style, follow the step-by-step guide below.

Step-by-Step Guide

1. Brush your hair

Start by brushing your hair to remove any tangles. This will make it easier to work with.

2. Section your hair

If you're doing a ponytail or bun, gather all of your hair at the crown of your head and secure it with a hair tie. If you're doing a half-up, half-down style, section off the top half of your hair and secure it with a hair tie.

3. Create your style

Now it's time to create your style. If you're doing a ponytail or bun, wrap your hair around the hair tie and secure it with bobby pins. If you're doing braids, follow the same steps as you normally would. If you're doing a half-up, half-down style, leave the top half of your hair loose and style it as desired.

4. Secure with hair spray

To ensure your style stays in place all day, spritz it with a bit of hair spray. This will also help tame any flyaways.

Tips for Tying Thick Hair Up

Here are a few tips to keep in mind when tying thick hair up:

  • Use a thick hair tie or two to ensure your hair stays in place
  • If you have layers, use bobby pins to secure any loose pieces
  • Don't tie your hair up too tightly, as this can cause breakage
  • Experiment with different styles to find what works best for you

FAQs

Can I tie my thick hair up every day?

Yes, you can tie your hair up every day, but it's important to give your hair a break every once in a while. Try wearing your hair down one day a week to prevent breakage.

What's the best way to prevent breakage when tying thick hair up?

Use a thick hair tie or two to prevent your hair from pulling and breaking. Additionally, don't tie your hair up too tightly, as this can also cause breakage.

Hair Care for Thick Hair

Thick hair requires a bit of extra care to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are a few tips:

  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends

By following these tips and using the step-by-step guide above, you can tie your thick hair up in a way that is both stylish and practical. Happy styling!
