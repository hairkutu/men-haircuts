How To Get Rid Of Brassy Tones In Blonde Hair

Written by Frank Aug 10, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Blonde hair can be a beautiful and striking look, but it can also be prone to brassy tones. If you've ever noticed your blonde hair looking more yellow or orange than you'd like, you're not alone. The good news is that there are plenty of ways to get rid of those brassy tones and keep your blonde looking fresh and vibrant. Here's how.

Table of Contents

  1. How to Get Rid of Brassy Tones in Blonde Hair
  2. Step-by-Step Guide
  3. Tips for Maintaining Blonde Hair
  4. FAQs
  5. Hair Care Products for Blonde Hair

Step-by-Step Guide

There are several ways to get rid of brassy tones in blonde hair, so you can choose the method that works best for you:

1. Use a purple shampoo

Purple shampoo is a popular choice for toning down brassy tones in blonde hair. The purple pigment in the shampoo neutralizes the yellow and orange tones, leaving your hair looking cooler and brighter. Use it once or twice a week to maintain your blonde color.

2. Apply a toner

A toner is a hair color product that helps to neutralize unwanted tones. You can apply a toner in a salon or at home, depending on your preference. Toners can be clear or colored, and they work by depositing pigments onto your hair to counteract the brassy tones.

3. Try a hair mask

A hair mask can be a great way to nourish and tone your blonde hair at the same time. Look for a mask that contains purple pigments or other toning ingredients, and leave it on your hair for the recommended time. Rinse it out and enjoy your refreshed blonde locks.

4. Get a professional color correction

If your brassy tones are particularly stubborn, you may need to visit a salon for a professional color correction. A stylist can help to remove the unwanted tones and restore your blonde hair to its former glory.

Tips for Maintaining Blonde Hair

Once you've gotten rid of the brassy tones in your blonde hair, here are some tips for keeping it looking great:

1. Use a sulfate-free shampoo

Sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause it to dry out, which can lead to damage and breakage. Look for a sulfate-free shampoo that is gentle on your hair and scalp.

2. Protect your hair from heat

Blow-drying, straightening, and curling your hair can all cause damage, especially if you do it frequently. Use a heat protectant spray or serum to shield your hair from the heat and prevent breakage.

3. Avoid over-washing your hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils and cause it to dry out. Try to wash your hair every two to three days, and use dry shampoo in between washes to keep your hair looking fresh.

4. Schedule regular trims

Regular trims can help to prevent split ends and breakage, which can make your blonde hair look dull and lifeless. Aim to get a trim every six to eight weeks to keep your hair looking healthy and vibrant.

FAQs

Q: Can I use regular shampoo on blonde hair?

A: Yes, you can use regular shampoo on blonde hair, but be sure to choose a gentle formula that won't strip your hair of its natural oils. Look for shampoos that are specially formulated for blonde hair, or that contain ingredients like chamomile or honey to enhance your color.

Q: How often should I use purple shampoo?

A: It's best to use purple shampoo once or twice a week, depending on your hair type and how often you wash your hair. If you use it too often, you may end up with a blue or purple tint to your hair.

Q: Can I tone my hair at home?

A: Yes, you can tone your hair at home using a toner kit from your local beauty supply store. However, if you're not experienced with hair color, it's a good idea to visit a salon for a professional toning.

Hair Care Products for Blonde Hair

Here are some hair care products that can help you maintain your blonde hair:

1. Purple Shampoo

Purple shampoo is a must-have for anyone with blonde hair. Look for a formula that is gentle on your hair and scalp, and that contains plenty of purple pigments to neutralize the brassy tones.

2. Hair Mask

A hair mask can be a great way to nourish and tone your blonde hair at the same time. Look for a formula that contains purple pigments or other toning ingredients, and leave it on your hair for the recommended time.

3. Heat Protectant Spray

If you use heat tools on your hair, a heat protectant spray or serum can help to prevent damage and breakage. Look for a formula that is lightweight and won't weigh your hair down.

With these tips and products, you can keep your blonde hair looking fresh, vibrant, and brassy-tone free. Happy styling!
