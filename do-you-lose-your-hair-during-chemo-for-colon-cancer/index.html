Do You Lose Your Hair During Chemo For Colon Cancer

Written by Bardi Jun 11, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you or someone you know has been diagnosed with colon cancer, you may be wondering what to expect during treatment. One common question asked by many patients is, "Do you lose your hair during chemo for colon cancer?" The answer is not a simple yes or no, as it can vary depending on the type of chemotherapy being administered. In this article, we will explore the different types of chemotherapy for colon cancer and their potential effects on hair loss, as well as provide tips for hair care during treatment.

Types of Chemotherapy for Colon Cancer

There are several types of chemotherapy drugs used to treat colon cancer, including:

  • 5-fluorouracil (5-FU)
  • CapeOX (capecitabine and oxaliplatin)
  • FOLFIRI (folinic acid, 5-FU, and irinotecan)
  • FOLFOX (folinic acid, 5-FU, and oxaliplatin)

Some of these drugs are more likely to cause hair loss than others. For example, 5-FU and FOLFIRI are known to cause hair loss in some patients, while CapeOX and FOLFOX are less likely to have this side effect.

How Hair Loss Occurs During Chemotherapy

Chemotherapy drugs work by targeting rapidly dividing cells in the body, including cancer cells. Unfortunately, they can also damage healthy cells, such as hair follicles, which can lead to hair loss. Hair loss during chemotherapy typically occurs two to three weeks after the first treatment, and can continue throughout the course of treatment.

Tips for Hair Care During Chemotherapy

If you are undergoing chemotherapy for colon cancer and are experiencing hair loss, there are several things you can do to take care of your hair:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using hot tools such as blow dryers, curling irons, and straighteners
  • Avoid tight hairstyles such as ponytails and braids
  • Wear a soft cap or scarf to protect your scalp from the sun and cold weather

It is important to note that hair loss during chemotherapy is temporary, and most patients will see regrowth within a few months after treatment ends.

FAQs

Q: Will all chemotherapy drugs cause hair loss?

A: No, not all chemotherapy drugs cause hair loss. Some drugs are more likely to cause hair loss than others.

Q: How long does hair loss last during chemotherapy?

A: Hair loss during chemotherapy is temporary, and most patients will see regrowth within a few months after treatment ends.

Q: Is there anything I can do to prevent hair loss during chemotherapy?

A: Unfortunately, there is no way to completely prevent hair loss during chemotherapy. However, maintaining good hair care habits can help minimize hair loss and promote regrowth.

In Conclusion

While hair loss can be a difficult side effect of chemotherapy for colon cancer, it is important to remember that it is temporary and that regrowth will occur. By taking care of your hair during treatment and practicing good hair care habits, you can help minimize hair loss and promote healthy regrowth.
