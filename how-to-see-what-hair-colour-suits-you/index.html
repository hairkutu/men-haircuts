How To See What Hair Colour Suits You In 2023

Written by Bardi Apr 22, 2023 · 8 min read

Choosing the right hair colour can transform your look and give you a confidence boost. However, with so many options available, it can be overwhelming to decide which colour suits you best. Here’s a step-by-step guide to finding the perfect hair colour for you.

Table of Contents

  • Step 1: Determine Your Skin Tone
  • Step 2: Consider Your Eye Color
  • Step 3: Think About Your Natural Hair Colour
  • Step 4: Try Virtual Hair Colour Tools
  • Step 5: Seek Professional Advice
  • Tips for Choosing the Right Hair Colour
  • FAQs About Choosing the Right Hair Colour
  • Hair Care Tips for Coloured Hair

Step 1: Determine Your Skin Tone

Knowing your skin tone is key in finding the right hair colour. The easiest way to determine your skin tone is by looking at the veins on the inside of your wrist. If they appear blue, you have a cool skin tone. If they appear green, you have a warm skin tone. Those with neutral skin tones have veins that appear blue-green.

For cool skin tones, hair colours like platinum blonde, ash blonde, and cool brown shades like espresso and chestnut work well. Those with warm skin tones can try hair colours like golden blonde, copper, and warm brown shades like caramel and auburn. Neutral skin tones can experiment with both cool and warm tones.

Step 2: Consider Your Eye Color

Another factor to consider is your eye color. Certain hair colours can make your eyes pop and enhance their natural beauty. For example, those with blue eyes can try warm hair colours like honey blonde and light brown shades. Those with green eyes can experiment with red and copper tones. Those with brown eyes can opt for deep shades like chocolate brown and burgundy.

Step 3: Think About Your Natural Hair Colour

Your natural hair colour can also influence which hair colours suit you best. If you have dark hair, going blonde may require multiple sessions and can damage your hair. Similarly, if you have lighter hair, going darker can be a drastic change. It’s important to consider how much maintenance and upkeep you’re willing to commit to.

Step 4: Try Virtual Hair Colour Tools

If you’re still unsure which hair colour to choose, try using virtual hair colour tools. These tools allow you to upload a photo of yourself and try on different hair colours to see what suits you best. You can also experiment with different shades and highlights.

Step 5: Seek Professional Advice

Consulting with a professional hair stylist can also help you find the perfect hair colour. They can assess your skin tone, eye colour, and natural hair colour to recommend the best shades for you. They can also offer advice on how to maintain and care for your coloured hair.

Tips for Choosing the Right Hair Colour

  • Consider your lifestyle and how much maintenance you’re willing to commit to.
  • Take inspiration from celebrities and magazines, but remember to choose a hair colour that suits you.
  • Invest in quality hair care products specifically designed for coloured hair.
  • Try temporary hair colour options like hair chalk or temporary hair dye before committing to a permanent change.

FAQs About Choosing the Right Hair Colour

How often do I need to touch up my roots?

It depends on your hair growth rate and the contrast between your natural hair colour and the dyed hair colour. Generally, root touch-ups are needed every 4-6 weeks.

Will bleaching my hair damage it?

Bleaching can damage your hair if not done properly. It’s important to seek professional advice and use quality hair care products to maintain the health of your hair.

Can I dye my hair at home?

Yes, you can dye your hair at home. However, it’s important to follow the instructions carefully and choose a hair dye that suits your hair type and natural colour.

Hair Care Tips for Coloured Hair

  • Use a colour-safe shampoo and conditioner to prevent fading.
  • Avoid using hot tools like flat irons and curling wands too frequently, as they can damage coloured hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protectant hair product.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and maintain the health of your hair.

Choosing the right hair colour can be a fun and exciting process. By considering your skin tone, eye colour, and natural hair colour, as well as trying virtual hair colour tools and seeking professional advice, you can find the perfect shade for you. Remember to also take good care of your coloured hair to keep it looking vibrant and healthy.
