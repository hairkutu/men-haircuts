Best Straightening Iron For Thick Curly Hair In 2023 A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Christine Mar 22, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of dealing with frizzy, unmanageable hair? Look no further than a straightening iron to tame those wild curls. But, with so many options on the market, how do you know which one is right for you? In this guide, we'll take a closer look at the best straightening iron for thick curly hair, including how to use it, tips for achieving the perfect look, and more. Let's get started!

Table of Contents:

  1. Description
  2. How to Use a Straightening Iron
  3. Step-by-Step Guide
  4. Tips for Achieving the Perfect Look
  5. FAQs
  6. Hair Care for Thick Curly Hair

Description

When it comes to straightening thick curly hair, you want a straightening iron that can handle the job. Look for an iron with ceramic or titanium plates to evenly distribute heat and protect your hair from damage. You'll also want an iron with adjustable temperature settings, as high heat can cause even more frizz and damage to your hair. Finally, consider the size of the plates - wider plates are better for thick hair, while narrower plates are better for shorter or finer hair.

How to Use a Straightening Iron

Before using a straightening iron, it's important to start with clean, dry hair. Apply a heat protectant spray to your hair to prevent damage, and use a comb to detangle any knots. Then, divide your hair into sections and clip them up, starting with the bottom layer. Take small sections of hair and run the straightening iron over them from root to tip, making sure to keep the iron moving and not leaving it in one spot for too long. Repeat this process until you've straightened all of the sections.

Step-by-Step Guide:

Step 1: Wash Your Hair

Start by washing your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner. Use a towel to dry your hair until it's about 80% dry.

Step 2: Apply Heat Protectant Spray

Before using a straightening iron, apply a heat protectant spray to your hair to prevent damage. Make sure to apply it evenly and comb it through your hair.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Divide your hair into sections and clip them up, starting with the bottom layer. Use a comb to detangle any knots.

Step 4: Straighten Your Hair

Take small sections of hair and run the straightening iron over them from root to tip, making sure to keep the iron moving and not leaving it in one spot for too long. Repeat this process until you've straightened all of the sections.

Tips for Achieving the Perfect Look

  • Use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage
  • Divide your hair into small sections for easier straightening
  • Use a comb to detangle any knots before straightening
  • Keep the straightening iron moving and don't leave it in one spot for too long
  • Use a finishing serum or oil to add shine and reduce frizz

FAQs

Q: Can I straighten my hair every day?

A: It's best to limit heat styling to once or twice a week to prevent damage to your hair.

Q: Can I use a straightening iron on wet hair?

A: No, always make sure your hair is dry before using a straightening iron to prevent damage.

Hair Care for Thick Curly Hair

When it comes to thick curly hair, it's important to use products specifically designed for your hair type. Look for shampoos and conditioners that are moisturizing and sulfate-free, as sulfates can strip your hair of its natural oils. Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy. Finally, consider using a leave-in conditioner or curl cream to define your curls and reduce frizz.

With the right straightening iron and hair care routine, you can achieve smooth, sleek hair that lasts all day. Remember to always use a heat protectant spray and limit heat styling to prevent damage to your hair. Happy straightening!
