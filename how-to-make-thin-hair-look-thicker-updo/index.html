How To Make Thin Hair Look Thicker Updo Tips And Tricks

Written by Frank Sep 09, 2023 Â· 10 min read

Are you tired of trying to style your thin hair only to end up with a lackluster look? Do you want to make your hair look thicker for an upcoming event or just for everyday wear? Creating an updo is a great way to make your hair look thicker, and we have some tips and tricks to help you achieve the perfect updo for your hair type.

Description

Thin hair can be challenging to style, and it's frustrating when you don't get the results you want. Updos are a great way to add volume and thickness to your hair, but it's important to know the right techniques to make it work for you. This article will provide you with tips and tricks on how to create the perfect updo for thin hair.

How to Make Thin Hair Look Thicker Updo

If you want to make your thin hair look thicker in an updo, here are some tips to get you started:

1. Use the right products

Choose products that are specifically designed for thin hair, such as volumizing shampoo and conditioner. These products will help to add volume to your hair, making it easier to style. You can also use a volumizing mousse or spray to give your hair an extra boost.

2. Tease your hair

Teasing your hair is a great way to add volume and thickness to your hair. Use a teasing comb to gently backcomb your hair at the roots. Be careful not to overdo it, as this can damage your hair.

3. Choose the right updo

Not all updos are created equal when it comes to thin hair. Choose an updo that will add volume to your hair, such as a messy bun or a braided updo. Avoid tight, sleek updos that will only make your hair look thinner.

4. Use hair extensions

If you want to add extra thickness to your hair, consider using hair extensions. Clip-in extensions are easy to use and can be added to your hair in minutes. Just make sure to choose extensions that match your hair color and texture.

5. Add texture

Adding texture to your hair can make it look thicker and more voluminous. You can achieve this by curling your hair with a curling iron or adding waves with a flat iron. Alternatively, you can use a texturizing spray to add some grit and texture to your hair.

Step by Step Guide

Now that you know the tips and tricks for making thin hair look thicker in an updo, here's a step-by-step guide:

Step 1: Wash and condition your hair with volumizing products.

Start by washing and conditioning your hair with products that are specifically designed for thin hair. This will help to add volume and thickness to your hair.

Step 2: Tease your hair at the roots.

Use a teasing comb to gently backcomb your hair at the roots. This will help to add volume and lift to your hair.

Step 3: Choose the right updo.

Choose an updo that will add volume to your hair, such as a messy bun or a braided updo. Avoid tight, sleek updos that will only make your hair look thinner.

Step 4: Add hair extensions (optional).

If you want to add extra thickness to your hair, consider using clip-in hair extensions. Make sure to choose extensions that match your hair color and texture.

Step 5: Curl or wave your hair (optional).

If you want to add texture to your hair, curl it with a curling iron or add waves with a flat iron. Alternatively, you can use a texturizing spray to add some grit and texture to your hair.

Step 6: Secure your updo.

Once you've created your updo, secure it in place with bobby pins or hair elastics. Use hairspray to keep your hair in place.

Tips for Making Thin Hair Look Thicker Updo

Here are some additional tips to help you make your thin hair look thicker in an updo:

1. Don't wash your hair every day.

Washing your hair every day can strip it of its natural oils, making it look thinner. Try to wash your hair every other day or use dry shampoo to extend the time between washes.

2. Use a heat protectant.

If you're using heat styling tools, make sure to use a heat protectant to prevent damage to your hair.

3. Avoid using heavy products.

Avoid using heavy products, such as oils or serums, as these can weigh your hair down and make it look thinner.

4. Get regular trims.

Getting regular trims can help to keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends, which can make your hair look thinner.

FAQ

Q: Can I create an updo with short thin hair?

A: Yes, you can create an updo with short thin hair. Try a messy bun or a half-up, half-down style.

Q: Will hair extensions damage my hair?

A: Hair extensions can damage your hair if they're not applied or removed properly. Make sure to follow the instructions carefully and don't leave them in for too long.

Q: Can I create an updo without teasing my hair?

A: Yes, you can create an updo without teasing your hair. You can use volumizing products and hair extensions to add volume and thickness to your hair.

Hair Care for Thin Hair

Here are some additional tips for taking care of thin hair:

1. Use a wide-tooth comb.

Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair instead of a brush, which can pull and damage your hair.

2. Be gentle with your hair.

Avoid pulling or tugging at your hair, especially when it's wet, as this can cause breakage.

3. Avoid using hot tools every day.

Using hot tools every day can damage your hair and make it look thinner. Try to limit your use of hot tools or use them on a lower heat setting.

Conclusion

Creating an updo is a great way to make your thin hair look thicker and more voluminous. By using the right products, teasing your hair, and choosing the right updo, you can achieve the perfect look for any occasion. Remember to take care of your hair by using gentle products and avoiding excessive heat styling.
