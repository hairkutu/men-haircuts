How To Keep From Losing Your Hair During Chemo

Written by Daniel Jun 02, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Dealing with cancer is never easy, and one of the most distressing side effects of chemotherapy can be hair loss. Losing your hair can be a difficult experience, but there are ways to help prevent it from happening. In this article, we'll go over tips and strategies for keeping your hair during chemo.

Table of Contents

  • How to Keep from Losing Your Hair During Chemo
  • Hair Care During Chemo
  • FAQ

How to Keep from Losing Your Hair During Chemo

The first step in keeping your hair during chemo is to talk to your doctor. They may be able to recommend medications or therapies that can help prevent hair loss. Here are some other strategies you can try:

Eat a Healthy Diet

Eating a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals can help keep your hair healthy during chemo. Focus on foods that are high in protein, iron, and zinc, such as lean meats, fish, and legumes. You may also want to consider taking a multivitamin or other supplements recommended by your doctor.

Get Enough Sleep

Getting enough sleep is essential for overall health, and it can also help keep your hair during chemo. Aim for at least 7-8 hours of sleep per night, and try to establish a regular bedtime routine to help you relax and fall asleep more easily.

Avoid Harsh Chemicals

Chemicals found in many hair care products can be damaging to your hair, especially during chemo. Avoid using products that contain harsh chemicals like sulfates and parabens, and opt for gentle, natural products instead. Be sure to check with your doctor before using any new products during treatment.

Use a Cold Cap

A cold cap is a device that can help prevent hair loss during chemo. It works by reducing blood flow to the scalp, which can help protect hair follicles from the effects of chemotherapy. Talk to your doctor to see if a cold cap is right for you.

Hair Care During Chemo

If you're undergoing chemotherapy, it's important to take extra care of your hair to help prevent damage and loss. Here are some tips for hair care during chemo:

Wash Your Hair Less Often

Washing your hair every day can be damaging to your hair, especially during chemo. Try washing your hair every other day, or even less often if possible. Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner, and avoid hot water, which can be drying to your hair and scalp.

Avoid Heat Styling

Using heat styling tools like curling irons and straighteners can be damaging to your hair, especially during chemo. Avoid using these tools as much as possible, and opt for gentle styling techniques like air-drying and braiding instead.

Protect Your Scalp

During chemo, your scalp may become more sensitive and prone to irritation. Protect your scalp from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf, and avoid using products that can be drying or irritating to your scalp.

FAQ

Can hair loss during chemo be prevented?

While hair loss is a common side effect of chemotherapy, there are strategies that can help prevent it. Talk to your doctor about medications, supplements, and other therapies that may be effective in preventing hair loss.

Is it safe to use hair dye during chemo?

It's generally not recommended to use hair dye during chemo, as the chemicals in hair dye can be damaging to your hair and scalp. Talk to your doctor before using any new hair care products during treatment.

Will my hair grow back after chemo?

Many people experience hair regrowth after chemotherapy, although the rate and pattern of regrowth can vary. It may take several months for your hair to grow back fully, and the texture and color of your hair may be different than before treatment.

What can I do to boost hair growth after chemo?

Eating a healthy diet, getting enough sleep, and practicing good hair care habits can all help promote hair growth after chemo. You may also want to talk to your doctor about supplements or other therapies that may be effective.

Remember, losing your hair during chemo can be a difficult experience, but there are ways to help prevent it from happening. Talk to your doctor and try these strategies to keep your hair healthy during treatment.
