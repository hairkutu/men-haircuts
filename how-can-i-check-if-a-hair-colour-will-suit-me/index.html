How Can I Check If A Hair Colour Will Suit Me Tips And Tricks

Written by Luffy Aug 01, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Changing your hair colour can be a big decision, and it's understandable to want to make sure that the new shade will flatter your skin tone and features. If you're wondering how can I check if a hair colour will suit me, there are several ways to find out before making the switch. In this article, we'll share some tips and tricks that will help you choose the perfect hair colour for you.

  • Understanding the Colour Wheel
  • Try Before You Buy

How to Determine Your Skin Tone

One of the most important factors in choosing a hair colour that suits you is your skin tone. There are three main types of skin tone: cool, warm, and neutral. To determine your skin tone, look at the veins on the inside of your wrist:

  • If your veins are blue or purple, you have a cool skin tone.
  • If your veins are green, you have a warm skin tone.
  • If your veins are a mix of blue and green, you have a neutral skin tone.

Another way to determine your skin tone is by looking at the undertones in your skin:

  • Cool undertones have a blue, pink, or red tint.
  • Warm undertones have a yellow, peach, or golden tint.
  • Neutral undertones have a mix of cool and warm tones.

Knowing your skin tone will help you choose a hair colour that complements your complexion and brings out your best features.

Understanding the Colour Wheel

Another useful tool for choosing a hair colour that suits you is the colour wheel. The colour wheel shows the relationship between different shades and can help you choose a colour that will complement your skin tone:

  • Complementary colours are opposite each other on the colour wheel and can create a bold, striking look.
  • Analogous colours are next to each other on the colour wheel and can create a more subtle, harmonious look.
  • Monochromatic colours are different shades of the same colour and can create a cohesive, polished look.

When choosing a hair colour, it's important to consider the relationship between the colour and your skin tone. For example, if you have a warm skin tone, you may want to choose a hair colour with warm undertones, such as golden blonde or copper red. If you have a cool skin tone, you may want to choose a hair colour with cool undertones, such as ash blonde or chocolate brown.

Try Before You Buy

Before making a big change to your hair colour, it's a good idea to try it out first. You can do this by using temporary hair dye or a wig in the desired shade. This will give you a chance to see how the colour looks against your skin tone and whether it flatters your features.

If you're not ready to commit to a full head of colour, you can also try highlights or lowlights to add dimension to your natural hair colour. This can be a good way to test out a new shade without fully committing to it.

Ask an Expert

If you're still unsure about which hair colour will suit you best, it's a good idea to consult with a professional stylist. They can help you determine your skin tone and recommend colours that will flatter your features. They can also offer advice on the best hair care products to use to maintain your new colour and keep your hair healthy.

FAQs

What hair colour suits warm skin tones?

If you have a warm skin tone, hair colours with warm undertones, such as golden blonde or copper red, can complement your skin and bring out your features.

What hair colour suits cool skin tones?

If you have a cool skin tone, hair colours with cool undertones, such as ash blonde or chocolate brown, can complement your complexion and bring out your best features.

Can I dye my hair if I have sensitive skin?

If you have sensitive skin, it's important to use hair dye that is gentle and free of harsh chemicals. Look for products that are labeled "hypoallergenic" or "sensitive skin."

Hair Care Tips for Coloured Hair

Once you've found the perfect hair colour, it's important to take care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and vibrant. Here are some tips for maintaining coloured hair:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that are specifically formulated for coloured hair.
  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can strip the colour and make it fade faster.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as flat irons and curling irons, as these can damage the hair and cause the colour to fade.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV-protective spray.

By following these tips, you can keep your coloured hair looking vibrant and healthy for weeks to come.
