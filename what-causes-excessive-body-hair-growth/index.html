What Causes Excessive Body Hair Growth

Written by Petter Jul 17, 2023 · 7 min read

Do you feel like you have an excessive amount of body hair? Are you wondering what might be causing it? Excessive body hair growth can be caused by a number of factors, including genetics, hormonal imbalances, and certain medical conditions. In this article, we’ll explore the different causes of excessive body hair growth and what you can do about it.

Table of Contents

  • Genetics
  • Hormonal Imbalances
  • Medical Conditions
  • How to Reduce Excessive Hair Growth
  • Hair Care Tips
  • FAQ

Genetics

Some people are simply more prone to excessive body hair growth due to their genetics. If your parents or other family members have a lot of body hair, you may be more likely to as well. This is especially true if you come from a family with a history of hirsutism, which is a condition characterized by excessive hair growth in women.

If you have a genetic predisposition to excessive body hair growth, there isn’t much you can do to prevent it. However, there are ways to manage it, which we’ll discuss later in this article.

Hormonal Imbalances

Hormonal imbalances are another common cause of excessive body hair growth. Hormones like testosterone can cause hair to grow in places where it wouldn’t normally, such as on the face, chest, and back.

There are a number of things that can cause hormonal imbalances, including:

  • Polycystic ovary syndrome (PCOS)
  • Hypothyroidism
  • Cushing’s syndrome
  • Adrenal gland disorders
  • Pituitary gland disorders

If you suspect that a hormonal imbalance is causing your excessive body hair growth, it’s important to speak with a healthcare provider. They can run tests to determine if there is an underlying condition that needs to be treated.

Medical Conditions

In some cases, excessive body hair growth may be a symptom of an underlying medical condition. For example, some women with ovarian or adrenal tumors may experience hirsutism as a result of the excess hormones produced by these tumors.

Other medical conditions that can cause excessive body hair growth include:

  • Congenital adrenal hyperplasia
  • Acromegaly
  • Cancer
  • Medication side effects

If you’re experiencing excessive body hair growth and are unsure of the cause, it’s important to speak with a healthcare provider. They can help determine if there is an underlying medical condition that needs to be addressed.

How to Reduce Excessive Hair Growth

While there isn’t a cure for excessive body hair growth, there are ways to manage it. Here are some things you can do:

  • Shave, wax, or use depilatory creams to remove hair
  • Use laser hair removal to permanently reduce hair growth
  • Take medication to block the production of androgens, such as birth control pills or spironolactone
  • Undergo electrolysis to permanently remove hair

It’s important to speak with a healthcare provider before starting any treatment for excessive body hair growth. They can help determine the best course of action for your specific situation.

Hair Care Tips

If you’re dealing with excessive body hair growth, it’s important to take care of your skin and hair. Here are some tips:

  • Moisturize your skin regularly to prevent irritation
  • Use a gentle exfoliant to prevent ingrown hairs
  • Wear loose clothing to prevent chafing
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your skin
  • Use a sunscreen to protect your skin from UV damage

FAQ

Is excessive body hair growth normal?

Excessive body hair growth can be normal for some people, especially if it runs in their family. However, if you’re concerned about your hair growth, it’s important to speak with a healthcare provider.

Can excessive body hair growth be a sign of a medical condition?

Yes, excessive body hair growth can be a symptom of certain medical conditions, such as PCOS or adrenal gland disorders. If you’re experiencing excessive body hair growth, it’s important to speak with a healthcare provider to determine if there is an underlying condition that needs to be addressed.

Can excessive body hair growth be treated?

While there isn’t a cure for excessive body hair growth, there are ways to manage it. Treatment options include shaving, waxing, laser hair removal, medication, and electrolysis.

What can I do to take care of my skin and hair if I have excessive body hair growth?

To take care of your skin and hair if you have excessive body hair growth, it’s important to moisturize regularly, use a gentle exfoliant, wear loose clothing, avoid harsh chemicals, and use sunscreen.
