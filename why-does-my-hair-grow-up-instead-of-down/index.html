Why Does My Hair Grow Up Instead Of Down

Written by Oliver Apr 16, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Have you ever wondered why your hair seems to grow up instead of down? This is a common question that many people ask, especially those with curly or coily hair. In this article, we will explore the reasons behind this phenomenon and provide you with tips on how to care for your hair.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

When we talk about hair growing up instead of down, we are referring to the direction in which the hair points. For many people, their hair seems to grow towards the sky, rather than hanging down towards the ground. This can be particularly noticeable in those with curly or coily hair, as the curls can create a lot of volume and lift the hair up.

The reason why hair grows up instead of down has to do with the shape of the hair follicles. Hair follicles are small sacs located just below the skin's surface, which produce hair. The shape of the follicle determines the shape of the hair that grows out of it. Straight hair follicles produce straight hair, while curly or coily hair follicles produce hair that grows in a spiral shape.

How to

If you want to encourage your hair to grow downwards, there are a few things you can do. First, try using a wide-toothed comb or a detangling brush to gently comb through your hair while it is wet. This will help to distribute the natural oils in your hair and prevent tangles, which can cause your hair to stick up.

You can also try using a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to help weigh your hair down. Apply the product to the ends of your hair and work your way up towards the roots, being careful not to apply too much to the scalp. This will help to moisturize your hair and prevent frizz, which can also contribute to hair sticking up.

Step by step

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to care for your hair to encourage it to grow downwards:

  1. Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  2. Towel dry your hair gently, being careful not to rub it too hard.
  3. Apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to the ends of your hair.
  4. Comb through your hair using a wide-toothed comb or detangling brush.
  5. Blow dry your hair on a low heat setting, pointing the nozzle downwards to encourage your hair to dry in a downwards direction.
  6. Avoid using heavy styling products, as these can weigh your hair down and make it look greasy.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for caring for your hair:

  • Avoid using hot tools such as straighteners or curling irons, as these can damage your hair and cause it to break.
  • Protect your hair from the sun by wearing a hat or using a UV protection spray.
  • Drink plenty of water and eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals, as this can help to promote healthy hair growth.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage.

FAQ

Q: Is it normal for hair to grow upwards?

A: Yes, it is normal for hair to grow upwards, especially in those with curly or coily hair. This is due to the shape of the hair follicles, which produce hair that grows in a spiral shape.

Q: Can I change the direction in which my hair grows?

A: While you cannot change the direction in which your hair grows, you can take steps to encourage it to grow downwards. This includes using a wide-toothed comb or detangling brush, applying a leave-in conditioner or hair oil, and avoiding heavy styling products.

Q: Will cutting my hair make it grow downwards?

A: No, cutting your hair will not change the direction in which it grows. However, getting regular trims can help to prevent split ends and breakage, which can make your hair look healthier and less prone to sticking up.

Hair Care

Caring for your hair is important to keep it looking healthy and shiny. By following the tips and advice in this article, you can help to encourage your hair to grow downwards and prevent it from sticking up. Remember to be gentle with your hair, avoid harsh chemicals and hot tools, and nourish it with plenty of moisture and nutrients.
