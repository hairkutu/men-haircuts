What Was The Style In The 1960S

The 1960s were a time of great change in many aspects of society, including fashion. This era was marked by a shift towards more liberal attitudes and a rejection of traditional values. The fashion trends of the 1960s reflected these changes, with a focus on individual expression and experimentation.

The Hippie Look

The Hippie look was a popular style in the 1960s. It was characterized by long hair, tie-dye clothing, and a rejection of mainstream culture. This look was inspired by the counterculture movement, which was a reaction to the political and social changes of the time. The Hippie look was all about expressing yourself, and it was often associated with peace, love, and freedom.

The Mod Look

The Mod look was another popular style in the 1960s. It was characterized by short hair, bold patterns, and bright colors. This look was inspired by the modernist movement, which was a reaction to the traditional values of the time. The Mod look was all about being modern and fashionable, and it was often associated with youth culture.

The Twiggy Look

The Twiggy look was a popular style in the mid-1960s. It was characterized by a short, boyish haircut, and a focus on the eyes. This look was inspired by the British model Twiggy, who was known for her androgynous look. The Twiggy look was all about being young and fresh, and it was often associated with the fashion industry.

How to Achieve the 1960s Look

If you want to achieve the 1960s look, there are a few key elements you need to consider. First, you need to decide which style you want to emulate â Hippie, Mod, or Twiggy. Once you have decided on a style, you can start to think about the clothing, hair, and makeup that will help you achieve the look.

Step-by-Step Guide to Achieving the 1960s Look

To achieve the Hippie look, you will need to wear loose-fitting clothing, such as bell-bottoms and peasant blouses. You should also wear a lot of jewelry, such as beaded necklaces and bracelets. For hair, go for long, wavy locks, and for makeup, keep it natural with a touch of eyeliner.

To achieve the Mod look, you will need to wear bold patterns and bright colors. A mini-skirt is a must, and you should pair it with knee-high boots. For hair, go for a short, sleek cut, and for makeup, keep it simple with a winged eyeliner and bright lipstick.

To achieve the Twiggy look, you will need to cut your hair into a short, boyish style. For clothing, go for mini-dresses and shift dresses. For makeup, focus on the eyes with heavy eyeliner and false lashes.

Tips for Achieving the 1960s Look

If you want to achieve the 1960s look, here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Don't be afraid to experiment with different styles.
  • Accessorize with jewelry and scarves.
  • Invest in a good pair of knee-high boots.
  • Go for bold patterns and bright colors.

FAQs About the 1960s Look

Q: What was the most popular hairstyle in the 1960s?
A: The most popular hairstyle in the 1960s was long, wavy hair.

Q: What was the most popular clothing item in the 1960s?
A: The most popular clothing item in the 1960s was the mini-skirt.

Q: What kind of makeup was popular in the 1960s?
A: Heavy eyeliner and false lashes were popular in the 1960s.

Hair Care Tips for the 1960s Look

If you want to achieve the 1960s look, you will need to take care of your hair. Here are some hair care tips to keep in mind:

  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.
  • Invest in a good quality shampoo and conditioner.
  • Consider getting regular trims to keep your hair healthy.

Conclusion

The 1960s were a time of great change in fashion, with a focus on individual expression and experimentation. Whether you want to achieve the Hippie look, the Mod look, or the Twiggy look, there are plenty of options to choose from. By following these tips and taking care of your hair, you can achieve the 1960s look and embrace the spirit of this era.
