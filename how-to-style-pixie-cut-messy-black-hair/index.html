Description

Pixie cuts are a popular hairstyle choice for those who want a low maintenance, short haircut. Messy black hair is a style that adds texture and volume to the hair. When combined, a pixie cut messy black hair can create an edgy, yet chic look. In this blog post, we will provide a step-by-step guide on how to style your pixie cut messy black hair to achieve this look. We will also provide tips and tricks to help you maintain your hairstyle and answer frequently asked questions.

How to Style Pixie Cut Messy Black Hair

Styling a pixie cut messy black hair can be quick and easy. Below are the steps to achieve this style:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair. Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add texture to your hair.
  2. Apply a small amount of hair mousse or texturizing spray to the roots of your hair. This will help add volume and texture to your hair.
  3. Blow dry your hair using your fingers to create volume and texture. Use a diffuser attachment to prevent frizz.
  4. Use a flat iron to add more texture to your hair. Gently twist and turn the flat iron as you run it through your hair to create a messy, textured look.
  5. Apply a small amount of hair wax or pomade to your fingertips and run it through the ends of your hair. This will help define the texture and create a messy look.
  6. Finish with a light hold hairspray to keep your hair in place.

Step by Step Guide

Step 1: Start with clean, dry hair

Before you start styling your hair, make sure it is clean and dry. Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add texture and volume to your hair. Rinse thoroughly and towel dry your hair.

Step 2: Apply a small amount of hair mousse or texturizing spray to the roots of your hair

Take a small amount of hair mousse or texturizing spray and apply it to the roots of your hair. This will help add volume and texture to your hair.

Step 3: Blow dry your hair using your fingers

Use your fingers to lift and tousle your hair as you blow dry it. Use a diffuser attachment to prevent frizz and create more volume.

Step 4: Use a flat iron to add more texture to your hair

Take small sections of your hair and use a flat iron to create a messy, textured look. Gently twist and turn the flat iron as you run it through your hair to create waves and curls.

Step 5: Apply hair wax or pomade to the ends of your hair

Take a small amount of hair wax or pomade and apply it to your fingertips. Run your fingers through the ends of your hair to define the texture and create a messy look.

Step 6: Finish with a light hold hairspray

Spray a light hold hairspray over your hair to keep it in place. Use your fingers to tousle your hair and create more volume.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips and tricks to help you maintain your pixie cut messy black hair:

  • Use a dry shampoo to refresh your hair between washes.
  • Avoid using too much product as it can weigh down your hair.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair and prevent breakage.
  • Trim your hair regularly to maintain the shape of your pixie cut.
  • Experiment with different hair accessories such as headbands, clips, and scarves.

FAQs

What face shape is best for a pixie cut?

A pixie cut can work for any face shape, but it is important to choose a style that complements your features. For example, if you have a round face, a pixie cut with longer layers can help elongate your face. If you have a square face, a pixie cut with bangs can soften your features.

How often do I need to trim my pixie cut?

It is recommended to trim your hair every 4-6 weeks to maintain the shape of your pixie cut.

Can I still style my hair if it is not black?

Of course! The steps to style a pixie cut messy hair are the same regardless of your hair color. However, if you want to achieve the same edgy look, you can try adding temporary black hair dye or highlights.

Hair Care for Pixie Cut Messy Black Hair

Proper hair care is essential to maintain the health and appearance of your hair. Here are some tips for caring for your pixie cut messy black hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to prevent stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid using hot tools too often as it can damage your hair.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a conditioning spray.
  • Avoid over-washing your hair as it can dry out your scalp and hair.

By following these tips and tricks, you can achieve an edgy and chic look with your pixie cut messy black hair. Remember to experiment with different styles and accessories to make the look your own. With proper hair care, your hair will remain healthy and vibrant.
