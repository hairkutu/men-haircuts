Why Do Orthodox Priests Have Long Hair And Beards

Written by Oliver Mar 17, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Orthodox priests are easily recognizable by their long hair and beards. This is a common practice among Orthodox priests and has been a tradition for centuries. In this article, we will discuss the reasons behind this practice and why it is still followed today.

Table of Contents

  • Religious Significance
  • Historical Origins
  • Cultural Tradition
  • Hair Care Tips
  • FAQ

Religious Significance

There are several religious reasons why Orthodox priests grow their hair and beards. In the Old Testament, it was considered a sin to shave or cut one's hair. This was because hair and beards were seen as a symbol of strength and masculinity. Orthodox priests believe that by growing their hair and beards, they are honoring this tradition and following in the footsteps of the prophets and saints of the Old Testament.

In addition, the New Testament also mentions the importance of hair and beards. Saint Paul advises men to let their hair grow long and to not cut their beards. This is seen as a sign of respect for God and a way to show humility and submission.

Historical Origins

The tradition of Orthodox priests growing their hair and beards has its roots in ancient Christianity. During the early days of Christianity, monks and other religious figures grew their hair and beards as a sign of humility and dedication to their faith. This practice was eventually adopted by Orthodox priests and has been passed down through the centuries.

During the Ottoman Empire, which lasted from the 14th to the early 20th century, the Orthodox Church was forced to operate underground. Priests and monks grew their hair and beards as a way to disguise themselves and avoid persecution. This practice continued even after the Ottoman Empire fell, and today it is seen as a symbol of the Church's endurance and resilience.

Cultural Tradition

Aside from its religious significance, growing long hair and beards is also a cultural tradition among Orthodox priests. In many Eastern European countries, men are expected to have long hair and beards as a sign of masculinity and strength. This cultural tradition has become intertwined with the religious tradition of Orthodox priests, and it is now seen as a way to show respect for both God and one's cultural heritage.

Hair Care Tips

For Orthodox priests, growing their hair and beards is not just a matter of tradition, it is also a matter of personal grooming. Here are some tips for taking care of long hair and beards:

  • Regularly wash and condition your hair and beard to keep them clean and hydrated.
  • Use a natural bristle brush to distribute the natural oils in your hair and beard.
  • Trim split ends and shape your beard regularly to keep it looking neat and tidy.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or heat styling tools on your hair and beard.

FAQ

Q: Do all Orthodox priests have long hair and beards?

A: No, not all Orthodox priests follow this tradition. It is up to each individual priest to decide whether or not to grow their hair and beard.

Q: Can Orthodox priests cut their hair and beards?

A: Yes, they can. While growing long hair and beards is a tradition, it is not a requirement. Some Orthodox priests choose to cut their hair and beards for personal or practical reasons.

Q: Do Orthodox nuns have to grow their hair?

A: No, Orthodox nuns do not have to grow their hair. In fact, many Orthodox nuns choose to shave their heads as a symbol of their devotion and dedication to God.

In conclusion, the tradition of Orthodox priests growing their hair and beards has its roots in both religious and cultural traditions. While it is not a requirement, it is seen as a sign of respect and devotion to God and one's cultural heritage. By taking care of their hair and beards, Orthodox priests are not only honoring tradition, but also taking pride in their personal grooming.
