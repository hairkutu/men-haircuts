How To Fix Receding Temples A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 05, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Receding temples can be a cause of concern for many people. It can affect both men and women, and it usually occurs due to aging, genetics, or certain medical conditions. If you are one of those who are experiencing receding temples, don't worry! There are various ways to fix it. In this article, we will guide you on how to fix receding temples effectively.

Table of Contents

  • Description
  • How to Fix Receding Temples
  • Step-by-Step Guide
  • Tips to Prevent Receding Temples
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care for Receding Temples

Description

Receding temples occur when the hairline starts to recede from the sides of the forehead. It can cause embarrassment and make you look older than your actual age. Receding temples are usually caused due to a lack of blood flow to the hair follicles, which leads to hair loss. It can also occur due to genetics, hormonal changes, stress, and certain medical conditions such as alopecia areata and scalp psoriasis.

How to Fix Receding Temples

There are various ways to fix receding temples, such as medications, hair transplant surgery, and natural remedies. However, the best way to fix receding temples is by using natural remedies and making lifestyle changes.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to fix receding temples:

  1. Massage your scalp regularly to increase blood flow to the hair follicles. You can use essential oils such as rosemary, peppermint, and lavender oil for better results.
  2. Use a hair growth shampoo and conditioner to promote hair growth and prevent hair loss. Look for products that contain biotin, keratin, and other essential vitamins and minerals.
  3. Eat a healthy and balanced diet that includes plenty of protein, vitamins, and minerals. Foods such as eggs, fish, nuts, and leafy greens are excellent for promoting hair growth.
  4. Avoid using harsh chemicals and heat styling tools on your hair, as it can damage the hair follicles and lead to hair loss.
  5. Try natural remedies such as onion juice, aloe vera gel, and coconut oil to promote hair growth and prevent hair loss. Apply these remedies on your scalp and hair regularly for better results.
  6. If natural remedies don't work, consider using medications such as minoxidil and finasteride to promote hair growth and prevent hair loss.
  7. If all else fails, consider undergoing hair transplant surgery to restore your hairline and fix receding temples. Consult with a hair transplant specialist for more information.

Tips to Prevent Receding Temples

Here are some tips to prevent receding temples:

  • Avoid smoking and excessive alcohol consumption, as it can lead to hair loss and other health issues.
  • Exercise regularly to improve blood circulation and promote hair growth.
  • Avoid wearing tight hairstyles such as braids and ponytails, as it can damage the hair follicles and lead to hair loss.
  • Manage stress levels by practicing meditation, yoga, or other relaxation techniques.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about receding temples:

Can receding temples be reversed?

Yes, receding temples can be reversed by using natural remedies, making lifestyle changes, and undergoing hair transplant surgery.

What causes receding temples?

Receding temples are usually caused due to a lack of blood flow to the hair follicles, which leads to hair loss. It can also occur due to genetics, hormonal changes, stress, and certain medical conditions such as alopecia areata and scalp psoriasis.

How long does it take to fix receding temples?

It varies from person to person, but it can take anywhere from a few months to a year to fix receding temples using natural remedies and making lifestyle changes.

Hair Care for Receding Temples

Here are some hair care tips for receding temples:

  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair, as it is less damaging than a brush.
  • Avoid using hot water to wash your hair, as it can damage the hair follicles and lead to hair loss. Use lukewarm or cool water instead.
  • Avoid using hair styling products that contain alcohol, as it can dry out your hair and lead to hair loss.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather conditions by wearing a hat or using an umbrella.

By following these tips and making lifestyle changes, you can fix receding temples and promote hair growth naturally. However, if you don't see any improvement or if you are experiencing severe hair loss, consult with a doctor or a hair transplant specialist for the best course of action.
