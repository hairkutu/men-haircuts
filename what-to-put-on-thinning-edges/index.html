What To Put On Thinning Edges A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Eveline May 15, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you struggling with thinning edges and looking for solutions? Thinning edges can be a common problem for many people, but fortunately, there are a variety of products and techniques you can use to help promote hair growth and protect your edges. In this article, we'll cover everything you need to know about what to put on thinning edges and how to care for your hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Identify Thinning Edges
  • What to Put on Thinning Edges
  • Step-by-Step Guide for Using Hair Products

How to Identify Thinning Edges

Thinning edges can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetics, stress, and harsh styling practices. Some common signs of thinning edges include:

  • Visible patches of hair loss on the hairline
  • Receding hairline
  • Slow hair growth around the hairline
  • Brittle, dry hair

If you notice any of these symptoms, it's important to take action to promote hair growth and protect your edges.

What to Put on Thinning Edges

There are a variety of products you can put on thinning edges to help promote hair growth and protect your hairline. Some of the best options include:

  • Castor oil
  • Coconut oil
  • Jojoba oil
  • Shea butter
  • Peppermint oil

These products can help to moisturize and nourish your hair, improve circulation to the scalp, and promote healthy hair growth.

Step-by-Step Guide for Using Hair Products

If you're not sure how to use these products on your hair, here's a step-by-step guide:

  1. Start with clean, dry hair.
  2. Apply a small amount of the product to your fingertips.
  3. Massage the product into your scalp, focusing on the areas where your hair is thinning.
  4. Use a wide-tooth comb to distribute the product evenly throughout your hair.
  5. Cover your hair with a plastic cap and leave the product on for at least 30 minutes.
  6. Rinse the product out with warm water and shampoo your hair as usual.

Tips for Promoting Hair Growth

In addition to using hair products, there are a variety of other things you can do to help promote hair growth and protect your edges:

  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hairline, such as braids and weaves.
  • Take a break from heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons.
  • Eat a healthy diet that's rich in vitamins and minerals, such as biotin and iron.
  • Get regular exercise to improve circulation to the scalp.
  • Reduce stress through activities such as yoga or meditation.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about what to put on thinning edges:

Can I use hair growth supplements to help promote hair growth?

While hair growth supplements can be effective for some people, it's important to talk to a healthcare professional before starting any new supplements.

How often should I use hair products on my edges?

You can use hair products on your edges as often as needed, but it's important to avoid overusing them, as this can lead to product buildup and scalp irritation.

Should I avoid using heat styling tools altogether?

While it's important to reduce your use of heat styling tools, you don't necessarily have to avoid them altogether. Just make sure to use a heat protectant spray and avoid using high temperatures.

Hair Care Tips for Thinning Edges

Finally, here are some general hair care tips to help protect your edges and promote healthy hair growth:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner that's free from sulfates and parabens.
  • Avoid brushing or combing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf.
  • Don't wash your hair too frequently, as this can strip your hair of its natural oils.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

By following these tips and using the right products, you can help to promote hair growth and protect your edges. Remember to be patient, as it may take time to see results, but with consistent care, you can achieve healthy, full hair.
