How To Cut Medium Length Layered Hair Yourself Diy Guide

Written by Aprilia Mar 17, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of spending money on haircuts at the salon every few months? Do you want to try something new and give yourself a fresh look? Cutting your own hair at home can seem daunting, but with the right tools and technique, it can be a successful DIY project. In this article, we will guide you through how to cut medium length layered hair yourself in a relaxed and easy-to-understand language.

How to

Before starting your haircut, it's important to gather all the necessary tools. You will need a pair of sharp hair cutting scissors, a comb, and hair clips to section off your hair. It's also helpful to have a mirror and good lighting.

1. Start by shampooing and conditioning your hair, then dry it completely.

2. Use your comb to create a middle part, then section off your hair into four equal parts, two in the front and two in the back, using hair clips to hold them in place.

3. Beginning with one of the front sections, comb your hair straight down and hold it between your index and middle fingers. Use your scissors to cut the ends at a slight angle, starting from the bottom and working your way up towards the middle of your hair. Repeat this step on the other front section.

4. Move on to the back sections, starting with one side at a time. Comb your hair straight down and hold it between your fingers, then cut the ends at an angle, starting from the bottom and working your way up towards the middle of your hair. Repeat this step on the other back section.

5. Once you've finished cutting all four sections, use your scissors to feather the ends of your hair by cutting into them at a diagonal angle. This will create a layered effect.

Step by Step

1. Shampoo and condition your hair, then dry it completely.

2. Create a middle part with your comb and section off your hair into four equal parts using hair clips.

3. Starting with one of the front sections, comb your hair straight down and hold it between your fingers. Cut the ends at a slight angle, starting from the bottom and working your way up towards the middle of your hair.

4. Repeat step 3 on the other front section.

5. Move on to the back sections, starting with one side at a time. Comb your hair straight down and hold it between your fingers, then cut the ends at an angle, starting from the bottom and working your way up towards the middle of your hair.

6. Repeat step 5 on the other back section.

7. Use your scissors to feather the ends of your hair by cutting into them at a diagonal angle.

Tips

1. Take your time and be patient. Cutting your own hair can be nerve-wracking, but rushing through it can lead to mistakes.

2. Don't cut too much at once. It's better to cut a little and then see how it looks before cutting more.

3. Use sharp scissors. Dull scissors can cause split ends and make your hair look uneven.

4. Watch tutorials online. There are many helpful videos on YouTube that can guide you through the process step-by-step.

FAQ

Q: Can I cut my hair when it's wet or should it be dry?

A: It's best to cut your hair when it's dry, as wet hair can stretch and make your cut uneven.

Q: How often should I cut my medium length layered hair?

A: It's recommended to get a trim every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy hair and prevent split ends.

Q: What if I make a mistake while cutting my hair?

A: Don't panic! Mistakes can be fixed. If you accidentally cut too much, try to blend it in with the rest of your hair by feathering the ends. If it's a major mistake, consider going to a professional for help.

Hair Care

After cutting your hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its health and shine. Here are some tips:

1. Use a nourishing shampoo and conditioner that's appropriate for your hair type.

2. Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.

3. Apply a hair mask once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

4. Get regular trims to prevent split ends and maintain your haircut.

By following these simple steps, you can successfully cut your own medium length layered hair at home. Remember to be patient, take your time, and use the right tools. With practice, you'll become a pro at DIY haircuts!
