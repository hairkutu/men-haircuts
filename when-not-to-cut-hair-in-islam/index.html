When Not To Cut Hair In Islam A Comprehensive Guide

Written by William Aug 19, 2023 Â· 9 min read

For Muslims, hair is considered an important aspect of one's physical appearance and a reflection of one's spiritual beliefs. As such, there are specific times when cutting hair is prohibited in Islam. In this article, we will explore when not to cut hair in Islam, and provide some tips on hair care in general.

  2. When not to cut hair in Islam

Why is hair important in Islam?

Hair is considered a symbol of beauty and vitality in many cultures, including in Islam. Muslims are encouraged to take care of their hair and keep it neat and clean. In fact, Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him) is reported to have said: "Cleanliness is half of faith" (Sahih Muslim).

Therefore, hair care is an important aspect of Islamic hygiene, and there are specific guidelines that Muslims should follow when it comes to cutting and grooming their hair.

When not to cut hair in Islam

There are certain times when Muslims are prohibited from cutting their hair. These include:

During the first ten days of Dhul-Hijjah

The first ten days of Dhul-Hijjah are considered the most sacred days in the Islamic calendar, and cutting hair during this period is not allowed. This is because these days are associated with the Hajj pilgrimage to Mecca, and Muslims who are not performing the pilgrimage are encouraged to observe the sacredness of this time.

On the day of Eid al-Adha

Eid al-Adha, also known as the Feast of Sacrifice, is one of the two major festivals in Islam. It is celebrated on the 10th day of Dhul-Hijjah, and Muslims are prohibited from cutting their hair on this day.

During menstruation

Women who are menstruating are also prohibited from cutting their hair, as well as performing certain other acts of worship, such as praying or fasting. This is because menstruation is considered a period of impurity in Islam, and Muslims are encouraged to avoid certain activities during this time.

It is important to note that these prohibitions only apply to cutting hair. Trimming or grooming hair is generally allowed, as long as it does not involve cutting the hair to a significant length.

How to take care of your hair

Now that we know when not to cut hair in Islam, let's take a look at some general tips for hair care:

Wash your hair regularly

Keeping your hair clean is an important aspect of hair care. Muslims are encouraged to perform wudu (ablution) before each prayer, which includes washing the hands, face, and hair. Additionally, it is recommended to wash your hair at least once a week.

Use natural products

Using natural hair care products, such as oils and herbs, can help keep your hair healthy and strong. Some popular natural hair care products in Islam include black seed oil, olive oil, and henna.

Avoid using heat tools

Excessive use of heat tools, such as hair dryers and straighteners, can damage your hair and make it more prone to breakage. It is best to avoid using these tools as much as possible, or at least use them sparingly.

Step-by-step guide for hair care

Here's a step-by-step guide for taking care of your hair:

  1. Wash your hair with a gentle, natural shampoo
  2. Apply a natural hair conditioner, such as coconut oil or aloe vera gel
  3. Massage your scalp to stimulate blood flow
  4. Rinse your hair thoroughly with lukewarm water
  5. Gently towel-dry your hair
  6. Avoid using a hair dryer, if possible
  7. Apply a natural hair serum or oil to moisturize your hair
  8. Comb or brush your hair gently, starting at the ends and working your way up to the roots
  9. Avoid pulling or tugging on your hair, as this can cause breakage
  10. Wrap your hair in a towel or scarf to protect it while it dries

Tips for healthy hair

Here are a few additional tips for keeping your hair healthy:

  • Eat a healthy diet, rich in vitamins and minerals
  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water
  • Avoid smoking and excessive alcohol consumption, as these can damage your hair
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends
  • Avoid tight hairstyles, such as braids or ponytails, as these can cause hair breakage

FAQs about hair care in Islam

Can I dye my hair in Islam?

Yes, dyeing your hair is generally allowed in Islam, as long as the dye is natural and does not contain any haram (forbidden) ingredients.

Can men shave their heads in Islam?

Yes, men are allowed to shave their heads in Islam, as long as it is done for a valid reason, such as performing the Hajj pilgrimage or for medical reasons.

Is it haram to have long hair in Islam?

No, having long hair is not haram in Islam, as long as it is kept clean and well-groomed.

Can I cut my hair during Ramadan?

Yes, there are no specific prohibitions on cutting hair during Ramadan. However, Muslims are encouraged to observe the sacredness of this month and refrain from any actions that may distract them from their worship.

In conclusion, hair care is an important aspect of Islamic hygiene, and there are certain times when cutting hair is prohibited in Islam. By following these guidelines and taking care of your hair, you can maintain healthy, strong hair that reflects your spiritual beliefs and enhances your physical appearance.
