How To Cut Medium Length Wavy Hair Yourself A Beginner s Guide

Written by Petter Aug 25, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Are you tired of spending a fortune on haircuts every few months? Do you want to save some money and learn how to cut your own hair? If you have medium length wavy hair, you're in luck. With a few simple tools, some patience, and the right technique, you can achieve a salon-worthy haircut from the comfort of your own home. In this article, we'll guide you through the process of how to cut medium length wavy hair yourself.

Table of Contents

  1. Description
  2. How to Cut Medium Length Wavy Hair Yourself
  3. Step-by-Step Guide
  4. Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair
  5. FAQs
  6. Hair Care Tips

Description

Medium length wavy hair is a versatile and flattering hairstyle that suits many face shapes and hair types. It's a popular choice for those who want a low-maintenance style that can be dressed up or down. However, regular trips to the salon can be expensive and time-consuming, especially if you have a busy schedule.

By learning how to cut medium length wavy hair yourself, you can save money and time while achieving a fresh new look. With a few essential tools and some basic techniques, you can create a professional-looking haircut that suits your individual style.

How to Cut Medium Length Wavy Hair Yourself

Before you begin, it's important to gather the necessary tools and equipment. You'll need:

  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips or ties
  • Spray bottle

It's also helpful to have a mirror and a comfortable chair or stool to sit on. Once you have everything you need, you can start cutting your hair.

Step-by-Step Guide

Follow these simple steps to cut your medium length wavy hair:

  1. Wash your hair and let it air dry or blow dry it with a diffuser attachment. This will help you see the natural texture of your hair and make it easier to cut.
  2. Part your hair down the middle and clip or tie one side up. Start cutting the other side in small sections, using the comb to guide the scissors. Cut vertically, taking off a small amount of hair at a time. Repeat on the other side.
  3. Trim any split ends or damaged hair with sharp scissors. Cut at an angle for a more natural look.
  4. Blend the layers by taking small sections of hair and cutting at a slight angle. This will create a soft, layered look.
  5. Check the length by letting your hair down and looking in the mirror. Make any necessary adjustments.
  6. Finish with a spritz of hairspray or texturizing spray to add volume and hold.

Tips for Cutting Your Own Hair

Cutting your own hair can be daunting, but with these tips, you'll be able to achieve a professional-looking cut:

  • Start with small sections of hair and cut vertically to avoid making a blunt line.
  • If you're unsure about the length, cut less rather than more. You can always go back and trim more later.
  • Use a mirror to check the back of your head and make sure it's even.
  • Don't cut your hair when it's wet as it will be longer when it dries. Instead, cut it when it's dry and natural.
  • Invest in a good pair of scissors specifically designed for cutting hair.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about cutting medium length wavy hair:

Q: How often should I cut my hair?

A: It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly it becomes damaged or split. On average, it's recommended to cut your hair every 6-8 weeks.

Q: Can I cut my hair if it's curly?

A: Yes, you can cut curly hair, but it requires a different technique. It's best to seek advice from a professional stylist or do some research on how to cut curly hair yourself.

Hair Care Tips

Once you've cut your hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain its health and shine. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a good quality shampoo and conditioner that suits your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often as they can damage your hair. If you must use them, use a heat protectant spray.
  • Apply a hair mask once a week to nourish and hydrate your hair.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and maintain your haircut.

By following these tips and techniques, you can cut your medium length wavy hair yourself and achieve a stylish, flattering haircut. With a bit of practice and patience, you'll be able to save money and time while looking and feeling your best.
