How To Cut A Short Pixie Bob A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Jul 11, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you're looking for an edgy, low-maintenance hairstyle, a short pixie bob might be the perfect choice for you. This cut is versatile, stylish, and easy to maintain, making it a popular choice among women of all ages. In this guide, we'll walk you through the process of cutting a short pixie bob, from choosing the right style to finishing touches.

Section 1: Choosing the Right Style

The first step in cutting a short pixie bob is choosing the right style for your face shape and hair type. Pixie bobs can be tailored to suit any face shape, but there are a few general guidelines to follow:

  • Oval faces: Almost any style will work well with an oval face.
  • Round faces: Look for styles that add volume at the top and elongate the face.
  • Square faces: Soften your features with a layered or choppy style.
  • Heart-shaped faces: Opt for a style that adds volume at the top and tapers at the nape of the neck.

If you have thick hair, you may want to consider a choppy or layered style to help thin it out. If you have fine hair, a blunt cut will add volume and texture.

Section 2: Preparing Your Hair

Before you begin cutting your hair, you'll need to wash and dry it thoroughly. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any product buildup, and then blow-dry your hair straight.

Once your hair is dry, you can begin sectioning it off. Use a comb to create a side part, and then separate the front section of your hair from the back. Clip the back section up and out of the way.

Section 3: Cutting Your Hair

Now it's time to start cutting your hair. Here's a step-by-step guide:

  1. Start with the front section of your hair. Use your fingers to comb through it and create a guide for your scissors.
  2. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it straight across.
  3. Work your way back through the front section, cutting each piece to the same length.
  4. Once you've finished the front section, move on to the sides. Use your fingers to comb through the hair and create a guide for your scissors.
  5. Cut the hair straight across, working your way from the front to the back.
  6. Repeat on the other side.
  7. Finally, unclip the back section of your hair and use a comb to create a guide for your scissors.
  8. Cut the hair straight across, working your way from the bottom up.

Remember to take your time and cut small sections at a time. It's better to go slowly and make small adjustments than to cut too much at once.

Section 4: Styling Your Pixie Bob

Once your hair is cut, you can start styling it. Here are a few options to consider:

  • Blow-dry your hair straight for a sleek look.
  • Use a curling iron to add loose waves or curls.
  • Add texture with a sea salt spray or texturizing spray.
  • Use a pomade or wax to create a messy, tousled look.

Experiment with different styles to find what works best for you.

Section 5: Tips for Maintaining Your Pixie Bob

Pixie bobs are low-maintenance, but there are a few things you can do to keep your hair looking its best:

  • Trim your hair every 4-6 weeks to keep it looking fresh.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair healthy.
  • Avoid using too much heat on your hair, as this can damage it.
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to prevent your hair from drying out.

Section 6: FAQ

Q: Can I cut a pixie bob myself?

A: It's best to have your hair cut by a professional, but if you're confident in your abilities, you can try cutting it yourself. Just remember to take your time and cut small sections at a time.

Q: How do I know if a pixie bob will suit me?

A: Pixie bobs can be tailored to suit any face shape, but it's always a good idea to consult with a hairstylist before making a drastic change to your hair.

Section 7: Hair Care

Taking care of your hair is important, especially if you've just cut it. Here are a few tips to keep your hair healthy:

  • Use a heat protectant spray before using any heat styling tools.
  • Avoid washing your hair every day, as this can strip it of its natural oils.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair, as this will cause less damage than a brush.
  • Eat a healthy, balanced diet to promote healthy hair growth.

With these tips and tricks, you'll be able to cut and maintain a short pixie bob with ease. Remember to take your time, experiment with different styles, and have fun with your new look!
