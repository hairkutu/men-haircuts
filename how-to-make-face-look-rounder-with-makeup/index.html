How To Make Your Face Look Rounder With Makeup Tips And Tricks

Are you looking for ways to make your face look rounder with makeup? If so, you've come to the right place! In this article, we'll discuss some tips and tricks to help you achieve that round face shape you desire. From contouring to highlighting, there are many makeup techniques you can use to create the illusion of a rounder face.

How to Make Your Face Look Rounder with Makeup

The key to making your face look rounder with makeup is to use a combination of contouring and highlighting techniques. Contouring involves using a darker shade of makeup to create the illusion of shadows, while highlighting involves using a lighter shade of makeup to draw attention to certain areas of your face. Here are some steps to follow:

Step 1: Apply Foundation

Start by applying a foundation that matches your skin tone. This will create an even base for your makeup and help to smooth out any imperfections. Use a makeup sponge or brush to apply the foundation, blending it evenly across your face.

Step 2: Contour Your Cheeks

Next, apply a dark shade of contouring powder or cream in the hollows of your cheeks. To find the hollows, make a fish face by sucking in your cheeks. Apply the contouring product in a diagonal line from your ear to the corner of your mouth, blending it well with a brush. This will create the illusion of shadows and make your cheeks look more defined.

Step 3: Highlight Your Cheekbones

To make your cheekbones stand out, apply a lighter shade of highlighting powder or cream on top of your cheekbones. Use a small brush to apply the highlighter in a diagonal line from the top of your cheekbones to the corner of your eye. This will draw attention to your cheeks and make them look more lifted.

Step 4: Define Your Jawline

To make your jawline look more defined, apply a dark shade of contouring powder or cream along your jawline, blending it well with a brush. This will create the illusion of shadows and make your jawline look more chiseled.

Step 5: Highlight Your Forehead

Finally, to balance out your face shape, apply a lighter shade of highlighting powder or cream on your forehead. Use a small brush to apply the highlighter in a diagonal line from the center of your forehead to your hairline. This will draw attention to your forehead and make your face look more oval-shaped.

Step-by-Step Tutorial

Here's a step-by-step tutorial to help you create a rounder face shape with makeup:

  1. Apply foundation that matches your skin tone.
  2. Contour your cheeks with a dark shade of powder or cream.
  3. Highlight your cheekbones with a lighter shade of powder or cream.
  4. Define your jawline with a dark shade of powder or cream.
  5. Highlight your forehead with a lighter shade of powder or cream.

Remember to blend your makeup well for a natural-looking finish.

Tips for Creating a Rounder Face Shape

Here are some additional tips to help you create a rounder face shape with makeup:

  • Use a lighter shade of foundation on the center of your face to create the illusion of a more rounded shape.
  • Avoid applying too much product, as this can create a harsh, unnatural look.
  • Experiment with different shades of contouring and highlighting products to find the ones that work best for your skin tone.
  • Use a small brush to apply your makeup, as this will give you more control over the placement of the product.

FAQs

Q: Can I use bronzer instead of contouring powder or cream?

A: Yes, bronzer can work well for contouring if you choose a shade that's a few shades darker than your skin tone.

Q: Can I use a cream highlighter instead of a powder?

A: Yes, cream highlighter can work well for creating a dewy, natural-looking finish.

Q: How do I choose the right shade of contouring and highlighting products?

A: Look for shades that are a few shades darker or lighter than your skin tone, depending on whether you're using a contouring or highlighting product. Test the product on your skin before purchasing to make sure it's a good match.

Hair Care Tips for Round Face Shapes

If you have a round face shape, you may want to consider a hairstyle that will help to balance out your features. Here are some tips:

  • Avoid hairstyles that add volume to the sides of your face, such as a bob that ends at your chin.
  • Opt for hairstyles that add volume to the top of your head, such as a layered cut or a side-swept bang.
  • Consider a hairstyle that frames your face, such as a long, layered cut with face-framing layers.

By following these tips, you can create a rounder face shape with makeup and a hairstyle that flatters your features.
