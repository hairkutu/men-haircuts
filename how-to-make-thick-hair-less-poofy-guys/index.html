How To Make Thick Hair Less Poofy Guys A Complete Guide

Written by Robby Aug 14, 2023 · 8 min read

Thick hair can be a blessing and a curse. While it certainly looks great and can be styled in many ways, it can also be difficult to manage and often appears poofy. If you're a guy with thick, poofy hair, you're not alone. In fact, many men struggle with the same issue. Fortunately, there are several ways to make thick hair less poofy. In this article, we'll provide you with a complete guide on how to achieve the perfect hairstyle for your thick hair.

Description

Thick hair is a result of having a lot of hair follicles per square inch on your scalp. This can make your hair appear fuller and more voluminous, but it can also make it more difficult to manage. Poofy hair, on the other hand, is caused by frizz and lack of moisture in the hair. It can make your hair look unpolished and untidy. If you have thick hair, you may find that it's more prone to becoming poofy, especially if you live in a humid environment.

How to Make Thick Hair Less Poofy Guys: Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Use the Right Shampoo and Conditioner

The first step to making thick hair less poofy is to use the right shampoo and conditioner. Look for products that are designed for thick or curly hair. These products will help to moisturize your hair and prevent it from becoming frizzy. Avoid using products that contain sulfates, as these can strip your hair of its natural oils and make it more prone to frizz.

Step 2: Don't Over-Wash Your Hair

Washing your hair too often can strip it of its natural oils, which can make it more prone to frizz. Instead, try washing your hair every other day or every two days. This will help to keep your hair moisturized and prevent it from becoming poofy.

Step 3: Use a Leave-In Conditioner

Using a leave-in conditioner can help to keep your hair moisturized and prevent it from becoming frizzy. Apply a small amount of leave-in conditioner to your hair after you shower, and then comb it through to distribute it evenly.

Step 4: Use a Wide-Tooth Comb

When you're combing your hair, use a wide-tooth comb. This will help to prevent your hair from becoming tangled and will also help to distribute the natural oils throughout your hair.

Step 5: Don't Blow Dry Your Hair

Blow drying your hair can cause it to become more poofy. Instead, let your hair air dry naturally. If you need to speed up the drying process, use a towel to pat your hair dry, but avoid rubbing your hair vigorously, as this can cause frizz.

Step 6: Use a Styling Product

Using a styling product can help to keep your hair in place and prevent it from becoming poofy. Look for a product that is designed for thick or curly hair, such as a pomade, wax, or gel. Apply a small amount of product to your hair and then style it as desired.

Tips for Making Thick Hair Less Poofy

Here are a few additional tips to help you make your thick hair less poofy:

  • Avoid brushing your hair when it's dry. This can cause it to become more poofy.
  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Trim your hair regularly to get rid of split ends and keep it looking healthy.
  • Avoid using hot tools, such as straighteners and curling irons, as these can damage your hair and make it more prone to frizz.
  • Consider getting a keratin treatment or other smoothing treatment to help tame your hair.

FAQ

How do I know if I have thick hair?

You can tell if you have thick hair by looking at the density of your hair. If you have a lot of hair follicles per square inch on your scalp, you likely have thick hair. You can also tell by the circumference of your ponytail or bun. If it's thicker than a quarter, you have thick hair.

Can I use regular shampoo and conditioner on my thick hair?

You can use regular shampoo and conditioner on your thick hair, but it's best to use products that are designed for thick or curly hair, as these will help to moisturize your hair and prevent it from becoming frizzy.

Can I blow dry my hair if I have thick hair?

You can blow dry your hair if you have thick hair, but it's best to avoid doing so, as it can cause your hair to become more poofy. Instead, let your hair air dry naturally.

Hair Care: Final Thoughts

Thick hair can be a challenge to manage, but with the right techniques and products, you can make it less poofy and more manageable. By following the tips in this article, you'll be able to achieve the perfect hairstyle for your thick hair.
