Popular Styles of the 60s

The 60s was a decade of style, and there were many popular trends that emerged during this period. Some of the most iconic styles of the 60s include:

1. Mini Skirts

The mini skirt was a revolutionary fashion item of the 60s. It was first introduced by British fashion designer Mary Quant in 1965, and it quickly became a symbol of youth and rebellion. Mini skirts were usually made of bold prints and bright colors, and they were often paired with knee-high boots or ballet flats.

2. Bell Bottoms

Bell bottoms were another popular fashion item of the 60s. They were first worn by sailors in the 19th century, but they became fashionable in the 60s when hippies started wearing them. Bell bottoms were usually made of denim or corduroy, and they flared out at the bottom, creating a bell-like shape.

3. Go-Go Boots

Go-go boots were a staple of 60s fashion. They were usually made of patent leather and had a block heel. Go-go boots were often worn with mini skirts or shift dresses, and they were a symbol of youth and rebellion.

4. Shift Dresses

The shift dress was a simple, straight-cut dress that was popular in the 60s. It was made of lightweight fabrics like cotton or linen, and it often had bold prints and bright colors. The shift dress was a symbol of the youth culture, and it was worn by women of all ages.

5. Psychedelic Prints

Psychedelic prints were a hallmark of 60s fashion. They were often bright, bold, and colorful, and they featured patterns like swirls, paisleys, and flowers. Psychedelic prints were used on everything from dresses to scarves to wallpaper.

How to Achieve 60s Style

If you want to achieve 60s style, there are a few key elements that you need to keep in mind. Here are some tips:

1. Embrace Bright Colors

One of the defining features of 60s fashion was the use of bright, bold colors. To achieve 60s style, you should embrace bright colors like orange, pink, and yellow.

2. Wear Bold Prints

60s fashion was all about bold prints, so don't be afraid to wear prints like paisley, polka dots, and stripes.

3. Accessorize

Accessories were a key part of 60s style. To achieve the look, you should wear accessories like hoop earrings, chunky bracelets, and statement necklaces.

4. Experiment with Makeup

Makeup was another important part of 60s style. To achieve the look, you should experiment with bold eyeliner, bright lipstick, and false lashes.

Step by Step Guide

If you want to achieve 60s style, here is a step by step guide:

1. Start with a Mini Skirt or Shift Dress

The first step to achieving 60s style is to start with a mini skirt or shift dress. Look for pieces that are made of lightweight fabrics and have bold prints or bright colors.

2. Add Go-Go Boots

Next, add a pair of go-go boots. Look for boots that are made of patent leather and have a block heel.

3. Accessorize

Accessorize with bold jewelry like hoop earrings, chunky bracelets, and statement necklaces. Don't be afraid to mix and match different pieces.

4. Experiment with Makeup

Finish off the look by experimenting with bold eyeliner, bright lipstick, and false lashes. Don't be afraid to go all out!

Tips for 60s Style

Here are some tips to help you achieve 60s style:

1. Don't Be Afraid to Mix and Match

60s fashion was all about experimenting and taking risks. Don't be afraid to mix and match different prints, colors, and textures.

2. Invest in Statement Pieces

To achieve 60s style, you should invest in statement pieces like a mini skirt, bell bottoms, or a shift dress. These pieces will form the foundation of your wardrobe.

3. Have Fun!

60s fashion was all about having fun and expressing yourself. Don't take it too seriously and have fun with your style!

Frequently Asked Questions

1. What were the popular hairstyles of the 60s?

The popular hairstyles of the 60s included the beehive, the bouffant, and the pixie cut.

2. What were the popular materials used in 60s fashion?

The popular materials used in 60s fashion included denim, corduroy, and lightweight fabrics like cotton and linen.

3. What accessories were popular in 60s fashion?

The popular accessories in 60s fashion included hoop earrings, chunky bracelets, and statement necklaces.

Hair Care for 60s Style

If you want to achieve 60s style, here are some tips for hair care:

1. Use a Volumizing Shampoo

60s hairstyles were all about volume, so use a volumizing shampoo to give your hair some lift.

2. Use a Root Lifter

To add even more volume, use a root lifter on your hair before blow drying.

3. Use a Curling Iron

To achieve 60s curls, use a curling iron on your hair. Wrap small sections of hair around the iron and hold for a few seconds.

4. Finish with Hairspray

To keep your 60s style in place, finish with a generous spritz of hairspray.

That concludes our article on the popular styles of the 60s. We hope you found it informative and helpful. Remember to have fun with your style and don't be afraid to experiment!
