How To Use Thinning Scissors Men s Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Mar 28, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you're looking to achieve a textured, layered look without sacrificing too much length, thinning scissors can be a great tool to have in your kit. But, like any hair-cutting tool, they require some skill and technique to use effectively. In this guide, we'll walk you through how to use thinning scissors on men's hair, from choosing the right scissors to finishing touches.

Description: What are Thinning Scissors?

Thinning scissors, also known as texturizing or blending scissors, are a type of scissors that have one serrated blade and one straight blade. When used on hair, the serrated blade removes some of the hair while the straight blade leaves the remaining hair intact, creating a more textured, layered look.

How to Choose the Right Thinning Scissors

Before you start using thinning scissors on your hair, you'll need to choose the right pair. Here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Look for a pair of scissors with a serrated blade that has teeth that are spaced fairly far apart. This will give you more control over how much hair you're removing.
  • Make sure the scissors are sharp. Dull scissors can damage your hair and make it harder to achieve the desired look.
  • Consider the length of the scissors. Longer scissors can be more versatile, but shorter scissors can be easier to control.

Step-by-Step Guide on How to Use Thinning Scissors Men's Hair

Now, let's get into how to use thinning scissors properly:

Step 1: Wash and Dry Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair as you normally would. This will help you see the natural texture and shape of your hair, which will make it easier to use the thinning scissors effectively.

Step 2: Section Your Hair

Using a comb, divide your hair into sections. For best results, work on one section at a time.

Step 3: Hold the Scissors Properly

Hold the thinning scissors with your thumb and ring finger in the holes of the handle, and your middle finger on the blade. This will give you the most control over the scissors.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Starting at the ends of your hair, use the thinning scissors to remove small sections of hair. Work your way up the section, cutting at different angles and depths to create a textured look. Be sure to work slowly and carefully, and don't remove too much hair at once.

Step 5: Blend the Layers

Once you've finished cutting all of the sections, use a comb to blend the layers together. This will help create a more natural, textured look.

Step 6: Finish with Styling Products

Finally, use your favorite styling products to finish off the look. A texturizing spray or pomade can help enhance the texture and hold the style in place.

Tips for Using Thinning Scissors

  • Work slowly and carefully, removing small sections of hair at a time.
  • Don't cut too close to the scalp, as this can create uneven layers and damage your hair.
  • Use a comb to keep your sections neat and tidy, and to help you see where you've already cut.
  • Experiment with different cutting angles and depths to create a more natural, layered look.

FAQ About Using Thinning Scissors on Men's Hair

Can thinning scissors be used on all hair types?

Yes, thinning scissors can be used on all hair types, although they may be more effective on thicker, coarser hair.

How often should I use thinning scissors on my hair?

This depends on your hair type and how quickly it grows. As a general rule, you should use thinning scissors every 6-8 weeks to maintain your desired look.

Can I use thinning scissors to create a specific hairstyle?

Thinning scissors can be a great tool for creating a textured, layered look, but they may not be the best choice for creating a precise, structured hairstyle.

Hair Care Tips After Using Thinning Scissors

After using thinning scissors on your hair, it's important to take good care of it to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using hot tools like blow dryers and straighteners too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to maintain your desired length and shape.
  • Consider using a hair mask or oil treatment once a week to nourish your hair.

Using thinning scissors on men's hair can be a great way to achieve a natural, textured look. By following these tips and techniques, you can use thinning scissors effectively and confidently.
