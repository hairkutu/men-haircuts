How To Cut A Layered Haircut Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Christine May 26, 2023 · 6 min read

Cutting your own hair can be a daunting task, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done. If you're looking to cut a layered haircut yourself, this guide will take you through the steps to achieve a professional-looking cut.

Table of Contents

  • Tools You'll Need
  • Prepping Your Hair
  • Sectioning Your Hair
  • Cutting Your Layers
  • Blending Your Layers
  • Tips for a Successful Cut
  • FAQs
  • Hair Care After Your Cut

Tools You'll Need

Before you start cutting your hair, make sure you have the following tools:

  • Sharp scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clips
  • Haircutting cape or towel
  • Spray bottle with water

Prepping Your Hair

Start by washing and drying your hair. Once your hair is completely dry, comb it out and make sure there are no tangles.

Next, put on your haircutting cape or towel to protect your clothing. Fill your spray bottle with water and dampen your hair.

Sectioning Your Hair

Sectioning your hair is important to ensure an even cut. Start by parting your hair down the middle and then again from ear to ear. This will leave you with four sections of hair.

Clip up three of the sections, leaving one section of hair at the back of your head. This will be the first section you cut.

Cutting Your Layers

Take a small section of hair from the back of your head and comb it straight down. Hold the hair between your fingers and cut it at an angle, starting at the length you want your shortest layer to be.

Continue taking small sections of hair and cutting at an angle until you've cut all of the hair in that section.

Repeat this process with the other three sections of hair, making sure to cut at the same angle and length for each section.

Blending Your Layers

Once you've cut all of your layers, it's time to blend them together. Take small sections of hair from the top of your head and comb them down over the layers you just cut. Use your scissors to lightly trim the ends of the top layer, blending it with the layers underneath.

Continue blending the layers until you're happy with the look.

Tips for a Successful Cut

  • Start with small sections of hair to ensure an even cut.
  • Don't cut too much hair at once. You can always cut more, but you can't add hair back once it's been cut.
  • Use sharp scissors to ensure clean cuts.
  • Take breaks if you need to. Cutting your own hair can be tiring.

FAQs

Can I cut my own hair if it's curly?

Yes, you can. Just be sure to cut your hair when it's dry and in its natural state.

What if I make a mistake?

If you make a mistake, don't worry. Hair grows back. You can always go to a professional stylist to have them fix it.

How often should I cut my hair?

It's recommended to get a haircut every 6-8 weeks to maintain healthy hair.

Hair Care After Your Cut

After you've cut your hair, it's important to take care of it to maintain the health of your hair. Here are some tips:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair healthy and moisturized.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often.
  • Get regular trims to maintain the shape of your haircut.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and chlorine by wearing a hat or using a protective spray.

Cutting a layered haircut yourself can seem intimidating, but with the right tools and techniques, it can be done. Follow these steps and you'll have a professional-looking cut in no time.
