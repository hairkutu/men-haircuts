What Happens When You Thin Curly Hair

Written by Daniel Jul 11, 2023 · 7 min read

Curly hair can be both a blessing and a curse. While it’s gorgeous and unique, it can also be difficult to manage, especially when it comes to thinning. Thinning curly hair is a popular hair styling technique that can give your hair more volume and manageability. However, it’s important to know what happens when you thin curly hair and how to take care of it afterwards.

Table of Contents

  • How to Thin Curly Hair

  • Tips for Thin Curly Hair

Description

Thinning curly hair involves a process where a hair stylist uses thinning shears to remove some of the hair’s bulkiness. The process creates layers and texture that make it easier to style the hair. It also makes the hair look less thick, which can be great for people who want to reduce the volume of their hair.

How to Thin Curly Hair

Thinning curly hair should only be done by a professional hair stylist. Attempting to thin your hair at home can result in a disastrous outcome. A professional hair stylist has the necessary tools and experience to get the job done correctly. They can also help you choose the right technique for your hair type and texture.

Step by Step Guide

Here are the basic steps involved in thinning curly hair:

  1. Wash and dry your hair. It’s important to start with clean, dry hair to get the best results.
  2. Section your hair. Use hair clips to separate your hair into sections. This will make it easier to work with.
  3. Choose the right technique. Your hair stylist will determine the best technique to use based on your hair type and texture. They may use a razor or thinning shears to remove some of the bulkiness.
  4. Start thinning. Your hair stylist will start thinning your hair one section at a time. They will carefully remove some of the hair’s bulkiness, creating layers and texture.
  5. Style your hair. Once the thinning process is complete, your hair stylist will style your hair to show off the new layers and texture.

Tips for Thin Curly Hair

Here are some tips to help you take care of your thinned curly hair:

  • Use a wide-toothed comb to detangle your hair. This will prevent breakage and damage to your hair.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools. Heat can damage your hair and make it more brittle.
  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner. Curly hair tends to be drier than other hair types, so it’s important to keep it moisturized.
  • Avoid brushing your hair when it’s wet. Wet hair is more prone to breakage and damage.
  • Use a hair mask once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.

FAQ

Can thinning curly hair cause hair loss?

No, thinning curly hair should not cause hair loss. However, if it’s not done correctly, it can cause damage to the hair, which can lead to breakage and split ends.

How often should you thin curly hair?

It’s recommended to thin curly hair every 6 to 8 weeks, depending on how fast your hair grows.

Will thinning curly hair make it easier to manage?

Yes, thinning curly hair can make it easier to manage. It creates texture and layers that make it easier to style and comb through.

Hair Care for Thinned Curly Hair

Curly hair requires special care to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some tips to help you take care of your thinned curly hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to prevent dryness and breakage.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair, as heat can damage your hair.
  • Use a leave-in conditioner to keep your hair moisturized.
  • Avoid using hair elastics that are too tight, as they can cause breakage and damage to your hair.
  • Use a silk or satin pillowcase to prevent frizz and breakage while you sleep.

Thinning curly hair can be a great way to give your hair more manageability and volume. However, it’s important to know what happens when you thin curly hair and how to take care of it afterwards. By following these tips, you can keep your thinned curly hair looking healthy and gorgeous.
