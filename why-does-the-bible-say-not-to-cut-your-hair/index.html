Why Does The Bible Say Not To Cut Your Hair

Written by Eveline Apr 29, 2023 · 8 min read

Have you ever wondered why the Bible tells us not to cut our hair? It’s a question that has puzzled many people over the years. In this blog post, we’ll explore the reasons behind this commandment and what it means for us today.

Table of Contents

Why Does the Bible Say Not to Cut Your Hair?

The Bible says not to cut your hair because it is a symbol of strength and power. In ancient times, long hair was seen as a sign of virility and masculinity. In the Bible, Samson’s strength was famously linked to his long hair. In 1 Corinthians 11:14, it says “Does not the very nature of things teach you that if a man has long hair, it is a disgrace to him?”

For women, long hair was seen as a sign of beauty and femininity. In 1 Corinthians 11:15, it says “But if a woman has long hair, it is her glory? For long hair is given to her as a covering.”

So, the Bible commands us not to cut our hair as a way of honoring our gender and the roles that God has given us.

How to Follow the Commandment

If you want to follow the commandment of not cutting your hair, there are a few things you need to keep in mind:

  • Make sure to keep your hair clean and well-groomed.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools that can damage your hair.
  • Use natural oils and treatments to keep your hair healthy and strong.
  • Avoid harsh chemicals and dyes that can strip your hair of its natural oils.

Step-by-Step Guide to Not Cutting Your Hair

Here’s a step-by-step guide to help you grow and maintain long hair:

  1. Start with a healthy scalp. Make sure to keep your scalp clean and moisturized.
  2. Avoid using harsh shampoos and conditioners that can dry out your hair.
  3. Massage your scalp regularly to stimulate hair growth.
  4. Use natural oils like coconut, olive, or argan oil to nourish and strengthen your hair.
  5. Avoid using heat styling tools like straighteners and curling irons.
  6. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair and avoid breakage.
  7. Trim your hair regularly to avoid split ends.
  8. Avoid harsh chemicals and dyes that can damage your hair.
  9. Wear protective styles like braids or buns to keep your hair healthy and strong.
  10. Be patient! Growing long hair takes time and requires dedication.

Tips for Maintaining Long Hair

Here are a few tips to help you maintain your long hair:

  • Use a satin pillowcase to avoid hair breakage while you sleep.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair and cause breakage.
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals to promote hair growth.
  • Stay hydrated to keep your hair moisturized from the inside out.
  • Protect your hair from the sun and wind by wearing a hat or scarf.

FAQs About the Hair Commandment

Is it a sin to cut your hair?

No, it is not a sin to cut your hair. The Bible commands us not to cut our hair as a way of honoring our gender and the roles that God has given us, but it is not a sin to cut it.

Can I trim my hair?

Yes, you can trim your hair. Trimming your hair regularly can actually help it grow longer and stronger by preventing split ends.

Can I dye my hair?

Yes, you can dye your hair. However, it’s important to avoid harsh chemicals and dyes that can strip your hair of its natural oils and cause damage.

Hair Care for Long Hair

Here are a few tips for caring for your long hair:

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair.
  • Apply natural oils like coconut or argan oil to your hair to keep it healthy and strong.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like straighteners and curling irons.
  • Trim your hair regularly to avoid split ends.
  • Avoid harsh chemicals and dyes that can damage your hair.
  • Wear protective styles like braids or buns to keep your hair healthy and strong.

Following the commandment not to cut your hair is a way of honoring the roles that God has given us as men and women. While it may require some extra effort to maintain long hair, the results can be beautiful and rewarding.
