How To Get A Good Pixie Cut A Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Jun 05, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you're looking for a low-maintenance and trendy hairstyle, a pixie cut might be the perfect choice for you. But before you book an appointment with your hairstylist, there are a few things you should know to make sure you get the best possible result. In this article, we'll give you a step-by-step guide on how to get a good pixie cut, as well as some tips on how to take care of your new hairstyle.

Table of Contents

  • How to Prepare for a Pixie Cut
  • Choosing the Right Pixie Cut for Your Face Shape
  • Communicating with Your Hairstylist
  • The Pixie Cut: Step-by-Step
  • Tips for Maintaining Your Pixie Cut
  • FAQs
  • Final Thoughts

How to Prepare for a Pixie Cut

Before you get a pixie cut, it's important to prepare your hair for the change. Here are some tips:

1. Make sure your hair is healthy

A pixie cut will remove a lot of your hair, so it's important that your hair is healthy to begin with. If your hair is damaged or brittle, it may not be able to handle the cut. To make sure your hair is healthy, use a deep conditioning treatment once a week for a few weeks before your appointment.

2. Don't wash your hair the day of your appointment

Your hair should have some natural oils in it to make it easier for your hairstylist to work with. If you wash your hair the day of your appointment, it may be too slippery and difficult to cut.

3. Bring pictures of the style you want

It's always a good idea to bring pictures of the pixie cut you want to your appointment. This will help your hairstylist understand exactly what you're looking for.

Choosing the Right Pixie Cut for Your Face Shape

Not all pixie cuts are created equal. The right cut for you will depend on your face shape. Here are some guidelines:

1. Round face

If you have a round face, you'll want to choose a pixie cut that has volume on top and is shorter on the sides. This will help elongate your face.

2. Square face

A square face looks best with a pixie cut that has soft, wispy bangs and layers on top.

3. Oval face

Lucky you! An oval face can pull off almost any pixie cut. Consider a cut with longer layers on top for added volume.

Communicating with Your Hairstylist

Communication is key when it comes to getting the perfect pixie cut. Here's what you should tell your hairstylist:

1. How short you want your hair

Be specific about how short you want your hair. Do you want it to be a super-short buzz cut, or a longer pixie cut with bangs?

2. How you want your hair styled

Do you want your hair styled forward, to the side, or spiked up? Let your hairstylist know what your preferences are.

3. Any concerns you have

If you're nervous about getting a pixie cut, tell your hairstylist. They can help put your mind at ease and answer any questions you may have.

The Pixie Cut: Step-by-Step

Here's how your hairstylist will likely cut your hair:

1. Section your hair

Your hairstylist will likely section your hair and cut it one section at a time.

2. Cut the sides and back

Your hairstylist will start by cutting the sides and back of your hair. They may use clippers or scissors.

3. Cut the top

Once the sides and back are cut, your hairstylist will move on to the top of your hair. They may use scissors or a razor to add texture.

4. Style your hair

Once your hair is cut, your hairstylist will style it to your liking. They may add product to give your hair texture and hold.

Tips for Maintaining Your Pixie Cut

Here are some tips for keeping your pixie cut looking great:

1. Get regular trims

A pixie cut requires regular trims to maintain its shape. Plan to get a trim every 4-6 weeks.

2. Use the right products

Use products that are specifically designed for short hair, such as texturizing sprays or pomades.

3. Experiment with styling

There are a lot of different ways to style a pixie cut. Experiment with different products and techniques to find what works best for you.

FAQs

1. Will a pixie cut look good on me?

Pixie cuts can look great on a variety of face shapes and hair types. Talk to your hairstylist to find out if a pixie cut is right for you.

2. How do I know if a pixie cut is too short for me?

If you're nervous about going too short, start with a longer pixie cut and work your way up to a shorter cut.

3. How can I style my pixie cut for a special occasion?

There are a lot of ways to dress up a pixie cut, such as adding a headband or a sparkly hair accessory. Talk to your hairstylist for more ideas.

Final Thoughts

Getting a pixie cut is a bold and fun choice. By following these tips and guidelines, you can make sure you get a pixie cut that looks great and is easy to maintain. So go ahead, take the plunge!
