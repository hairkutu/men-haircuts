Can Alopecia Areata Be Caused By Stress

Written by Robby Aug 08, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Have you ever noticed a sudden loss of hair in patches on your scalp or body? This could be a result of Alopecia Areata, an autoimmune disease that causes hair loss. While the exact cause of Alopecia Areata is still unknown, research suggests that stress can be a contributing factor. In this article, we will explore the relationship between Alopecia Areata and stress, how to manage stress, and hair care tips for those with Alopecia Areata.

  • What is Alopecia Areata?
  • Can Alopecia Areata be Caused by Stress?

  • FAQ

What is Alopecia Areata?

Alopecia Areata is an autoimmune disease that affects the hair follicles, causing hair loss in patches on the scalp or other parts of the body. The immune system attacks the hair follicles, which results in hair loss. The cause of Alopecia Areata is still unknown, but research has suggested that it can be triggered by stress, genetics, and environmental factors.

Can Alopecia Areata be Caused by Stress?

Stress is a common trigger for many health conditions, including Alopecia Areata. Stress causes the body to release cortisol, a hormone that can affect the immune system's response. When cortisol levels are high, it can weaken the immune system, making the body susceptible to autoimmune diseases like Alopecia Areata. While stress is not the only cause of Alopecia Areata, it can exacerbate the condition and make it harder to manage.

How to Manage Stress?

Stress management is essential for those with Alopecia Areata. Here are some ways to manage stress:

  • Practice relaxation techniques like deep breathing, meditation, or yoga.
  • Exercise regularly to release endorphins and reduce stress levels.
  • Get enough sleep. Lack of sleep can increase stress levels.
  • Connect with friends and family.
  • Take breaks when needed.

Hair Care Tips for Alopecia Areata Patients

People with Alopecia Areata may experience hair loss, which can be frustrating and challenging. Here are some hair care tips for Alopecia Areata patients:

  • Avoid harsh chemicals and treatments that can damage the hair follicles.
  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to prevent further hair loss.
  • Avoid heat styling tools like curling irons and straighteners.
  • Wear a wig, scarf, or hat to protect the scalp from the sun and cold weather.
  • Consider hair growth treatments like minoxidil or corticosteroids.

FAQ

Q: Can Alopecia Areata be cured?

A: There is currently no cure for Alopecia Areata, but there are treatments available to manage the condition.

Q: Can stress cause hair loss in other areas of the body?

A: Yes, stress can cause hair loss in other areas of the body, including the eyebrows and eyelashes.

Q: Is Alopecia Areata contagious?

A: No, Alopecia Areata is not contagious.


In conclusion, stress can be a contributing factor to Alopecia Areata, an autoimmune disease that causes hair loss. Managing stress through relaxation techniques, exercise, and social connections can help reduce the severity of Alopecia Areata. Additionally, taking care of your hair with gentle products and treatments can help promote hair growth. If you are experiencing hair loss or other symptoms of Alopecia Areata, consult with a healthcare professional for proper diagnosis and treatment.
