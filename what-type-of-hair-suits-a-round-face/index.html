What Type Of Hair Suits A Round Face

Written by Frank May 23, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you have a round face, you may be wondering what type of hair suits your face shape. The good news is that there are plenty of hairstyles that can flatter a round face and make it look more angular. In this article, we'll go over some of the best hairstyles for round faces, as well as some tips for caring for your hair.

Description

A round face is characterized by full cheeks and a curved jawline. If you have a round face, you may also have a short forehead and a wide chin. To balance out your face shape, you'll want to choose a hairstyle that adds height and length to your face.

How to

When choosing a hairstyle for a round face, you'll want to consider the length and texture of your hair. Here are some tips:

1. Long Layers

Long layers can help elongate a round face by adding movement and texture to your hair. If you have long hair, ask your stylist for long layers that start at your chin or below.

2. Side-Swept Bangs

Side-swept bangs can help balance out a round face by drawing attention to your eyes and cheekbones. Make sure the bangs are long enough to cover your forehead and sweep to the side.

3. Pixie Cut

A pixie cut can be a great option for a round face, especially if you have fine hair. The short length can help create the illusion of height, while the layers can add texture and movement.

4. Bob

A bob can be a flattering hairstyle for a round face, as long as it's cut to the right length. A chin-length bob with long layers can help elongate your face and add volume to your hair.

Step by Step

Here's a step-by-step guide to getting a bob haircut that flatters a round face:

1. Consult with your stylist.

Before getting a bob haircut, you'll want to consult with your stylist to make sure it's the right length and style for your face shape. Your stylist can recommend a length and style that will flatter your features.

2. Start with clean, dry hair.

Make sure your hair is clean and dry before getting a bob haircut. This will make it easier for your stylist to cut your hair evenly.

3. Section your hair.

Section your hair into four parts: two in the front and two in the back. Use hair clips to keep each section in place.

4. Cut the back section first.

Starting with the back section, cut your hair to the desired length. Your stylist may use a razor or scissors to create texture and movement.

5. Cut the front section.

Once the back section is cut, move on to the front sections. Cut your hair to the desired length, making sure to add long layers that start at your chin or below.

6. Style your hair.

Once your hair is cut, your stylist can style it to your liking. You may want to add some volumizing mousse or hairspray to create volume and texture.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for caring for your hair:

1. Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner.

Using a volumizing shampoo and conditioner can help add body and texture to your hair. Look for products that are specifically designed for your hair type.

2. Avoid heavy styling products.

Heavy styling products can weigh down your hair and make it look flat. Opt for lightweight products that add texture and volume.

3. Get regular trims.

Getting regular trims can help keep your hair healthy and prevent split ends. Aim to get a trim every six to eight weeks.

FAQ

What hairstyles should I avoid if I have a round face?

Avoid hairstyles that add volume to the sides of your face, such as wide curls or a blunt bob. You should also avoid hairstyles that are too short, as they can make your face appear even rounder.

Can I wear my hair in a ponytail if I have a round face?

Yes, you can wear your hair in a ponytail if you have a round face. Just make sure to add some volume at the crown of your head to elongate your face.

Hair Care

Caring for your hair is an important part of maintaining a healthy, beautiful look. Here are some tips for keeping your hair looking its best:

1. Wash your hair regularly.

Washing your hair regularly can help remove dirt and oil that can weigh down your hair. Aim to wash your hair every two to three days.

2. Use a heat protectant.

If you use heat styling tools, such as a flat iron or curling iron, make sure to use a heat protectant spray to prevent damage to your hair.

3. Avoid over-styling.

Over-styling your hair can lead to split ends and breakage. Try to limit your use of heat styling tools and avoid tight hairstyles that can pull on your hair.

4. Get regular trims.

Getting regular trims can help keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends. Aim to get a trim every six to eight weeks.

In conclusion, there are plenty of hairstyles that can flatter a round face. By choosing the right length and texture for your hair, you can create a look that elongates your face and adds volume and movement. With these tips and tricks, you can keep your hair looking healthy and beautiful for years to come.
