How To Hide Growing Undercut A Step By Step Guide

Written by William May 30, 2023 Â· 9 min read

If you have an undercut, you know that it can be a tricky hairstyle to maintain. While it looks great when it's fresh, the growing-out stage can be a real pain. In this guide, we'll show you how to hide your growing undercut until you're ready to embrace it again.

Description

An undercut is a hairstyle where the hair on the sides and back of the head is shaved or cut very short, while the hair on top is left longer. It's a trendy and edgy look, but it requires regular maintenance to keep it looking sharp. When you're ready to grow out your undercut, it can be a frustrating process. The hair on the sides and back will grow faster than the hair on top, leaving you with an awkward in-between stage. However, there are ways to hide your growing undercut until it reaches a length you're comfortable with.

How to Hide Growing Undercut

Here are some steps you can take to hide your growing undercut:

1. Use Hair Accessories

One of the easiest ways to hide your growing undercut is to use hair accessories. Headbands, hair clips, and bobby pins can all help keep your hair in place and cover up the shorter sections. You can experiment with different styles and colors to find the look that works best for you.

2. Try a Different Part

If you usually part your hair in the middle, try parting it on the side instead. This can help cover up the shorter sections and make your hair look more even. You can also experiment with different parting styles, such as zigzag or diagonal parts.

3. Use Hair Gel or Wax

Hair gel or wax can help keep your hair in place and create the illusion of a more even length. Apply a small amount to your fingertips and work it through your hair, paying extra attention to the shorter sections. This can help blend the lengths together and make your hair look more uniform.

4. Experiment with Hairstyles

There are plenty of hairstyles that can help hide your growing undercut. Try a messy bun, a low ponytail, or a half-up, half-down style. These can help keep your hair in place and cover up the shorter sections.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here's a step-by-step guide to hiding your growing undercut:

Step 1: Start with Clean, Dry Hair

Wash and dry your hair before attempting any of these styles. This will help make your hair more manageable and easier to work with.

Step 2: Apply Hair Gel or Wax

Apply a small amount of hair gel or wax to your fingertips and work it through your hair, paying extra attention to the shorter sections. This will help blend the lengths together and create a more even look.

Step 3: Experiment with Hairstyles

Try different hairstyles, such as a messy bun, a low ponytail, or a half-up, half-down style. Use hair accessories like headbands, hair clips, and bobby pins to help keep your hair in place and cover up the shorter sections.

Step 4: Maintain Your Hair

As your hair grows out, it's important to maintain it. Regular trims can help keep your hair looking neat and healthy. Consider using hair masks and oils to nourish your hair and promote growth.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you hide your growing undercut:

  • Experiment with different hairstyles and hair accessories to find the look that works best for you.
  • Consider using hair gel or wax to help blend the lengths together and create a more even look.
  • Try parting your hair in a different style to help cover up the shorter sections.
  • Regular trims can help keep your hair looking neat and healthy as it grows out.

FAQ

How long does it take to grow out an undercut?

The length of time it takes to grow out an undercut can vary depending on how short the sides and back are and how fast your hair grows. On average, it can take anywhere from 6 months to a year to fully grow out an undercut.

Can I color my hair while growing out an undercut?

Yes, you can color your hair while growing out an undercut. However, it's important to keep in mind that coloring can damage your hair and make it harder to grow out. Consider using hair masks and oils to help nourish your hair and promote growth.

Hair Care

While you're growing out your undercut, it's important to take care of your hair. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a gentle shampoo and conditioner to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Consider using hair masks and oils to help nourish your hair and promote growth.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons, as these can damage your hair and make it harder to grow out.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking neat and healthy as it grows out.

By following these tips and tricks, you can successfully hide your growing undercut until it reaches a length you're comfortable with. Remember to be patient and take good care of your hair during the growing-out process.
