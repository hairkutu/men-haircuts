How to Do Freeform Dreads

Freeform dreads, also known as organic dreads, are a hairstyle that involves allowing the hair to naturally mat and form into dreadlocks over time. Unlike traditional dreadlocks, freeform dreads are not formed using any tools or techniques. Instead, they are created by simply not combing or brushing your hair for an extended period of time.

Step by Step

Here are the steps to follow if you want to create freeform dreads:

  1. Stop combing or brushing your hair. This is the most important step, as it allows your hair to naturally tangle and form into dreadlocks.
  2. Wash your hair regularly. It is important to keep your hair clean and healthy while it is forming into dreads. Use a residue-free shampoo and avoid using any products that contain wax or other heavy ingredients.
  3. Separate your hair as it begins to lock. As your hair starts to form into dreadlocks, you may notice that some sections are larger than others. Use your fingers to gently separate these sections and encourage them to form into individual dreadlocks.
  4. Be patient. Freeform dreads take time to form and mature. It may take several months or even years for your hair to fully lock and form into mature dreadlocks.

Tips

Here are some tips to keep in mind as you create your freeform dreads:

  • Be gentle with your hair. Avoid over-manipulating your hair or using too much force when separating sections. This can cause damage and slow down the locking process.
  • Avoid using conditioner. Conditioner can soften the hair and make it more difficult for dreadlocks to form.
  • Use a satin or silk pillowcase. This can help reduce frizz and tangling while you sleep.
  • Experiment with different hairstyles. Freeform dreads can be styled in a variety of ways, from updos to loose waves. Try different styles to find what works best for you.

FAQ

Here are some frequently asked questions about freeform dreads:

Q: Can I create freeform dreads if I have straight hair?

A: Yes, freeform dreads can be created with any hair texture. However, it may take longer for straight hair to fully lock and form into mature dreadlocks.

Q: Do I need to shave my head to create freeform dreads?

A: No, you do not need to shave your head. Simply stop combing or brushing your hair and allow it to naturally form into dreadlocks.

Q: Can I dye my hair if I have freeform dreads?

A: Yes, you can dye your hair with freeform dreads. However, it is important to use a dye that is safe for dreadlocks and to avoid over-processing your hair.

Hair Care

Here are some tips for taking care of your freeform dreads:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a residue-free shampoo. Avoid using any products that contain wax or heavy ingredients.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as these can cause damage to your hair.
  • Use a dreadlock-specific oil or spray to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.
  • Be gentle when separating sections of hair. Pulling too hard can cause damage and slow down the locking process.

With these tips and steps, you can create unique and low-maintenance freeform dreads. Remember to be patient and gentle with your hair, and enjoy the process of watching your locks form and mature over time.
