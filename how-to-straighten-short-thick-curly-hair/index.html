How To Straighten Short Thick Curly Hair

Written by Robby May 17, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you tired of dealing with unruly, frizzy curls? Do you want to achieve sleek, straight locks that are easy to style? If you have short, thick, curly hair, achieving straight hair may seem like an impossible feat. But fear not, we are here to help you achieve your hair goals. In this article, we will provide you with step-by-step instructions on how to straighten short thick curly hair.

Table of Contents

  • How to Straighten Short Thick Curly Hair

  • Tips for Straightening Short Thick Curly Hair

Description

Short thick curly hair can be difficult to manage, especially if you want to straighten it. However, with the right tools and techniques, it is possible to achieve a sleek and straight hairstyle. Straightening your hair can be damaging if done incorrectly, so it is important to use the right products and tools to protect your hair.

How to Straighten Short Thick Curly Hair

The first step in straightening short thick curly hair is to wash and condition your hair with products that are designed to smooth and straighten curly hair. Look for products that contain ingredients such as keratin, argan oil, or coconut oil, which can help to tame frizz and soften your hair.

After washing your hair, apply a heat protectant spray or serum to your hair. This will help to protect your hair from heat damage caused by styling tools such as straighteners.

Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to straighten short thick curly hair:

  1. Wash and condition your hair with products designed for curly hair.
  2. Apply a heat protectant spray or serum to your hair.
  3. Blow dry your hair on a low heat setting until it is almost dry.
  4. Divide your hair into sections and clip each section up.
  5. Starting at the back of your head, take a small section of hair and run a straightener through it, starting at the roots and working your way down to the ends.
  6. Repeat this process with each section of hair, working from the back to the front of your head.
  7. Once you have straightened all of your hair, apply a small amount of hair oil or serum to add shine and smoothness to your hair.

Tips for Straightening Short Thick Curly Hair

Here are some tips to help you straighten short thick curly hair:

  • Use a high-quality straightener with ceramic plates that heat evenly and quickly.
  • Divide your hair into small sections to ensure that you straighten every strand of hair.
  • Don't go over the same section of hair too many times with the straightener, as this can cause damage to your hair.
  • Use a heat protectant spray or serum to protect your hair from heat damage.
  • Apply a small amount of hair oil or serum after straightening your hair to add shine and smoothness.

FAQs

Here are some frequently asked questions about straightening short thick curly hair:

Q: Can I straighten my hair with a blow dryer?
A: Yes, you can straighten your hair with a blow dryer, but it may take longer than using a straightener. Q: How often should I straighten my hair?
A: It is best to limit the amount of heat you apply to your hair. Try to straighten your hair no more than once a week. Q: How can I protect my hair from heat damage?
A: Use a heat protectant spray or serum before using any styling tools that apply heat to your hair.

Hair Care Tips for Straightened Hair

Here are some tips to help you care for your straightened hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair clean and healthy.
  • Avoid using heavy styling products that can weigh down your hair, making it look greasy and flat.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as this can cause damage to your hair over time.
  • Apply a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair moisturized and healthy.

By following these tips and techniques, you can achieve sleek, straight hair that is easy to style and maintain. Remember to always use products that are designed for your hair type and to protect your hair from heat damage when using styling tools.
