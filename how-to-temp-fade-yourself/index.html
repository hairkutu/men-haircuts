How To Temp Fade Yourself A Step By Step Guide

Written by Bardi Aug 23, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking for a fresh new haircut, a temp fade might be just what you need. A temp fade is a type of haircut that gradually fades from longer hair at the top of the head to shorter hair on the sides and back. It's a popular style among men, and it's easy to see why. Not only does it look great, but it's also relatively easy to do yourself. In this article, we'll walk you through the steps to achieve a temp fade haircut at home.

How to Temp Fade Yourself: A Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Gather Your Supplies

Before you start cutting your hair, you'll need to gather a few supplies. Here's what you'll need:

  • Clippers
  • Scissors
  • Comb
  • Hair clip
  • Mirror
  • Towel

Make sure your clippers are fully charged or plugged in and that the blades are clean and oiled. You'll also want to have a towel handy to catch any loose hair.

Step 2: Start with a Longer Guard

Begin by selecting a guard size that will leave your hair relatively long on top. You can always go shorter later, so it's best to start with a longer guard and work your way down. Use the clippers to trim the hair on the top of your head evenly.

Step 3: Create a Guide Line

Next, create a guide line around your head. This will help you get an even fade. Use the clippers without a guard to trim a straight line around your head, about an inch above your ears. Use the comb to make sure the line is straight.

Step 4: Start Fading

Now it's time to start fading. Switch to a shorter guard on your clippers and begin trimming the sides of your head. Start at the bottom and work your way up, using the longer guard as a guide. Be sure to blend the shorter hair into the longer hair at the top of your head.

Step 5: Blend the Top

Once you've trimmed the sides of your head, it's time to blend the top. Use your scissors to cut the longer hair on top, blending it into the shorter hair on the sides. Use the comb to make sure everything is even.

Step 6: Refine the Fade

Now that you've created a basic fade, it's time to refine it. Switch to an even shorter guard on your clippers and use it to fade the sides and back of your head even more. Be sure to blend the hair in smoothly so that there are no harsh lines.

Step 7: Clean Up the Edges

Use your clippers to clean up the edges around your hairline and ears. You can use a trimmer or razor to clean up any stray hairs or create a more defined hairline.

Tips for Creating a Great Temp Fade

Here are a few tips to keep in mind as you create your temp fade:

  • Start with a longer guard and work your way down
  • Take your time and be patient
  • Use a mirror to check your progress
  • Blend the hair in smoothly
  • Clean up the edges for a polished look

FAQ About Temp Fades

Here are some commonly asked questions about temp fades:

Can I do a temp fade on myself?

Yes, you can. It's relatively easy to do a temp fade at home with the right supplies and a little patience.

What guard size should I start with?

Start with a longer guard and work your way down. You can always go shorter later.

How do I blend the hair in smoothly?

Use your clippers and scissors to blend the hair in smoothly, taking care to avoid harsh lines.

How often should I get a temp fade?

It depends on how quickly your hair grows and how often you prefer to get your hair cut. Most people get a temp fade every 2-4 weeks.

Hair Care Tips for Temp Fades

Here are a few tips to keep your temp fade looking great:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo
  • Avoid using too much product, as it can weigh your hair down
  • Keep your hair moisturized with a leave-in conditioner
  • Get regular trims to maintain your fade

With these tips and a little practice, you can achieve a great temp fade haircut at home. Remember to take your time, be patient, and have fun!
