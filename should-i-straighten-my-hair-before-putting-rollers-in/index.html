Should I Straighten My Hair Before Putting Rollers In

Written by Daniel Aug 28, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Rollers are a great way to add volume and curls to your hair. But before you start putting rollers in your hair, you might be wondering whether you should straighten your hair first. In this article, we'll explore the pros and cons of straightening your hair before putting rollers in and answer some frequently asked questions about this topic.

Table of Contents:

  1. Description
  2. How to
  3. Step by step
  4. Tips
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care

Description

Straightening your hair before putting rollers in can help to create a smoother and more uniform base for your curls. It can also help to reduce frizz and make your curls last longer. However, straightening can also cause damage to your hair and make it more prone to breakage.

How to

If you decide to straighten your hair before putting rollers in, here are some steps to follow:

  1. Wash your hair and apply a heat protectant.
  2. Blow dry your hair using a round brush to create a smooth base.
  3. Straighten your hair using a flat iron or straightening brush.
  4. Apply a light hold styling product to help your curls hold.
  5. Section your hair and put in your rollers.
  6. Leave the rollers in for the recommended time and then remove them.
  7. Gently finger comb your hair to separate the curls.

Step by step

Here's a step-by-step guide to putting rollers in your hair:

  1. Wash your hair and let it air dry until it's about 80% dry.
  2. Apply a light hold styling product to your hair.
  3. Section your hair into small sections, starting with the bottom layer.
  4. Take a small section of hair and wrap it around the roller, starting at the ends and rolling towards your scalp.
  5. Secure the roller in place with a clip or bobby pin.
  6. Repeat with the rest of your hair.
  7. Leave the rollers in for the recommended time, usually 20-30 minutes.
  8. Remove the rollers and gently finger comb your hair to separate the curls.
  9. Apply a light hold hairspray to help your curls hold.

Tips

Here are some tips to help you get the best results when using rollers:

  • Choose the right size rollers for your hair. Larger rollers will create bigger curls, while smaller rollers will create tighter curls.
  • Don't overload your hair with too many rollers. This can make your hair too curly and difficult to manage.
  • Use a light hold styling product to help your curls hold.
  • Don't brush or comb your hair after you've put the rollers in. This can cause frizz and ruin your curls.
  • Use a heat protectant when straightening your hair.
  • Don't straighten your hair too often, as this can cause damage and breakage.

FAQ

Q: Can I put rollers in my hair without straightening it first?

A: Yes, you can put rollers in your hair without straightening it first. However, if you have naturally curly or frizzy hair, straightening it first can help to create a smoother base for your curls.

Q: What type of rollers should I use?

A: There are many types of rollers to choose from, including foam rollers, velcro rollers, and hot rollers. The best type of rollers for you will depend on your hair type and the type of curls you want to create. Experiment with different types of rollers to find the ones that work best for you.

Q: How long should I leave the rollers in?

A: The length of time you should leave the rollers in will depend on the type of rollers you're using and the type of curls you want to create. Generally, 20-30 minutes is a good length of time to leave the rollers in.

Hair Care

Using rollers can be a great way to create beautiful curls without using too much heat on your hair. However, it's important to take care of your hair to prevent damage and breakage. Here are some tips for caring for your hair:

  • Use a deep conditioning treatment once a week to keep your hair hydrated and healthy.
  • Don't use too much heat on your hair. If you're using rollers, you may not need to use a curling iron or flat iron as well.
  • Avoid using too many styling products, as this can weigh down your hair and make it look greasy.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends and breakage.
  • Avoid brushing or combing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage.

Overall, whether or not you should straighten your hair before putting rollers in will depend on your hair type and the type of curls you want to create. Experiment with different techniques to find the ones that work best for you, and always take care of your hair to prevent damage and breakage.
