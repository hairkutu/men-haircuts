Why Is My Dog Losing Fur Around His Eyes

Written by William Aug 08, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you notice that your furry friend is losing fur around his eyes, it can be concerning. There are many reasons why this can happen, and it is important to identify the cause to help your dog feel better.

Description

Dogs can lose fur around their eyes due to a variety of reasons, including allergies, infections, or parasites. In some cases, it could also be due to a hormonal imbalance or underlying medical condition.

How to Identify Hair Loss Around Your Dog's Eyes

If you suspect that your dog is losing fur around his eyes, it is important to look for the signs. Here are some common symptoms to watch out for:

  • Red or irritated skin around the eyes
  • Bald patches around the eyes
  • Excessive scratching or rubbing of the eyes
  • Tears or discharge from the eyes

If you notice any of these symptoms, it is important to take your dog to the vet for a proper diagnosis.

Step-by-Step Guide to Treating Hair Loss Around Your Dog's Eyes

Once your vet has identified the cause of your dog's hair loss, they will recommend a treatment plan. Here are some common treatments for hair loss around your dog's eyes:

  1. Medications: If your dog's hair loss is due to an infection or parasite, your vet may prescribe medication to treat the underlying issue.
  2. Special Shampoos: If your dog's hair loss is due to allergies, your vet may recommend a special shampoo to help soothe the skin and reduce itching.
  3. Dietary Changes: In some cases, dietary changes may be necessary to help address hormonal imbalances or underlying medical conditions that are causing hair loss.
  4. Supplements: Your vet may recommend supplements to help support your dog's skin and coat health, especially if the hair loss is due to nutritional deficiencies.

Tips for Preventing Hair Loss Around Your Dog's Eyes

There are some things you can do to help prevent hair loss around your dog's eyes, including:

  • Regular grooming: Regular grooming can help prevent matting and tangles that can cause hair loss.
  • Keeping the area clean: Keeping the area around your dog's eyes clean can help prevent infections and irritation.
  • Using gentle products: When using products around your dog's eyes, make sure to use gentle, non-irritating products.
  • Keeping your dog's diet balanced: A balanced diet can help support your dog's overall health, including the health of their skin and coat.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long does it take for fur to grow back around my dog's eyes?

The time it takes for fur to grow back around your dog's eyes will depend on the underlying cause of the hair loss. In some cases, it may take several weeks or even months for the fur to grow back.

Can stress cause hair loss around my dog's eyes?

Yes, stress can be a contributing factor to hair loss in dogs. If you suspect that your dog's hair loss is due to stress, it is important to work with your vet to identify the cause and develop a treatment plan.

Is hair loss around my dog's eyes always a cause for concern?

While hair loss around your dog's eyes can be a cause for concern, it is not always a serious issue. However, it is important to have your dog examined by a vet to determine the underlying cause and develop an appropriate treatment plan.

Hair Care for Your Dog

Regular grooming and proper nutrition can help keep your dog's skin and coat healthy. Here are some tips for maintaining your dog's hair:

  • Brush your dog's coat regularly to prevent matting and tangles.
  • Bathe your dog with a gentle, non-irritating shampoo designed for dogs.
  • Keep your dog's nails trimmed to prevent them from scratching or injuring their skin.
  • Provide your dog with a balanced diet that includes essential fatty acids and other nutrients that support skin and coat health.

If you notice any changes in your dog's skin or coat, it is important to take them to the vet for an evaluation. Early detection and treatment can help prevent more serious issues down the road.
