How To Fix Messed Up Dyed Hair A Step By Step Guide

Written by Robby Aug 11, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you've recently dyed your hair and it's turned out to be a disaster, don't panic! There are several ways to fix messed up dyed hair without having to chop off all your locks. In this blog post, we'll walk you through the steps you need to take to fix your hair and get it looking beautiful again.

How to Fix Messed Up Dyed Hair

Fixing messed up dyed hair requires a few steps, but the process is simple and can be done at home. Here's what you need to do:

Step 1: Assess the Damage

The first step is to assess the damage done to your hair. Determine the extent of the damage, whether it's just a few patches of uneven color or a full-blown disaster. This step will help you decide what course of action to take.

Step 2: Clarify Your Hair

Clarifying your hair is an important step in fixing messed up dyed hair. Use a clarifying shampoo to remove any build-up on your hair. This will help the color to penetrate your hair more evenly.

Step 3: Choose the Right Products

Choosing the right products is crucial when it comes to fixing messed up dyed hair. Use a color-correcting shampoo or conditioner that's specifically designed for your hair color. These products will help to neutralize any unwanted tones and restore the vibrancy of your hair color.

Step 4: Consider Using a Toner

If your hair has turned out brassy or yellow, consider using a toner to neutralize the unwanted tones. A toner can help to restore the natural color of your hair and give it a more even tone.

Step 5: Deep Condition Your Hair

Deep conditioning your hair is an important step in fixing messed up dyed hair. Use a deep conditioning treatment to help repair any damage done to your hair. This will help to restore the health and shine of your hair.

Step 6: Trim Your Hair

If the damage to your hair is severe, you may need to trim your hair to remove any damaged ends. This will help to prevent further damage and give your hair a fresh start.

Tips for Fixing Messed Up Dyed Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you fix your messed up dyed hair:

  • Avoid washing your hair too often, as this can cause the color to fade more quickly.
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair, as this can cause further damage.
  • Avoid chlorine and saltwater, as these can also cause the color to fade more quickly.
  • Consider using a hair mask once a week to help keep your hair healthy and moisturized.

FAQ

Can I fix my messed up dyed hair at home?

Yes, you can fix your messed up dyed hair at home using the steps outlined in this blog post. However, if the damage is severe, it's best to seek professional help.

How long does it take to fix messed up dyed hair?

The amount of time it takes to fix messed up dyed hair depends on the extent of the damage. It can take anywhere from a few days to a few weeks to get your hair looking beautiful again.

What should I do if my hair is still messed up after following these steps?

If your hair is still messed up after following these steps, it's best to seek professional help. A hair stylist can help to fix any damage and give you the best possible outcome.

Hair Care

It's important to take care of your hair to prevent damage and keep it looking beautiful. Here are some tips for maintaining healthy hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo and conditioner
  • Avoid using hot tools on your hair
  • Deep condition your hair once a week
  • Eat a healthy diet rich in vitamins and minerals
  • Protect your hair from the sun and harsh weather conditions

By following these tips, you can help to keep your hair healthy and beautiful.

Conclusion

Fixing messed up dyed hair is possible with the right products and techniques. By following the steps outlined in this blog post, you can get your hair looking beautiful again in no time. Remember to take care of your hair to prevent damage and maintain its health.
