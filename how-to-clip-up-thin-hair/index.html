How To Clip Up Thin Hair Tips And Tricks

Written by William Sep 04, 2023 Â· 8 min read

If you have thin hair, you know how challenging it can be to find hairstyles that work for you. Whether you are looking for a way to keep your hair out of your face during the day or want to create a stylish updo for a special occasion, clipping your hair up can be a great solution. However, if you don't know how to clip up thin hair properly, you may end up with a hairstyle that falls flat or looks messy. In this article, we will share some tips and tricks to help you clip up your thin hair like a pro.

How to Clip Up Thin Hair

Clipping up thin hair can be a bit tricky, as you need to find the right balance between holding your hair in place and not creating too much tension. Here are the steps to follow:

Step 1: Prepare Your Hair

Start by washing and conditioning your hair. Use a volumizing shampoo and conditioner to add body and thickness to your hair. Towel-dry your hair gently, and apply a heat protectant spray if you plan to use heat styling tools.

Step 2: Choose the Right Clip

Not all hair clips are created equal. If you have thin hair, look for clips that are lightweight, gentle, and have a strong grip. Avoid clips that are too heavy or tight, as they can damage your hair or create a crease. Some good options include claw clips, jaw clips, and bobby pins.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Use a comb to create a section of hair that you want to clip up. You can choose to clip up all your hair or just a portion of it. If you have fine hair, it's best to clip up smaller sections, as too much hair can be heavy and hard to manage.

Step 4: Clip Your Hair

Take your chosen clip and gently gather your hair into a ponytail or bun. Make sure the clip is secure but not too tight. If you want to create volume, tease your hair before clipping it up. You can also use a hair accessory like a headband or scarf to add some flair.

Tips for Clipping Up Thin Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you clip up your thin hair like a pro:

Tip 1: Use a Dry Shampoo

If your hair tends to get oily quickly, use a dry shampoo before clipping it up. This will add texture and hold to your hair, helping it stay in place for longer.

Tip 2: Experiment with Different Clips

There are many types of hair clips available, so don't be afraid to try different ones to see what works best for your hair type and style. Some clips work better for creating volume, while others are better for holding hair in place.

Tip 3: Don't Overdo It

It's tempting to clip up your hair tightly to keep it in place, but this can actually make your hair look flatter and thinner. Use clips sparingly, and aim for a loose, effortless look.

FAQs about Clipping Up Thin Hair

Here are some common questions about clipping up thin hair:

Q: Can I clip up my hair if it's very thin?

A: Yes, you can! Just make sure to choose the right type of clip and be gentle when handling your hair.

Q: How long can I leave my hair clipped up?

A: It's best to give your hair a break from being clipped up for long periods of time. Try not to leave your hair clipped up for more than a few hours at a time, and take breaks throughout the day.

Q: Can I clip up my hair if I have a short haircut?

A: Yes, you can! You may need to use smaller clips or bobby pins, but you can still create a stylish updo with short hair.

Hair Care for Thin Hair

Finally, it's important to take good care of your thin hair to keep it healthy and strong. Here are some tips:

Tip 1: Use a Gentle Shampoo

Choose a shampoo that is gentle and doesn't strip your hair of its natural oils. Look for products that are specifically designed for thin or fine hair.

Tip 2: Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons can damage your hair and make it look even thinner. Try to avoid using these tools as much as possible, or use a heat protectant spray if you must.

Tip 3: Get Regular Trims

Regular trims can help keep your hair looking healthy and prevent split ends. Aim to get a trim every 6-8 weeks.

By following these tips and tricks, you can learn how to clip up your thin hair like a pro. Remember to be gentle with your hair, experiment with different clips, and take good care of your hair to keep it healthy and strong.
