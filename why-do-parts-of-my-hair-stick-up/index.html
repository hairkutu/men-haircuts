Why Do Parts Of My Hair Stick Up

Written by Robby Jun 27, 2023 Â· 7 min read

As much as we love our hair, it can be frustrating when certain parts of it refuse to cooperate. One of the most common hair problems is hair that sticks up in odd places, making us look unkempt and unprofessional. But why does this happen? Is there anything we can do to prevent it? In this article, we'll explore the reasons behind this hair problem and provide some tips on how to deal with it.

Table of Contents:

  1. Description
  2. How To
  3. Step by Step
  4. Tips
  5. FAQ
  6. Hair Care

Description

When we say that parts of our hair "stick up," we're usually referring to hair that is standing up straight or at odd angles, rather than laying flat against our heads. This can happen for a number of reasons:

  • Frizz: If your hair is prone to frizz, it may stick up in all directions, especially in humid weather.
  • Cowlicks: Some people have naturally-occurring cowlicks, which are areas of hair that grow in a circular or spiral pattern. These can be difficult to tame and may stick up no matter what you do.
  • Damaged hair: Hair that is damaged or dry may be more prone to sticking up, especially if it has been styled with heat tools or chemical treatments.

How To

If you're dealing with hair that sticks up, there are a few things you can try to tame it:

Use a smoothing product

A smoothing product, such as a serum or leave-in conditioner, can help weigh down hair that is prone to sticking up. Apply a small amount to your hair (avoiding the roots) and comb through for best results.

Blow-dry your hair

If you're dealing with frizz or flyaways, blow-drying your hair can help smooth it down. Use a round brush to pull your hair taut as you blow-dry, and finish with a blast of cool air to set the style.

Try a different hairstyle

If you have a cowlick or other area of hair that refuses to lay flat, try styling your hair in a way that works with the natural growth pattern. For example, if you have a cowlick at your hairline, try a side part rather than a middle part.

Step by Step

Here are some step-by-step instructions for taming hair that sticks up:

  1. Apply a smoothing product to your hair, avoiding the roots.
  2. Blow-dry your hair using a round brush to pull it taut.
  3. Finish with a blast of cool air to set the style.

Tips

Here are some additional tips for dealing with hair that sticks up:

  • Avoid using heat tools on damaged hair, as this can make the problem worse.
  • Experiment with different hairstyles to find one that works with your hair's natural growth pattern.
  • Consider getting a haircut that is specifically designed to work with your hair's texture and growth pattern.

FAQ

Q: Is there anything I can do to prevent hair from sticking up in the first place?

A: Keeping your hair healthy and moisturized can help prevent frizz and damage, which are two common causes of hair that sticks up. Using a heat protectant spray when styling your hair can also help prevent damage.

Q: Is it bad for my hair to use a lot of product to weigh it down?

A: Using too much product can leave your hair looking greasy and weighed down. Stick to a small amount of product and avoid applying it directly to your roots.

Hair Care

Finally, it's important to take good care of your hair in order to prevent problems like hair that sticks up. Here are some tips for keeping your hair healthy:

  • Wash your hair regularly with a gentle shampoo and conditioner.
  • Avoid using heat tools on a daily basis.
  • Eat a healthy diet that includes plenty of vitamins and minerals.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

By following these tips and tricks, you can say goodbye to hair that sticks up and hello to smooth, sleek locks!
