How Do I Cut My Own Hair With Thinning Shears

Written by Robby Apr 06, 2023 Â· 7 min read

If you're looking to give your hair a little trim and want to use thinning shears, you're in the right place! Thinning shears are great for getting rid of split ends and making your hair look fuller. In this post, we'll go over how to use thinning shears on your own hair, step by step.

Description

Thinning shears are scissors with teeth that are used to remove bulk from hair. They can be used to thin out thick hair or to create texture and movement in fine hair. Thinning shears come in different sizes and shapes, so it's important to choose the right ones for your hair type and the style you want to achieve.

How to Use Thinning Shears on Your Own Hair

Step 1: Gather Your Tools

Before you start cutting your hair with thinning shears, you'll need to gather a few tools:

  • Thinning shears
  • Regular scissors (optional)
  • Comb
  • Hair clips (optional)

Step 2: Wash and Dry Your Hair

It's best to cut your hair when it's clean and dry. If your hair is wet, it will be harder to see how much you're cutting and you may end up taking off more than you intended.

Step 3: Section Your Hair

Use the comb to section your hair into manageable sections. You can use hair clips to keep the sections in place if you need to.

Step 4: Start Cutting

Hold the thinning shears at a 45-degree angle and make small, diagonal cuts into the hair. Start at the bottom of each section and work your way up. Be careful not to cut too much at once, as you can always go back and cut more later.

Step 5: Blend with Regular Scissors (Optional)

If you want a more polished look, you can use regular scissors to blend the hair that you've cut with the thinning shears. This will help to create a smoother, more natural-looking finish.

Step 6: Check Your Work

After you've finished cutting your hair, check it in a mirror to make sure it looks even and the way you want it to. If you need to make any adjustments, go back and use the thinning shears or regular scissors to trim any areas that need it.

Tips for Using Thinning Shears on Your Own Hair

Here are some tips to keep in mind when using thinning shears on your own hair:

  • Start with small cuts and work your way up. You can always cut more, but you can't put hair back once you've cut it.
  • Use the right type of thinning shears for your hair type and the style you want to achieve.
  • Don't use thinning shears on wet hair, as it will be harder to see how much you're cutting.
  • Be patient and take your time. Rushing can lead to uneven cuts and mistakes.

FAQ

Can I use thinning shears on curly hair?

Yes, you can use thinning shears on curly hair. However, you'll need to be careful not to cut too much, as curly hair tends to shrink up when it dries.

Can I use thinning shears to create layers?

Yes, you can use thinning shears to create layers in your hair. However, it's best to leave this to a professional stylist, as it can be tricky to get the layers just right.

Hair Care

After you've cut your hair with thinning shears, it's important to take care of it to keep it healthy and looking its best. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Use a shampoo and conditioner that are suited to your hair type.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your hair.
  • Get regular trims to keep your hair looking fresh and healthy.
  • Use a hair mask or deep conditioner once a week to nourish your hair.

Now that you know how to use thinning shears on your own hair, you can give yourself a little trim whenever you need it. Just remember to take your time and be patient, and you'll have great-looking hair in no time!
