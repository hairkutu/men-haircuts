How To Get Curly Hair For Black Males A Step By Step Guide

Written by Daniel Aug 10, 2023 · 11 min read

Are you a black male who wants to achieve curly hair? You're not alone. Many black men struggle with hair texture and want to switch things up from time to time. Fortunately, there are several ways to get curly hair for black males. In this article, we'll guide you through the process with a step-by-step guide and share some tips and tricks to help you achieve the perfect curls.

How to Get Curly Hair for Black Males

The first step to getting curly hair for black males is to determine your hair type. There are four main hair types: straight, wavy, curly, and coily. Black men typically have hair that falls into the coily or curly category, but it's important to determine your specific hair type so you can choose the right method for achieving curly hair.

Once you've determined your hair type, you can choose the method that works best for you. The three most common methods for achieving curly hair are using a curling iron, using a perm, or using a natural hair product. We'll go into more detail about each of these methods in the next section.

Step 1: Determine Your Hair Type

The first step to getting curly hair is to determine your hair type. This will help you choose the right method for achieving curly hair and ensure that you're using the right products for your hair. To determine your hair type, look at the texture of your hair. Is it straight, wavy, curly, or coily? Black men typically have hair that falls into the coily or curly category.

If you're not sure what your hair type is, you can take a quiz online or visit a hair stylist who can help you determine your hair type.

Step 2: Choose Your Curling Method

There are three main methods for achieving curly hair: using a curling iron, using a perm, or using a natural hair product. Each method has its pros and cons, so it's important to choose the method that works best for you.

Curling Iron: Using a curling iron is a quick and easy way to achieve curly hair. Simply wrap small sections of your hair around the curling iron barrel and hold for a few seconds. This method is best for those who want temporary curls and don't want to commit to a permanent change.

Perm: A perm is a chemical process that permanently changes the texture of your hair. This method is best for those who want long-lasting curls and are willing to commit to a permanent change. However, perms can be damaging to your hair if not done properly.

Natural Hair Product: Using a natural hair product is a great way to achieve curly hair without using chemicals or heat. There are several natural hair products on the market that can help define curls and add volume to your hair.

Step 3: Prepare Your Hair

Before you start curling your hair, it's important to prepare your hair for the process. This means washing your hair with a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to ensure that your hair is clean and hydrated. You can also apply a leave-in conditioner or hair oil to help protect your hair during the curling process.

Step 4: Apply the Product

Once your hair is prepared, it's time to apply the product. If you're using a curling iron, you can apply a heat protectant spray to your hair before you start curling. If you're using a perm, you'll need to apply the perm solution to your hair according to the instructions on the package. If you're using a natural hair product, you can apply the product to your hair while it's damp.

Step 5: Style Your Hair

After you've applied the product, it's time to style your hair. If you're using a curling iron, you can start curling small sections of your hair from the bottom up. If you're using a perm, you'll need to wrap your hair around perm rods and leave the solution on for the amount of time specified on the package. If you're using a natural hair product, you can scrunch your hair to define your curls.

Once your hair is styled, you can use a hair spray or hair gel to hold your curls in place. And voila! You now have curly hair.

Tips for Maintaining Curly Hair

Once you've achieved curly hair, it's important to maintain your curls to keep them looking their best. Here are some tips for maintaining curly hair:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your curls.
  • Sleep with a satin or silk pillowcase to prevent frizz.
  • Avoid brushing or combing your hair when it's dry, as this can cause breakage.
  • Use a diffuser when blow-drying your hair to help define your curls.

FAQs

Can I achieve curly hair without using chemicals or heat?

Yes, you can achieve curly hair without using chemicals or heat. There are several natural hair products on the market that can help define curls and add volume to your hair.

Will a perm damage my hair?

A perm can be damaging to your hair if not done properly. It's important to visit a professional hair stylist who has experience with perms to ensure that your hair isn't damaged during the process.

How often should I wash my curly hair?

It's best to wash your curly hair every 2-3 days to prevent it from drying out. However, this may vary depending on your hair type and personal preference.

Hair Care for Curly Hair

Curly hair requires a bit of extra care to keep it looking its best. Here are some tips for taking care of your curly hair:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools too often, as they can damage your curls.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to detangle your hair when it's wet.
  • Sleep with a satin or silk pillowcase to prevent frizz.
  • Avoid using products that contain sulfates, as they can strip your hair of its natural oils.

With these tips and tricks, you can achieve curly hair as a black male and keep your curls looking their best.
