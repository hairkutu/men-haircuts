How To Fix Over Highlighted Hair At Home A Step By Step Guide

Written by Luffy Jun 03, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you struggling with over highlighted hair? Does your hair look fried, damaged and unhealthy? Don't worry, you're not alone. Over highlighting can cause serious damage to your locks and leave you with a less-than-desirable look. But don't worry, you don't need to go to a salon to fix this problem. You can fix over highlighted hair at home. In this post, we'll give you a step-by-step guide on how to fix your over highlighted hair and get your locks looking healthy and beautiful again.

Description

Over highlighting is a common problem that many of us have experienced. It can happen when you use too much bleach or when you leave the bleach on for too long. Over highlighting can leave your hair looking dry, dull and damaged. But there's no need to panic, because there are things you can do to fix this problem. By using the right products and techniques, you can restore your hair to its former glory.

How to Fix Over Highlighted Hair at Home

Here's what you need to do to fix your over highlighted hair at home:

Step 1: Stop Bleaching Your Hair

The first step in fixing over highlighted hair is to stop bleaching your hair. Avoid any further damage by giving your hair a break from bleach. You can switch to a semi-permanent hair color that's close to your natural hair color to cover up the highlights.

Step 2: Use a Deep Conditioning Treatment

Deep conditioning treatments can help repair damaged hair caused by over highlighting. Look for a treatment that's specifically designed for damaged hair. Apply the treatment to your hair and leave it on for the recommended time. Rinse it out with cool water to seal the cuticle and help your hair retain moisture.

Step 3: Trim Your Hair

If your hair is severely damaged, you may need to trim it. Trimming your hair can help get rid of split ends and prevent further damage. A fresh haircut can also give your hair a healthier appearance.

Step 4: Use a Protein Treatment

Protein treatments can help rebuild the strength of your hair. Look for a protein treatment that's specifically designed for damaged hair. Apply the treatment to your hair and leave it on for the recommended time. Rinse it out with cool water.

Step 5: Avoid Heat Styling

Heat styling can cause further damage to your already over highlighted hair. Avoid using heat styling tools like flat irons and curling irons. If you must use heat styling tools, use a heat protectant spray to help protect your hair from damage.

Tips for Fixing Over Highlighted Hair

Here are some additional tips to help you fix your over highlighted hair:

  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo to avoid stripping your hair of its natural oils.
  • Avoid washing your hair every day to prevent further damage.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your hair instead of a brush.
  • Avoid using hair elastics that can cause breakage.
  • Avoid chlorine and saltwater, which can dry out your hair.

FAQ

Can I fix over highlighted hair at home?

Yes, you can fix over highlighted hair at home by following the steps we've outlined in this post.

What products should I use to fix over highlighted hair?

You should use deep conditioning treatments, protein treatments, and sulfate-free shampoo to fix over highlighted hair.

How long will it take to fix over highlighted hair?

The amount of time it takes to fix over highlighted hair depends on the severity of the damage. It may take several weeks or even months to fully restore your hair.

Hair Care

Proper hair care is essential to keep your hair healthy and prevent damage. Here are some tips to help you take care of your hair:

  • Avoid using harsh chemicals on your hair.
  • Use a heat protectant spray before using heat styling tools.
  • Eat a healthy diet that's rich in vitamins and minerals.
  • Drink plenty of water to keep your hair hydrated.
  • Get regular trims to prevent split ends.

Conclusion

Over highlighting can cause serious damage to your hair, but there are things you can do to fix it. By following the steps we've outlined in this post, you can restore your hair to its former glory. Remember to take care of your hair to prevent further damage and keep it looking healthy and beautiful.
