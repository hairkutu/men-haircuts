Does Your Hair Fall Out During Pregnancy

Written by Luffy Jul 01, 2023 Â· 8 min read

As a woman, pregnancy is one of the most exciting experiences you can have. You get to prepare for a new life, and your body undergoes some significant changes to accommodate the growing fetus. However, not all these changes are pleasant, and one that worries many expectant mothers is hair loss. Does your hair fall out during pregnancy? Let's find out.

Table of Contents

  • Why Hair Loss During Pregnancy?
  • When Does It Happen?
  • How Much Hair Do You Lose?
  • Can You Prevent It?
  • How to Take Care of Your Hair During Pregnancy
  • Frequently Asked Questions

Why Hair Loss During Pregnancy?

During pregnancy, your body's hormones go through a significant shift. This shift can lead to many changes, including the texture and thickness of your hair. The pregnancy hormone, estrogen, can make your hair look healthy and fuller than usual. However, once you give birth, the hormone levels change again.

The sudden change in hormone levels can shock your hair follicles, leading to hair loss. Additionally, the physical stress of childbirth, coupled with the emotional stress of being a new mother, can trigger hair loss.

When Does It Happen?

Postpartum hair loss, also known as telogen effluvium, is common and can start any time from a few weeks to a few months after giving birth. Most women notice hair loss when their baby is around three months old. The hair loss can last up to six months, after which your hair will start growing back.

How Much Hair Do You Lose?

It's normal to lose a few strands of hair every day. During pregnancy, your hair goes into a resting phase, and you lose less hair than usual. However, once you give birth, the hair that should have fallen out during pregnancy starts shedding.

The amount of hair you lose during postpartum varies from woman to woman. Some women only lose a little, while others lose a lot. However, you shouldn't worry too much about it. It's a temporary condition, and your hair will grow back.

Can You Prevent It?

Unfortunately, there's no surefire way to prevent postpartum hair loss. However, there are a few things you can do to minimize the impact.

  • Continue taking your prenatal vitamins.
  • Eat a healthy and balanced diet.
  • Avoid harsh chemicals and treatments, such as hair dyes and relaxers.
  • Gently brush your hair and avoid pulling it back into tight hairstyles.

How to Take Care of Your Hair During Pregnancy

While you can't prevent postpartum hair loss, you can take care of your hair during pregnancy to keep it healthy.

  • Wash your hair regularly with a mild shampoo.
  • Condition your hair after every wash.
  • Avoid using heat-styling tools too often.
  • Trim your hair regularly to prevent split ends.
  • Avoid tight hairstyles that pull on your hair.
  • Wear a hat or scarf when you're out in the sun to protect your hair from UV damage.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does hair loss during pregnancy mean I'm deficient in nutrients?

Not necessarily. Hair loss during pregnancy is a normal occurrence that happens due to hormone fluctuations. However, it's essential to eat a healthy and balanced diet to ensure you and your baby get all the necessary nutrients.

Will my hair grow back after postpartum hair loss?

Yes, your hair will grow back after postpartum hair loss. However, it may take up to a year for your hair to return to its pre-pregnancy state.

Is it safe to dye my hair during pregnancy?

It's best to avoid using hair dyes during pregnancy, especially in the first trimester. While there's no conclusive evidence that hair dyes are harmful to pregnant women, it's better to err on the side of caution.

Can stress cause hair loss during pregnancy?

Yes, stress can cause hair loss during pregnancy. However, it's not the primary cause of postpartum hair loss.

What can I do to make my hair grow back faster?

There's no magical cure for hair loss, but you can take steps to promote hair growth. Eating a healthy and balanced diet, taking your prenatal vitamins, and reducing stress can all help your hair grow back faster.

Conclusion

Postpartum hair loss is a common occurrence that happens due to hormonal changes. It's essential to take care of your hair during pregnancy to keep it healthy. While you can't prevent postpartum hair loss, you can minimize the impact by eating a healthy diet, avoiding harsh chemicals, and taking care of your hair. Remember, postpartum hair loss is temporary, and your hair will grow back.
