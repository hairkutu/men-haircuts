How To Know If A Hairstyle Fits You

How to Know if a Hairstyle Fits You

If you’re thinking about changing your hairstyle but aren’t sure if it will suit you, don’t worry! In this article, we’ll show you how to know if a hairstyle fits you. From face shape to hair texture, there are a few things you should consider before making the change. Read on to find out more.

Table of Contents:

  • Consider Your Face Shape
  • Think About Your Hair Texture
  • Consider Your Lifestyle
  • Know Your Hair Type
  • Take Care of Your Hair
  • FAQs

Consider Your Face Shape

One of the most important factors to consider when choosing a new hairstyle is your face shape. Different styles suit different face shapes, so it’s essential to choose a style that complements your features.

If you have a round face, for example, a long, layered cut can help to elongate your face and make it appear slimmer. If you have a square face, soft layers around your face can help to soften your features. If you’re not sure what your face shape is, ask your hairstylist for advice.

Think About Your Hair Texture

Your hair texture is another important factor to consider when choosing a new hairstyle. If you have fine hair, for example, a blunt bob can help to create the illusion of thickness. If you have curly hair, a layered cut can help to define your curls and add volume.

It’s also important to consider how much time you’re willing to spend styling your hair. If you have thick, unruly hair, a short, low-maintenance cut may be more suitable than a longer, high-maintenance style.

Consider Your Lifestyle

Your lifestyle should also play a role in choosing a new hairstyle. If you’re a busy mom, for example, a low-maintenance style that can be easily styled in the morning may be more suitable than a high-maintenance cut that requires a lot of styling time.

If you’re an athlete or spend a lot of time outdoors, you may want to consider a style that can be easily tied back or secured in place to keep your hair out of your face.

Know Your Hair Type

Knowing your hair type is essential when choosing a new hairstyle. If you have fine, straight hair, for example, a blunt cut can help to add volume and thickness. If you have thick, curly hair, a layered cut can help to define your curls and add shape.

It’s also important to consider any hair issues you may have, such as frizz or breakage. If you have damaged hair, a shorter cut may be more suitable than a longer style that requires a lot of styling and heat tools.

Take Care of Your Hair

Finally, taking care of your hair is essential if you want to keep it looking healthy and shiny. Regular trims, deep conditioning treatments, and using the right products for your hair type can help to keep your hair in great condition.

It’s also important to protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray before styling with hot tools, and avoiding excessive heat styling.

FAQs

Q: How do I know if a hairstyle will suit me?

A: Consider your face shape, hair texture, lifestyle, and hair type when choosing a new hairstyle.

Q: Should I choose a high-maintenance or low-maintenance hairstyle?

A: It depends on your lifestyle and how much time you’re willing to spend styling your hair.

Q: How can I keep my hair looking healthy?

A: Regular trims, deep conditioning treatments, and using the right products for your hair type can help to keep your hair in great condition.

Q: Should I protect my hair from heat damage?

A: Yes, using a heat protectant spray before styling with hot tools, and avoiding excessive heat styling can help to protect your hair from damage.

Now that you know how to choose a hairstyle that suits you, it’s time to head to the salon and make the change. Remember to consider your face shape, hair texture, lifestyle, and hair type when choosing a new style, and take care of your hair to keep it looking healthy and shiny.
