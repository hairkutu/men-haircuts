How To Keep Long Curly Hair Slicked Back A Comprehensive Guide

If you have long curly hair, you know that keeping it slicked back can be a challenge. However, with the right techniques and products, you can achieve a sleek and stylish look that stays in place all day. In this article, we'll provide you with a step-by-step guide on how to keep long curly hair slicked back, as well as tips, FAQs, and hair care advice to help you maintain healthy, beautiful locks.

How to Keep Long Curly Hair Slicked Back

Before we get into the specifics of keeping long curly hair slicked back, it's important to understand what products and tools you'll need. Here's what you'll need:

  • Wide-tooth comb
  • Boar bristle brush
  • Hair elastic
  • Hair gel or pomade
  • Hair spray

Now, let's take a look at the steps you need to follow to keep your long curly hair slicked back:

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Start with clean, damp hair. Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle your curls, starting from the ends and working your way up to the roots.
  2. Apply a small amount of hair gel or pomade to your palms and rub them together. Then, distribute the product evenly throughout your hair, making sure to cover all of your curls.
  3. Using a boar bristle brush, brush your hair back and away from your face. Make sure to brush all of your curls in the same direction.
  4. Once all of your hair is brushed back, use a hair elastic to secure it in a low ponytail. Make sure the ponytail is tight enough to keep your curls in place, but not so tight that it causes discomfort or breakage.
  5. Apply a small amount of hair spray to your hair to set the style in place. Hold the can about six inches away from your head and spray in short bursts.
  6. Use your fingers to smooth down any flyaways or frizz, and add more hair spray if necessary.
  7. Your long curly hair is now slicked back and ready to go! Throughout the day, you may need to touch up your hair with a bit of hair spray or gel to maintain the style.

Tips for Keeping Long Curly Hair Slicked Back

Here are some additional tips to help you keep your long curly hair slicked back:

  • Use a moisturizing shampoo and conditioner to keep your curls hydrated and healthy.
  • Avoid using too much product, as this can weigh down your curls and make them look greasy.
  • Consider using a satin or silk pillowcase to reduce frizz and breakage while you sleep.
  • Try different hair elastics until you find one that works best for your hair type and texture.

FAQs

How often should I wash my long curly hair?

It depends on your hair type and texture, but in general, you should aim to wash your hair every two to three days. Over-washing can strip your hair of its natural oils, which can lead to dryness and breakage.

What's the best way to detangle long curly hair?

Start at the ends and work your way up to the roots, using a wide-tooth comb or your fingers to gently remove any knots or tangles. Avoid brushing your hair when it's wet, as this can cause breakage.

Can I use hair gel or pomade on my curly hair?

Yes, but you should use a small amount and make sure to distribute it evenly throughout your hair. Too much product can weigh down your curls and make them look greasy.

Hair Care for Long Curly Hair

Keeping your long curly hair healthy and beautiful requires a bit of effort, but it's worth it in the end. Here are some tips to help you care for your curls:

  • Use a deep conditioner once a week to help nourish and hydrate your curls.
  • Avoid using heat styling tools, as these can damage your hair and lead to breakage.
  • Consider getting regular trims to keep your ends healthy and prevent split ends.
  • Avoid using harsh chemicals or dyes on your hair, as these can cause damage and dryness.

By following these tips and using the right products and tools, you can keep your long curly hair slicked back and looking its best. With a little patience and practice, you'll be able to achieve the perfect style every time!
