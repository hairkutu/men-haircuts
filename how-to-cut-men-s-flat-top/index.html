How To Cut Men s Flat Top Step By Step Guide

Written by Eveline Aug 14, 2023 · 8 min read

Are you looking to give your client a classic and stylish look? Consider cutting a flat top haircut. This style has stood the test of time and remains a popular choice among men. It is a versatile cut that can be customized to suit each client's facial features and hair texture. Here is a step-by-step guide on how to cut men's flat top.

Description of Men's Flat Top

A flat top is a style where the hair on the top of the head is cut flat, creating a horizontal plane. The sides and back of the hair are usually tapered or faded. The length and shape of the flat top can vary, depending on the client's preference. This style is suitable for a variety of hair types, including straight, wavy, and curly hair.

How to Cut Men's Flat Top

Step 1: Prepare Your Tools

Before you start cutting, make sure you have all the necessary tools. You will need a clipper, scissors, comb, and hair spray. It is also essential to have a good quality mirror to show your client the back of their head.

Step 2: Section the Hair

Start by sectioning the hair on the top of the head. Use a comb to create a rectangular section, with the front hairline as the top boundary and the crown as the bottom boundary. Then, clip the hair on the sides and back of the head to keep them out of the way.

Step 3: Cut the Top Hair

Using scissors, cut the hair on the top of the head to the desired length. Start at the front and work your way towards the back, maintaining the rectangular shape. Use the comb to lift the hair and cut it straight across. You can also use clippers for a faster cut.

Step 4: Taper the Sides and Back

Now it's time to taper the sides and back of the head. Use clippers to create a fade, blending the longer hair on top with the shorter hair on the sides and back. Start with a longer guard and work your way down to a shorter one, creating a gradual transition.

Step 5: Style the Hair

Finally, style the hair using a comb and hair spray. Use the comb to lift the hair on the top of the head and create the flat top shape. Apply hair spray to hold the hair in place.

Tips for Cutting Men's Flat Top

  • Choose the right length for the top hair, depending on the client's face shape and hair texture.
  • Use a mirror to show the client the back of their head and get their feedback.
  • Start with a longer guard when tapering the sides and back and work your way down gradually.
  • Use scissors to create a more precise cut on the top hair.
  • Apply hair spray in small amounts to avoid a stiff and unnatural look.

FAQs About Men's Flat Top

1. How often should I cut my flat top?

It depends on how fast your hair grows and how quickly the style loses its shape. Generally, you should get a trim every four to six weeks to maintain the flat top shape.

2. Can I get a flat top if I have curly hair?

Yes, you can. However, you may need to use a flat iron to straighten the hair on the top of the head before cutting it. Talk to your stylist about the best approach for your hair type.

3. Is a flat top suitable for all face shapes?

A flat top can work for most face shapes. However, it is particularly flattering for clients with a round or square face, as it creates the illusion of a longer and more angular face shape.

Hair Care Tips for Men's Flat Top

  • Wash your hair with a gentle shampoo and conditioner to keep it healthy and moisturized.
  • Avoid using too much hair product, as it can make the hair look greasy and weighed down.
  • Use a wide-tooth comb to detangle the hair without causing damage or breakage.
  • Protect your hair from heat damage by using a heat protectant spray before using a flat iron or blow dryer.
  • Visit your stylist regularly for trims and touch-ups to maintain the shape of your flat top.

Conclusion

A flat top is a classic and stylish haircut that can suit a variety of face shapes and hair types. Follow these steps to cut a men's flat top and use the tips and hair care advice to keep it looking its best. Remember to consult with your client, show them the back of their head, and use a mirror for a precise cut. With a little practice and patience, you can master this timeless haircut.
