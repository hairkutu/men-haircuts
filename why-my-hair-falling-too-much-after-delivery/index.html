Why My Hair Falling Too Much After Delivery Understanding Postpartum Hair Loss

Written by Frank May 05, 2023 Â· 6 min read

After giving birth, many women experience a period of hair loss known as postpartum hair loss. This can be a distressing experience, especially for those who have always had thick and healthy hair. In this article, we will explore the reasons behind why your hair may be falling out after delivery, as well as provide tips and advice on how to care for your hair during this time.

What Causes Postpartum Hair Loss?

During pregnancy, your body experiences an increase in estrogen levels, which can cause hair to become thicker and more lustrous. However, after giving birth, estrogen levels drop dramatically, which can trigger hair loss. This is because the hair follicles enter a resting phase and begin to shed more hair than usual. In some cases, this shedding can be quite severe and can last for several months.

Another factor that can contribute to postpartum hair loss is a lack of nutrients in the mother's diet. During pregnancy, the body prioritizes the baby's needs, which can sometimes result in a deficiency in certain vitamins and minerals. This can lead to hair loss, as well as other symptoms such as fatigue and weakness.

How to Care for Your Hair During Postpartum Hair Loss

If you are experiencing postpartum hair loss, there are several things you can do to care for your hair and promote healthy growth. Here are some tips:

1. Avoid Tight Hairstyles

Tight hairstyles such as braids, ponytails, and buns can put stress on the hair follicles, which can exacerbate hair loss. Instead, opt for loose hairstyles that do not pull on the hair, such as a messy bun or a loose braid.

2. Use Gentle Hair Products

Harsh chemicals in hair products can strip the hair of its natural oils, which can make it more prone to breakage and hair loss. Use gentle, sulfate-free shampoos and conditioners that are designed for thinning hair.

3. Eat a Balanced Diet

Eating a balanced diet that is rich in vitamins and minerals can help to promote healthy hair growth. Be sure to consume plenty of protein, as this is essential for healthy hair.

4. Take Supplements

If you are deficient in certain vitamins and minerals, taking supplements can help to promote healthy hair growth. Be sure to talk to your doctor before taking any supplements.

5. Be Patient

Postpartum hair loss can be a frustrating experience, but it is important to be patient and not panic. In most cases, hair will grow back on its own within a few months.

FAQ

1. How long does postpartum hair loss last?

Postpartum hair loss typically lasts for 3-6 months, but can sometimes last up to a year.

2. Can postpartum hair loss be prevented?

Unfortunately, postpartum hair loss cannot be prevented. However, taking good care of your hair and eating a balanced diet can help to promote healthy hair growth.

3. Should I see a doctor if I am experiencing postpartum hair loss?

If you are experiencing severe hair loss or other symptoms such as fatigue and weakness, you should see a doctor to rule out any underlying medical conditions.

Conclusion

Postpartum hair loss can be a challenging experience, but it is important to remember that it is a normal part of the postpartum period. By taking good care of your hair and eating a balanced diet, you can help to promote healthy hair growth and minimize the effects of hair loss. Remember to be patient and kind to yourself during this time.
