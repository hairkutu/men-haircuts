How To Go From Perm To Natural Hair Without Cutting It

Written by Daniel Apr 02, 2023 · 6 min read

If you've been thinking about transitioning from relaxed hair to natural hair, but the thought of cutting your hair is not appealing, then this blog post is for you. Going from perm to natural hair without cutting it is possible, and it's a process that requires patience, commitment, and a lot of love for your hair. In this blog post, we'll show you how to do it step-by-step, provide you with some tips, and answer some frequently asked questions. So, let's get started!

Table of Content

  • Description
  • How to
  • Step by step
  • Tips
  • FAQ
  • Hair Care

Description

Perming your hair involves using chemicals to break down the natural curl pattern of your hair. This process leaves your hair straight, but it can also damage your hair and scalp. Transitioning from permed to natural hair without cutting it involves allowing your hair to grow out while taking care of both the new growth and the relaxed ends. This process can take anywhere from six months to a year or longer, depending on the length of your hair and your hair care routine.

How to

The first step in transitioning from perm to natural hair without cutting it is to stop getting perms. This will allow your natural hair to grow out, and you'll start to see a line of demarcation where your natural hair meets the relaxed ends. You'll need to take extra care of your hair during this period because your hair will be fragile at the line of demarcation. You can do this by:

Step by step

Step 1: Stop getting perms

Step 2: Moisturize your hair regularly

Step 3: Detangle your hair gently

Step 4: Use protective hairstyles

Step 5: Trim your ends as needed

Tips

Here are some tips to help you transition from perm to natural hair without cutting it:

  • Be patient - this process takes time
  • Use a sulfate-free shampoo
  • Avoid heat styling tools
  • Use a deep conditioner weekly
  • Consult a professional stylist for advice

FAQ

Q: Will my hair look weird during the transition?

A: Your hair may look uneven during the transition period, but it's a normal part of the process. You can use protective hairstyles to hide the two different textures and reduce breakage.

Q: How long will it take for my hair to grow out?

A: The length of time it takes for your hair to grow out depends on the length of your hair and your hair care routine. It can take anywhere from six months to a year or longer.

Hair Care

During the transition period, it's essential to take extra care of your hair to prevent breakage and damage. Here are some hair care tips:

  • Moisturize your hair regularly with a leave-in conditioner
  • Detangle your hair gently using a wide-tooth comb
  • Use protective hairstyles to reduce breakage
  • Trim your ends as needed to prevent split ends
  • Avoid heat styling tools, such as flat irons and curling irons

Transitioning from perm to natural hair without cutting it is a journey that requires patience, commitment, and a lot of love for your hair. By following these steps and tips, you can successfully transition to natural hair without cutting it. Remember to take care of your hair during the transition period, and if you need advice or guidance, consult a professional stylist.
